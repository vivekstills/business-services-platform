# FDI filing (foreign direct investment reporting)

## Overview

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) filing is mandatory under the **Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999** and is administered by the **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)**. When an Indian company receives foreign investment through equity, compulsorily convertible instruments, or other approved routes, the transaction must be reported on the **FIRMS** portal (Foreign Investment Reporting and Management System) within the timelines the RBI sets for the relevant form.

FDI compliance is not optional. Late or incomplete reporting can lead to compounding, scrutiny, and constraints on follow-on investment. The obligation applies to startups with NRI angels, private and public companies with offshore investors, and wholly owned subsidiaries receiving capital from overseas parents, subject to the facts of each case.

We support accurate **FC-GPR** and **FC-TRS** class filings, sector cap and route checks, **FMV**-aligned pricing, and downstream investment reporting. Confirm portal names, form versions, and timelines on the **RBI** website for your filing year.

## When FDI reporting applies (summary)

- Indian companies (and **LLPs** where FEMA allows) that receive **foreign** investment
- Issue of equity, preference shares, or **CCDs/CCPs** to non-residents
- **Resident** / **non-resident** **share** transfers that trigger **FC-TRS**-class reporting
- **FPI** positions crossing thresholds (as per current **RBI/SEBI** rules)
- **Downstream** investments by FDI-funded entities, where reporting applies
- **JVs** and structures where foreign investment is placed into India
- **ESOP** or similar grants to **non-residents** where a reporting form is prescribed

FEMA and RBI reporting are **central**: applicable across all states and UTs. Sectoral policy (DPIIT **Consolidated FDI Policy** and **automatic** vs **government** route) is separate from, but must align with, the **FIRMS** **reporting** line.

## Key reporting forms (indicative)

| Form | Purpose | Typical timeline |
|------|---------|------------------|
| **FC-GPR** | Allotment of instruments to a foreign investor | **30** days of **allotment** (confirm circular) |
| **FC-TRS** | Share transfer between resident and non-resident | **60** days from transfer / remittance, as per rule |
| **LLP-I** / **LLP-II** | LLP FDI in / change in LLP FDI | **30** / **60** day class windows (see current RBI text) |
| **ESOP** | Grant to **non-residents** | **30** days of **allot** (as prescribed) |
| **DI** | Downstream | **30** days |
| **CN** | Convert ECB or fee into equity | **30** days |

## Valuation and pricing (short)

- Allotment to non-residents must be at or above **FMV** from a **SEBI**-registered merchant banker or a **Chartered Accountant** (DCF, or **NAV** when permitted).
- Transfers to non-residents **below** FMV, or to residents **above** FMV, are generally not allowed in the FEMA pricing framework. Map each tranche with your FEMA and tax team.

## Benefits

- Avoids **FEMA** compounding and keeps a defensible, auditable cap table and **RBI** record for **diligence**, **follow-on** rounds, and **exit** work.
- Investors and their counsel expect **FIRMS**-level proof before further tranches.
- Repatriation and corporate actions (dividend, buyback) sit on a cleaner record when the **inward** leg is properly reported.
- **IPO/DRHP** and **M&A** teams often review **FEMA** history; timely filings reduce **friction** in **Q&A**.

## Limitations

- **Negative** and **prohibited** **sectors** cannot receive FDI (policy list).
- **FMV** rules reduce freedom to price **tranches** at steep **discounts**.
- **Downstream** and **group** **structures** add more reporting and mapping work.
- **Government**-route sectors can be **slow** (weeks to months).
- **LLP** FDI is only in sectors with **100%** automatic FDI. LLPs cannot use the government route for FDI in the way companies can in some sectors.
- **OPC** cannot accept FDI. Convert to a private limited company first if foreign equity is required.

## Process (typical)

1. Map sector, FDI cap, automatic vs government route, and the negative list. Confirm the investee can legally receive the investment.
2. Obtain **FMV** certificate (merchant banker or **CA** as applicable).
3. Route inward remittance through the **AD** bank and obtain **FIRC** (or bank certificate as required).
4. Pass board and shareholder steps as the **Companies Act** requires; allot shares in time; file **PAS-3** to MCA in the statutory window.
5. File on **FIRMS** (e.g. **FC-GPR** or the correct form) with KYC and remittance evidence.
6. The **AD** bank authenticates the filing on **FIRMS** as per process.
7. Retain the RBI acknowledgement and reference (**UIN**) for audit, FLA, and follow-on tranches.
8. Meet annual **FLA** and any other FEMA returns that apply to your fact pattern (see the FLA and ODI pages where relevant).

## Applicability

FIRMS and FEMA reporting are **pan-India**. The **DPIIT Consolidated FDI Policy** and **RBI** master directions still fix sector caps and automatic vs government route for what inward FDI is allowed, separate from the reporting obligation.

## Compliance reminders

- **FC-GPR** generally within 30 days of allotment; **FC-TRS** within 60 days of the transfer, subject to the rules in force.
- **FLA Return** by **15 July** for entities with FDI/ODI stock (see FLA service).
- **Downstream** **investment** reporting where applicable. Late filings may need **compounding** before RBI; keep the **AD** bank in the loop.

## Frequently asked questions

**If I miss the 30-day FC-GPR deadline, what happens?** Late filing is a FEMA default. You typically file a **compounding** application before RBI. Penalties depend on the amount and delay. Seek compounding early; repeated or large cases can draw wider scrutiny.

**Can a small startup skip valuation?** **No**. Every issue to a non-resident must be at or above **FMV** certified as per FEMA, generally without a de minimis exemption for small cheques in the way founders hope.

**Can an OPC take FDI?** **No** under the usual FEMA position. The entity should convert to a **private** limited company if foreign equity is required.

**FDI vs FPI for reporting?** Broadly, **FDI** is a ≥10% style strategic stake and uses **FIRMS** (FC-GPR, FC-TRS). **FPI** is typically below 10% via the SEBI route. If a holder crosses 10%, conversion to the FDI reporting line may be required in the prescribed window. Confirm the live circular.

**Is FC-TRS needed when both parties are non-residents?** For shares of an **Indian** company, **FC-TRS**-style reporting can still be required in the 60-day window. The **Indian** company and any **resident** link in the chain, plus **AD** bank routes, are central to who signs what on **FIRMS**.
