# Private Limited Company Registration

## Overview

A Private Limited Company is the most preferred business structure in India for startups, SMEs, and growing businesses. Governed by the Companies Act, 2013, it offers a separate legal identity, limited liability, perpetual succession, and ease of raising capital. It's the go-to structure for venture capital, angel investment, and structured growth. Over 10 lakh private limited companies are registered in India.

## Features

Minimum 2 shareholders and 2 directors are required, with at least one director being an Indian resident. Maximum 200 shareholders are allowed. There is no minimum capital requirement. Shares are privately held and cannot be publicly traded. The word "Private Limited" must appear at the end of the name. It cannot issue prospectus to the public.

## Advantages

Private limited companies offer limited liability protection, separate legal entity status, perpetual succession, ease of fundraising from investors, enhanced credibility with banks and vendors, tax benefits under Section 115BAA (22%) or 115BAB (15% for new manufacturers), and easy transferability of shares. Startups enjoy additional incentives under DPIIT recognition.

## Limitations

Compliance is stringent ,  including mandatory board meetings, AGM, annual filings (AOC-4, MGT-7), statutory audit regardless of turnover, and DIR-3 KYC. Shares cannot be freely transferred without board approval. Cannot invite public subscription. Operational costs are higher than proprietorship/partnership/LLP. Dissolution requires formal winding-up process.

## Applicability

Private limited company registration is available across all Indian states and UTs. Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi NCR, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, and Bihar see the highest volume of registrations. Incorporation is centralized through the MCA portal.

## Process

Obtain DSC for all directors. Reserve the name through SPICe+ Part A (2 name options, up to 1 resubmission). File SPICe+ Part B along with MOA, AOA, INC-9, AGILE-PRO (for PAN, TAN, GST, EPFO, ESIC, bank account), and other declarations. On approval, the Certificate of Incorporation is issued along with PAN, TAN, and other registrations. Open a bank account to commence business.

## Timeline

Private limited company registration typically takes 10 - 15 working days. DSC: 1-2 days. Name approval: 2-3 days. SPICe+ filing and incorporation: 7-10 days. Bank account activation: 3-7 days post-incorporation.

## FAQs

**Q1. What is the minimum capital required?** There is no minimum paid-up capital requirement for a private limited company. You can incorporate with as low as Rs. 1 of paid-up capital, though authorized capital carries associated stamp duty.

**Q2. Can a private limited company be converted into a public limited company?** Yes, a private limited company can be converted into a public limited company by altering its articles of association, passing a special resolution, and filing Form MGT-14 and INC-27 with the MCA.

**Q3. Is GST registration automatic with incorporation?** GST can be applied for through the AGILE-PRO form at the time of incorporation. It is optional and becomes mandatory only when the turnover threshold is crossed or interstate supply is made.

**Q4. Can a foreign national be a director of an Indian private limited company?** Yes, a foreign national can be a director, subject to obtaining DIN. However, at least one director on the board must be a resident of India (having stayed at least 182 days in the previous calendar year).

**Q5. What is the difference between authorized capital and paid-up capital?** Authorized capital is the maximum capital a company is legally permitted to issue to shareholders. Paid-up capital is the actual capital received from shareholders against issued shares.
