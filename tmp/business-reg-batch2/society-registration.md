# Society Registration

## Overview

A Society is a non-profit organization formed for promoting literary, scientific, charitable, educational, social, cultural, or sports-related purposes. It is governed by the Societies Registration Act, 1860 (central), along with respective state amendments. Societies are popular for educational institutions, cultural associations, sports clubs, welfare associations, and community organizations. Registration is done with the state Registrar of Societies.

## Features

A society requires a minimum of 7 members for state-level registration and 8 (with members from 7 different states) for national-level registration. It is governed by a Memorandum of Association (MOA) and Rules & Regulations. A Governing Body manages day-to-day affairs. Members do not have personal financial interest in the society. It has a separate legal identity once registered.

## Advantages

Societies offer a simple, low-cost formation process for charitable purposes. They are eligible for 12A and 80G tax exemptions, CSR funds, and government grants. Compliance is relatively moderate compared to Section 8 companies. They can accept donations, including foreign donations with FCRA registration. Membership-based democratic governance allows collective decision-making.

## Limitations

Societies are regulated at the state level, leading to variations in procedures across states. They have lower credibility than Section 8 companies for institutional funders. Governance is often diluted due to membership-based voting. Dissolution can be complex. Not suitable for commercial activities ,  only for charitable, literary, or similar purposes listed in the Act.

## Applicability

Society registration is state-specific under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, with state amendments. States like Maharashtra (Bombay Public Trusts Act also applies), Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Delhi, Gujarat, West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Kerala, Punjab, Haryana, and Rajasthan have their own variations. Societies can be state-level or national-level.

## Process

Choose a unique society name that does not conflict with an existing society. Draft the MOA (containing name, objects, members) and Rules & Regulations. Hold the first meeting and appoint the governing body (President, Secretary, Treasurer, etc.). Execute MOA and Rules on non-judicial stamp paper. File with the Registrar of Societies in the prescribed format along with fees. Upon scrutiny, the Certificate of Registration is issued.

## Timeline

Society registration typically takes 15 - 30 days depending on the state. Drafting documents: 2-3 days. Filing with ROS: same day. Scrutiny and approval: 10-25 days. PAN issuance: 7-15 days. 12A/80G application post-registration: 1-3 months.

## FAQs

**Q1. What is the difference between a society and a trust?** A society is governed by members democratically with an elected governing body, while a trust is managed by trustees. Societies are suitable for membership-based organizations, while trusts are ideal for fixed-purpose charitable activities.

**Q2. Can a society carry out commercial activities?** Societies cannot engage in profit-making commercial activities. However, they can generate income through activities incidental to their charitable objectives (e.g., a hospital society charging patient fees).

**Q3. Can a society be registered at both state and national levels?** Yes, a society with members from at least 7 different states can be registered nationally with the Registrar of Societies in Delhi. State-level societies operate within state boundaries.

**Q4. Is annual compliance required for societies?** Yes, societies must file an annual list of governing body members with the Registrar, maintain books of accounts, undergo audit, and file income tax returns (ITR-7). FCRA-registered societies must file FC-4 annually.

**Q5. How can a society accept foreign donations?** A society can accept foreign contributions only after obtaining FCRA registration from the Ministry of Home Affairs. FCRA requires the society to be at least 3 years old and have spent Rs. 10 lakh on its objectives.
