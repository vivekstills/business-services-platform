# Indian Subsidiary OF Foreign Company Registration

## Overview

An Indian Subsidiary is a company incorporated in India where a foreign entity holds more than 50% of the paid-up share capital. It is governed by the Companies Act, 2013, FEMA, 1999, and RBI regulations. This is the most popular mode for foreign companies to establish operations in India, as it offers limited liability, 100% FDI under the automatic route (in most sectors), and perpetual succession.

## Features

The subsidiary has a separate legal identity from its parent company. It can be a Wholly-Owned Subsidiary (100% foreign ownership) or partly owned. Minimum of 2 shareholders and 2 directors are required, with at least one director being an Indian resident. There is no minimum paid-up capital requirement. FDI is permitted under automatic or approval routes depending on the sector.

## Advantages

Foreign parents enjoy limited liability and easy repatriation of profits. India offers a large consumer market, skilled workforce, and competitive costs. The subsidiary can independently enter into contracts, own property, sue and be sued. It enables localization of operations, tax efficiency through DTAA benefits, and easier access to Indian banking and labour.

## Limitations

Compliance is extensive ,  including MCA filings, RBI reporting, transfer pricing, and tax audits. Sectors like retail multi-brand, defence, and atomic energy have FDI restrictions. FEMA compliance for inward remittances and share allotments is strict. Repatriation requires proper documentation and compliance with transfer pricing norms.

## Applicability

Indian subsidiaries can be incorporated across all Indian states ,  Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi NCR, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, and UTs. States like Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Telangana are top FDI destinations due to their IT infrastructure and pro-business policies.

## Process

The process begins with obtaining DSCs for all proposed directors. Name approval is sought through SPICe+ Part A. Incorporation documents (SPICe+ Part B, AGILE-PRO, INC-9, MOA, AOA) are filed along with declarations. Post-approval, the Certificate of Incorporation, PAN, TAN, and bank account are issued. RBI reporting through FC-GPR is done within 30 days of share allotment.

## Timeline

Overall incorporation typically takes 15 - 25 working days. DSC: 1-2 days. Name approval: 2-3 days. Apostille of foreign documents: 7-15 days. SPICe+ filing and incorporation: 7-10 days. PAN, TAN, bank account: 3-5 days post-incorporation.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can a foreign company own 100% of an Indian subsidiary?** Yes, in most sectors under the automatic FDI route. Certain sectors like multi-brand retail, defence, and insurance have caps or require government approval.

**Q2. Is it necessary to have an Indian resident director?** Yes, at least one director on the board must be a resident of India, having stayed at least 182 days in the previous calendar year.

**Q3. How are profits of an Indian subsidiary repatriated?** Profits can be repatriated as dividends (after payment of Dividend Distribution Tax or TDS), royalty, technical fees, or buyback of shares, subject to FEMA compliance.

**Q4. What is FC-GPR and when is it filed?** FC-GPR is a form filed with RBI within 30 days of allotting shares to foreign investors, reporting the inward remittance and share allotment.

**Q5. Are foreign directors required to visit India for incorporation?** No, foreign directors can complete the entire process remotely using apostilled documents and Digital Signature Certificates obtained using their passport.
