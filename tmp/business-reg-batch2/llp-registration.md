# LLP Registration

## Overview

A Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) is a hybrid business structure introduced in India through the LLP Act, 2008. It combines the flexibility of a traditional partnership with the limited liability benefits of a company. LLPs are ideal for professional firms, small businesses, and startups that want to avoid the rigidity of a company structure while enjoying corporate advantages.

## Features

An LLP has a separate legal identity and perpetual succession. Minimum 2 designated partners are required, with at least one being an Indian resident. There is no minimum capital requirement. Partners have limited liability up to their contribution. The LLP is governed by the LLP Agreement between partners. It has fewer compliance requirements than private limited companies.

## Advantages

LLPs offer limited liability protection, separate legal entity status, no minimum capital requirement, lower compliance costs, no audit requirement up to specified thresholds, no dividend distribution tax, and flexible internal management through the LLP Agreement. Profits are taxed once at the LLP level, and partners' share of profit is tax-free.

## Limitations

LLPs cannot raise equity from the public or issue shares, making them unsuitable for venture capital funding. Some foreign investors and lenders prefer private limited companies over LLPs. FDI in LLPs is allowed only in sectors with 100% FDI under the automatic route. Conversion into a company requires approval and formalities.

## Applicability

LLP registration is available pan-India in states such as Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, and Union Territories. The registration is centralized through the MCA portal irrespective of the state.

## Process

Start by obtaining DSC for designated partners. File Form RUN-LLP for name reservation (2 name options). Once approved, file FiLLiP form for incorporation along with subscriber sheet. On approval, the Certificate of Incorporation is issued with LLPIN. Within 30 days, file Form 3 with the LLP Agreement. Apply for PAN, TAN, and bank account.

## Timeline

LLP registration typically takes 10 - 15 working days. DSC: 1-2 days. Name approval: 2-3 days. FiLLiP filing and incorporation: 5-7 days. LLP Agreement filing (Form 3): within 30 days of incorporation. PAN, TAN, bank account: 5-7 days post-incorporation.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can an LLP be converted into a private limited company?** Yes, an LLP can be converted into a private limited company through a prescribed process under Section 366 of the Companies Act, 2013, with approval from MCA.

**Q2. What is the minimum capital required for an LLP?** There is no minimum capital requirement for LLP registration. Partners can contribute any amount as mutually decided in the LLP Agreement.

**Q3. Is audit mandatory for an LLP?** Audit is mandatory only if annual turnover exceeds Rs. 40 lakh or the contribution exceeds Rs. 25 lakh. Otherwise, LLPs are exempt from statutory audit.

**Q4. Can a foreign national be a partner in an LLP?** Yes, foreign nationals and NRIs can be partners in an LLP, subject to FEMA and FDI regulations. However, at least one designated partner must be a resident of India.

**Q5. What is the difference between a partner and designated partner?** Designated partners are responsible for the LLP's statutory compliance and are named in the incorporation document, while other partners contribute capital and share profits but have no compliance obligations.
