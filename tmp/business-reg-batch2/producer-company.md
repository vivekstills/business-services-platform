# Producer Company Registration

## Overview

A Producer Company is a corporate form specifically designed for primary producers such as farmers, agriculturists, dairy farmers, fishermen, horticulturists, and artisans. Introduced under Part IXA of the Companies Act, 1956 (now reflected in Chapter XXIA of the Companies Act, 2013), it combines the benefits of a cooperative society with the efficiency and governance of a private limited company. Its primary objective is to facilitate production, harvesting, procurement, marketing, and sale of produce by its members.

## Features

Minimum 10 individual producers OR 2 producer institutions OR a combination of both are required. Minimum 5 directors are required, with a maximum of 15. There is no maximum limit on members. Minimum authorized capital is Rs. 5 lakh. The name must end with "Producer Company Limited". Only primary producers can be members. Operates on the principle of "one member, one vote" irrespective of shareholding.

## Advantages

Producer Companies enjoy the benefits of limited liability, separate legal identity, perpetual succession, and democratic governance. Members receive patronage bonus based on their business with the company. Income from agricultural activities is eligible for tax exemptions under Section 10(1). They can avail government subsidies, NABARD funding, and other agricultural schemes. Surplus is distributed as bonus and limited return on share capital.

## Limitations

Only primary producers can be members ,  non-producers are excluded. Business activities are restricted to those listed in Section 581B (production, harvesting, processing, marketing of members' produce). Shares are not freely transferable ,  only among members. Voting rights are capped at one vote per member. Raising external equity is limited. Complex governance structure compared to private limited companies.

## Applicability

Producer Companies are popular in agricultural states like Maharashtra, Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Rajasthan, West Bengal, Kerala, Bihar, and Odisha. Government of India and NABARD actively promote Producer Companies through FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) schemes.

## Process

Obtain DSCs for all directors. Apply for name approval via SPICe+ Part A (name must end with "Producer Company Limited"). File SPICe+ Part B with MOA (with producer-specific objects), AOA, INC-9, AGILE-PRO, and proof of members being primary producers. On approval, Certificate of Incorporation, PAN, TAN are issued. Open bank account and commence activities.

## Timeline

Producer Company registration takes 15 - 25 working days. DSC: 1-2 days. Name approval: 2-3 days. SPICe+ filing (with producer proof verification): 10-15 days. Bank account and NABARD/FPO empanelment can take additional 15-30 days.

## FAQs

**Q1. Who can be a member of a Producer Company?** Only primary producers such as farmers, agriculturists, horticulturists, fishermen, artisans, beekeepers, and similar individuals, or institutions of primary producers, can be members of a Producer Company.

**Q2. What tax benefits are available to Producer Companies?** Income derived from agricultural activities is 100% exempt under Section 10(1). Additionally, Section 80PA allows 100% deduction on profits from specified activities (marketing of agricultural produce) for FPOs with turnover up to Rs. 100 crore.

**Q3. Can a Producer Company raise FDI?** No, FDI is not allowed in Producer Companies. However, they can raise funds through NABARD, SFAC, government schemes, and grants under FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) initiatives.

**Q4. What is the minimum capital requirement?** Minimum authorized capital is Rs. 5 lakh for a Producer Company. There is no minimum paid-up capital prescribed, but sufficient capital should be subscribed to meet operational needs.

**Q5. How is a Producer Company different from a cooperative?** Producer Companies are governed by the Companies Act and MCA (central regulation), offering corporate-style governance, while cooperatives are state-registered under respective Cooperative Societies Acts with significant state control and restrictions.
