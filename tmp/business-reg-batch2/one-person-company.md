# ONE Person Company Registration

## Overview

A One Person Company (OPC) is a distinctive business structure introduced under the Companies Act, 2013 that enables a single entrepreneur to operate a corporate entity with limited liability. OPC combines the benefits of sole proprietorship and private limited company, offering solo entrepreneurs the privileges of a separate legal entity, perpetual succession, and easier access to credit.

## Features

An OPC has only one shareholder (who is also typically the sole director). A nominee must be appointed at the time of incorporation, who takes over if the sole member dies or becomes incapacitated. The suffix "OPC Private Limited" is used. There is no requirement for AGM. Only Indian resident citizens can form an OPC.

## Advantages

OPC provides limited liability protection to the single owner, a separate legal identity, perpetual existence, easier access to bank loans, better credibility than proprietorship, and fewer compliance requirements than private limited companies. It allows a solo entrepreneur to participate in tenders and scale up professionally.

## Limitations

Only Indian residents (staying 120+ days in the previous FY) can form an OPC. A person can be a member of only one OPC at a time. Conversion to private limited is mandatory if paid-up capital exceeds Rs. 50 lakh or turnover exceeds Rs. 2 crore for 3 consecutive years (this threshold restriction was later relaxed but conversion triggers still apply in certain cases). FDI is not allowed in an OPC. NRIs can now form an OPC, but certain restrictions apply.

## Applicability

OPC registration is available across all states and UTs including Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Punjab, Bihar, Odisha, and others. Registration is centralized through the MCA portal.

## Process

Obtain DSC for the sole director. Apply for name reservation via SPICe+ Part A. File SPICe+ Part B along with MOA, AOA, INC-3 (nominee consent), INC-9 (declaration), and AGILE-PRO (for PAN, TAN, GST, EPFO, ESIC, bank account). Post-approval, the Certificate of Incorporation, PAN, TAN, and other registrations are issued. Open a bank account to commence operations.

## Timeline

OPC registration typically takes 10 - 15 working days. DSC: 1-2 days. Name approval: 2-3 days. SPICe+ filing and incorporation: 7-10 days. Bank account and post-incorporation setup: 3-5 days.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can an NRI form an OPC?** Yes, as per the 2021 amendment, NRIs can form an OPC in India. Prior to this, only resident Indians could form OPCs. The residency requirement is now 120 days in the preceding financial year.

**Q2. Who is a nominee in an OPC and what role does the nominee play?** A nominee is a person who takes over the OPC in the event of the member's death or incapacity. The nominee must be an Indian citizen and must consent in writing (Form INC-3).

**Q3. Is OPC required to hold AGM?** No, OPCs are exempt from holding Annual General Meetings. However, they must file annual returns (MGT-7A) and financial statements (AOC-4) with the MCA.

**Q4. When must an OPC convert into a Pvt Ltd Company?** The mandatory conversion threshold (based on paid-up capital of Rs. 50 lakh or average turnover of Rs. 2 crore) was removed in 2021, allowing OPCs to grow without mandatory conversion. Voluntary conversion is allowed anytime after 2 years.

**Q5. Can one person form multiple OPCs?** No, a person can be a member of only one OPC at a time. The same restriction applies to being a nominee in more than one OPC.
