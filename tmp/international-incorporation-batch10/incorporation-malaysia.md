# Company incorporation in Malaysia

## Overview

Malaysia is an increasingly popular destination for Indian entrepreneurs seeking a Southeast Asian base. With a majority English-speaking business environment, strong digital infrastructure, MSC Malaysia status benefits for tech companies, and competitive operating costs, Malaysia offers a compelling mid-tier option between Hong Kong/Singapore and lower-cost ASEAN markets. The primary incorporation structure for foreign investors is the Sendirian Berhad (Sdn Bhd), the equivalent of a private limited company.

Malaysia is also a popular destination for Indian IT firms, BPO/KPO services, and manufacturing operations targeting ASEAN markets, thanks to its ASEAN membership, bilateral trade ties with India, and Malaysia-India DTAA.

## Key benefits

- Competitive corporate tax rate: 24% (standard) with SME concessions
- Pioneer Status and Investment Tax Allowance (ITA) for qualifying tech and manufacturing sectors
- MSC Malaysia (Multimedia Super Corridor) status for digital/tech companies, significant tax incentives
- 100% foreign ownership allowed in most sectors (subject to equity guidelines in regulated industries)
- English widely used in business and government
- Strong ASEAN supply chain access, Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia
- Lower operational costs than Singapore
- India-Malaysia DTAA and India-ASEAN FTA
- Labuan IBFC (International Business and Financial Centre), offshore business structure available

## Limitations

- Some sectors (retail, agriculture, certain services) require local Bumiputera (indigenous Malay) equity participation
- At least one locally resident director required
- Not as globally prestigious as Singapore for international investors
- Banking for non-resident Indian founders can be slow and documentation-heavy
- Bumiputera equity policies can limit 100% foreign control in certain sectors
- Company Secretary must be a Malaysian resident member of MAICSA or a licensed company secretary

## Documents required

For Indian founders incorporating a Sdn Bhd in Malaysia:

- Passport copy (certified true copy) of all shareholders and directors
- Residential address proof
- Email address and contact details of all directors and shareholders
- Proposed company name (3 options recommended)
- Company objectives / nature of business
- Registered office address in Malaysia
- Consent to Act as Director (Form 49 equivalent)
- For Indian corporate shareholder: apostilled Certificate of Incorporation, Board Resolution, MoA/AoA

## Step-by-step process

1. **Step 1 - Name Reservation:** Search and reserve company name on MyCoID portal (Companies Commission of Malaysia, SSM). Name availability confirmed in 1–2 days.
2. **Step 2 - Prepare Incorporation Documents:** Constitution (if adopting custom), director/shareholder consent forms, registered agent documents.
3. **Step 3 - File with SSM (Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia):** Submit incorporation application online through MyCoID 2016. All directors must have a MyCoID account (foreigners can register online).
4. **Step 4 - Certificate of Incorporation:** Issued digitally. In Malaysia, the Certificate of Incorporation is now replaced by the Notice of Registration upon incorporation under the Companies Act 2016.
5. **Step 5 - Appoint Company Secretary:** Must be appointed within 30 days of incorporation. Must be a licensed company secretary under MAICSA.
6. **Step 6 - Open Bank Account:** Major banks include Maybank, CIMB, RHB, Public Bank, HSBC Malaysia.
7. **Step 7 - Tax Registration:** Register with LHDN (Inland Revenue Board) for Income Tax and IRBM for SST (if applicable).
8. **Step 8 - Work Passes & Employment:** Malaysia Employment Pass required for foreign directors/employees. MM2H (Malaysia My Second Home) visa for longer-term residency.

## Tax implications for Indian founders

Malaysia's standard corporate tax is 24%. SMEs with paid-up capital ≤ MYR 2.5 million pay 17% on the first MYR 600,000 of chargeable income.

Pioneer Status (up to 10 years tax exemption) and Investment Tax Allowance are available for qualifying manufacturing and tech companies through MIDA (Malaysian Investment Development Authority).

MSC Malaysia status companies enjoy 10 years of full tax exemption on statutory income from qualifying MSC activities.

India-Malaysia DTAA: Dividends, 5% withholding (if ≥25% holding); Royalties, 10%.

Indian residents must report Malaysian company ownership under Schedule FA in ITR and comply with FEMA ODI regulations.

Labuan IBFC companies (offshore Malaysia) pay only 3% tax on net audited profits, an alternative for holding and trading structures.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q1. Is Malaysia's Sdn Bhd suitable for Indian IT companies expanding to ASEAN?** Yes. Malaysia is one of the most cost-effective and English-friendly bases for Indian IT companies expanding into ASEAN. MSC Malaysia status offers 10-year tax exemption and access to world-class tech infrastructure (Cyberjaya corridor). It also qualifies for Malaysia Digital Economy initiatives.

**Q2. What is Labuan IBFC and how is it different from a regular Malaysian company?** Labuan IBFC is Malaysia's offshore financial jurisdiction (similar in concept to Dubai's free zones). A Labuan company pays only 3% corporate tax on net audited profits for trading activities, or MYR 20,000 flat for non-trading (holding) activities. It is used primarily for international trading, holding, and financial services, not for conducting business within Malaysia.

**Q3. Can an Indian national be the sole shareholder of a Malaysian Sdn Bhd?** Yes. A foreigner (including Indian nationals) can be the 100% shareholder of a Malaysian Sdn Bhd in most sectors. However, certain sectors, retail, agriculture, construction, education, require local Malaysian equity participation. Always verify the sector's equity policy with the relevant ministry or MIDA before incorporating.

**Q4. What is the minimum number of directors required for a Malaysian Sdn Bhd?** At least one director who is ordinarily resident in Malaysia (i.e., has their principal place of residence in Malaysia). A foreign founder who is not resident in Malaysia must appoint a locally resident nominee director or establish their own residency through an Employment Pass or MM2H visa.

**Q5. Which Indian industries are most active in Malaysian incorporation?** IT and software services (Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai), gloves and rubber manufacturing (due to supply chains), palm oil and agri-commodity trading, BPO/KPO firms, and textile companies (Tamil Nadu, Gujarat) are the major Indian sectors with Malaysian entities.
