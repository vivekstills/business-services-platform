# Company incorporation in UK

## Overview

The United Kingdom remains one of the most accessible and globally respected jurisdictions for company incorporation. A UK Private Limited Company (Ltd) can be registered in under 24 hours, has no minimum share capital, is fully accessible to non-residents, and carries immense brand credibility with global clients. Companies House (the UK's registrar) maintains a transparent public register that serves as a trust signal for Indian businesses serving UK and European clients.

Post-Brexit, the UK has accelerated its bilateral trade agenda, the India-UK Free Trade Agreement (FTA) is under negotiation and expected to significantly open market access for Indian services, IT, and pharmaceutical companies. The UK is also home to the world's largest Indian diaspora (~1.8 million), making it an ideal destination for Indian entrepreneurs.

## Key benefits

- Fastest incorporation globally, 24 hours or less, fully online
- No minimum share capital
- No requirement for UK-resident directors or shareholders
- High global credibility, UK Ltd is trusted by global clients and investors
- Potential FTA benefits under India-UK FTA (under negotiation)
- UK-India DTAA in place, efficient tax structure
- Straightforward banking with fintechs (Wise Business, Revolut Business, Monzo Business)
- No requirement for physical UK office to incorporate
- Access to UK SME finance, R&D tax credits (25% credit for SMEs)
- Strong IP protection under UK IP law

## Limitations

- Corporate Tax increased to 25% (April 2023) for profits above GBP 250,000
- Annual accounts, confirmation statements, and tax returns are publicly accessible
- VAT registration mandatory once turnover exceeds GBP 90,000, complex compliance
- Full HMRC tax and payroll compliance required if UK employees are hired
- Post-Brexit loss of EU market access, separate EU entity needed for EU operations
- UK banking for non-resident Indian directors can face KYC challenges (use fintech alternatives)

## Documents required

For Indian nationals incorporating a UK Ltd:

- Valid passport (scan/copy, no notarisation required for online registration)
- Residential address (any country)
- Proposed company name
- Registered office address in the UK (can use agent address)
- Director and shareholder details (name, date of birth, nationality, service address)
- Service address for directors (UK address can be used)
- Share capital and shareholder structure
- Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) code for business activity
- PSC (Person with Significant Control) declaration

## Step-by-step process

1. **Step 1 - Choose Company Name:** Check availability at Companies House online register. Must be unique and comply with naming rules.
2. **Step 2 - Register on Companies House Portal:** File online via Companies House WebFiling or through a registered agent.
3. **Step 3 - Submit Form IN01:** This is the application for incorporation, includes director details, shareholder structure, share capital, and registered office.
4. **Step 4 - Receive Certificate of Incorporation:** Issued digitally by Companies House, typically within hours.
5. **Step 5 - Register for Corporation Tax:** Register with HMRC within 3 months of starting business activity.
6. **Step 6 - Apply for UTR (Unique Taxpayer Reference):** Issued by HMRC after CT registration.
7. **Step 7 - Open Business Bank Account:** Neobanks like Wise Business, Revolut Business, Monzo Business allow non-resident directors to open accounts online. Traditional banks (Barclays, HSBC, Lloyds) require UK residency or in-branch visits.
8. **Step 8 - Register for VAT:** Mandatory above GBP 90,000 annual turnover. Voluntary registration below this threshold is beneficial if your clients are VAT-registered businesses.
9. **Step 9 - Payroll / PAYE:** Register with HMRC if UK employees are hired.
10. **Step 10 - Annual Confirmation Statement + Accounts:** File annually with Companies House. File Corporation Tax return with HMRC annually.

## Tax implications for Indian founders

UK Corporate Tax: 19% for profits up to GBP 50,000 (small profits rate); 25% for profits above GBP 250,000 (main rate). Marginal relief applies between GBP 50,000–250,000.

R&D Tax Credits: SMEs can claim 25% of qualifying R&D expenditure as a tax credit, very valuable for Indian-owned UK tech or pharma companies.

Dividends paid from UK company to Indian shareholders: 10% withholding tax under India-UK DTAA (if beneficial owner holds < 25%); 15% otherwise. UK domestic law imposes no withholding on dividends in most cases.

Indian founders must declare UK company shareholding in Schedule FA of Indian ITR.

FEMA ODI compliance required for Indian residents investing in UK entities.

Transfer pricing rules apply to related-party transactions between UK company and Indian group entities.

Place of Effective Management (POEM) risk: If UK company management and decision-making occurs from India, CBDT may treat it as an Indian resident company and tax at 40% (foreign company rate).

## Frequently asked questions

**Q1. Can an Indian national be the sole director and shareholder of a UK Ltd company?** Yes. There is no requirement for UK residency or citizenship for directors or shareholders. An Indian national living in India can be the sole director and sole shareholder of a UK Ltd company, with no local director required. This is one of the UK's most attractive features for Indian founders.

**Q2. Do I need a UK bank account to run a UK Ltd company?** No legal requirement, but practically necessary for UK client invoicing, HMRC tax payments, and VAT. Fintech neobanks (Wise Business, Revolut Business, Airwallex UK) have significantly simplified non-resident business banking in the UK and can be opened entirely online without UK residency.

**Q3. What is the impact of Brexit on Indian businesses incorporating in the UK?** Post-Brexit, UK companies no longer have automatic access to the EU single market. Indian businesses targeting both UK and EU customers typically now need two entities, a UK Ltd and an EU entity (often an Irish Ltd or Dutch BV). However, for businesses focused purely on UK clients or using the UK as a global hub, Brexit has limited impact on the company structure itself.

**Q4. What is the PSC register and what are Indian founders' obligations?** Every UK company must maintain a Register of People with Significant Control (PSC), individuals who own 25%+ shares, voting rights, or right to appoint directors. This information is public on Companies House. Indian founders are required to register as PSCs, provide their personal details, and keep the register updated. Non-compliance is a criminal offence.

**Q5. Can Indian IT companies use a UK Ltd to invoice UK and US clients and then remit profits to India?** Yes. An Indian IT company can own a UK Ltd subsidiary that invoices UK/global clients. The UK entity pays UK Corporation Tax on profits, and remaining profits can be remitted to India as dividends (subject to India-UK DTAA rates). This structure is common for IT service companies in Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, and Chennai targeting British enterprises.
