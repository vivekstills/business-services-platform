# Company incorporation in Germany

## Overview

Germany is the largest economy in Europe and a global hub for engineering, manufacturing, automotive, chemicals, and industrial technology. For Indian businesses targeting European markets, Germany offers unmatched infrastructure, a highly skilled workforce, and the credibility of a European Union member state. The most common structures for foreign investors are the GmbH (Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung, Private Limited Company) and the UG (Unternehmergesellschaft, a low-capital startup vehicle).

Access to the EU single market (450 million consumers), EU harmonised regulations, and German engineering reputation make this an attractive destination for Indian exporters, manufacturing firms, tech companies, and professional service providers.

## Key benefits

- Full access to EU single market, 27 countries, 450 million consumers
- Gateway to European supply chains, R&D infrastructure, and talent
- Stable rule of law and predictable regulatory environment
- India-EU bilateral trade growth, India-EU FTA negotiations underway
- Strong IP protection under EU frameworks
- Germany's Mittelstand ecosystem, ideal for B2B manufacturing and services
- German credibility, strong brand value for Indian companies selling to EU clients
- Skilled workforce and engineering talent

## Limitations

- High labour costs and strict employee protection laws
- Corporate and trade tax combined effective rate of 28%–33%
- GmbH requires minimum share capital of EUR 25,000
- German language mandatory for statutory filings, contracts, and court proceedings
- Bureaucratic and complex registration process
- Commercial register (Handelsregister) process can take 2–4 weeks
- Mandatory social security contributions for employees
- Complex VAT regime across EU member states

## Documents required

For Indian founders registering a GmbH in Germany:

- Notarised and apostilled copies of passport (all shareholders/directors)
- Specimen signatures (notarised)
- Articles of Association (Gesellschaftsvertrag), must be notarised by a German notary
- List of shareholders and their addresses
- Declaration of shareholder list
- Proof of registered address in Germany (office lease or address service agreement)
- Bank confirmation of share capital deposit (EUR 12,500 minimum half of minimum capital)
- Managing director appointment letter
- For Indian corporate shareholder: apostilled Certificate of Incorporation, Board Resolution, MoA/AoA

## Step-by-step process

1. **Step 1 - Draft Articles of Association:** Must be drafted in German and notarised by a German notary (Notar). Articles define shareholders, management, and share structure.
2. **Step 2 - Open a Blocked Bank Account:** Open a German business bank account in blocked status. Transfer minimum share capital (EUR 25,000 for GmbH; EUR 1 for UG). Obtain bank confirmation.
3. **Step 3 - Notarial Authentication:** All founding documents, AoA, shareholder list, managing director appointment, must be certified by a German notary.
4. **Step 4 - Register with the Commercial Court (Handelsregister):** The notary files registration application with the local Amtsgericht (District Court). This is mandatory for GmbH.
5. **Step 5 - Tax Registration with Finanzamt:** Register for trade tax (Gewerbesteuer) and VAT (Umsatzsteuer/Mehrwertsteuer) with the local tax office.
6. **Step 6 - Register for Trade License (Gewerbeanmeldung):** Required for commercial activities in Germany.
7. **Step 7 - Obtain VAT ID:** Apply for EU VAT registration number for cross-border EU transactions.
8. **Step 8 - Unblock Bank Account:** After Commercial Register entry, the company can unblock the bank account for operations.
9. **Step 9 - Register with Social Security & Employment Bodies:** If hiring German employees.
10. **Step 10 - Annual Compliance:** Statutory accounts, trade tax return, CIT return, VAT filings.

## Tax implications for Indian founders

Germany levies Corporate Income Tax (CIT) at 15% + 5.5% solidarity surcharge + municipal Trade Tax (Gewerbesteuer) of ~14%–17%, making the effective combined rate approximately 28%–33%.

Dividends distributed to Indian shareholders attract 10% withholding tax under the India-Germany DTAA.

Interest and royalties are also covered under the DTAA with reduced rates.

Indian residents investing in Germany must comply with FEMA ODI regulations and report to RBI.

German VAT must be charged on goods/services sold within Germany and the EU.

Transfer pricing rules (both Indian and German) apply on related-party transactions.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q1. Can an Indian national be the sole director (managing director) of a German GmbH?** Yes. There is no requirement for a German or EU-resident director. An Indian national can be the sole managing director (Geschäftsführer) of a GmbH. However, having a local German representative for day-to-day matters is practically advisable, especially for dealing with banks and government bodies.

**Q2. What is the difference between a GmbH and a UG in Germany?** A GmbH requires a minimum share capital of EUR 25,000 and is treated as a fully-fledged private limited company. A UG (Unternehmergesellschaft haftungsbeschränkt) is the startup-friendly variant that can be formed with as little as EUR 1 in share capital. However, a UG must retain 25% of profits each year until accumulated reserves reach EUR 25,000, at which point it can be converted to a GmbH.

**Q3. Is German language mandatory for running the GmbH?** All statutory documents, Commercial Register filings, tax returns, and legal contracts in Germany must be in German. Most government interactions also happen in German. It is strongly recommended to engage a German-speaking accountant (Steuerberater) and legal counsel for compliance management.

**Q4. Does a German company get automatic EU VAT registration?** No. You must separately register for German VAT (Umsatzsteuer) with the Finanzamt. Once registered, you receive a German VAT number (Steuernummer) and can apply for an EU VAT ID number (Umsatzsteuer-Identifikationsnummer) for intra-EU transactions.

**Q5. Which Indian sectors are most active in German incorporation?** Automotive components (Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu), specialty chemicals (Gujarat), IT and engineering consulting (Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad), and pharma companies (Hyderabad, Ahmedabad) are the most active in establishing German entities, primarily to support EU sales and supply chain operations.
