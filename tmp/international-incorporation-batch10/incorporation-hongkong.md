# Company incorporation in Hong Kong

## Overview

Hong Kong remains one of Asia's premier business and financial hubs despite political changes since 2019. For Indian businesses, Hong Kong offers a common law legal system, zero capital gains tax, territorial taxation, a convertible currency (HKD pegged to USD), and deep integration with mainland China's economy. The standard structure is a Private Limited Company, which can be formed quickly, has no minimum share capital, and imposes no restrictions on foreign ownership.

Hong Kong is ideal for trading companies, regional holding structures, fintech businesses, professional services firms, and any Indian company seeking a credible Asia-Pacific base with mainland China access.

## Key benefits

- Territorial tax, only Hong Kong-sourced profits taxed (16.5% standard, 8.25% for first HKD 2 million)
- Zero capital gains tax, no inheritance tax, no VAT/GST
- 100% foreign ownership, no local shareholder required
- Common law legal system, familiar and predictable for Indian businesses
- No minimum share capital requirement
- Quick incorporation, 1–5 days
- Gateway to mainland China market
- Strong banking sector, HSBC, Standard Chartered, DBS, OCBC
- Over 40 DTAA treaties globally
- India-Hong Kong DTAA signed (2018)

## Limitations

- Recent political changes have reduced Hong Kong's autonomy and predictability
- Onshore profits taxed at 16.5%, not zero tax for local operations
- Banks have become more stringent on KYC for non-resident Indian founders
- Must maintain local registered address and company secretary
- Annual government fees and audit requirements
- Not ideal for businesses needing EU market access
- India-Hong Kong DTAA has anti-avoidance provisions (PPT, Principal Purpose Test)

## Documents required

For Indian nationals incorporating in Hong Kong:

- Passport copy (notarised) of all shareholders and directors
- Residential address proof (utility bill or bank statement, within 3 months)
- Proposed company name (in English and/or Chinese)
- Particulars of directors and company secretary
- Registered office address in Hong Kong (can use registered agent's address)
- Signed consent to act as director/company secretary
- For Indian corporate shareholder: apostilled Certificate of Incorporation, Board Resolution, MoA/AoA

## Step-by-step process

1. **Step 1 - Name Search and Approval:** Check name availability on Companies Registry database (WebSearch system). Names must be unique and not misleading.
2. **Step 2 - Prepare Incorporation Documents:** Prepare the Articles of Association, NNC1 (Notice of Incorporation) form, and director/shareholder details.
3. **Step 3 - File with the Companies Registry:** Submit NNC1, Articles, and identification documents. Can be filed online or in person.
4. **Step 4 - Receive Certificate of Incorporation:** Typically issued within 1–5 business days. The Business Registration Certificate from the IRD (Inland Revenue Department) is also required.
5. **Step 5 - Appoint Company Secretary:** Every Hong Kong company must have a locally resident company secretary (individual or corporate).
6. **Step 6 - Open Bank Account:** Major banks include HSBC, Standard Chartered, Hang Seng, DBS (HK). Non-resident account opening now requires strong business substance documentation.
7. **Step 7 - Register for Profits Tax:** No separate registration required, automatic under the IRD.
8. **Step 8 - File Annual Return and Audited Accounts:** Required annually. Financial year can be chosen by the company.

## Tax implications for Indian founders

Hong Kong Profits Tax is territorial, only profits arising from or derived in Hong Kong are taxed. Offshore profits (e.g., a Hong Kong trading company billing international clients with no Hong Kong-based operations) may be exempt from Hong Kong Profits Tax on application.

Rate: 8.25% on first HKD 2 million of assessable profits; 16.5% above that (for corporations).

India-Hong Kong DTAA (effective 2018): Dividends, 5% withholding (if Indian company holds ≥25%); otherwise 10%. Royalties, 10%.

Indian residents holding Hong Kong companies must report under Schedule FA in ITR and comply with FEMA ODI regulations.

Economic substance regulations in Hong Kong now require genuine local activity for offshore tax claim. Pure letterbox companies face scrutiny.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q1. Is Hong Kong still a reliable jurisdiction for Indian businesses given the 2019–2020 political changes?** Hong Kong retains its common law legal system, separate tax regime, independent courts, and convertible currency under the "One Country, Two Systems" framework (until 2047). However, the National Security Law (2020) and electoral changes have reduced political autonomy. For commercial activity, Hong Kong remains functional and credible, but Indian businesses with sensitive IP or politically sensitive operations may prefer Singapore as an alternative.

**Q2. Can a Hong Kong company claim offshore tax exemption on profits earned entirely outside Hong Kong?** Yes, in principle. If a company can demonstrate that all profit-generating activities occurred outside Hong Kong (e.g., overseas buyers, overseas suppliers, no Hong Kong employees involved in the transaction), it may apply for an Offshore Claim with the IRD. Post-BEPS reforms have made this scrutiny more rigorous, genuine economic substance must be demonstrated.

**Q3. Is a physical office mandatory in Hong Kong?** No. A registered address (provided by a company secretary firm or service provider) is sufficient for incorporation. However, for bank account opening and offshore tax claims, a genuine business address with demonstrated local activity is increasingly important.

**Q4. How does the India-Hong Kong DTAA benefit Indian businesses?** The India-Hong Kong DTAA (effective 2018) reduces withholding tax on dividends (5% if holding ≥25%, else 10%), interest (10%), and royalties (10%). It also provides clarity on Permanent Establishment rules and eliminates double taxation, making dividend repatriation and royalty flows from Hong Kong to India more tax-efficient.

**Q5. Which Indian businesses use Hong Kong as their Asia base?** Diamond and precious metals traders (Surat, Mumbai), textile export companies (Gujarat, Tamil Nadu), IT and fintech firms, pharma companies (Hyderabad, Ahmedabad), and commodity trading groups are the dominant Indian users of Hong Kong structures.
