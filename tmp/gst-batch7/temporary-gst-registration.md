# Temporary GST registration (casual taxable person)

## Overview

A casual taxable person who makes taxable supply in a state or union territory where he is not already registered can use temporary registration, pay an advance of tax in line with the expected liability, and file the returns the Act and portal require for the validity period. Validity is typically ninety days, extendable in line with the law, not a substitute for a regular GSTIN in a state where the business is permanent. Use this for exhibitions, short events, and similar trade, with GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, and a clean end when the event and registration end.

## Key features

- Advance tax before use of the temp GSTIN, in the amount and class the year’s rule and form give.
- Short validity, extension only where the law allows, then cancel or 10- class end.
- CTP cannot use the composition scheme, on advice on the class of person in force.

## Benefits

- Lawful inter-state and intra-state B2B and B2C in the event state without a full new REG-01 in every edge case, when your facts are truly casual.

## Process

1. Compute expected base and tax for the event, with your CA, and plan advance payment.
2. File the casual taxable person form on the portal in the path in force, pay advance, and get GSTIN.
3. File 1 and 3B for each period, match books, and close with cancel or 10 in line with the end state of the CTP when the time runs out.

## Applicability

True casual taxable person under the Act, not a regular branch, with state law and a clear event, on advice of your professional.

## Common mistakes (short)

- CTP in a year-round state where a normal GSTIN in that state is the right path.
- No advance, or 3B, 1 after validity date; late fee and 50- class risk.
- 10 and open stock when the CTP class needs a 10, in line with the year’s GSTR-10 and ITC-03 rule.

## Frequently asked questions

**Two events, same year, same state?** Map a one-off path with your CA, extension, and tax on each event, not a fake CTP in a year-round set-up.
