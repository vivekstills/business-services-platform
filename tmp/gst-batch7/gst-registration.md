# GST registration

## Overview

GST registration is how a person or firm in India, or a foreign business where the law allows, gets a **GSTIN** to charge **IGST, CGST,** and **SGST/UTGST,** and to file **GSTR-1, GSTR-3B,** and other returns on **https://www.gst.gov.in.** The usual form is **GST REG-01** in **Part A** and **Part B.** Many applicants complete **Aadhaar-** **based** authentication. When **taxable** turnover crosses the threshold or a **compulsory** case applies, you file within the period the **CGST** Act and **CBIC** updates give, or face late fees and **DRC-** class exposure in serious cases.

## Key features

- One **PAN** can have one **GSTIN** per state where a **place** of **business** exists.
- **Principal** place, **constitution** documents, **PAN,** **bank** account, and **address** proof are the usual uploads.
- You pick **HSN** and **SAC** for your goods and services in line with your business.

## Benefits

- Lawful issue of **tax** invoices, **B2B**-style **ITC** for customers, and access to the **GSTR-1**-**2B**-**3B** stack.
- **E-** way bill and the rest of the **GST** compliance menu where your facts match.

## Process

1. Start **Form GST REG-01,** **Part** **A,** to get a **TRN;** gather **constitution, PAN,** address, and signatory details.
2. Complete **Part** **B** with business details, **bank** account, **HSN** and **SAC** choices, and premises proof.
3. Finish **Aadhaar-** **based** authentication or other checks the portal requests.
4. Reply to any **query** or **SCN-** style notice on the application with fresh evidence.
5. After **GSTIN** is issued, plan **GSTR-1, GSTR-3B,** and **e-** way bill use for your trade.

## Applicability

Any person or firm that meets the **turnover** or **nature** of **supply** test, **voluntary** **registration** under the Act, or other **compulsory** class such as some **TDS,** **OIDAR,** and **E**-**commerce**-**operator**-**style** **cases,** in line with the **CBIC** list in force.

## Common mistakes (short)

- Wrong state jurisdiction for your principal place of business.
- HSN and SAC that do not match the goods and services you really supply.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can we have two GSTINs in one state?** Usually one GSTIN per state; extra places in the same state are often on the same registration with additional places, unless the law and portal allow a different path for your case.

**Registration came after the threshold date. Is there a penalty?** A late fee on tax for the time you were liable to be registered and full compliance from the effective date are the main themes; a CA-led note on your facts is best.

**E commerce operator, first time. Any extra forms?** Follow the e commerce and OIDAR class rules and the portal rows for the year, on advice of your professional.
