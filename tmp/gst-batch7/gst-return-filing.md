# GST return filing (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, and related)

## Overview

Regular taxpayers in India file outward supply data in GSTR-1, summary payment and input tax in GSTR-3B, and use GSTR-2B as the read-only book of credit for a period, with a few other returns for special cases (ISD, TDS, TCS, and similar). The due dates, late fees, and interest depend on the person type and the CBIC extension order for the month, so the portal notice for the year is the source of the exact calendar. Clean 1, 2B, and 3B are the main defence in audit and in GST annual return work.

## Key features

- GSTR-1: invoice-level or summary lines by period as the HSN, B2B, and export story require.
- GSTR-3B: output tax, ITC, RCM, and net payment, with 2B as the key ITC line for many.
- Reconciliation of books to 1 and 2B, and 3B to 2B, before lock of the return where your process allows it.

## Benefits

- Ongoing input credit for B2B buyers and a clear trail for 9, 9C, and refund work.
- Lower late fee and 50-style interest if you file in time, subject to the law in force.

## Process

1. Close books for the return period; get 2B, vendor 1, and your sales master in one file.
2. File GSTR-1 by the due date, then 3B in the 1, 2B, 3B order your adviser maps.
3. Pay tax in 3B or use the credit ledger, then save proof of payment.
4. Reconcile 1, 2B, and 3B, and set next-month vendor and internal fixes.

## Applicability

All normal, SEZ, and most regular GSTINs that are not on composition, except where a special return replaces 1, 2B, 3B for that person.

## Common mistakes (short)

- Filing 3B before 1, or 1 after the lock window when your facts need an amend path.
- ITC in 3B with no 2B or rule-based credit story.
- Wrong HSN in 1, which breaks your buyer’s 2B and your own 9.

## Frequently asked questions

**Extension for the 11th?** See the order for the month; do not trust an old post.

**Amend 1?** The portal amend window and table rules change; a CA note on the year is best.
