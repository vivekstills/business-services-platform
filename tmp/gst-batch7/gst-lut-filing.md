# GST LUT (Letter of Undertaking) filing

## Overview

A **Letter of Undertaking (LUT)** is submitted on the GST portal so a registered person can make **zero-rated** supplies (export of goods or services, and supplies to SEZ where the law allows) without collecting **IGST** on the tax invoice. Instead, you route compliance through the credit and refund framework, including **Form RFD-01** where unutilized input tax credit or the facts require it. The legal framework sits in the **IGST** Act and **Rule 96A**; the online **LUT** facility largely replaced older physical bond-style processes for typical exporters. You normally **renew the LUT for each financial year** before you rely on it for new invoices. If **LUT** is not available in law for your case (for example, certain serious prosecution-based exclusions), a **bond** with bank guarantee or payment of **IGST** and refund on **RFD-01** may be the path your adviser maps out.

## Key features

- Filing is **free**; authentication with **DSC** or **EVC** as the portal allows.
- One **LUT** applies for the selected financial year; download and retain the **acknowledgement (ARN reference)**.
- Use correct reporting in **GSTR-1** for exports and **SEZ**-linked lines; **BRC-** and **FIRC-** linked refund stories sit on **RFD-01** when applicable.
- Without a valid **LUT** for the year, the cash flow and compliance path moves to **IGST**-paid-and-refund or **bond** routes, which are heavier operationally.

## Benefits

- Reduces the need to pay **IGST** up front and wait for cash refunds on every export, compared with a pay-and-refund model, when **LUT** is the chosen route and facts support it.
- Simpler for many SMEs than maintaining a **bond** with **bank** guarantee, when **LUT** is permitted.
- One annual filing covers eligible **SEZ** and export-style transactions for the year, subject to honest declarations on the form.

## Process

1. Log in to **https://www.gst.gov.in** and open the **Furnish LUT** service (navigation may sit under **Services** for your login type).
2. Select the **financial year**, complete signatory and witness details, and answer the self-declaration **truthfully**.
3. **Sign** with **DSC** or **EVC** and submit; save the **acknowledgement**.
4. In **GSTR-1**, report **exports** and **SEZ** supplies in the correct tables, consistent with your contracts and e-way or shipping documentation where relevant.
5. For refunds of unutilized **ITC** or other eligible amounts, work **RFD-01** with your **FIRC-** and **BRC-** class evidence in line with the category you select on the portal.
6. **Renew** before the new year’s export or **SEZ** billing, typically in **late March** or **early April**, so **April** transactions are not left relying on a lapsed year’s **ACK**.

## Applicability

Registered persons who make **zero-rated** supplies and are not excluded from **LUT** under the **Rule 96A** / portal conditions (for example, certain prosecution-based bars). B2B and B2C-style service exports, and **SEZ**-linked **B2B** supplies, typically use the same annual **ACK** if the fact pattern and law match.

## Common mistakes (short)

- **Starting the new year’s exports** before filing the new year’s **LUT** and still issuing **IGST-** free invoices.
- **GSTR-1** or contract treatment that does not match your **BRC-** and **FIRC-** class story on **RFD-01.**
- Choosing the **wrong** **RFD-01** bucket (for example, **IGST** paid *versus* unutilized **ITC** for an export class refund).

## Frequently asked questions

**We export software to the US. Do we need a fresh LUT every year?** Yes. File **LUT** for each financial year you rely on; treat supplies per **GSTR-1** and **3B** as your adviser maps, and run **BRC-**tied and **FIRC-**sensible **RFD-01** if you are claiming **ITC**-linked refunds, within time limits in force.

**We shipped in April before the new LUT. What should we do?** File the current year’s **LUT** without delay, preserve **BRC,** and ask your **CA** whether earlier invoices need a **DRC-03,** a **RFD-01 (IGST paid)**-style line, or another clean-up, based on the portal options and law.

**SEZ** supply without a valid **LUT?** You may have to follow **IGST**-charge and **RFD-01 (IGST)**-type refund, or a **bond** path if **LUT** is not in place, which typically hurts **cash** flow, so do not skip the **annual** file.
