# Input tax credit reconciliation (2B, books, and 3B)

## Overview

Input tax credit in GST is read from GSTR-2B, matched to your purchase books and 3B, and limited by the Act for time, fake invoice, ineligible use, and block list risk. A monthly or quarterly GSTR-2B, GSTR-3B, and book reconciliation, with a vendor follow-up list, is the main control for ITC, 9, and refund, and the main way to keep DRC-01 and 04 risk low for your board.

## Key features

- 2B: auto-generated, read-only; your “allowed” set for a period as the portal and law in force work.
- 1 of vendors: the mirror for your 2A period story and for ANX-1 in many years.
- 3B: where you self-assess to take ITC, with 2B as the first gate and books as the final gate.

## Benefits

- Stops 9, 9C, and 13 show-down with one bridge file from 2B, books, 3B, and 2A where needed.
- Safer 50 and 74 class defence when your chain is in order and your 3B and 1 match.

## Process

1. Load 2B, vendor 1, and your books for the month or quarter, on one control sheet.
2. Match line by line; flag 2B, no book, and book, no 2B, and 2B, no 1 with your vendor and internal fix.
3. Take ITC in 3B in line with 2B, section 16, 17, 18, and 34 as your CA maps, with DRC-03 when you must.
4. Keep a one-page RCM, import, and SEZ map so 3B does not double credit.

## Applicability

All regular persons who use ITC in 3B, in every month or quarter, not on pure composition.

## Common mistakes (short)

- Claim in 3B with no 2B and no 16(4) class story, if you use the 1, 2B path.
- No bridge from 2B, book, 3B, for 9 and for any 2B, 1, 1A fix you said you did in the year.
- 17(5) ineligible, but 3B still takes full ITC, with no 16, 17, 3B, 1 review.

## Frequently asked questions

**2B, no 1?** Nudge vendor, and use the 1, 1A and book path on advice, not a fake 1.

**2B, after time in 16(4), can we?** 16(4) and 18 time rules are strict; a one-line CA note is best.
