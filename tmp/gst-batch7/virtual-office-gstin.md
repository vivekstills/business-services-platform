# Virtual office and GSTIN (address and place of business)

## Overview

A virtual office, co-work seat, or shared business address can support a valid GSTIN when you have a real lease, NOC, board resolution, and day-to-day use that matches the state in which you register. A mail box alone, with all core work in another state, can draw scrutiny, show-cause, and cancellation under the law. Aadhaar, bank, and signatory KYC on the portal must match the business story your ledgers, contracts, and e-way class moves support.

## Key features

- Lease, rent receipt, and owner NOC, or a valid sub-lease, for the co-work or virtual plan you buy.
- Signatory, mobile, and bank that pass portal checks and, if a visit is done, a desk or room that is not a fake.
- 1, 2B, 3B, and e-way that look like a real firm in the state, not a shell.

## Benefits

- Legitimate multi-state presence, bank, and customer trust without a full long lease in the first year, when the fact pattern is real.

## Process

1. Choose a vetted co-work, get a long-form address line, and keep rent and NOC in your GST file.
2. File normal REG-01, pass Aadhaar, and keep the same PPOB for years unless you really move, then amend REG-12 class.
3. Reconcile 1, 2B, 3B, and e-way, with no supply line from a state you never work in, on advice for your class.

## Applicability

Firms with a true need for a second state, start-ups, and light teams in co-work, not a scheme to get an extra GSTIN in a no-tax state, on your CA view.

## Common mistakes (short)

- One mail box, five GSTIN, no e-way, no 1, in that state.
- REG-12 move every year, with no new lease, only to chase input or rate play; high risk.
- 9 and 9C, with PPOB and book not in the same state as the 1, 2B, 3B set.

## Frequently asked questions

**We work from home; can we use co-work?** If co-work is the PPOB you run from and your lease, NOC, and signatory are real, the law can allow it, on a fact note from your team.
