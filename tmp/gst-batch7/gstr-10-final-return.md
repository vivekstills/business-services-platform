# GSTR-10 final return (on cancellation and similar end states)

## Overview

GSTR-10 is the final return to be filed when registration is cancelled or surrendered. It is not the same as the annual 9, and the due date in law is a fixed window from the date of cancellation, subject to the year’s form and the portal text. The return captures closing stock, ITC, output tax, and the bridge to the ITC-03 story where the rules need it, so that 9 and audit can see a full path from open to close.

## Key features

- One return per event of cancellation, with 16 and 10 links and stock truth.
- ITC-03: reversal or payment on stock, inputs, and capital goods as the year’s rule and form ask.
- DRC-03 in the same story if 10 or review finds a gap in tax for the tranche.

## Benefits

- Clean closure, fewer 50 and DRC-04 class issues on a dead GSTIN.
- A single, dated pack for 9, bank, and M&A due diligence on that GSTIN.

## Process

1. Reconcile 1, 2B, 3B, and stock up to the effective date of cancel.
2. File ITC-03 when in scope, and pay if the law does not let you just reverse in the 10.
3. File 10 in the time window, with 16 and the cancel order in hand.
4. File DRC-03 if 10 or working finds a tax gap.

## Documents

| Item | Use |
|------|-----|
| Cancel order, 16, last 1, 2B, 3B | 10 and date trail |
| Stock, WIP, capital goods, ITC-03 | Credit and 10 line |
| Bank, DRC, payment of tax | 10, DRC-03 |

## Applicability

Every GSTIN that is cancelled, except where a specific exemption in the Act applies for a class of person, on advice.

## Common mistakes (short)

- 10 with stock that is not the same as the last 3B and 1.
- Forgetting 03, or 10 after the 03 window, when the law needs both in a set order.
- 9 filed but 10 still open, or 10 in the wrong financial year of effect.

## Frequently asked questions

**Cancel in March, 10 in new FY?** Map the “from date of order” and the 10 form text for the year; do not use a web post from 2018.
