# GST registration cancellation

## Overview

A GST registration may be cancelled when you apply to close it because the business has ended, or when a proper officer cancels for non-filing, fraud, or other grounds in the Act. Voluntary cancellation is usually through Form GST REG-16 (or the portal step in force for the year) after you have filed the required return in GSTR-10, paid outstanding tax, and reversed or disclosed credit on stock and capital goods as the law requires, including Form GST ITC-03 in cases where the rules apply. After the effective date, RCM, purchase from unregistered person, and other post-cancellation tax rules for your class should be re-read on advice.

## Key features

- Voluntary: REG-16 path, GSTR-10, stock and credit treatment, and proof of business closure.
- Officer-led: order with reasons, appeal and revocation timelines where the law provides.
- Merged or demerged: follow the order of a court or the notified scheme, with registration updates as rules say.

## Benefits

- Clean closure: fewer ongoing late fees, notices, and litigation risk from ignored GSTINs.
- Clear end date: helps lenders, M&A, and your own compliance calendar.

## Process

1. Settle GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, and any open orders up to the effective date, on professional advice.
2. File the cancellation application, stock and ITC-03 (if in scope), and GSTR-10 in the right order the portal and law require.
3. Pay DRC-03 and other payments if 10 uncovers a gap.
4. Retain the cancellation order and a short compliance pack for audit.

## Applicability

Any registered person who truly stops taxable activity in that GSTIN, or is cancelled by the department, and needs an orderly 10, ITC, and DRC-03 story.

## Common mistakes (short)

- Filing 10 with ledgers that do not match the last 3B and 1.
- Forgetting ITC-03 in cases of stock, capital goods, and inputs the rules list.
- Treating a cancelled GSTIN as if RCM and purchase rules for unregistered did not change.

## Frequently asked questions

**We sold the business. Can we just cancel?** Use the transfer, merger, and succession rules, including registration of the buyer and any deemed supply; cancellation alone may be wrong. Take CA-led advice.

**Officer cancelled; can we work again?** Use revocation (REG-21 class) in the window the law and order give, and fix the root cause, or reapply if allowed.
