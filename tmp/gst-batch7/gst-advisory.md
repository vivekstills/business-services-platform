# GST advisory

## Overview

**GST advisory** is specialist consulting to navigate India’s **Goods and Services Tax** (introduced 1 July 2017), which replaced a stack of pre-GST indirect taxes (excise, service tax, VAT, CST, entry tax, and others) with a destination-based, multi-slab tax. The framework is still operationally complex: many rates, **HSN**/**SAC** rules, **ITC** limits, e-invoicing, **RCM**, **AAR/AAAR**, and frequent **CBIC** circulars, so many businesses use ongoing professional advice, not one-off form filing.

Advisory can cover **registration** and supply chain set-up, **classification**, **ITC** and **17(5)** **blocked** credit, place of **supply**, **export/SEZ** and **LUT**, **notices, audits,** and **litigation** support before the **AAR, AAAR,** and courts, depending on the mandate.

## Key features

- **HSN/SAC** and rate opinion from notifications and case law
- **Place of supply** and inter-state v. **IGST** for digital, job work, and cross-border
- **ITC** eligibility, **Rule 42/43** common credit, and **AAR** / advance ruling where needed
- **GSTR-1, 3B, 9, 9C** alignment and pre-audit “health” reviews
- **E-way bill,** **e-invoice,** and **2B/3B** reconciliation as part of risk control
- **Department** representation on scrutiny and assessment

## Benefits

- Lower risk of **wrong** tax, **excess** **ITC**, and **penalty/interest** (18% p.a. on many defaults, plus late fee patterns on returns)
- Better **working capital** when **ITC** is right-sized and defensible, not over-claimed
- **Contract and pricing** design that is GST-efficient in **real** **estate,** **works,** and **BFSI**-style mixes
- **Fewer** disputes on **classification** and **ITC** if the position is documented early
- **Export** and **SaaS** to foreign clients, **OIDAR,** and **M&A** **diligence** on **GST** where facts support it

## Process

1. **Scope the issue:** model, states, B2B v. B2C, and where pain sits (filing, ITC, notice, or deal).
2. **Collect** returns, sample invoices, **contracts,** and any **order** or **AAR.**
3. **Research** Act, rules, **notifications,** and **courts/AAAR** for your fact pattern.
4. **Advisory** memo: law, **risk,** and **practical** **steps** (invoicing, **RCM,** or **AAR** filing).
5. **Implement** in ledgers, **GSTR,** and **agreements,** and keep a **reconciliation** trail.
6. **AAR** or **appeal** only if the amount or uncertainty justifies the **time and fee.**

## Applicability

**All** **regular** and **growing** filers, **e-commerce,** **export,** **SEZ,** **real** **estate,** **IT/ITES,** BFSI, and anyone with **multi-state,** **RCM,** or **dispute** risk. **Pan-India,** as **law** is central and **jurisdiction** follows **place** of **registration** and **supply.**

## Compliance and reminders

- **Track** **CBIC** and **state** **circulars**; rates and **procedures** **move** often
- **Match** your **AAR/AAAR** to **GSTR,** or explain **deviation** with **advice**
- **Keep** **evidence** for **ITC** and **class** for the **statute** period (commonly **six** years in practice for many **GST** records)
- **Year-end** **42/43** and **2B/3B** **logic** to avoid **interest** on **reversal**

## Common mistakes (short)

- **HSN** **digits** **wrong** for **turnover** tier on **1** and **e-invoice**
- **ITC** on **17(5)** **blocked** or **2B**-**busting** without **defence**
- **RCM** **not** **paid,** or **IGST** v. **CGST/SGST** **wrong** on **POS**
- **Not** using **2B** as the **practical** **ceiling** for **safe** **ITC** under **Rule** **36**-style **practice**

## Frequently asked questions

**Mixed taxable and exempt: how to handle common ITC?** Use **Rule** **42** (and **43** for **capital** **goods** where it applies) to **apportion** common **ITC;** your **GSTR-9** **line** and **9C** will **ask** for **clarity,** and **R42** **reversal** is a **frequent** **advisory** **topic.

**AAR** **unfavourable?** **Appeal** to **AAAR** in **thirty** **days**; **after** that, some **write** to **High** **Courts** on **jurisdiction** or **substantive** **error,** on **advice** only.

**Can** **advice** **cut** **tax** **lawfully?** **Yes,** by **right** **structure,** **right** **rate,** and **right** **ITC,** not **by** **suppression.**

**SaaS** to **abroad?** If **it** is **an** **export of** **services** and **place** and **receipts** **meet** **the** **law,** you **zero-rate**; **wrong** as **local** **B2B** **costs** **cash** and **refund** **churn.**

**M&A: what** to **check?** **GSTR,** **2B,** **notices,** **e-invoice,** **RCM,** and **deferred** **ITC/assessment** in **the** **SPA,** and **use** a **dd** list **in** the **user** **narrative** above as your **own** **checklist.**
