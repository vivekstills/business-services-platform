## MoA amendment

### Detailed overview

The Memorandum of Association (MoA) is the constitutional document of a company defining its name, registered office state, objects (main and ancillary), liability of members, capital structure, and subscriber details. Amendment of MoA is governed by Sections 13 and 4 of the Companies Act, 2013, and requires special resolution of shareholders for most clauses.

Common MoA amendments include object clause expansion (adding new business activities), capital clause modification (along with SH-7 for capital increase), name change (alongside other forms), and registered office state change.

### Key features

- Special resolution, MGT-14, and stamp where applicable
- Name or state change may need Central Government or RD in addition
- Commercial decisions should stay within valid objects, ultra vires risk if not

## Applicability

Applicable to all companies. Pan-India.

## Compliance requirements

| Clause | Approval required |
| Name | Special resolution and Central Government approval |
| Registered office state | Special resolution and RD approval |
| Objects | Special resolution |
| Liability | Special resolution |
| Capital | Ordinary resolution (or special as per AOA) |

## Process

1. Board approves proposed MoA changes
2. EGM notice (21 days)
3. EGM passes special resolution
4. Pay stamp duty if applicable
5. File MGT-14 within 30 days
6. Update MoA copy
7. Special procedures apply for name change, state change

## Government fees

| Form | Fee |
| MGT-14 | ₹200 to ₹600 |
| Late fee | ₹100 per day |

## Timeline

30 to 45 days for object clause, 60 to 180 days for state change.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can MoA objects be expanded without limits?** Practically yes, companies can add multiple ancillary objects. However, very broad object clauses can attract regulatory scrutiny. Best practice is to keep objects relevant to actual business intent.

**2. Is RoC approval needed for MoA amendments?** For most clauses (including object), MGT-14 filing is intimation only, no prior approval. Name change and state change require additional Central Government or RD approval.

**3. What happens to existing contracts if objects clause is amended?** Existing contracts within original objects remain valid. New activities under amended objects can commence post-amendment.

**4. Can a Section 8 company amend its objects?** Section 8 company MoA can be amended but with restrictions, non-profit nature and specified objects must be preserved. Amendments may require Central Government approval.

**5. Does MoA amendment affect existing PAN and GST registrations?** Name change requires PAN and GST update. Object change typically does not require PAN and GST change but may affect business categorisation in GST.
