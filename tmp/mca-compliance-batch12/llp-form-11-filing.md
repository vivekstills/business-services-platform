## LLP Form 11 filing

### Detailed overview

Form 11 is the Annual Return of an LLP filed with the Registrar of Companies (RoC) under the LLP Act, 2008. The form captures the LLP's structural information, partner details, contributions, business activities, and any changes during the financial year, analogous to MGT-7 for companies.

Form 11 is one of the two foundational annual MCA filings for LLPs (the other being Form 8 for financial statements). All LLPs must file Form 11 annually regardless of business activity.

### Key features

- Annual structural return by May 30 for March year-end
- Designated partner DSC, agreement and partner data reconciled
- Complements Form 8 (accounts and solvency)

## Applicability

Mandatory for all LLPs registered in India. Pan-India applicability.

## Compliance requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| Form | LLP Form 11 |
| Due date | May 30 each year (FY ending March 31) |
| Filing period | Within 60 days of FY end |
| Designated Partner DSC | Mandatory |
| Pre-conditions | LLP Agreement up to date, partner records current |

## Benefits

- Statutory annual MCA compliance
- Maintains LLP active status
- Public record of LLP structure
- Prevents non-compliance penalties

## Risks and penalties

| Default | Penalty |
| Late filing | ₹100 per day (no upper cap historically, recent caps may apply) |
| Continued non-filing | LLP marked inactive, restrictions on operations |
| Strike-off risk | After persistent non-compliance |

## Documents required

| Document | Purpose |
| LLP agreement | Reference |
| Updated partner list | With contributions |
| List of designated partners | |
| Form 11 | MCA prescribed |

## Process

1. Compile partner data and contributions
2. Verify against existing LLP Agreement
3. Prepare Form 11
4. DSC signing by designated partner
5. File on the MCA portal by May 30

## Government fees

| Capital contribution | Fee |

Late fee: ₹100 per day.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Is Form 11 required for inactive LLPs?** Yes. Even inactive LLPs must file Form 11 annually until formal closure. Non-filing accumulates significant late fees over time.

**2. What is the difference between Form 11 and Form 8?** Form 11 = Annual Return (structural information). Form 8 = Statement of Account and Solvency (financial information). Both mandatory annually.

**3. Can Form 11 be filed without finalising LLP accounts?** Form 11 is structural and non-financial, does not require finalised accounts. Form 8 requires finalised financials.

**4. What is the consequence of multi-year Form 11 non-filing?** Significant accumulated late fees (₹100 per day per year), MCA marking LLP as defaulter, restrictions on filings, eventual strike-off risk.

**5. Are partners personally liable for Form 11 non-compliance?** Designated Partners bear primary responsibility. Penalties under LLP Act can be imposed on Designated Partners for non-compliance.
