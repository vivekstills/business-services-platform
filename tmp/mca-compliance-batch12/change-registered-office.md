## Change registered office of company

### Detailed overview

The Registered Office of a company is its official address for receiving statutory communications, government notices, legal documents, and stakeholder correspondence. Change of registered office is a common corporate action, driven by office relocations, business expansion, or strategic reasons.

The procedure under Section 12 and 13 of the Companies Act, 2013 varies based on the scope of change. Within the same city, a simple board resolution. Within the same state but a different city, board and shareholder approval. From one state to another, RD approval, public notice, and creditor consents.

### Key features

- INC-22 is central, MGT-14 and INC-23 when inter-state or special resolution
- Address proof, NOC, and newspaper steps per case type
- Update GST and other registrations after the RoC change

## Applicability

Applicable to all companies, private, public, OPC, Section 8, etc. Pan-India.

## Compliance requirements

| Change type | Approval required | Form |
| Within same city or town | Board resolution | INC-22 (within 30 days) |
| Within same state, different RoC jurisdiction | Special resolution | MGT-14 and INC-22 |
| One state to another | Special resolution and RD approval | MGT-14, INC-23, INC-22 |

## Benefits

- Operational alignment with business location
- Cost optimisation (relocating from expensive to affordable areas)
- Better tax and regulatory environment in some states
- Strategic positioning

## Process

**Within same city:** Board resolution approving change, file INC-22 within 30 days.

**Within same state but different RoC:** Board resolution and EGM approval (special resolution), file MGT-14, file INC-22 within 30 days.

**State to state change:** Special resolution, file MGT-14, public notice in newspapers, apply to RD via INC-23 with creditor and member consent, RD approval (typically 30 to 60 days), file INC-22 with new RoC, update company records.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can registered office be at a residential address?** Yes, particularly common for startups and small companies. Society NOC and proper utility bill documentation are needed. Some commercial activities may not be permitted at residential addresses.

**2. Can the registered office be a virtual office or coworking space?** Yes, if the virtual office provider issues proper NOC and utility bills, MCA accepts coworking and virtual office addresses. This is common practice in tech and consulting sectors.

**3. What is the difference between registered office and corporate office?** Registered office is the legal address for MCA and government communications. Corporate office is the operational headquarters where business is conducted. They can be the same or different.

**4. How long does state-to-state change take and why?** 60 to 180 days typically. The process involves special resolution, public notice (30 days for objections), RD approval, and final filing, multiple stages requiring sequential clearances.

**5. Does change of registered office require updating GST registration?** Yes. Change of registered office affects GST records. File GST amendment (Form REG-14) within 15 days of address change.
