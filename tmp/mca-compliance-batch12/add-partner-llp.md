## Add partner to LLP

### Detailed overview

Adding a partner to a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) involves the formal induction of a new partner into the LLP through amendment of the LLP Agreement and filing of the necessary forms with the Registrar of Companies under the LLP Act, 2008.

LLPs distinguish between Designated Partners (responsible for compliance, equivalent to directors of a company) and ordinary partners. Adding either category requires structured compliance with LLP Act provisions, particularly Section 7 and the LLP Rules.

### Key features

- Partner consent, agreement amendment, and Form 4 (and Form 3 if agreement is modified)
- DPIN and DSC for designated partners, KYC for all incoming partners
- Stamp duty and notarisation may apply by state

## Applicability

Applicable to all LLPs registered in India. Pan-India applicability.

| Partner type | Notes |
| Designated partner | Min 2 required at all times, needs DPIN, DSC |
| Ordinary partner | No DPIN required, routine partner role |
| Nominee partner | For corporate body partners |

## Process

1. Identify and verify new partner.
2. Existing partners pass resolution approving addition (as per LLP Agreement).
3. New partner provides KYC and signs consent.
4. Apply for DPIN (Form DIR-3 with LLP scope) if designated partner.
5. Obtain DSC for new partner if required.
6. Draft supplementary LLP Agreement or amended agreement reflecting new partner's profit share, capital contribution, etc.
7. Execute amended agreement (notarised in some states).
8. File Form 4 within 30 days of change, Form 3 if agreement is modified.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can a company be added as a partner in an LLP?** Yes. Companies can be partners in LLPs. The company must designate a nominee individual to represent it. The nominee acts on behalf of the corporate partner.

**2. What is the difference between Designated Partner and ordinary partner?** Designated Partners bear primary responsibility for LLP compliance, at least 2 DPs are mandatory, one must be resident Indian. Ordinary partners contribute capital and share profits but do not have statutory compliance responsibilities. Both can have voting rights as per the LLP Agreement.

**3. Is stamp duty required on the amended LLP Agreement?** Yes. Stamp duty on LLP Agreement amendments varies by state (typically ₹100 to ₹500 plus capital-linked component). Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and other states have varying rates.

**4. Can a minor be added as a partner in an LLP?** No. Under the Indian Partnership Act and LLP Act, minors cannot be partners. They can only be admitted to the benefits of partnership, not as full partners.

**5. Does adding a partner require unanimous consent of existing partners?** This depends on the LLP Agreement. Most agreements require either unanimous consent or majority approval (specified percentage) for adding new partners. Check the existing LLP Agreement for the specific procedure.
