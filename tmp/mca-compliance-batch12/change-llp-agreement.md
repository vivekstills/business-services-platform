## Change LLP agreement

### Detailed overview

The LLP Agreement is the foundational governance document of a Limited Liability Partnership defining partner roles, capital contribution, profit sharing, decision-making, and operational matters. Changes to the LLP Agreement are common, partner additions or exits, capital changes, profit-sharing modifications, governance changes, and require execution of supplementary deeds and filing with the MCA via Form 3.

### Key features

- Supplementary deed or fresh agreement, partner consent as per contract
- Form 3 within 30 days, stamp and notarisation as per state practice
- Aligns commercial terms with current partner expectations

## Applicability

Applicable to all LLPs in India undertaking changes in their existing LLP Agreement.

## Compliance requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| Supplementary deed | Documenting agreement changes |
| Stamp duty | Per state stamp duty laws on supplementary deed |
| Notarisation | Recommended in most states |
| Form 3 | Filed within 30 days of agreement modification |
| Partner consent | As per existing agreement |

## Process

1. Identify changes needed.
2. Draft supplementary deed.
3. Obtain partner approvals as per LLP Agreement.
4. Execute supplementary deed on stamp paper.
5. Notarise (state-specific).
6. File Form 3 within 30 days.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can the LLP Agreement be amended through a board resolution alone?** No. LLP Agreements are partnership contracts and require partner consent (as per existing agreement provisions) and execution of a supplementary deed, not just resolutions.

**2. Is unanimous consent of all partners required for agreement changes?** This depends on the existing LLP Agreement. Most agreements require either unanimous consent or specified majority (e.g. 75%). Check the specific provisions.

**3. Does the supplementary deed require fresh execution by all partners?** Yes. All current partners (and any new partners being added) must sign the supplementary deed.

**4. What stamp duty applies to LLP Agreement changes?** Varies by state, typically ₹100 to ₹2,000 base plus additional duty if capital contribution increases. Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu rates differ.

**5. Are LLP Agreement changes effective from execution date or filing date?** Generally effective from the execution date of the supplementary deed (as agreed by partners), but legally enforceable against third parties only after MCA filing via Form 3.
