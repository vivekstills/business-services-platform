## MGT-7A filing

### Detailed overview

Form MGT-7A is the simplified Annual Return form prescribed for small companies and One Person Companies (OPCs) under the Companies Act, 2013. Introduced through MCA notification on March 5, 2021, MGT-7A replaced the earlier MGT-7 filing requirement for these specific categories of companies, significantly reducing the compliance burden for small businesses.

The form captures essential annual information, shareholders, directors, capital, and key changes, but with fewer mandatory schedules and disclosures compared to the full MGT-7. This recognition of the smaller scale of operations of these companies makes MCA compliance more accessible for startups, SMEs, and one-person businesses.

### Key features

- Simplified annual return for eligible small companies and all OPCs
- Fewer schedules than MGT-7, no MGT-8 in the same way as full MGT-7
- Director DSC pathway without mandatory practising CS in the same manner as MGT-7 for many filers

## Applicability

MGT-7A applies to small companies, where paid-up capital ≤ ₹4 crore and turnover ≤ ₹40 crore, and to one person companies (OPCs), all OPCs regardless of size. A "Small Company" under Section 2(85) of the Companies Act must satisfy both conditions on paid-up capital and turnover. If either threshold is breached, the company must file regular MGT-7. It is applicable pan-India.

## Compliance requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| Form | MGT-7A |
| Due date | Within 60 days of AGM (regular companies) |
| Due date for OPC | Within 60 days from completion of 6 months from FY end (no AGM) |
| Documents | Shareholding pattern, director details, debt particulars |
| Certification | DSC of director (CS certification not mandatory) |
| Simplified vs MGT-7 | No MGT-8 required as described for the large-company MGT-7 track |

## Benefits

- Simplified compliance, fewer disclosures
- Lower professional fees (CS certification not mandatory in the same way)
- Faster preparation
- Recognition of small company and OPC status
- Same legal validity as MGT-7 for eligible filers

## Risks and penalties

| Default | Penalty |
| Late filing | ₹100 per day |
| Continued non-filing (3 years) | Director disqualification |
| Strike-off risk | After persistent non-compliance |

## Documents required

| Document | Purpose |
| Shareholding pattern | Current shareholders with shares held |
| Director particulars | DIN, dates of appointment or cessation |
| Debt and charges | If any |
| AGM or board approval | Confirming the annual return |
| Director's DSC | For form signing |

## Process

1. Confirm small company or OPC eligibility (verify paid-up capital and turnover thresholds).
2. Compile annual data.
3. Hold AGM (for small companies) or use board resolution (for OPC).
4. Prepare MGT-7A.
5. Director DSC signing.
6. File on the MCA portal.

## Government fees

| Authorized capital | Fee |
| Up to ₹1 lakh | ₹200 |
| ₹1 lakh – ₹5 lakh | ₹300 |
| ₹5 lakh – ₹25 lakh | ₹400 |
| ₹25 lakh – ₹1 crore | ₹500 |
| Above ₹1 crore | ₹600 |

Late fee: ₹100 per day.

## Timeline

60 days from AGM date for small companies, for OPCs, 60 days after 6 months from FY end (i.e., by November 27 for FY ending March 31).

## Frequently asked questions

**1. If our company crosses the small company threshold mid-year, do we file MGT-7A or MGT-7?** Eligibility is determined based on the data as on the AGM date or relevant balance sheet date. If the company crossed thresholds during the year, MGT-7 (regular) applies for that year onwards.

**2. Does MGT-7A require Practising Company Secretary certification?** No, unlike MGT-7 which requires CS certification for many companies, MGT-7A does not require mandatory CS certification, only director's DSC is sufficient.

**3. Can a startup that grew rapidly continue filing MGT-7A?** Only if both paid-up capital remains ≤ ₹4 crore and turnover remains ≤ ₹40 crore. Crossing either threshold mandates MGT-7 filing.

**4. What is the OPC-specific MGT-7A timeline?** OPCs do not hold AGMs. MGT-7A for OPCs is due 60 days after the expiry of 6 months from FY end, typically by November 27 for FY ending March 31.

**5. Is MGT-7A filing applicable to dormant small companies?** Yes. Small companies, even if dormant, must file MGT-7A annually unless they obtain formal Dormant Company status under Section 455.
