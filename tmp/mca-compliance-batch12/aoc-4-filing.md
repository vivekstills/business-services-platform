## AOC-4 filing

### Detailed overview

Form AOC-4 is the prescribed form under Section 137 of the Companies Act, 2013 read with Rule 12 of the Companies (Accounts) Rules, 2014, for filing the financial statements of a company with the Registrar of Companies (RoC). Every company incorporated in India, whether private limited, public limited, OPC, Section 8, or producer company, is required to file Form AOC-4 annually with the MCA, capturing its audited Balance Sheet, Profit and Loss Account, Cash Flow Statement, Notes to Accounts, and Directors' Report.

AOC-4 is one of the two foundational annual MCA filings (the other being MGT-7) and represents a company's compliance with statutory financial disclosure obligations. The financial statements filed via AOC-4 become part of the public record on the MCA portal, accessible to investors, lenders, vendors, and regulators.

The form has variants depending on the type of the company.

- AOC-4, for unlisted companies with non-XBRL filings
- AOC-4 XBRL, for companies required to file in XBRL format (listed companies, large unlisted companies)
- AOC-4 NBFC, for NBFCs
- AOC-4 CFS, for consolidated financial statements

### Key features

- Prescribed form for audited balance sheet, profit and loss, cash flow (where applicable), notes, and Directors' Report with RoC
- One of the two foundational annual MCA filings with MGT-7
- Public record disclosure on the MCA portal for investors, lenders, vendors, and regulators
- Pan-India filing across all states and UTs

## Applicability

AOC-4 is applicable to all companies incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013 (or earlier Companies Act, 1956), including private limited companies, public limited companies (listed and unlisted), one person companies (OPCs), Section 8 companies (non-profit), producer companies, and Nidhi companies. The form must be filed pan-India regardless of the state of incorporation, including Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Delhi, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, and all other states and UTs.

## Compliance requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| Form filing | AOC-4, AOC-4 XBRL, or AOC-4 NBFC as applicable |
| Due date | Within 30 days of the AGM |
| AGM cutoff | September 30 (for FY ending March 31) |
| Standard AOC-4 due date | October 30 |
| Documents to attach | Audited financials, board report, auditor report, CSR report (if applicable) |
| Digital signing | DSC of one director and DSC of a practising professional (CA, CS, or CMA) |
| Pre-condition | Audit completion and AGM held |

## Benefits

- Statutory MCA compliance maintenance
- Public disclosure of financials supports lender and vendor due diligence
- Enables annual return filing (MGT-7 follow-on filing)
- Required for company status as Active on the MCA portal
- Critical for due diligence in M&A, fundraising, and IPO
- Establishes a track record of good corporate governance

## Risks and penalties

| Default type | Penalty |
| Late filing, first 15 days | ₹100 per day (after due date) |
| Continued delay | ₹100 per day until filing |
| Maximum late fee | No statutory cap, accumulates indefinitely |
| Company in default | Company status moves to default, which restricts further filings |
| Director disqualification | If default exceeds 3 years (with MGT-7), directors disqualified under Section 164(2) |
| Strike-off risk | Continued non-filing leads to strike-off of the company by RoC |

## Process

1. **Audit completion:** Statutory auditor completes audit of financial statements.
2. **Board approval:** Board approves audited financials and the Directors' Report.
3. **AGM convening:** Annual General Meeting is held by September 30 (for FY ending March 31), where shareholders approve the financials.
4. **Form preparation:** AOC-4 is prepared with all attachments, financials, reports, and supporting documents.
5. **Professional certification:** A practising CA, CS, or CMA verifies the form.
6. **DSC signing:** The form is digitally signed by a director and the certifying professional.
7. **MCA portal filing:** The form is uploaded on mca.gov.in within 30 days of the AGM.
8. **SRN generation:** The system generates a Service Request Number as confirmation of filing.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. What happens if a company misses the AOC-4 filing deadline by 6 months?** The company accrues a late fee of ₹100 per day from the due date, for 6 months of delay, this works out to approximately ₹18,000 in late fees. Beyond financial penalty, the company's compliance status is flagged as in default on the MCA portal, which can affect its ability to file other statutory forms, raise loans, or enter into business contracts that require compliance verification.

**2. Can AOC-4 be revised after filing?** Yes, AOC-4 can be revised through a fresh filing. If any error or omission is discovered after the original filing, the company can file a revised AOC-4 with the corrected information. The revised filing must be approved by the board and shareholders (in case of material changes) and filed with the MCA.

**3. Is AOC-4 mandatory for an OPC (One Person Company)?** Yes, OPCs must file AOC-4 annually like any other company. However, OPCs file AOC-4 within 180 days from the end of the financial year (i.e., by September 27 for FY ending March 31), and they do not need to hold an AGM, financial statements need only be approved by the sole member.

**4. What is AOC-4 XBRL and which companies must file it?** AOC-4 XBRL is the XBRL (eXtensible Business Reporting Language) format of AOC-4, mandatory for: (a) all listed companies and their subsidiaries, (b) companies with paid-up capital of ₹5 crore or more, (c) companies with turnover of ₹100 crore or more, (d) all companies required to prepare consolidated financial statements. XBRL filing requires specialised software and CA or CS expertise.

**5. Does AOC-4 need to be filed even if a company had no business activity during the year?** Yes. Even dormant or non-operational companies must file AOC-4 with NIL or minimal financial statements. The only exception is companies that have obtained Dormant Company status under Section 455, they have separate, simplified filing requirements.
