## Close an LLP

### Detailed overview

Closing an LLP in India can be done through strike-off under the LLP Act for defunct LLPs (Form 24), voluntary winding up by partners, or compulsory winding up by Tribunal or Court. The most common is strike-off via Form 24, available for LLPs that have not commenced business or have stopped business for at least 1 year.

### Key features

- Form 24 route for eligible LLPs with Form 8 and 11 in order
- Partner resolutions, accounts, and indemnities aligned with RoC checklists
- Distinguish from compulsory winding and litigation-driven exits

## Applicability

Applicable to all LLPs wishing to close. Pan-India.

## Compliance requirements

| Method | Pre-conditions |
| Strike-off (Form 24) | No business for 1+ year (or never commenced), no liabilities, all returns filed |
| Voluntary winding up | Resolution by all partners, appointed liquidator |
| Compulsory winding up | Tribunal order, specific grounds |

## Benefits

- Clean closure with no future liabilities
- End of LLP compliance obligations
- Cost savings on annual filings

## Risks and penalties

| Risk | Detail |
| Pending compliance | Form 8, 11 must be up to date |
| Pending audit | Complete audit before closure |
| Active assets or liabilities | Settle and distribute before closure |

## Documents required

| Document | Purpose |
| Form 24 | Strike-off application |
| Indemnity bond | From all partners |
| Affidavit | Confirming no business, no liabilities |
| Statement of accounts | Recent (within 30 days) |
| All pending Form 8 and Form 11 | Must be filed |
| Income tax returns | Up to date |
| Bank account closure proof | |
| NOC from creditors | If any liabilities historically existed |

## Process

1. Verify eligibility (no business for 1+ year)
2. Settle all liabilities and assets
3. File pending Form 8, 11
4. Close bank accounts
5. Partners approve closure
6. File Form 24 with attachments
7. RoC publishes notice
8. 30-day objection period
9. Strike-off order if no objections

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can an LLP be closed in the same year of incorporation?** No. Form 24 strike-off requires the LLP to not have commenced business for at least 1 year. Newly incorporated LLPs must wait or use other closure methods.

**2. What if my LLP has filed all returns but had minor business activity?** Even minor activity disqualifies Form 24 strike-off. Either continue and close after 1 year of inactivity, or pursue voluntary winding up.

**3. Can partners be held liable post-closure?** Indemnity bond signed by partners protects against future claims. However, if it later emerges that the LLP had hidden liabilities, partners may face personal liability.

**4. Is closure allowed if LLP has pending tax disputes?** No. All tax matters must be settled. Pending IT proceedings must be concluded before closure.

**5. What happens to the LLP's PAN after closure?** PAN technically continues but becomes inactive. Income Tax department is informed via the closure of LLP.
