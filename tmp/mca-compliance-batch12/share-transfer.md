## Share transfer

### Detailed overview

Share transfer is the legal process of transferring ownership of shares from one shareholder (transferor) to another (transferee). Governed by Section 56 of the Companies Act, 2013, share transfers can be private (unlisted companies, through Form SH-4) or through stock exchange (listed companies, automated via depository system).

For private companies, AOA may impose restrictions on share transfer (right of first refusal, board approval, etc.), common for protecting shareholding integrity.

### Key features

- SH-4 and stamp for unlisted, demat and exchange settlement for listed
- Board can refuse in private companies if AOA allows, with reasons
- FEMA and pricing rules for non-resident leg of transfers

## Applicability

All companies (private and public). Process varies based on listing status. Pan-India.

## Compliance requirements

| Type | Process |
| Unlisted private company | Form SH-4 and share certificate, board approval if AOA requires, stamp duty |
| Unlisted public company | Form SH-4 and share certificate, stamp duty |
| Listed company | Demat transfer through depository, broker-mediated |

## Benefits

- Liquidity for shareholders
- Capital exit mechanism
- Strategic equity restructuring
- Investor exit and entry

| Risk | Detail |

## Process

1. Transferor and transferee execute Form SH-4 with transferee's particulars
2. Pay stamp duty (0.25% of consideration value)
3. Submit share certificate and SH-4 to company
4. Board considers (within 30 days for private, immediate for public)
5. If approved, name updated in register, new certificate issued
6. If refused, written reasons within 30 days, appeal to NCLT possible

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can private company share transfer be refused by the board?** Yes, if AOA grants such power. Board can refuse on stipulated grounds (e.g. transferee not approved). Refusal must be communicated within 30 days, transferor can appeal to NCLT.

**2. What is the stamp duty on share transfer?** 0.25% of consideration value (Indian Stamp Act). State-specific variations apply. Adhesive stamps or e-stamping accepted.

**3. Are gifts of shares treated as transfers?** Yes. Gift of shares is a transfer requiring SH-4, stamp duty (on market value of shares), and procedure. Tax implications under the Income Tax Act also apply.

**4. Can shares of a private company be transferred to non-residents?** Subject to FEMA compliance, particularly Section 6 of FEMA (capital account transactions). Pricing, sectoral caps, and FC-TRS filing may apply for transfers to non-residents.

**5. What happens if SH-4 is lost before company processing?** Reissue with proper indemnity from transferor. Stamp duty already paid is not refundable, new SH-4 with fresh stamp may be required (state-specific).
