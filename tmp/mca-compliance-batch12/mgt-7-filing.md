## MGT-7 filing

### Detailed overview

Form MGT-7 is the prescribed form under Section 92 of the Companies Act, 2013 read with Rule 11 of the Companies (Management and Administration) Rules, 2014, for filing the Annual Return of a company with the Registrar of Companies. The Annual Return captures comprehensive information about the company's structure, governance, capital, shareholding, debt, and changes during the financial year.

While AOC-4 captures financial statements, MGT-7 captures non-financial corporate information, making it the second pillar of a company's annual MCA compliance. The Annual Return is a critical disclosure document used by regulators, investors, and stakeholders to understand a company's governance and structural status.

For small companies and OPCs, a simplified form MGT-7A is prescribed (covered separately).

### Key features

- Annual return of corporate, governance, and capital information with RoC
- Complements AOC-4 (financials) with non-financial disclosures
- Listed and certain unlisted companies may require a practising company secretary in the loop for certification, as applicable

## Applicability

MGT-7 is applicable to all Indian companies (private limited, public limited, listed), Section 8 companies (above the small company threshold), producer companies, and foreign companies operating in India (with modifications). Exception: small companies (paid-up capital ≤ ₹4 crore and turnover ≤ ₹40 crore) and OPCs file MGT-7A instead. It is applicable across all states and UTs in India.

## Compliance requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| Form | MGT-7 (or MGT-7A for small companies and OPCs) |
| Due date | Within 60 days of AGM |
| AGM cutoff | September 30 (FY ending March 31) |
| MGT-7 due date | November 29 (typically) |
| Pre-conditions | All other annual filings done, share transfer register updated |
| Certification | DSC of director and DSC of CS in practice (mandatory for listed and certain unlisted companies) |
| MGT-8 attachment | For companies with paid-up capital ≥ ₹10 crore or turnover ≥ ₹50 crore |

## Benefits

- Statutory annual MCA compliance
- Public record of company structure and governance
- Required for company status maintenance
- Supports M&A, IPO, and fundraising due diligence
- Director and shareholder records maintained on the MCA portal

## Risks and penalties

| Default | Consequence |
| Late filing | ₹100 per day late fee |
| Continued non-filing (3+ years) | Director disqualification under Section 164(2) for 5 years |
| Company default status | Restricts further MCA filings |
| Strike-off | RoC may strike off non-compliant companies |
| Shareholder challenges | Outdated records create disputes |

| Share transfer details | Transfers during the year |
| AGM minutes | Confirming approval of the annual return |
| Form MGT-8 | For applicable companies (CS certification) |
| DSC of director and CS | For form signing |

## Process

1. Compile all annual data: shareholders, directors, capital changes, share transfers.
2. Update statutory registers (share transfer register, members register).
3. Hold AGM and approve the annual return.
4. Prepare MGT-7 with all schedules and attachments.
5. Engage a practising CS for MGT-8 certification (if applicable).
6. DSC signing by director and certifying CS.
7. File MGT-7 on the MCA portal within 60 days of the AGM.
8. SRN generation and acknowledgement.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Is MGT-7 different for small companies and large companies?** Yes. Small companies (paid-up capital ≤ ₹4 crore and turnover ≤ ₹40 crore) and OPCs file MGT-7A, a simplified version. All other companies file MGT-7. Large companies (paid-up capital ≥ ₹10 crore or turnover ≥ ₹50 crore) additionally need MGT-8 (CS certification).

**2. What is MGT-8 and when is it required?** MGT-8 is a Secretarial Audit Report attached to MGT-7 for companies with paid-up capital ≥ ₹10 crore or turnover ≥ ₹50 crore. It is certified by a Practising Company Secretary and provides assurance on annual return contents.

**3. Can MGT-7 be revised after filing?** Yes, similar to AOC-4. If errors are discovered, a revised MGT-7 can be filed. Major revisions may require board and shareholder approval depending on the nature of changes.

**4. Are foreign companies operating in India required to file MGT-7?** Foreign companies file Form FC-3 instead of MGT-7, covering similar information but adapted for the foreign company structure. Subsidiary Indian companies of foreign parents file regular MGT-7.

**5. What is the difference between AOC-4 and MGT-7?** AOC-4 captures financial statements (audited balance sheet, P&L, cash flow), MGT-7 captures non-financial corporate information (shareholders, directors, share transfers, debt, charges). Both are mandatory annual filings, AOC-4 within 30 days of AGM, MGT-7 within 60 days of AGM.
