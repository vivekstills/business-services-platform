## Remove directors

### Detailed overview

Removal of a director from a company is a significant corporate action governed by Section 169 of the Companies Act, 2013. A director can be removed by voluntary resignation, director resigns, Form DIR-12 filed, removal by shareholders through ordinary resolution at general meeting, disqualification automatic under Section 164 (e.g. MCA filing default for 3 years), cessation by operation of law, death, insolvency, conviction.

Removal must comply with statutory notice and procedure to avoid procedural challenges.

### Key features

- Resignation vs shareholder removal, different notice and hearing rules
- DIR-12 in 30 days in each case, registers updated
- Section 164 disqualification is separate from board-driven removal

## Applicability

Applicable to all companies seeking to remove directors. Pan-India.

## Compliance requirements

| Removal type | Procedure |
| Voluntary resignation | Director gives written notice, board accepts, DIR-12 within 30 days |
| Shareholder removal | Special notice (14 days), ordinary resolution at general meeting, DIR-12 within 30 days |
| Disqualification | Automatic, intimation via DIR-12 |
| Death | DIR-12 with death certificate within 30 days |

## Benefits

- Board restructuring
- Compliance with director limits
- Removal of underperforming or disqualified directors
- Investor accommodation post-funding

## Process

**Voluntary resignation:** director submits resignation letter, board acknowledges, file DIR-12 within 30 days.

**Shareholder removal:** special notice received (14 days before meeting), notice circulated to director (right to representation), EGM convened, director has right to speak, ordinary resolution passed, file DIR-12 within 30 days.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can a director be removed without notice or hearing?** No. Section 169 requires special notice (14 days) for shareholder removal, and the director has right to representation or being heard. Improper removal can be reversed by NCLT.

**2. Can a director resign without board acceptance?** Resignation is effective from the date specified in the resignation letter (or date of receipt by company if not specified). Board acceptance is procedural. However, DIR-12 must be filed regardless.

**3. What happens to directors disqualified under Section 164(2)?** Disqualified directors are deemed removed from all companies they are directors of. They cannot be directors for 5 years. Reinstatement requires lifting of disqualification.

**4. Can a removed director claim compensation?** Generally yes, particularly for executive directors with employment contracts. Termination compensation depends on contract terms and notice period provisions.

**5. Can a director be removed in absentia?** Yes, if proper notice has been served and the director chooses not to attend or respond. The right to representation must be provided, whether exercised is the director's choice.
