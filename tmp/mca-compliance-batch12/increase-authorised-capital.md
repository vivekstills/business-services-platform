## Increase authorised capital

### Detailed overview

Authorised Capital is the maximum amount of share capital a company is authorised to issue under its MoA. When a company's growth requires issuing additional shares beyond the existing authorised capital, the authorised capital must be increased through MoA amendment under Sections 13, 61, and 64 of the Companies Act, 2013.

The process involves shareholder approval (ordinary resolution typically), MoA amendment, payment of stamp duty on the increase, and filing with the MCA via Form SH-7.

### Key features

- MoA capital clause change with SH-7 in 30 days and stamp on incremental amount
- Special resolution if articles or case demands it beyond ordinary resolution
- Allotment beyond authorised cap is void, plan capital before issuing shares

## Applicability

Applicable to companies (private and public) needing to issue shares beyond current authorised capital. Pan-India.

## Compliance requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| Ordinary resolution | Sufficient (75% special resolution if AOA requires) |
| MoA amendment | New authorised capital amount |
| Form SH-7 | Filed within 30 days |
| Form MGT-14 | If special resolution |
| Stamp duty | On the increase amount (state-specific) |

## Benefits

- Enables share issue and fundraising
- Removes regulatory cap for share allotments
- Required for IPO or large funding rounds
- Strategic flexibility

## Risks and penalties

| Default | Penalty |
| Allotment beyond authorised capital | Allotment void, severe penalty |
| Late SH-7 filing | ₹100 per day |
| Inadequate stamp duty | Documents inadmissible |

## Process

1. Board resolution proposing capital increase and convening EGM
2. EGM notice (21 days)
3. EGM and resolution passed
4. Pay stamp duty on increase
5. File SH-7 within 30 days
6. File MGT-14 if special resolution
7. Update MoA copy at office

## Government fees

| Component | Fee |
| SH-7 | ₹200 to ₹600 |
| Stamp duty | State-specific (typically 0.15% to 0.5% of increase) |
| MGT-14 (if applicable) | ₹200 to ₹600 |

## Timeline

30 to 45 days.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. What is the difference between authorised, issued, and paid-up capital?** Authorised: maximum allowed by MoA. Issued: actually issued to shareholders. Paid-up: amount actually paid by shareholders. Authorised ≥ issued ≥ paid-up.

**2. Can authorised capital be reduced?** Yes, through capital reduction process, more complex, requires NCLT approval typically. Increase is simpler than reduction.

**3. What stamp duty applies to capital increase?** Varies by state, Maharashtra (0.2% with cap), Karnataka (1% on increase), Delhi (0.15%), Tamil Nadu (varies). Check specific state stamp duty laws.

**4. Can a private company increase authorised capital without limit?** Yes, no statutory upper limit. The company can increase as needed, subject to stamp duty payment and MCA filing.

**5. Is fresh stamp duty required if authorised capital is increased?** Yes, stamp duty is payable on the incremental increase amount, not on the new total. Already-paid stamp duty on existing capital is not refunded or re-paid.
