## MSME-1 form filing

### Detailed overview

Form MSME-1 is the half-yearly return required to be filed by every specified company (defined as a company that receives goods or services from a Micro or Small Enterprise) under Section 405 of the Companies Act, 2013 read with Companies (Furnishing of Information about Payment to Micro and Small Enterprises) Order, 2019.

The form captures details of payments due to MSME suppliers exceeding 45 days from acceptance, protecting MSMEs from delayed payments by larger companies and ensuring compliance with the MSMED Act, 2006.

### Key features

- Half-yearly filing for payments outstanding beyond 45 days to micro and small enterprises
- NIL return if no such dues, still mandatory
- Udyam-based supplier identification in practice

## Applicability

Filer: all companies receiving supplies from MSMEs, particularly applicable to companies with significant procurement from small vendors. Companies must file even if there are no outstanding dues to MSMEs (NIL return).

## Compliance requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| Form | MSME-1 |
| Frequency | Half-yearly |
| Due dates | April 30 (for October to March period), October 31 (for April to September period) |
| Information | Outstanding dues to MSMEs greater than 45 days as on the period-end |
| Director DSC | For signing |

## Benefits

- Compliance with MSME protection regulations
- Avoids penalties under the Companies Act
- Demonstrates ethical procurement practices
- Aligns with government MSME support agenda

## Process

1. Identify all MSME suppliers (verify Udyam registrations)
2. Identify outstanding payments greater than 45 days as on period end
3. Compile list with invoice details and outstanding amounts
4. Prepare Form MSME-1
5. File on the MCA portal by deadline

## Government fees

| Component | Fee |
| MSME-1 | ₹200 to ₹600 |
| Late fee | ₹100 per day |

## Timeline

Half-yearly, April 30 and October 31.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. How do we identify MSME suppliers?** Suppliers should provide their Udyam Registration Number. Without Udyam registration, suppliers do not qualify as MSMEs and are not tracked in MSME-1.

**2. What if there are no outstanding payments to MSMEs?** A NIL Form MSME-1 should be filed. Filing is mandatory regardless of whether dues exist.

**3. Are dues to medium enterprises (above small) tracked?** No. MSME-1 is specifically for Micro and Small enterprise dues. Medium enterprise dues are not tracked.

**4. What if an MSME supplier has not claimed Udyam registration but qualifies?** The MSMED Act protections apply only to registered MSMEs. Without Udyam, supplier does not get statutory protections, but commercial best practice is to track and pay all suppliers timely.

**5. Can interest be charged on MSME dues greater than 45 days?** Yes, the MSMED Act allows MSMEs to claim interest at 3x the bank rate (compounding) on overdue payments. Significant cost to defaulting buyers.
