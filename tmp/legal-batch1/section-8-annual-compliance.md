## Overview

A Section 8 Company is a non-profit formed for objects such as commerce, art, science, sports, education, research, social welfare, religion, charity, or environment. It is registered under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013. Profits, if any, must be applied to objects and not distributed as dividends. Annual compliance is similar to other companies, with additional NGO, tax exemption, and FCRA dimensions where applicable.

## Applicability

Section 8 compliance applies across India. Companies receiving foreign contribution must also comply with FCRA.

## Benefits

- Protects 12A and 80G tax position where granted
- Eligibility to receive CSR from companies where rules allow
- Maintains FCRA permission for foreign donations
- Donor and public trust, plus access to some grants

> [!WARNING] Distributions
> Section 8 companies must not distribute profits to members. Surplus is applied to objects.

## Penalties and risks

| Default | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| AOC-4 / MGT-7 delay | Rs. 100 per day |
| Revocation of Section 8 licence | Company cannot continue as Section 8 |
| Serious default | Substantial Central Government fine bands under law |
| Director liability | Ranges from Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 25 lakh in serious cases |
| Loss of 12A / 80G | Donor deductions and exemption chain breaks |
| FCRA violation | Up to 5x of amount and licence cancellation in serious cases |

## Process and timeline

1. Close books, statutory and program audit.
2. Hold AGM by September 30 (typical for March year-end), approve financials.
3. File ADT-1, AOC-4, MGT-7, ITR-7 in order.
4. File FCRA (FC-4) by December 31 for the preceding FY, including nil receipt cases where required to file.
5. Track 10A/10AB windows for 12A/80G.

## Frequently asked questions

### Q1. Can a Section 8 company distribute profits?

No. Profits cannot be distributed to members; they must be applied to charitable or permitted objects.

### Q2. Is 12A and 80G registration mandatory?

Not mandatory to incorporate, but 12A supports exemption in the trust's hands and 80G supports donor deductions. Plan renewal filings on time.

### Q3. What is FCRA and when is it needed?

FCRA registration or prior permission is required to receive foreign contributions. There are tests around existence, spend on objects, and documentation.

### Q4. Can a Section 8 company convert to a private limited company?

Conversion is possible with Central Government approval and strict conditions, including use of surpluses as law requires.

### Q5. Do Section 8 companies pay income tax?

With valid 12A, income applied to charitable objects is generally exempt, subject to accumulation and other conditions; income not applied as per law can be taxable.
