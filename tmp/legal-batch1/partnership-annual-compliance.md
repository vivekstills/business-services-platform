## Overview

A Partnership Firm is governed by the Indian Partnership Act, 1932 and is one of the simplest forms of business structure in India. Unlike companies or LLPs, partnership firms are not required to file annual returns with the MCA. However, they must comply with income tax regulations, GST laws (if applicable), TDS requirements, and state-specific registrations.

## Applicability

Partnership firm compliance applies across India. Registration is at the state Registrar of Firms; compliance is mostly uniform except for professional tax and Shops and Establishment rules.

## Key features

| Theme | What it covers |
| --- | --- |
| Income tax | ITR-5, tax audit if triggered |
| GST / TDS | Returns and deposits as per registration |
| Labour / state | PT, ESI, PF, Shops Act as applicable |
| Deed | Updates to partnership deed when terms change |

## Compliance requirements

| Compliance | Form | Authority | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Income tax return | ITR-5 | IT Dept | Annual |
| Tax audit | Form 3CB-3CD | IT Dept | If turnover > Rs. 1 crore |
| GST returns | GSTR-1, 3B, 9 | GST Dept | Monthly / annual |
| TDS returns | 24Q, 26Q, 27Q | IT Dept | Quarterly |
| Professional tax | PT return | State | Monthly / annual |
| ESI / PF | As applicable | EPFO / ESIC | Monthly |
| Shops and Establishment | Renewal | State | As per state law |
| Partnership deed | Update if needed | State Registrar | As needed |

## Benefits

- Maintains legal standing and clean tax history
- Supports claiming deductions for partners' salary and interest on capital where allowed
- Reduces interest and penalty exposure
- Improves eligibility for tenders and bank loans
- Easier dissolution or conversion later

> [!NOTE] Registration
> Registration with the Registrar of Firms is not always mandatory, but an unregistered firm cannot file certain suits in court against third parties or partners in some situations.

## Penalties and risks

| Default | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Late ITR filing | Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000 |
| Tax audit non-compliance | 0.5% of turnover (max Rs. 1.5 lakh) |
| GST return delay | Rs. 50 per day (Rs. 20 for nil) + 18% interest |
| TDS default | 1% to 1.5% per month interest + penalty |
| No PAN for firm | Rs. 10,000 |
| Deed not registered (in some states) | Cannot file suits (civil remedy limits) |

## Process and timeline

1. Close books at financial year end.
2. Complete tax audit by September if applicable.
3. File ITR by July 31 (non-audit) or October 31 (audit).
4. File GST annual return by December 31 where registered.
5. File TDS returns quarterly on due dates.
6. Renew state licences and PT as per local rules.

## Frequently asked questions

### Q1. Do partnership firms need to file returns with ROC?

No, registered or unregistered partnership firms do not file returns with the MCA or ROC. Compliance is under tax and labour laws.

### Q2. Is registration of a partnership firm mandatory?

Registration with the Registrar of Firms is not mandatory under the Indian Partnership Act, 1932, but an unregistered firm cannot file suits in court against third parties or partners in many cases.

### Q3. What tax rate applies to partnership firms?

Partnership firms are generally taxed at a flat rate of 30% plus surcharge (if applicable) and 4% health and education cess, subject to current law and exemptions.

### Q4. Can partners draw salary from the firm?

Yes, working partners can draw salary and interest on capital, subject to limits under Section 40(b) and the partnership deed.

### Q5. Is GST registration mandatory for partnership firms?

GST registration is required only if turnover exceeds Rs. 20 lakh for services or Rs. 40 lakh for goods, or if the firm is engaged in interstate supply or e-commerce, subject to current thresholds.
