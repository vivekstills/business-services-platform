## Overview

A Public Limited Company is governed by the Companies Act, 2013 and can raise capital from the public. It has stricter compliance than private companies: stronger disclosure, independent directors, audit committees, and for listed companies, SEBI and stock exchange requirements. Meticulous handling is required.

## Applicability

Public limited company compliance applies across India, including listed and unlisted public companies. Listed companies additionally follow SEBI LODR and market disclosures.

## Benefits

- Protects shareholders and market confidence
- Preserves ability to raise public capital and maintain listing
- Reduces regulatory fines, prosecution risk, and delisting issues
- Supports credit ratings and institutional interest

> [!NOTE] Secretarial audit
> Mandatory for every listed company, and for unlisted public companies meeting paid-up / turnover thresholds under law. Confirm each year.

## Process and timeline

1. March 31 financial year end: close books, complete statutory and secretarial audit as applicable.
2. Board approves results and recommended dividend, if any.
3. Hold AGM by September 30; file ADT-1, AOC-4, MGT-7 in prescribed windows.
4. Listed: publish quarterly / annual results per LODR timelines.
5. File ITR-6 by October 31; DPT-3 by June 30; DIR-3 KYC by September 30.

## Frequently asked questions

### Q1. What is the difference between AOC-4 and AOC-4 XBRL?

AOC-4 XBRL is mandatory for listed companies and others meeting paid-up or turnover tests under law. Others may file AOC-4. Confirm the applicable class each year.

### Q2. Is secretarial audit mandatory for all public companies?

Mandatory for all listed companies, and for unlisted public companies that exceed prescribed paid-up or turnover limits.

### Q3. How many independent directors are required?

Broadly, listed public companies need at least one third of the board as independent directors; unlisted public companies have different trigger rules. Use live MCA / SEBI rules at appointment.

### Q4. What is the CSR threshold?

CSR generally applies to companies with net worth of Rs. 500 crore+, turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore+, or net profit of Rs. 5 crore+ in the preceding year, as per current provisions.

### Q5. Do listed companies file with MCA and SEBI?

Yes, listed companies must satisfy Companies Act (MCA) and SEBI (LODR and other regulations) requirements, with separate disclosures to each as applicable.
