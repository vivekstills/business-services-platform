# Public Limited Company Annual Compliance

## Overview

A Public Limited Company is governed by the Companies Act, 2013 and can raise capital from the public by issuing shares. It has stricter compliance requirements than private limited companies, including enhanced disclosure norms, independent directors, audit committees, and SEBI/stock exchange compliance (for listed companies). Compliance is extensive and must be handled meticulously.

## Applicability

Public limited company compliance applies pan-India, covering listed and unlisted public companies in Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, and other states. Listed entities additionally comply with SEBI LODR Regulations.

## Benefits

Strict compliance safeguards shareholders' interests, preserves the company's ability to raise public capital, ensures listing continuity, boosts institutional investor confidence, attracts better credit ratings, and avoids reputational damage. It also helps avoid fines, prosecution of officers, and delisting risks.

## Documents Required

Certificate of Incorporation, MOA, AOA, PAN, TAN, audited financial statements, bank statements, shareholder register, minutes of board, AGM and committee meetings, auditor's and secretarial auditor's reports, CSR policy and reports, DSC of directors, and SEBI compliance reports (for listed entities).

## Process / Timeline

The FY ends on March 31. Audit is completed by July. Board meeting approves financials and recommends dividend. AGM is held by September 30. ADT-1, AOC-4, and MGT-7 are filed within prescribed timelines. Listed companies file quarterly results within 45 days of quarter end and audited annual results within 60 days of FY end.

## FAQs

**Q1. What is the difference between AOC-4 and AOC-4 XBRL?** AOC-4 XBRL is mandatory for listed companies, companies with paid-up capital of Rs. 5 crore+ or turnover of Rs. 100 crore+. Others file regular AOC-4.

**Q2. Is secretarial audit mandatory for all public companies?** Secretarial audit is mandatory for every listed company, and unlisted public companies with paid-up capital of Rs. 50 crore+ or turnover of Rs. 250 crore+.

**Q3. How many independent directors are required?** Listed public companies need at least 1/3rd of the board as independent directors, while specified unlisted public companies need at least 2 independent directors.

**Q4. What is the CSR threshold for public companies?** CSR applies to companies with net worth of Rs. 500 crore+, turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore+, or net profit of Rs. 5 crore+ during the preceding financial year.

**Q5. Are listed companies required to file both MCA and SEBI compliances?** Yes, listed public companies must comply with both MCA (Companies Act) and SEBI (LODR Regulations), including overlapping disclosures made separately to each regulator.
