# ONE Person Company (opc) Annual Compliance

## Overview

A One Person Company (OPC), introduced under the Companies Act, 2013, allows a single entrepreneur to operate a corporate entity with limited liability. While it enjoys several relaxations compared to private limited companies, OPCs must still comply with various annual filing requirements under the MCA, Income Tax Department, and GST laws.

## Applicability

OPC annual compliance applies uniformly across India including Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Rajasthan, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, and Union Territories. Every OPC registered under the Companies Act, 2013 must comply, regardless of whether it has conducted business.

## Compliance Requirements

| Compliance | Form | Authority | Due Date |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Financial Statements | AOC-4 | MCA | Within 180 days of FY end |
| Annual Return | MGT-7A | MCA | Within 60 days of AGM equivalent |
| Director KYC | DIR-3 KYC | MCA | September 30 |
| Statutory Audit | Auditor Report | ICAI | Annual (mandatory) |
| Income Tax Return | ITR-6 | IT Dept | October 31 |
| Tax Audit | Form 3CA-3CD | IT Dept | If applicable |
| Board Meetings | Min 2 per year | Internal | 90-day gap |
| GST Returns | GSTR-1, 3B, 9 | GST | Monthly/Annual |

## Benefits

OPC compliance ensures continuity of limited liability protection, maintains the separate legal entity status, enables easy conversion to a private limited company once thresholds are crossed, and preserves the credibility required for bank loans and B2B contracts. Compliant OPCs also find it easier to attract investors when scaling up.

## Penalties / Risks

| Non-Compliance | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| AOC-4 delay | Rs. 100 per day |
| MGT-7A delay | Rs. 100 per day |
| Director KYC default | Rs. 5,000 + DIN deactivation |
| Audit default | Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 5 lakh |
| ITR non-filing | Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000 + interest |
| Continued default | Director disqualification, company strike off |

## Documents Required

Certificate of Incorporation, MOA and AOA, PAN and TAN, bank statements, invoices and purchase records, salary records, TDS challans, GST returns, DSC of director, financial statements, nominee details, and previous ITR.

## Process / Timeline

OPCs are exempt from holding AGMs. Financial statements must be signed by the director and submitted to the auditor. Once the auditor signs, AOC-4 must be filed within 180 days from the end of the financial year (i.e., by September 27). MGT-7A is filed within 60 days thereafter. ITR-6 is filed by October 31.

## FAQs

**Q1. Is an AGM required for an OPC?** No, OPCs are specifically exempted from holding Annual General Meetings. However, financial statements must still be prepared and filed.

**Q2. What is MGT-7A and how is it different from MGT-7?** MGT-7A is an abridged form of the annual return specifically designed for OPCs and small companies, requiring less information than MGT-7.

**Q3. Can an OPC skip audit if it has no revenue?** No, statutory audit is mandatory for every OPC regardless of turnover or business activity. Even zero-revenue OPCs must get books audited.

**Q4. When must an OPC convert to a private limited company?** An OPC must convert to a private limited company when its paid-up capital exceeds Rs. 50 lakh or average annual turnover exceeds Rs. 2 crore for three consecutive years.

**Q5. How many board meetings must an OPC conduct?** An OPC with more than one director must hold at least one board meeting in each half of the calendar year, with a minimum gap of 90 days between the two meetings.
