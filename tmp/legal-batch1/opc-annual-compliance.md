## Overview

A One Person Company (OPC), introduced under the Companies Act, 2013, allows a single entrepreneur to operate a corporate entity with limited liability. While it enjoys several relaxations compared to private limited companies, OPCs must still comply with annual filing requirements under the MCA, Income Tax Department, and GST laws where applicable.

## Applicability

OPC annual compliance applies uniformly across India. Every OPC registered under the Companies Act, 2013 must comply, regardless of whether it has conducted business.

## Key features

| Theme | What it covers |
| --- | --- |
| MCA | AOC-4, MGT-7A, director KYC |
| AGMs | OPCs are exempt from AGMs, but financials and filings are mandatory |
| Audit | Statutory audit is mandatory for every OPC |
| Tax | ITR-6, tax audit when applicable, GST as per registration |

## Compliance requirements

| Compliance | Form | Authority | Due date |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Financial statements | AOC-4 | MCA | Within 180 days of FY end |
| Annual return | MGT-7A | MCA | Within 60 days of AGM equivalent |
| Director KYC | DIR-3 KYC | MCA | September 30 |
| Statutory audit | Auditor report | ICAI | Annual (mandatory) |
| Income tax return | ITR-6 | IT Dept | October 31 |
| Tax audit | Form 3CA-3CD | IT Dept | If applicable |
| Board meetings | Minutes | Internal | Min 2 per year, 90-day gap |
| GST returns | GSTR-1, 3B, 9 | GST | Monthly / annual |

## Benefits

- Continuity of limited liability and separate legal entity status
- Easier path to convert to a private limited company when thresholds are crossed
- Better credibility for bank loans and B2B contracts
- Cleaner cap table and filings if you later bring in investors

> [!IMPORTANT] AGM exemption
> OPCs are not required to hold an AGM, but financial statements, audit, and prescribed filings must still be completed on time.

## Penalties and risks

| Non-compliance | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| AOC-4 delay | Rs. 100 per day |
| MGT-7A delay | Rs. 100 per day |
| Director KYC default | Rs. 5,000 + DIN deactivation |
| Audit default | Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 5 lakh |
| ITR non-filing | Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000 + interest |
| Continued default | Director disqualification, company strike off |

## Process and timeline

1. Close books and complete statutory audit (always required for OPC).
2. No AGM: director signs off financials per law.
3. File AOC-4 within 180 days from the end of the financial year (illustrative deadline around late September for March year-end).
4. File MGT-7A within 60 days thereafter.
5. File ITR-6 by October 31; align tax audit if applicable.
6. Keep board meetings and GST or TDS cycles on track.

## Frequently asked questions

### Q1. Is an AGM required for an OPC?

No, OPCs are specifically exempted from holding Annual General Meetings. However, financial statements must still be prepared and filed.

### Q2. What is MGT-7A and how is it different from MGT-7?

MGT-7A is an abridged annual return for OPCs and small companies, with less information than MGT-7.

### Q3. Can an OPC skip audit if it has no revenue?

No, statutory audit is mandatory for every OPC regardless of turnover or business activity, including zero-revenue years.

### Q4. When must an OPC convert to a private limited company?

An OPC must convert when paid-up capital exceeds Rs. 50 lakh or average annual turnover exceeds Rs. 2 crore for three consecutive years (verify current law and notifications at filing time).

### Q5. How many board meetings must an OPC conduct?

An OPC with more than one director must hold at least one board meeting in each half of the calendar year, with a minimum gap of 90 days between the two meetings.
