# LLP Annual Compliance

## Overview

A Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) is governed by the LLP Act, 2008 and combines the flexibility of a partnership with the benefits of limited liability. Unlike private companies, LLPs have simpler compliance requirements but must still file annual returns with the MCA and maintain proper books of accounts. Compliance is mandatory even if there is no business activity during the year.

## Applicability

LLP annual compliance applies to every LLP registered in India under the LLP Act, 2008, operational across states like Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Delhi, Telangana, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Bihar, Assam, and all other states and UTs. It applies to both active and dormant LLPs.

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| TDS Returns | Form 24Q, 26Q | IT Dept | Quarterly |

## Benefits

LLP compliance ensures continuous legal validity, protects partners' limited liability status, enables smooth loan processing and tender participation, preserves the LLP's good standing for conversion into a private limited company, and prevents designated partners from being disqualified. It also builds credibility with clients and vendors.

## Documents Required

LLP Agreement, PAN and TAN, Certificate of Incorporation, bank statements for the FY, list of partners with capital contribution details, details of creditors and debtors, fixed asset register, previous year's filings, DSC of designated partners, and details of loans and borrowings.

## Process / Timeline

The LLP first prepares its books of accounts, followed by preparation of Statement of Accounts and Solvency. Form 11 (annual return) is filed by May 30 each year. Form 8 (financials) is filed by October 30. If turnover exceeds Rs. 40 lakh or contribution exceeds Rs. 25 lakh, accounts must be audited. Income tax return is filed by July 31 (non-audit) or October 31 (audit cases).

## FAQs

**Q1. Is audit mandatory for all LLPs?** No, audit is required only if annual turnover exceeds Rs. 40 lakh or total contribution exceeds Rs. 25 lakh. Otherwise, LLPs are exempt from statutory audit.

**Q2. What happens if an LLP does no business in a year?** Even inactive LLPs must file Form 8 and Form 11 annually. Alternatively, they can apply for strike off using Form 24 if they wish to close down.

**Q3. Can LLP compliance be filed after the due date?** Yes, but with additional fees of Rs. 100 per day with no upper cap, which can become very expensive for long delays.

**Q4. Is GST registration required for all LLPs?** GST is required only if aggregate turnover exceeds Rs. 20 lakh (services) or Rs. 40 lakh (goods), or if engaged in interstate supply or e-commerce.

**Q5. What is the difference between LLP and Private Limited compliance?** LLPs have fewer compliances, no mandatory board meetings or AGMs, and lower ROC fees. However, penalties for non-compliance are equally strict.
