## Overview

A Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) is governed by the LLP Act, 2008 and combines the flexibility of a partnership with the benefits of limited liability. Unlike private companies, LLPs have simpler compliance requirements but must still file annual returns with the MCA and maintain proper books of accounts. Compliance is mandatory even if there is no business activity during the year.

## Applicability

LLP annual compliance applies to every LLP registered in India under the LLP Act, 2008. It applies to both active and dormant LLPs across all states and Union Territories.

## Key features

| Theme | What it covers |
| --- | --- |
| MCA | Form 11, Form 8, designated partner KYC |
| Income tax | ITR-5, tax audit when triggered |
| GST / TDS | GSTR cycle, 24Q / 26Q where applicable |
| Books | Maintained and supported for audit thresholds |

## Compliance requirements

| Compliance | Form | Authority | Due date |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Annual return | Form 11 | MCA | May 30 |
| Statement of accounts and solvency | Form 8 | MCA | October 30 |
| Income tax return | ITR-5 | IT Dept | July 31 / October 31 |
| Tax audit | Form 3CA/3CB-3CD | IT Dept | If turnover > Rs. 1 crore |
| Director KYC | DIR-3 KYC | MCA | September 30 |
| GST returns | GSTR-1, 3B, 9 | GST Dept | Monthly / annual |
| TDS returns | Form 24Q, 26Q | IT Dept | Quarterly |

## Benefits

- Continuous legal validity and LLP name on the register
- Protection of partners' limited liability status
- Smoother loans, tenders, and vendor credibility
- Good standing if you later convert to a private limited company
- Reduces risk of designated partner disqualification

> [!TIP] Audit trigger
> Audit is required only if annual turnover exceeds Rs. 40 lakh or total contribution exceeds Rs. 25 lakh (otherwise many LLPs avoid statutory audit).

## Penalties and risks

| Default | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Form 11 late filing | Rs. 100 per day (no cap) |
| Form 8 late filing | Rs. 100 per day (no cap) |
| Non-maintenance of books | Up to Rs. 5 lakh |
| Non-filing of ITR | Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000 |
| Designated partner default | Disqualification for 5 years |
| Continued default | Strike off by Registrar |

## Process and timeline

1. Close books of accounts for the financial year.
2. Prepare Statement of Accounts and Solvency.
3. File Form 11 by May 30 each year.
4. File Form 8 by October 30; get accounts audited if thresholds are crossed.
5. File ITR by July 31 (non-audit) or October 31 (audit cases).
6. Maintain GST and TDS cycles as applicable.

## Frequently asked questions

### Q1. Is audit mandatory for all LLPs?

No, audit is required only if annual turnover exceeds Rs. 40 lakh or total contribution exceeds Rs. 25 lakh. Otherwise, LLPs are exempt from statutory audit.

### Q2. What happens if an LLP does no business in a year?

Even inactive LLPs must file Form 8 and Form 11 annually. Alternatively, they can apply for strike off using Form 24 if they wish to close down.

### Q3. Can LLP compliance be filed after the due date?

Yes, but with additional fees of Rs. 100 per day with no upper cap, which can become very expensive for long delays.

### Q4. Is GST registration required for all LLPs?

GST is required only if aggregate turnover exceeds Rs. 20 lakh (services) or Rs. 40 lakh (goods), or if engaged in interstate supply or e-commerce.

### Q5. What is the difference between LLP and private limited compliance?

LLPs have fewer compliances, no mandatory board meetings or AGMs, and lower ROC fees. However, penalties for non-compliance are equally strict.
