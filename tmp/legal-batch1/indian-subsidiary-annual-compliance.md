# Indian Subsidiary Annual Compliance

## Overview

An Indian Subsidiary is a company incorporated in India whose majority shareholding (more than 50%) is held by a foreign parent company. It operates under the Companies Act, 2013, FEMA regulations, and RBI guidelines. Annual compliance is mandatory regardless of turnover or business activity and involves reporting to the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA), Reserve Bank of India (RBI), and Income Tax Department. Non-compliance invites heavy penalties and can affect the company's FDI status.

## Applicability

Annual compliance applies to all Indian Subsidiaries operating across all Indian states including Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Delhi NCR, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, Odisha, Madhya Pradesh, and all Union Territories. It applies whether the subsidiary is wholly-owned (WOS) or partially owned, and irrespective of whether it is currently generating revenue.

## Benefits

Timely compliance helps Indian Subsidiaries maintain a clean legal standing, enables smooth repatriation of profits to the foreign parent, builds investor and regulatory trust, prevents freezing of the company's status, and ensures uninterrupted banking and FDI transactions. It also simplifies future funding rounds and exit strategies.

## Penalties / Risks

| Non-Compliance | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Delay in filing AOC-4/MGT-7 | Rs. 100 per day per form (no cap) |
| Non-filing of FLA | Up to 3x the sum involved + Rs. 5,000/day |
| Non-filing of DIR-3 KYC | Rs. 5,000 + DIN deactivation |
| Default in Transfer Pricing | 2% of transaction value |
| Non-holding of AGM | Up to Rs. 1 lakh + Rs. 5,000/day |
| Tax Audit default | 0.5% of turnover or Rs. 1.5 lakh, whichever is lower |

## Documents Required

Certificate of Incorporation, MOA and AOA, PAN and TAN, bank statements, financial statements, board resolutions, minutes of meetings, list of shareholders and directors, DSC of directors, previous year's ITR, FEMA filings proof, foreign remittance documents (FIRC/KYC), and auditor's report.

## Process / Timeline

The process begins with statutory audit completion, followed by board meeting for approval of financials, AGM conducted within 6 months of financial year end (by September 30), filing of AOC-4 within 30 days of AGM, and filing of MGT-7 within 60 days of AGM. Income tax return is filed by October 31 (for audited companies), and FLA return by July 15.

## FAQs

**Q1. Is statutory audit mandatory for a zero-turnover Indian subsidiary?** Yes, statutory audit is mandatory regardless of turnover. Every Indian Subsidiary must get its books audited by a practicing Chartered Accountant annually.

**Q2. What is FLA return and who files it?** The Foreign Liabilities and Assets (FLA) return is filed annually with RBI by all Indian companies that have received FDI or made overseas investments. It must be filed by July 15 each year.

**Q3. Can a foreign director sign documents digitally for Indian filings?** Yes, foreign directors can obtain a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) in India using their passport and apostilled documents, enabling them to sign MCA forms electronically.

**Q4. What if the Indian subsidiary has no business activity?** Even dormant subsidiaries must complete all annual filings. Alternatively, they can apply for "Dormant Company" status under Section 455 to reduce compliance requirements.

**Q5. Is transfer pricing applicable to all Indian subsidiaries?** Transfer pricing provisions under Section 92 apply only if the subsidiary has international transactions with associated enterprises. Form 3CEB must be filed in such cases.
