## Overview

An Indian Subsidiary is a company incorporated in India whose majority shareholding (more than 50%) is held by a foreign parent company. It operates under the Companies Act, 2013, FEMA regulations, and RBI guidelines. Annual compliance is mandatory regardless of turnover or business activity and involves reporting to the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA), Reserve Bank of India (RBI), and Income Tax Department. Non-compliance invites heavy penalties and can affect the company's FDI status.

## Applicability

Annual compliance applies to all Indian Subsidiaries operating across all Indian states and Union Territories. It applies whether the subsidiary is wholly-owned (WOS) or partially owned, and irrespective of whether it is currently generating revenue.

## Key features

| Theme | What it covers |
| --- | --- |
| MCA filings | AOC-4, MGT-7, director KYC, AGM discipline |
| RBI / FEMA | FLA, APR, foreign remittance documentation |
| Income tax | ITR-6, tax audit, transfer pricing (where applicable) |
| Board governance | Minimum meetings, audit by ICAI member |

| Compliance | Form / return | Authority | Frequency |

| Director KYC | DIR-3 KYC | MCA | Annual |

## Benefits

- Maintains a clean legal standing and active company status
- Supports smooth repatriation of profits to the foreign parent
- Builds investor and regulatory trust
- Reduces risk of status freeze and disruption to banking and FDI flows
- Simplifies future funding rounds and exit planning

> [!NOTE] Why it matters
> Timely compliance helps avoid compounding late fees, DIN issues, and scrutiny on related-party and cross-border transactions.

## Penalties and risks

| Non-compliance | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Delay in filing AOC-4 / MGT-7 | Rs. 100 per day per form (no cap) |
| Non-filing of FLA | Up to 3x the sum involved + Rs. 5,000 per day |
| Non-filing of DIR-3 KYC | Rs. 5,000 + DIN deactivation |
| Default in transfer pricing | 2% of transaction value |
| Non-holding of AGM | Up to Rs. 1 lakh + Rs. 5,000 per day |
| Tax audit default | 0.5% of turnover or Rs. 1.5 lakh, whichever is lower |

## Process and timeline

1. Complete statutory audit of books.
2. Hold board meeting to approve financials and convene AGM.
3. Hold AGM within 6 months of financial year end (by September 30).
4. File AOC-4 within 30 days of AGM, MGT-7 within 60 days of AGM.
5. File ITR-6 by October 31 (for audited companies where applicable).
6. File FLA return by July 15 each year; align transfer pricing filings if triggered.

## Frequently asked questions

### Q1. Is statutory audit mandatory for a zero-turnover Indian subsidiary?

Yes, statutory audit is mandatory regardless of turnover. Every Indian Subsidiary must get its books audited by a practicing Chartered Accountant annually.

### Q2. What is FLA return and who files it?

The Foreign Liabilities and Assets (FLA) return is filed annually with RBI by all Indian companies that have received FDI or made overseas investments. It must be filed by July 15 each year.

### Q3. Can a foreign director sign documents digitally for Indian filings?

Yes, foreign directors can obtain a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) in India using their passport and apostilled documents, enabling them to sign MCA forms electronically.

### Q4. What if the Indian subsidiary has no business activity?

Even dormant subsidiaries must complete all annual filings. Alternatively, they can apply for "Dormant Company" status under Section 455 to reduce compliance requirements.

### Q5. Is transfer pricing applicable to all Indian subsidiaries?

Transfer pricing provisions under Section 92 apply only if the subsidiary has international transactions with associated enterprises. Form 3CEB must be filed in such cases.
