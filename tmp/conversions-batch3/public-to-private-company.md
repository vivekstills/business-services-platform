# Public to private limited company conversion

## Overview

A public limited company can reclassify as a private limited company by altering the M&A under Section 14 of the Companies Act, 2013. A proviso in Section 14(1) can require NCLT approval if the company has made a public issue of shares or has more than 200 shareholders. Listed companies usually complete a SEBI-regulated delisting before the RoC re-styles the name to add "Private" on the same CIN.

> [!TIP] **When to use**  
> After a de-listing, a take-private, or when you need pvt ltd governance, transfer restrictions, and a "Private" in the legal name on the same CIN.

## Key features

- MGT-14 and INC-27-class e-forms (V3 e-form set in force on the filing date) with M&A for a private company.
- NCLT if the Section 14(1) proviso matches your cap table and history; confirm the live proviso and rules with your company secretary.
- If BSE or NSE-listed, SEBI delisting and settlement with public holders typically comes before the pvt M&A and COI re-style on the CIN-continued file.

| Track | What to expect |
|-------|-----------------|
| Unlisted, 200 or fewer shareholders, no public offer | Simpler EGM, MGT-14, and INC-27-class path with the RoC, subject to the live MCA user manual. |
| Proviso facts or a listed name | NCLT order and/or SEBI delisting, then the pvt re-style; sequence and notices are part of a tight CS file. |

## Benefits

- The word "Private" in the name and a M&A that matches pvt ltd rules, usually on the same CIN.
- Share transfer restrictions in the AOA, which a pvt ltd can use as the Act and your deed read on the filing date.
- Lower public-disclosure and SEBI-LODR load after a de-list, versus a main-board listed CIN, subject to the facts in your re-style file.

> [!NOTE] **Limitations**  
> NCLT time and cost if the proviso applies. SEBI delisting is long and data-heavy for listed names. Public deposits, if any, must be repaid or squared before you lock the pvt re-style. Minority rights in NCLT and SEBI paths need a fair process.

## Process

1. With your CS, read whether the Section 14(1) proviso applies: public offer of shares or a cap table with more than 200 shareholders, as the Act in force on the filing date states.
2. If listed, run SEBI delisting in the order the regulations require; pay fair exit to public holders in the SEBI path you choose.
3. EGM: pass a special resolution for the pvt re-style and M&A change. File MGT-14 in the time the Act and form text give you.
4. If NCLT: petition, notices to shareholders and creditors, and a bench order, then file the order with the RoC as the manual requires.
5. File INC-27-class and M&A in the pvt set; the RoC re-issues or amends the COI so the name has "Private Limited" in the pvt class on the CIN-continued line, in the normal CIN-continued pvt re-style.
6. Update GST, banks, and CERSAI- and counterparty KYC; if you de-listed, close STT- and-listed-KYC on the bourses as the bourse and SEBI ask.

## Tax impact (summary)

A CIN-continued pvt re-style of a CIN-continued public, where no re-allocation of the whole balance sheet to a new CIN happens in your fact pattern, is often tax-neutral at entity level: same PAN and CIN, no fresh deemed transfer in the pvt re-style on the same CIN-continued CIN, subject to a CA read of your M&A, delisting, and buyback facts. A delisting buyback can attract buyback tax under Section 115QA in the company and Section 10(34A) read with capital gains in hands of holders; map each leg with a CA. STT and securities-line TDS fall away on delisted and off-bourse tranches as the year and the facts in your bourse/SEBI and IT read show.

## Applicability

Public limited companies in all states and UTs under the 2013 Act, filed on the V3 e-form set with the jurisdictional RoC, and, when the proviso applies, at the NCLT bench the manual routes you to, and, when the name is listed, on the SEBI and exchange set first.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is NCLT always required?** No. It is required when the proviso in Section 14(1) is met, for example a past public offer of shares or more than 200 shareholders, per the text in force on the filing date and your cap table. Your CS confirms.

**Can a listed company go private in one day?** No. Delisting and public-holder settlement are long; the pvt re-style and COI re-issue follow in a second wave after a clean bourse/SEBI line.

**How long does it take?** Unlisted, at most 200 shareholders, no public offer: often 2 to 4 months. Listed: 6 to 12+ months with delisting, then 2 to 4 months for the pvt re-style, as a very rough range.

**What about minority holders?** In listed paths, SEBI reverse book-building and fair price rules protect retail and non-promoter holders. In NCLT, minority rights and creditor notice are in the NCLT order text.

**Can we go public again later?** Yes. A pvt can convert back to a public and then list under the pvt to public and SEBI path when you are ready, subject to fresh resolutions and a new growth story in law.
