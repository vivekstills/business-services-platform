# Private to public limited company conversion

## Overview

A **private limited** company becomes a **public limited** company by altering its **MOA** and **AOA** under **Section 14 of the Companies Act, 2013**. The change is used when the board plans a wider shareholder base, a future **IPO**, or contracts that need **"Limited"** without **"Private"** in the name.

> [!TIP] **When to use it**  
> You expect **more than 200** shareholders, want to issue a **prospectus** or build toward **BSE/NSE** listing, or need a **public** company profile for **large** tenders and **institutional** **comfort**.

## Key features

- **EGM** and **special resolution** (at least **75%** votes) for **conversion** and **M&A** change.
- **MGT-14** and **INC-27** (or the applicable current **MCA** e-form) with public-company **MOA/AOA**.
- **Minimum 7** shareholders and **3** directors before or when the new certificate is issued. **"Private"** is **dropped** from the legal name in the **normal** case.

| Area | Public company note |
|------|---------------------|
| Transfer of shares | Freer transfer; still read **class** and **agreements** for any **restriction** on a **set** of **securities** |
| Governance | Heavier: independent directors, secretarial audit and CSR at **stated** **thresholds** |
| Listing | **Converting** to public and **listing** are different; SEBI and exchanges **govern** an **IPO** when you **choose** that path |

## Benefits

- A **larger** shareholder base and a path to **prospectus**-based and **public** **capital** when you choose.
- **Same PAN and CIN** in the standard conversion, so **continuity** of **books** and **credits** on one **entity** id.
- **Name** line drops **"Private"**; rest of the **name** is unchanged unless you run a **separate** **name** change.

> [!NOTE] **Limitations**  
> Ongoing **compliance** is **higher** than for a pvt ltd. **SEBI/ exchange** and **DRHP** work apply **only** if you **list**; a **public** CIN without listing still has a **higher** **governance** bar than a **pvt** **ltd** at similar scale.

## Process

1. **Board** then **EGM** pass a **special resolution** for conversion to **public** and **M&A** amendment.
2. **File MGT-14** within the statutory window (commonly **30 days**; confirm the live form).
3. **File INC-27** with **amended** **MOA/AOA** for a **public** company.
4. Ensure at least **7** shareholders and **3** directors; use **DIR-2/DSC** and **DIR-12** as applicable for new appointees.
5. **RoC** issues an amended **COI**; **"Private"** is **removed** from the **registered** name (e.g. **ABC Private Limited** becomes **ABC Limited**).
6. **INC-20A** and other post-incorporation steps as applicable; **update** **GST**, **lenders**, and **KYC** with the new **M&A** and **name** line.

## Tax impact (summary)

**Same** **entity**: **no** new **CIN** in a **typical** private to public conversion. **PAN** and **CIN** **continue**; unabsorbed **losses**, **depreciation**, and **credits** roll on if the **income** **tax** **continuity** **conditions** for that company are met. A simple **M&A** restyle to **"Limited"** is **broadly** **tax**-**neutral** at the **entity** level. **STT** and **securities**-driven **TDS** follow **securities** **trading** and **listing** work, not the **act** of **dropping** **"Private"** from the **name** alone. **ESOPs** and **securities** **perquisites** are taxed when they **arise** under the **IT** **Act**; reconfirm **115BAA** / **115BAB** and **DPIIT**-linked **positions** with your **CA** on the **filing** **date**.

## Applicability

**Private** **limited** **companies** under the **Companies Act, 2013** in **all** **states** and **UTs**, filed with the **jurisdictional** **RoC** on the **current** **MCA** **portal** **journey** **in** **force** when you file.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can a public company exist without an IPO?** **Yes**. **Listing** and **converting** to **public** are **independent** steps.

**How many** **shareholders and directors?** **At least** **7** **shareholders** and **3** **directors** for a public **limited** company (listed companies have **extra** **board** **and** **SEBI** **rules** when **applicable**).

**Must the name change?** The word **"Private"** is **removed** (for example, **ABC Private Limited** to **ABC Limited**). A **further** **name** **change** needs its **own** **MCA** process.

**Is shareholder approval** **required?** **Yes**, a **special** **resolution** (typically **75%** **voting** **power**) at an **EGM** is **required** to **authorise** **conversion** and **M&A** **amendment**.

**Can a public** **limited** return to **private?** A **listed** company may be **delisted** under **SEBI** rules and may **later** **convert** to **private** with **NCLT**-backed or **simplified** **paths** as **applicable**; **treat** it as a **separate** **governance** project with your **CS**.
