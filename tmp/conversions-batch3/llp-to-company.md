# LLP to company conversion

## Overview

Converting an LLP into a private limited or public limited company is governed by **Section 366 of the Companies Act, 2013** read with the **Companies (Authorised to Register) Rules, 2014**. A registered LLP can move into a company structure while its business, assets, liabilities, and contracts stay aligned with the new entity. The conversion dissolves the LLP; a new company **inherits operations without a break in business continuity**.

> [!TIP] **At a glance**
> Ideal when you need equity investment, ESOPs, or company-only eligibility (DPIIT, tenders, FDI) while the LLP form no longer fits.

## Key features

- **Full continuity**: Assets, liabilities, and contracts are carried into the new company on approval.
- **Equity and ESOPs**: A company can issue shares and run employee stock option plans; LLPs cannot offer the same.
- **Investor and tender fit**: VCs, PE, and many tenders expect a company, not an LLP.
- **MCA process**: Filing (including Form URC-1) and regional stamp duty, NOC, and professional support apply.

| Feature | What it means for you |
|--------|------------------------|
| Statutory base | Section 366 + URC-1 process with the Registrar. |
| Partner consent | **Unanimous** partner consent; one dissenting partner can block. |
| Creditor NOC | NOC from **secured** creditors is mandatory. |
| New CIN | You receive a **new** company CIN; the LLP is formally closed. |

## Benefits

- Issue **equity** and raise funds from angels, VCs, and institutions.
- Use **ESOPs** for retention; stronger **credit** and **limited liability** with perpetual succession.
- **DPIIT** startup recognition and cleaner **valuation** for investors.
- **FDI** on the automatic route is often easier in a company than in an LLP.

> [!NOTE] **Limitations to plan for**
> The process is **lengthy** and MCA-heavy. Compliance **rises** after conversion versus an LLP. Unanimity and creditor NOC are strict.

## Process

1. **Partners' resolution** consenting to conversion (unanimous).
2. **NOC** from all **secured** creditors.
3. **Statement of assets and liabilities** certified by a CA (not older than 6 months).
4. **File Form URC-1** with MOA, AOA, and supporting documents with the **Registrar** (CRC/MCA as applicable).
5. **Certificate of registration** of the company is issued; the **LLP is deemed dissolved** from that date.
6. **File Form 14 (LLP)** to intimate **dissolution** to the LLP Registrar.
7. Apply for **fresh PAN, TAN**; **update** bank, GST, and other registrations.

## Documents required

| Document | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| LLP agreement (latest amended) | Filed copy from MCA |
| Certificate of incorporation (LLP) | Original |
| Consent of all partners | Board resolution / written consent |
| NOC from secured creditors | Original signed |
| Statement of assets & liabilities | CA certified, not older than 6 months |
| List of partners with addresses | Self-attested |
| Declaration by designated partners | As prescribed |
| Form URC-1 | Prepared for filing |
| MOA & AOA of proposed company | Drafted |
| PAN, Aadhaar, address proof of directors | KYC |
| DSC of all proposed directors | Class 3 |
| Latest filed Form 8 & Form 11 | From MCA portal |
| Proof of registered office | Utility bill + NOC + rent agreement |

## Tax impact (summary)

Treated as a **tax-neutral** transfer under **Section 47(xiiib)** of the Income Tax Act, 1961, when conditions are met: all assets/liabilities of the LLP become those of the company, partners become shareholders **in proportion**, partners keep **at least 50% voting** for **5 years**, and no partner takes consideration **other than shares**. Breach within 5 years can **revoke** the exemption. **Stamp duty** on property transfer is often not applicable if conditions are met. **GST**: transfer as a **going concern** can be exempt under **Notification 12/2017** subject to conditions.

## Applicability

For **LLPs** registered under the **LLP Act, 2008** in **all Indian states** and **Union Territories**. Application is made to the **Central Registration Centre (CRC)** of the **MCA** (or as per current MCA routing).

## Frequently asked questions

**Does the LLP need to be wound up first?** No. The LLP is **automatically dissolved** on the new company's **Certificate of Registration**; the company **inherits** assets, liabilities, and contracts.

**Can tax losses of the LLP carry forward?** Yes, if **Section 47(xiiib)** and **Section 72A** conditions are met.

**Is unanimous partner consent mandatory?** **Yes**, one dissenting partner can block the conversion.

**What happens to GSTIN?** The LLP's **GSTIN is cancelled**; the company needs **fresh registration**. **ITC** can be moved with **Form GST ITC-02** where allowed.

**How long does it take?** Often **30 to 60 days**; NOC, stamp duty, and MCA load can **extend** this.
