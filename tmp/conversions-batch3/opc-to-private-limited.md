# OPC to private limited company conversion

## Overview

A **One Person Company (OPC)** can convert to a **private limited company** voluntarily or in certain **mandatory** cases under the **Companies Act, 2013**, governed by **Section 18** and the **Companies (Incorporation) Rules, 2014**. After conversion, the entity can have **more members**, bring in **investors**, and follow the **private limited** compliance framework. The **existing CIN** of the OPC **continues** with appropriate **amendments**.

> [!TIP] **When to choose this route**
> Use it when you need co-founders, a second director/shareholder, equity funding, or a structure that matches **tenders**, **DPIIT**, or **scale** that an OPC cannot hold alone.

## Key features

- **Same lineage**: PAN and **CIN** continue; business history is not reset.
- **Wider cap table**: New shareholders and directors; room for **ESOPs** and **investor** rounds.
- **Form INC-6**: The standard MCA route for OPC to private limited, with **altered** MOA/AOA.
- **Second person**: You must add **at least one** additional director and member before or with conversion.

| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Minimum | **2** shareholders and **2** directors; one director **resident in India** |
| Irreversibility (practical) | You do **not** convert back to OPC; plan ownership accordingly |
| Nominee | Nominee's **role ends**; **NOC/communication** is advisable |

## Benefits

- **Equity and governance**: New shareholders, **ESOPs**, and bank or partner credibility.
- **Continuity**: **Same** PAN, CIN, and contracts subject to name/structure updates on licenses.
- **Flexibility**: More suitable for **VC**, **lenders**, and **complex** shareholder agreements.

> [!NOTE] **Limitations**
> Compliance **increases**: board meetings, AGM, more MCA filings, audit. **Share transfer** terms follow your MOA/AOA. A **second director** is required. Conversion is a **one-way** move to pvt ltd in practice.

## Process

1. **Board meeting**: Resolve to convert; approve **amended** MOA and AOA.
2. **Appoint** at least **one** additional **director** and **member** (minimum 2 and 2).
3. **File Form INC-6** with the **RoC** with amended documents and attachments.
4. Pay **stamp duty** and **fees** as applicable.
5. On approval, the RoC issues a **Certificate of Incorporation** as a **private limited** company, **same CIN**.
6. **Update** GST, MSME, IEC, and other records where the **name or structure** changes on registers.

## Documents required

| Document | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Certificate of incorporation (OPC) | Existing |
| MOA & AOA (OPC) | Existing |
| Board resolution for conversion | Signed |
| Altered MOA & AOA | For private limited company |
| NOC from creditors (if any) | Signed |
| Financial statements (latest) | Audited |
| INC-6 | Filed online |
| PAN, Aadhaar, address proof | For new directors/members |
| DSC of directors | Class 3 |
| DIR-2 from new directors | Consent |
| List of shareholders | With shareholding details |

## Tax impact (summary)

Generally **tax-neutral** as a **form change** for the same entity. **PAN** continues; **no** deemed transfer of assets in the standard conversion. **Losses, depreciation, credits** continue. **GSTIN** typically **stays**; **update** details with the department. **DPIIT** and similar benefits **continue** if eligibility **still** holds.

**Voluntary conversion**: Under **amended rules (2021)**, an OPC can convert to private limited **after 2 years** from incorporation without old capital/turnover gates.

## Applicability

**All states and UTs** for OPCs under the **Companies Act, 2013**. Filed with the **jurisdictional RoC** (e.g. Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi-NCR, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Rajasthan, and others).

## Frequently asked questions

**Is a new PAN required?** **No**; the OPC's PAN **continues**.

**How many directors and shareholders after?** **Minimum 2** of each; one director **resident in India**.

**Do contracts and licenses auto-continue?** The **entity** is the same **CIN**; **update** names on GST, MSME, IEC, etc. where required.

**Is nominee consent needed?** **Yes**, inform the **nominee**; **NOC** is **advisable** and some RoCs expect it.
