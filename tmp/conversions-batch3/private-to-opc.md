# Private limited to OPC conversion

## Overview

A **private limited** company can convert to a **One Person Company (OPC)** under **Section 18 of the Companies Act, 2013** read with **Rule 6(4) of the Companies (Incorporation) Rules, 2014**. The route is uncommon but valid: one person ends up with **all** the shares, and the constitution is aligned to a **single-member** form while the **same CIN and PAN** continue in most cases.

> [!TIP] **When it is used**  
> After a co-founder exits, when one shareholder will hold **100%** of the equity, or when you want a **simpler** meeting and filing rhythm (for example, **MGT-7A** in place of full **MGT-7**, and no AGM) while keeping limited liability and an incorporated shell.

## Key features

- **Special resolution** and **Form MGT-14**; **Form INC-6** with **amended** MOA and AOA for an **OPC** cap table and governance.
- **Form INC-3** nominee **consent**; **NOC** from **secured** creditors if term debt is on the books.
- **One shareholder**; **at most 15** directors. **No FDI** in an OPC. **Rule 6(4)**: a private company must exist for at least **2 years** before it may **voluntarily** convert to OPC (confirm the current rule text when you file).

| Point | What to check |
|-------|----------------|
| Pre-conversion cap table | **All** issued equity should sit with one person, or a documented buy-out must complete first |
| FDI and scale | OPC cannot take **FDI**; if you need investors, plan a later **OPC to private** path |
| Re-conversion | If the business outgrows one-owner or hits statutory triggers, a **pvt ltd** re-conversion is a separate process |

## Benefits

- **Abridged** annual return and **no AGM** in the **OPC** pattern (per current rules; verify forms each year on MCA).
- **Same** legal person: **CIN and PAN** typically continue, so **bank**, **contracts**, and much of **KYC** stay on a single identifier subject to name line updates.
- **Governance** matches **operational** reality (one **economic** owner) while you retain a **body corporate**.

> [!NOTE] **Limitations**  
> **One** shareholder only; no **ESOP** path inside OPC like a full private limited. You must appoint a **nominee**. **Voluntary** conversion is **not** available within **2 years** of incorporation of the private company under **Rule 6(4)**. If you buy out a co-founder, **that** **sale** can trigger **capital gains** for the **seller**, separate from the **form** **change** of the company.

## Process

1. **Consolidate** shareholding so **one** person holds **all** the shares, or **complete** a **valid** **transfer** to that effect.
2. **Call an EGM** (or use the **sole** **member** path where the Act and **articles** allow) and pass a **special resolution** for **OPC** conversion and **M&A** amendments.
3. **File MGT-14** for the **special** **resolution** within the **statutory** time limit (typically **30 days**; confirm the current window on the live **form** and rules).
4. **File INC-6** with amended **MOA/AOA**, **INC-3** (nominee), **NOC** from creditors if there is **secured** debt, **audited** accounts, and other annexures the live **E-form** requires on **MCA**.
5. On approval, the **RoC** issues an amended **Certificate of Incorporation** as an **OPC**; **CIN** usually **continues**.
6. **Update GST, MSME, IE Code**, and **counterpart** agreements where the old **"Private Limited"** wording no longer matches the **register of members**.

## Tax impact (summary)

A straight **pvt ltd to OPC** form change is **tax-neutral** at the company level: **same PAN and CIN**, no deemed balance-sheet transfer. A **separate** share **buyout** to reach a single owner is taxed under **income** tax and **FEMA** rules for that transaction. **Loss** carryforward and **DPIIT**-type benefits **continue** if the underlying **eligibility** tests are still met. **GST** and **TAN** usually continue; **re-KYC** with banks and **portals** after the **amended COI** is received.

## Applicability

**Private limited** companies under the **Companies Act, 2013** in all states and UTs, filed with the **jurisdictional RoC** through the current **MCA** online workflow.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can a company with two shareholders convert?** **Yes**, but only after **one** person holds **100%** of the shares. Any buy-out should be **documented** before you file the conversion set.

**Is there a two-year waiting period?** **Rule 6(4)** requires the private company to have been in existence for **at least two years** before **voluntary** conversion to OPC. Confirm the current notification text on the date of filing.

**Is a nominee mandatory?** **Yes**. The nominee’s consent in **Form INC-3** is a core filing.

**Is stamp duty payable?** Stamp duty on **MOA/AOA** alteration **varies** by state. A pure **CIN-continued** form change is **not** the same as a **land** or **conveyance** event by itself; your CS will map duty to the **amendment** in your state.

**What becomes simpler?** **No** AGM in the **OPC** model; abridged **MGT-7A** instead of full **MGT-7** where the rules allow. **Statutory** audit and **income** tax still apply on normal triggers.
