# Partnership to company conversion

## Overview

A **registered** or **unregistered** partnership can become a **private** or **public limited company** under **Section 366 of the Companies Act, 2013** read with the **Companies (Authorised to Register) Rules, 2014**. Growing family or trading businesses use this to obtain **limited liability**, stronger **corporate** governance, and the tools to **raise equity** and bid for work that points at a **CIN**-based entity.

> [!TIP] **When it fits**  
> When a deed-only firm needs **tenders** and **bank** comfort, you want to **onboard** investors or key staff with **shares** or **ESOPs**, or you are ready for a **separate** legal person for **succession** and **funding** planning.

## Key features

- **Form URC-1** with new **MOA** and **AOA**; a **new company CIN**; partners are mapped to **shareholders** in a fixed **ratio**.
- **Unanimous** partner consent and **NOCs** from **secured creditors** are standard on this route.
- **Two** newspaper advertisements (English and **regional** as rules require) to give creditors and the public a clear **objection** window on the URC-1 file.

| Pillar | What you get |
|--------|--------------|
| Resolutions and NOCs | Confirms **unanimity** and **lender** sign-off for the **conversion** |
| URC-1 and ads | **MCA**-visible file with the **stated** **objection** **period** |
| Certificate | A **new** company is **born**; the **partnership** is **dissolved**; **migrations** of tax and **licence** **IDs** follow the **BTA/URC** design |

## Benefits

- **Limited liability** and **perpetual succession**; ability to **list**, issue **shares**, and follow **FDI** routes where the sector table allows a **company**.
- **ESOPs** and a governance stack that many **institutional** **lenders** and **investors** expect at scale.
- **Value** is held in **shares** for **succession** and **M&A** planning.

> [!NOTE] **Limitations**  
> The path needs **unanimity**, **NOCs**, and time. Ongoing **compliance** is **higher** than for a **firm**. You will **re-align** **PAN**, **GST**, **MSME**, **FSSAI**, and other **IDs**; the new company **often** has a **new** **PAN** (the firm’s **old** one does **not** **carry** **as**-**is**).

## Process

1. **Partners' resolution** agreeing to convert the firm into a **company**.
2. **NOC** from all **secured** **creditors**.
3. **Advertisements** in **two** **newspapers** (English and **regional** as **rules** require).
4. **Statement of assets and liabilities** certified by a **CA**; **audited** **accounts** (e.g. last **3** years) as **filing** **asks**.
5. **File** **URC-1** with the **RoC** with **MOA**, **AOA**, **ads**, and **affidavits** / **declarations** as **prescribed**.
6. The **RoC** issues a **Certificate of Registration** of the company; the **partnership** is **dissolved** by **force** of **law** on the **stated** **date**.
7. Open **new PAN** for the company; register or move **GST** (including **ITC-02** on a qualifying going-concern transfer), **bank** accounts, and all **licences**; close the firm’s old IDs where the law requires.

## Documents required

| Document | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|

| Consent of all partners | Written and signed |

## Tax impact (summary)

**Section 47(xiii)**: **tax-neutral** if **all** **assets/liabilities** of the **firm** become the **company’s**, **all** **partners** take **shares in proportion to capital**, they hold at least **50%** **voting** for **5** **years**, and **no** **partner** gets **any** **consideration** other than **shares**. A **break** in those **rules** in the **5-year** **window** can **tax** the **gains** in the **breach** **year**. **Goodwill** and **revaluation** can **attract** **CGT** if **dealt** in **differently**. **GST** on **going**-**concern** **moves** is often **exempt** under **12/2017**-style **entries**; run **TDS** and **cross**-**entity** **transactions** with your **CA** from **day** **one** after **birth**.

## Applicability

**Partnership** **firms** under **Section 366** in **all** **states** and **UTs**; **filing** with the **correct** **RoC** for **the** **proposed** **registered** **office** **jurisdiction**.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can an unregistered firm convert?** **Yes**, the **URC-1** **path** is **framed** to **include** **unregistered** **firms** in **provisions**; **ads** and **NOCs** still **apply** in **full**.

**Are two newspaper ads mandatory?** **Yes** for the **standard** **Section** **366** **process** as **laid** **down** (one **English** and one **regional** **in** the **state** **language** **where** **required**).

**What about the firm’s PAN?** The new company has its **own PAN**. The old firm’s PAN is **surrendered** or **inactivated** as NSDL/department rules and your CA advise.

**What about staff and PF/ESI?** A **BTA**-backed handover aims for **continuity of employment**; re-map **EPFO/ESI** and obtain fresh **employer** identifiers for the company where the authorities require that.

**Must partners put new cash?** Not necessarily. Book **capital** can be converted into **share capital** in the **agreed ratio** through the **MOA** scheme and **allotment** **resolutions** your **company secretary** implements.
