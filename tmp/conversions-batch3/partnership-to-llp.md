# Partnership to LLP conversion

## Overview

A **registered** partnership can convert to a **Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)** under **Section 55 of the LLP Act, 2008** and the **Second Schedule** of the Act. This route is common for professional and consulting firms that want **limited liability** and a **separate legal identity** without the full compliance load of a private limited company.

> [!NOTE] Unregistered partnership firms **cannot** use this statutory path. The firm must first be **registered** with the state **Registrar of Firms** before conversion.

## Key features

- **Form 17** with **Form FiLLiP**; **Form 3** for the **LLP agreement** (typically within 30 days of incorporation).
- **Every** partner of the existing firm must become a partner in the **LLP** at conversion. New partners can be added **after** through the **LLP agreement**.
- The LLP is a **new** entity: **new LLPIN**, **PAN**, and **GSTIN** (not a carry-forward of the firm’s CIN, because the firm is not a company).

| Comparison | LLP | Private limited (short) |
|------------|-----|-------------------------|
| Equity | **No** share capital; contributions only | **Yes**, shares and ESOPs |
| Compliance | Lighter than a company in most cases | Board, AGM, heavier MCA set |
| FDI / investors | **Sectoral** and rule-bound for LLPs | Wider for companies in many cases |

> [!TIP] **Audit in LLP**  
> Many LLPs are audit-exempt if turnover and contribution stay below prescribed limits (e.g. historical **Rs. 40 lakh**–type thresholds). **Confirm** the **current** limits and amendments with your **CA** before you rely on them for planning.

## When this conversion helps

- You want to **ring-fence** partner liability but avoid **full** company **compliance**.
- You need a **credible** legal wrapper for **banks**, **RFPs**, and **B2B** contracts.
- You may add **NRI** partners in **permitted** structures, subject to FEMA and FDI rules for LLPs.

> [!WARNING] **Limitations**  
> LLPs **cannot** issue shares or raise **equity** like a company. FDI in LLPs is **restricted** to certain sectors. Conversion needs **NOCs**, **Form 3** on time, and full attention to **fresh** tax and **GST** **positions**.

## Process

1. **Partners' resolution** approving conversion to LLP.
2. **NOC** from **secured** creditors, where required.
3. **Statement of assets and liabilities** certified by a **CA**.
4. File **Form 17** with **Form FiLLiP** on the **MCA** portal.
5. File the **LLP agreement** on **Form 3** within the **30-day** window after incorporation, as applicable.
6. On approval, the **Certificate of Incorporation** of the **LLP** is issued. The **partnership** is **deemed dissolved** from the **effective** date in the order.
7. Take **new PAN**, **GST** registration, **bank**, **MSME**, and other records in the **LLP** name. Surrender the firm’s old identities where the law requires; use **Form GST ITC-02** for **ITC** transfer when eligible.

## Tax impact (summary)

**Section 47(xii)**: the conversion is **tax-neutral** when all **assets and liabilities** of the firm pass to the **LLP**, all **partners** become **LLP** partners in the **same** proportion, and **no** partner receives **consideration** other than **partnership rights** in the **new** entity, subject to the **full** conditions in the **Act** and **rules**. **Section 72A**-style **loss** **carry** may apply only when **eligibility** is met. A **breach** within the **5-year** window can **reopen** **tax** on **gains** in the **breach** year. **GST** on transfer as a **going concern** is often **exempt** (Notification 12/2017 style entries); confirm **with** your **advisor** and **surrender** or **re-register** **GST** **correctly**.

## Applicability

All **states** and **UTs** where the partnership is **duly** **registered** with the **ROF**; the **LLP** is **registered** on the **MCA** system.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can an unregistered firm convert?** **No** on the **Section 55** path. **Register** the **firm** first, or use another **restructuring** **route** with your **advisor**.

**Must every partner join the LLP?** **Yes** at **conversion**; you can add partners **after** by **amending** the **LLP** **agreement**.

**Can the LLP keep the same name?** **Often** **yes** with the **"LLP"** **suffix**, subject to **MCA** **name** **availability** and **guidelines**.

**How long does it take?** Typically **15 to 25** **working** **days** after documents are **ready**; **NOCs** and **Form** **3** **timeline** are the usual **bottlenecks**.

**What happens to the firm’s PAN and GST?** The LLP gets **new** **PAN** and **GSTIN**. **ITC** may move via **ITC-02** when the **transaction** and **entity** **chain** **qualify** under **law**.
