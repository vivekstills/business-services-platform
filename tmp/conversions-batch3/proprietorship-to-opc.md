# Proprietorship to OPC transition

## Overview

Moving from a **sole proprietorship** to a **One Person Company (OPC)** is not a statutory “conversion” under the Companies Act. You **incorporate a new OPC** and **transfer** the business, assets, and liabilities through a **Business Transfer Agreement (BTA)**. This is a common path for solo founders who want **limited liability** and a **separate legal person** without a full re-brand of the business story.

> [!TIP] **When it fits**  
> You need a corporate profile for **banks**, **tenders**, or **B2B** papers; you want to separate personal and business risk; or you may later **convert the OPC to a private limited** if investors need shares.

## Key features

- **SPICe+** (or the current MCA e-incorporation route) to obtain **Certificate of Incorporation** and a **CIN** for a **new** OPC.
- **BTA** to move a **going concern** (assets, contracts, stock, agreed liabilities) from the proprietor to the company.
- **New PAN, GSTIN, and bank** in the company name; cancel or wind down the **proprietor’s** old registrations when rules require.

| Item | Note |
|------|------|
| Continuity | Not automatic like URC-1: licences and KYC are taken again in the new name |
| Nominee | **Form INC-3** nominee consent is **mandatory** for an OPC |
| FDI | **Not** in an OPC; if you need equity, plan **OPC to pvt ltd** later |

## Benefits

- **Limited liability** and a **separate** legal person; **perpetual succession** in company form.
- A **corporate** bank, clearer books, and a professional face for **MSME**, trade, and B2B KYC.
- **Corporate** tax **rates** (e.g. **25%** or **22%** under the sections you may elect) can beat top slab rates for high proprietorship income, subject to your CA’s run.

> [!NOTE] **Limitations**  
> A **BTA** can trigger **capital gains** and **business income** in the **proprietor’s** hands. You must re-licence **GST, trade, FSSAI, MSME**, and re-paper or novate key **contracts**.

## Process

1. **Name** and **file OPC** incorporation (SPICe+ or the form set in force on the filing date).
2. On **COI**, execute a **BTA** with a clear **asset/liability** list; open the **company bank**; pay **consideration** or **allot** shares as the **BTA** states.
3. **Apply** for **PAN, TAN, GST, MSME, IEC** (if needed) in the **OPC** name.
4. **Surrender** the proprietorship’s **GSTIN**; use **ITC-02** when a going-concern transfer and rules allow.
5. **Notify** **customers, vendors, and** **authorities**; novate or re-sign **contracts** that name the proprietor as an **individual** only.

## Tax impact (summary)

The **BTA** is a **taxable** event in the default case: **capital gains** on **capital** **assets**; business **income** on **stock**-in-trade; and **goodwill** can be **taxed** based on the **BTA** wording. A **reorg**-style **exemption** (e.g. **Section 47(xiv)**) may work if the proprietor **takes only shares** in the new company, holds at least **50%** **voting** for **5** **years**, and all **IT**-**Act** **conditions** are met. **GST** on a **going** **concern** handover is often **exempt** (Notification 12/2017) if conditions are met; **cancel** the old **GSTIN** and **obtain** a new one; use **ITC-02** for **ITC** where **permitted**.

## Applicability

Sole proprietors in **all** states and UTs. Re-read **FEMA, NRI**, and **OPC** **naming** **rules** in the **GSRs** in force on your **filing** **date** (with your **CS** and **CA**).

## Frequently asked questions

**Is this a URC-1 style conversion?** **No**. You form a **new** **OPC** and **BTA** the business in; it is not a CIN-on-CIN re-style of an existing company.

**Can the OPC use the same trade name?** **Yes** if the name is available under MCA and the legal name ends with **"OPC Private Limited"** as the rules require.

**What about the old GST?** **Cancel** the **proprietorship**’s **GSTIN** when the handover is complete. **Obtain** a **new** **GSTIN** for the **OPC**. **ITC** may move on **Form ITC-02** when a qualifying going-concern transfer and the current rules allow.

**Does the BTA attract stamp duty?** **Often** **yes** on the agreement value. Immovable property in the BTA can attract **state** stamp. Confirm with your **advocate** and **chartered accountant** for the state and assets.

**Is corporate tax always lower?** **Not** always. Compare slab rates, surcharge, and credits with a **model** your **CA** runs for the year in question.

**Can OPC take FDI?** **No** in the normal OPC rule set. You would typically **convert to private** **limited** when you need **equity** from foreign investors, subject to **FEMA** and **sector** rules.
