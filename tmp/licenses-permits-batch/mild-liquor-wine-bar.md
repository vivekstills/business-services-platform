# Mild Liquor and Wine Bar License

## Short overview

A Mild Liquor and Wine Bar License is a state-issued license authorising restaurants, hotels, and bars to serve mild liquor (beer with alcohol content typically below 8.75% ABV) and wine for on-premises consumption with food. This is a restricted form of liquor license, distinct from a full bar license that permits all categories of IMFL (Indian Made Foreign Liquor including whisky, rum, vodka, gin).

The license is popular among casual dining restaurants, cafes, microbreweries, and lounge bars that wish to serve beer and wine alongside food but don't want the operational complexity and higher fees of a full IMFL bar license. In states like Maharashtra and Karnataka, this license category has gained significant traction due to the growing pub and microbrewery culture.

## Key features

- On-premises service only with food
- Restricted alcoholic beverage range
- Annual license validity
- Premises must include kitchen and dining facility
- FSSAI License mandatory

## Benefits

- Lower license fees compared to full IMFL bar license
- Suitable for casual dining and pub-style establishments
- Wider customer appeal, beer and wine attract diverse demographics
- Lower compliance burden compared to spirits
- Attractive for women patrons and corporate diners
- Aligns with food-pairing concepts (wine with cuisine)

## Limitations

- Cannot serve spirits (whisky, rum, vodka, gin, brandy)
- Limited to mild liquor and wine only
- Same operational restrictions as full bar (timing, dry days, age verification)
- Customers seeking spirits will go elsewhere, competitive disadvantage in some markets
- License upgrade to full bar requires fresh application and higher fees

## Process

Similar to Beer Shoppee License process, with additional verification of restaurant infrastructure, kitchen facility, and dining area capacity. Typical timeline: 60 to 120 days.

1. Prepare documents and application as per state excise rules.
2. Submit to excise with restaurant and FSSAI proof.
3. Undergo site inspection of dining, kitchen, and bar areas.
4. Clear police and local body requirements where applicable.
5. Pay fees and receive license.

## Validity and renewal

Annual validity (financial year basis). Renewal 30 to 60 days before expiry. Compliance with operating conditions verified at renewal.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can I serve cocktails using beer or wine as base under this license?** Yes, cocktails using only beer or wine (without spirits) are permitted. Sangria, beer cocktails, wine spritzers are allowed.

**2. Can I upgrade from Mild Liquor and Wine Bar to Full Bar License later?** Yes. Upgrade requires fresh application, additional license fee, and possibly additional premises modifications. The upgrade is not automatic.

**3. Are imported beers and wines allowed under this license?** Yes, provided they are procured from authorised state wholesalers or importers and have valid customs clearance and label approval.

**4. What are typical operating hours allowed?** Most states permit 11 AM to 11 PM (some allow till midnight or 1 AM with extension fee). Dry days apply.

**5. Can I have a dance floor or DJ in my Mild Liquor and Wine Bar?** This requires additional permissions, dance floor license and Public Performance License. The liquor license alone doesn't cover entertainment.
