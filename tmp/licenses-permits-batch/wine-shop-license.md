# Wine Shoppee License

## Short overview

A Wine Shoppee License is a state-issued retail license authorising the sale of wine in sealed bottles for off-premises consumption. Similar to Beer Shoppee but exclusive to wine, this license caters to wine retail outlets, vineyard cellar door sales, wine boutique stores, and gourmet wine retailers.

## Key features

- Off-premises wine retail
- Sealed bottle sales only
- Annual validity
- Source restricted to authorised wholesalers or wineries

## Benefits

- Premium product positioning
- Lower license fees than IMFL retail in some states
- Educated, repeat customer base
- Supports vineyard direct-to-consumer sales
- Aligns with wine tourism

## Limitations

- Limited to wine only (narrower than IMFL or beer)
- Smaller customer base than mass-market liquor
- Requires wine knowledge for staff
- Higher per-unit inventory cost

## Applicability

Most active in Maharashtra (especially Nashik, Pune wine country), Karnataka (Bangalore), Goa, Delhi NCR.

## Documents required

Standard retail license documents: premises proof, NOCs, financial documents, applicant verification.

## Process

Standard retail liquor license process. Timeline: 60 to 120 days.

## Validity and renewal

Annual renewable.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can vineyards directly sell wine from their estate?** Yes, vineyards can have a Wine Shoppee License at their estate (cellar door sales). Often called "Estate Sales License."

**2. Can imported wines be sold under this license?** Yes, provided procured from authorised importers and proper customs clearance.

**3. Is online wine sale permitted?** Online wine sale rules vary by state, some allow with delivery licenses (Maharashtra, Goa), others restrict.

**4. What about wine accessories and books?** Selling wine accessories, books, and related merchandise alongside wine is typically permitted and doesn't require separate licenses.

**5. Can wine tasting be conducted at a wine shop?** This depends on state rules, some allow limited in-shop tasting, others require separate tasting permissions.
