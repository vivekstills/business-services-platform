# Contractual Labour License Renewal

## Short overview

CLRA License Renewal is the annual process of renewing the Contract Labour License before its expiry to maintain authorisation for deploying 50+ contract workers. Renewal involves verification of ongoing compliance with welfare facility provisions, worker registration accuracy, wage payment compliance, and continued engagement with Principal Employer.

## Key features

- Annual renewal cycle
- Compliance audit during renewal
- Updated worker register submission
- Continued PE engagement verification

## Benefits

- Continuity of contract labour deployment authorisation
- Demonstrated compliance track record
- Avoidance of cessation of operations
- Credibility with Principal Employers

## Limitations

- Annual renewal compliance effort
- Inspection-based renewal
- Late renewal penalties
- Documentation update requirements

## Applicability

All CLRA License holders must renew before expiry across all states.

## Process

1. Apply for renewal 30 days before expiry.
2. Submit updated documents.
3. Compliance verification by Labour Inspector.
4. Address any deficiencies.
5. Pay renewal fee.
6. Renewed License issuance.

Timeline: 30 to 60 days.

## Government fees

Same as initial license fees with possible late fees if delayed.

## Validity and renewal

Annual cycle.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. What if I miss the renewal deadline?** Late renewal possible with penalty (typically 25 to 50% additional). Beyond grace period, fresh license required.

**2. Can I add more workers at renewal?** Yes, increase in workforce can be reported and license amended at renewal. Higher fees may apply.

**3. Is fresh inspection required at renewal?** Yes, in most cases. Compliance with welfare facilities is verified.

**4. Can I change the principal employer at renewal?** Yes, new Form VI from new PE can be submitted at renewal.

**5. Are non-compliance penalties cleared at renewal?** Pending non-compliance must be addressed before renewal. Penalties assessed must be paid.
