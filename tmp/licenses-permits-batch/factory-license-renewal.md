# Factory License Renewal

## Short overview

Factory License Renewal is the periodic process of renewing the License under the Factories Act, 1948 to maintain legal authorisation for manufacturing operations. Renewal ensures continued compliance with safety, welfare, working conditions, and regulatory provisions.

## Key features

- 1 to 10 year renewal cycle (state-specific)
- Comprehensive compliance audit
- Worker welfare verification
- Safety equipment review

## Benefits

- Continuity of manufacturing operations
- Compliance demonstration
- Workforce safety assurance
- Avoidance of operational shutdowns

## Limitations

- Periodic compliance verification
- Inspection-based renewal
- Documentation effort
- Late renewal penalties

## Applicability

All Factory License holders must renew before expiry across all states.

## Documents required

| Document | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Renewal application | Form 2A or state equivalent |
| Existing Factory License | |

| Updated worker statistics | Number, categories |
| Safety committee minutes | Last 12 months |
| Accident register | If any incidents |
| Compliance certificates | Fire, PCB, electrical |
| Factory Manager's appointment | If changed |
| Welfare facility status | Updated |

## Process

Apply 30 to 60 days before expiry. DISH or Factory Inspector reviews documents, conducts inspection, addresses deficiencies, processes renewal upon fee payment.

Timeline: 60 to 120 days.

## Government fees

Same as initial license; late renewal attracts penalty up to 25%.

## Validity and renewal

State-specific (1, 5, or 10 year cycles).

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can I continue operations during renewal processing?** If renewal application is filed before expiry, operations can typically continue pending renewal decision.

**2. What happens if license expires before renewal?** Operations must cease until renewal granted. Operating with expired license attracts heavy penalties and prosecution.

**3. Can I request multi-year renewal?** Some states offer multi-year licenses (5 to 10 years) with proportionately higher fees. Check state Factory Rules.

**4. Are renewals more strictly inspected than initial grants?** Renewals focus on compliance with conditions imposed. Initial grants focus on infrastructure suitability. Both involve thorough inspection.

**5. Can renewal be denied for past violations?** Yes. Persistent non-compliance with safety provisions can result in renewal rejection.
