# Contractual Labour License Registration

## Short overview

Contractual Labour License (often called CLRA License) is mandatory under the Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 (CLRA Act) for contractors employing 50 or more contract workers in a workplace. The license ensures compliance with worker welfare provisions, prevents exploitation, and regulates the contractor-employer-worker relationship.

The license is granted by the Labour Commissioner or Licensing Officer of the respective state. The CLRA Act applies to both private and government establishments, and the contractor and the principal employer have respective compliance obligations.

## Key features

- Required for contractors with 50+ contract workers
- Workplace-specific or contractor-level license
- Annual validity
- Worker registration mandatory

## Benefits

- Legal authorisation to deploy contract labour
- Eligibility for tendering and contracts requiring CLRA compliance
- Worker welfare framework compliance
- Regulatory credibility
- Avoids penalties for unauthorised contract labour deployment

## Limitations

- Significant compliance burden
- Worker welfare facility requirements
- Periodic renewals and inspections
- Heavy penalties for violations
- Documentation-intensive

## Applicability

Mandatory under Central CLRA Act applicable nationally, with state Rules in each state. State-specific Labour Departments handle licensing.

Common applicants: manpower supply agencies, security agencies, housekeeping or facility management companies, construction contractors, industrial contract workforce providers.

## Documents required

| Document | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Application Form V | CLRA prescribed form |
| Form VI from Principal Employer | Confirming contractor engagement |
| List of contract workers | Details, identification |
| Worker welfare facility plan | Restrooms, drinking water, canteen |
| Wages structure | Compliance with minimum wages |
| Work order copy | From principal employer |
| Identity of contractor | PAN, registration |
| Establishment registration | If contractor is an entity |
| Bank guarantee | Some states |

## Process

1. Principal Employer obtains registration (separate).
2. Contractor applies for License with Form V and PE's Form VI.
3. Documentation review.
4. Inspection of welfare facilities.
5. Fee payment.
6. License issuance.

Timeline: 30 to 90 days.

## Government fees

| Worker strength | Approximate annual fee |
| --- | --- |
| 50 to 100 workers | ₹5,000 to ₹15,000 |
| 100 to 500 workers | ₹15,000 to ₹50,000 |
| 500+ workers | ₹50,000 to ₹2,00,000 |

Plus security deposits in some states.

## Validity and renewal

Annual validity. Renewal 30 days before expiry.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Is CLRA License required if I have less than 50 contract workers?** Below 50, license is not required, but Principal Employer's registration may still apply. State-specific lower thresholds may exist.

**2. Can I deploy contract workers in different establishments under one license?** Generally no. Each establishment requires separate compliance and CLRA registration of Principal Employer.

**3. Who pays workers, contractor or principal employer?** Contractor pays workers. Principal Employer is responsible for ensuring contractor pays minimum wages and provides welfare facilities.

**4. What welfare facilities are mandatory?** Restrooms, drinking water, canteen (for 100+ workers), creche (for 30+ women workers), washing facilities, first aid.

**5. What's the penalty for unlicensed contract labour deployment?** Imprisonment up to 3 months and/or fine up to ₹1,000, plus daily fines for continued violations.
