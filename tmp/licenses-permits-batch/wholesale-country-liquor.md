# Wholesale License for Country Liquor

## Short overview

A Wholesale License for Country Liquor authorises an entity to procure country liquor from licensed manufacturers or distilleries and supply it to retail country liquor shops, ON licensees, and authorised dealers within a specified geographic area. This is a B2B license, the wholesaler does not sell directly to consumers.

Country liquor wholesale is typically managed by state government corporations (KSBCL in Karnataka, MSBCL in Maharashtra for IMFL, BSBCL in Bihar prohibition state) or by private wholesale licensees in some states. The wholesale tier is critical to the country liquor distribution system.

## Key features

- B2B-only license
- Geographic territory specified
- Source-restricted procurement
- Daily reporting obligations

## Benefits

- Stable B2B business with regular institutional clients
- Higher transaction volumes than retail
- Lower per-unit margins but higher absolute returns
- Long-term contracts with retailers
- Government-supported pricing structure

## Limitations

- Very high license fees and security deposits
- Heavy compliance, daily stock movement reporting
- State government often has monopoly or near-monopoly
- Limited number of licenses per region
- Strict source restrictions (only from licensed distilleries)

## Applicability

Country liquor wholesale is more relevant in: Maharashtra (MSWC handles much of it), Karnataka (KSBCL monopoly), Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, Bihar (prohibition state, limited).

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can a private entity hold a country liquor wholesale license in all states?** No. Many states (Karnataka, Tamil Nadu) have state corporation monopolies. Private wholesale licenses are limited to specific states with private wholesale frameworks.

**2. What is the difference between IMFL wholesale and country liquor wholesale?** Country liquor wholesale handles locally produced traditional liquors at lower price points; IMFL wholesale handles "foreign" branded liquors at higher price points. Different license categories with different fee structures.

**3. Can a wholesaler operate in multiple districts under one license?** Generally one wholesale license covers one designated territory (district or zone). Multi-territory operations require multiple licenses.

**4. What is the minimum storage capacity required?** Typically 5,000 to 25,000 cases minimum, varying by state. Premises must have proper security, ventilation, and fire safety.

**5. Can the wholesaler sell directly to consumers?** No. The license restricts sales to licensed retailers only. Direct consumer sales are prohibited and attract heavy penalties.
