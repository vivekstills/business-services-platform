# Beer Shoppee License

## Short overview

A Beer Shoppee License is a state-issued retail liquor license that authorises the licensee to sell beer (and in some states, mild liquor with low alcohol content) in sealed bottles or cans for off-premises consumption. The license is granted under the respective state Excise Act and Excise Rules, liquor being a State Subject under Entry 8 of the State List in the Constitution of India, making this a state-specific license with significantly different procedures, fees, and conditions across states.

A Beer Shoppee (often referred to as a Beer Shop, Beer Outlet, or Mild Liquor Shop in different states) is distinct from a full-fledged liquor shop (which sells the entire range of IMFL, Indian Made Foreign Liquor, including spirits like whisky, rum, vodka, gin), it is restricted to beer and sometimes wine and mild alcoholic beverages with low alcohol content (typically below 8.75% alcohol by volume).

The license is highly regulated, limited number of licenses are issued in each district (often through auction or lottery), the location must comply with distance restrictions from schools, religious places, and hospitals, and the licensee must comply with operating hours, age verification, and quota purchase requirements from authorised state-licensed wholesalers or government corporations.

## Key features

- Premises-specific license, tied to a specific location
- Annual or multi-year validity (typically 1 year)
- Renewable subject to compliance and timely fee payment
- Subject to quota purchase from state-authorised sources
- Mandatory display of license certificate at premises
- Age verification mandatory (no sale to persons below legal drinking age, varies by state from 21 to 25 years)

## Benefits

- Lower investment compared to full IMFL retail license, lower license fee, smaller minimum stock requirement
- Wider customer base, beer is the most consumed alcoholic beverage in India, with growing demand from young consumers
- Stable monthly revenue once established, repeat customers and consistent demand patterns
- Beer is less price-sensitive than spirits, margins are more predictable
- Bulk procurement discounts from authorised wholesalers
- Eligibility for promotional support from beer brand owners (point-of-sale displays, refrigeration units, signage)

## Limitations

- Limited product range, restricted to beer (and sometimes mild liquor and wine) only, cannot sell spirits
- High regulatory scrutiny, frequent excise inspector visits, compliance audits, and stock verification
- Operating hour restrictions, typically 11 AM to 11 PM, with state-specific dry days (national holidays, election days, religious festivals)
- Quota-based purchases, must purchase from designated wholesalers or state corporations at fixed rates
- High annual license fees in metros and Tier 1 cities
- License cannot be transferred without state government approval
- Heavy penalties for selling to minors, selling above MRP, or stocking unauthorised brands

## Process

1. **Application submission:** Submit application in prescribed form along with documents and application fee to the state excise department.
2. **Site inspection:** Excise inspector visits the proposed premises to verify compliance with distance norms, premises specifications, and zonal restrictions.
3. **Police verification:** Police verification of applicant's character, antecedents, and any prior involvement in offences.
4. **Public notice / objections:** In some states, a public notice is published inviting objections to the proposed license.
5. **License fee payment:** Upon provisional approval, pay the prescribed annual license fee.
6. **License issuance:** Excise Commissioner or Collector issues the Beer Shoppee License.
7. **Premises setup and stock procurement:** Set up the premises as per specifications and procure initial beer stock from authorised wholesalers.
8. **Commencement of operations:** Begin retail beer sales after displaying the license at premises.

## Validity and renewal

**Validity:** Typically 1 year (April 1 to March 31 in most states)

**Renewal:** Apply 30 to 60 days before expiry with renewal fee, compliance affidavit, and fresh inspection (in some states). Late renewal attracts heavy penalty. License lapse means cessation of business; reapplication required if lapse exceeds prescribed period.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Can I sell wine and other low-alcohol beverages along with beer under a Beer Shoppee License?** This depends on the state. In Maharashtra, the FL/BR-II license covers beer and wine. In Karnataka, the CL-7 license specifically covers beer and wine. In some states, separate wine shop licenses are required. Always check the specific scope of license under your state's excise rules.

**2. What is the minimum age requirement for a Beer Shoppee License applicant?** The applicant must typically be at least 21 years old, of Indian nationality, and a resident of the state where the license is sought. Some states require minimum educational qualifications.

**3. Can I operate the Beer Shoppee from a residential building?** Generally no. Most state excise rules require the premises to be in commercial zones with proper municipal approval. Operating from residential premises violates zoning laws and the license terms.

**4. What is the distance restriction from schools and religious places?** Most states require minimum 100 meters distance from schools, religious places, and hospitals. Some states (like Karnataka) require 100 meters in urban and 200 meters in rural areas. State highway proximity also has restrictions following Supreme Court rulings.

**5. Can a single applicant hold multiple Beer Shoppee Licenses?** Most states restrict one applicant (or one immediate family) to one or two licenses to prevent monopolisation. Specific limits vary by state and are typically enforced strictly.
