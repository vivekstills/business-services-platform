# Trade License

## Short overview

A Trade License is a municipal authorisation issued by the local municipal corporation or municipality permitting a person or entity to carry out a specified business or trade at a specified location. It is governed by state Municipal Corporation Acts and is one of the foundational compliance requirements for any business operating from a fixed premises within municipal limits.

The license ensures that businesses operate in zones permitted under municipal planning, comply with local trade norms, and contribute to municipal revenue. Different categories of trades have different license requirements, hazardous, non-hazardous, food, manufacturing, services.

## Key features

- Municipal-level license
- Premises-specific
- Annual validity (typically)
- Trade category-specific fees

## Benefits

- Legal authorisation to operate business
- Required for various downstream registrations (GST, FSSAI, etc.)
- Required for bank account opening (current account)
- Tendering eligibility (government or PSU)
- Consumer trust
- Legal protection

## Limitations

- Multiple municipality registrations for multi-location operations
- Annual renewal
- Inspection-based renewal
- State and municipal variations
- Categorisation complexity for diverse businesses

## Applicability

Required pan-India in all municipal areas: BMC Mumbai (Trade License), MCD Delhi (Trade License), BBMP Bangalore (Trade License), Chennai Corporation (Trade License), all other municipalities.

Common applicants: shops, stores, retail outlets, restaurants, cafes, service businesses, manufacturing units, professional offices.

## Process

1. Apply at municipal office (online in most metros).
2. Documentation submission.
3. Inspection (if required).
4. Fee payment.
5. License issuance.

Timeline: 7 to 60 days.

## Validity and renewal

Annual validity (April to March in most municipalities). Renewal 30 days before expiry.

## Frequently asked questions

**1. Is Trade License needed for online businesses with no physical premises?** If you have a registered business address (even a small office), Trade License is typically required. Pure home-based online businesses below thresholds may be exempt in some municipalities.

**2. Is Trade License same as Shops and Establishments License?** Different licenses with overlap. Shops Act covers labour aspects; Trade License covers business permission. Both often required.

**3. Can multiple businesses share one Trade License at same address?** Generally no. Each distinct trade typically requires its own license.

**4. What categories are typically prohibited from Trade Licenses in residential areas?** Hazardous trades, manufacturing units, large retail, and certain service businesses are typically prohibited in residential zones.

**5. What's the renewal grace period after expiry?** Most municipalities allow 30 to 60 days grace with late fee. Beyond this, fresh application may be needed.
