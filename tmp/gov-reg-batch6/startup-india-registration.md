# Startup India registration (DPIIT recognition)

## Overview

Startup India is the government programme led by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT) to grow innovation-led businesses. The phrase “Startup India registration” in everyday use means the same as DPIIT startup recognition: you create a profile and apply for recognition on the Startup India portal (startupindia.gov.in), which is free. A recognised startup receives a certificate with a DPIIT recognition number. That number is the usual gate for Startup India–linked benefits, such as self-certification in listed labour and environment laws, IP fee rebates, the path to IMB and tax items where you qualify, GeM, seed schemes routed through the ecosystem, and visibility on the government hub. The detailed eligibility, documentation, and process mirror our DPIIT Registration service page, because the underlying application is one.

## Key features (programme)

- No government fee; online profile and one recognition application.
- The Startup India hub: information, some schemes, and links to other ministries.
- Seed Fund, SISFS, and other initiatives use DPIIT-recognised startups and incubators, each with their own call and cap.
- “Startup India” brand for events, state partnerships, and international missions.
- Same core eligibility as pure DPIIT: private company, registered partnership, or LLP; within 10 years of incorporation; under rupees one hundred crore in each year; innovation or strong scalable and employment/wealth-creation case; and not a mere split of an old business to game the test.

## Benefits (in addition to generic DPIIT benefits)

- SISFS and related seed and prototyping lines that require recognition and, often, a recognised incubator.
- Greater visibility in Startup India and partner listings for investors, corporates, and government.
- Some state governments mirror central tests with their own subventions; recognition is a common pre-check in those state portals.
- The same labour, environment, IP, procurement, and (where IMB approves) tax stack as the DPIIT page describes.

## Limitations (programme)

- The marketing name can confuse new founders: “registering for Startup India” is not a company incorporation step; you still need a proper legal entity, PAN, and compliance as usual.
- Not every scheme on the hub applies to you; most need extra forms and a fit in sector and stage.
- State startup policies are separate: Maharashtra, Karnataka, and others have their own portals and may need a fresh form even if you are DPIIT-recognised.
- Recognition is not a seal of product–market fit or a promise of investment.

## Process

The steps are the same as DPIIT recognition. In short: register on the portal, build the company profile, write the innovation or scalable business story, upload incorporation proof, self-declare eligibility, and wait for DPIIT. After the certificate, use the number on GeM, tax, IP, and any accelerator or state grant form that lists it. For 80-IAC, open the separate IMB track when you are ready, it is not automatic with recognition.

## Applicability

The same as DPIIT: private limited, LLP, or registered partnership, within 10 years, under the turnover line, and with a defensible innovative or high-scale model. All states. Foreign founders can be on the cap table, subject to the usual FEMA and company law.

## Validity

Same as DPIIT: you remain a “startup” in policy terms until 10 years from incorporation or until any year’s turnover goes above rupees one hundred crore, then benefits tied to the definition stop going forward. No annual renewal of the certificate by itself, but the portal has an update function for real changes to business, people, and funding.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is this different from the DPIIT page?** No for the form and certificate. The Startup India name is the programme brand; DPIIT is the recognising body.

**Partnership not LLP, still ok?** Yes, a registered partnership that meets the rest can be recognised; note LLPs and companies are usually easier for equity and ESOP, which matters for a venture path.

**₹15 crore edtech, year four: ok?** Age and turnover pass if you are under rupees one hundred crore. The file still needs a clear innovation, technology, or real scale story, not a generic content reseller pitch.

**Rejected once: what to do?** Tighten the innovation paragraph with concrete product, user, and tech, add proof points, and re-apply, or form a more suitable entity with advice.

**After recognition, is tax free?** Not automatic. 80-IAC needs a separate IMB process and, when granted, a chosen three-year run of assessment years, subject to law.
