# RCMC registration (import export council)

## Overview

The **RCMC (Registration-cum-Membership Certificate)** is issued by an Export Promotion Council (EPC) or Commodity Board to register the exporter as a member of that body. It is both your industry registration and your proof of membership. For many Foreign Trade Policy benefits (RoDTEP, Advance Authorisation, EPCG, and similar schemes) you need a valid RCMC from the council that covers your product line, and you must link it to your IEC on the DGFT portal. The correct EPC depends on the main HS heading you export, for example APEDA for a large share of food and agri, MPEDA for marine, EEPC for engineering, GJEPC for gems and jewellery, and others. Some firms need more than one RCMC if they export very different product families.

## Key features

- Dual use: registration with the council and membership fees in one certificate in the usual path.
- Must match the product and the council; a wrong council can block a benefit or a customs check in a few lines.
- IEC is the first step; RCMC comes after.
- Five-year style life in many councils, with annual membership **fees** during the period. Some bodies use a slightly different calendar; read the certificate.
- E-RCMC and DGFT linkage: the exporter’s profile on the DGFT site should show the RCMC so that scheme forms pre-fill or validate.

## Benefits

- Claim export benefits that the current FTP and the scheme condition list for an active RCMC.
- For some agri and marine lines, APEDA and MPEDA registration is also a gate to phytosanitary and export certification, not only for benefit.
- Access to buyer-seller meets, MDA, and EPC intelligence and help-desks.
- Certificate of origin and FTA help in some councils.
- Stronger supplier pass in RFPs that want an EPC card.

## Limitations

- Multi-product exporters may need many RCMCs and many annual fees.
- If you miss annual fees, the council can mark you as not in good standing; benefits and some clearances can fail.
- Wrong product-to-council mapping is a common fault; use the HS map and the council’s help desk.
- RCMC is not the same as IEC; both are often required, in that order.

## Process

1. Take an **IEC** from DGFT if you do not already have one.
2. Map your export product to the right EPC or board (HS and the council’s sector list).
3. File the online or offline form of that council with company, IEC, tax, and bank proof.
4. Pay the membership and certificate fee to the council.
5. On approval, receive the RCMC. Note the certificate number and valid years.
6. Log in to the DGFT portal and add the RCMC under RCMC management or the current path so that scheme and benefit tools see it.
7. Pay each year’s membership on time and refresh the RCMC when the five-year block ends if the council needs a new file.

## Applicability

Any Indian firm that exports and needs an EPC or board for FTP benefits, or that must show APEDA or MPEDA as a non-tariff gate for their line, in any state. The council is chosen by product, not by state.

## Validity

Many RCMCs are valid for five years from the start of the April block the council uses, with **annual** membership in between. At the end of the block, file a fresh RCMC if the council does not auto-extend. Always read the note on the PDF you hold.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I export without RCMC?** For many lines, yes, if you do not take FTP benefits and your product is not in a line that needs APEDA or MPEDA for port or health steps. For benefits, you almost always need the right RCMC.

**Garments and engineering: one or two RCMCs?** Two, if you export both; AEPC for apparel and EEPC for engineering, with the same IEC.

**RCMC vs IEC?** IEC is the 10-digit DGFT code for import and export. RCMC is membership in a sector council for that product; it is not a replacement for IEC.

**How to link RCMC to IEC?** On the DGFT portal, after login with the IEC, use the RCMC or similar service to key the council name, number, dates, and HS lines. The portal will try to match to the e-RCMC feed where live.

**Expired two months, can I claim old RoDTEP?** Many scheme engines test RCMC and IEC as of the **export** or **shipment** date in the file. If the RCMC was valid for that day, a late renewal does not by itself void that past ship if the claim window is open. For **future** ships, fix membership first.
