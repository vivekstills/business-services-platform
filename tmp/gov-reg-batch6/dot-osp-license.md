# DOT / OSP license (other service provider)

## Overview

A DOT OSP (other service provider) registration is required for companies that use the public telephone network, ISDN, or similar licensed telecom access to run business services such as BPO, call centres, and many IT/ITES operations with large-scale voice, without themselves holding a full telecom network licence. OSPs use capacity from licensed telecom service providers. The DoT reformed the OSP framework in November 2020 to relax several restrictions, support work-from-home agents with due controls, and support domestic and international OSP use cases. Registration is through the DoT Saral Sanchar portal. In the normal case, the DoT does not charge a licence fee for OSP registration.

## Key features

- Two categories: domestic OSP (serves only Indian customers) and international OSP (serves overseas customers).
- Online application and updates through Saral Sanchar (link from the DoT site).
- No government licence fee for OSP registration in the usual case.
- One OSP registration can list multiple service centres; add a new site on the file before you start OSP work there.
- Work-from-home agents are permitted in the 2020 framework, with expected VPN, access logging, and data security practice.
- EPABX and autodialler arrangements should meet DoT technical expectations.
- Annual self-declaration to DoT; use the current circular for the filing deadline.
- All international call routing must go through licensed gateways; grey route VOIP is out of compliance.

## Benefits

- Clear legal base for a customer-facing BPO, call centre, or scale voice helpdesk.
- TSPs (BSNL, Airtel, Jio, and others) and clients often need OSP evidence before high-volume PSTN, leased, or long-distance capacity.
- International outsourcing clients use DoT OSP in vendor and compliance checklists.
- WFH and hybrid work can be run with a defendable file if the firm keeps logs and security discipline.

## Limitations

- Use only licensed TSP/ISP access; you cannot resell raw telecom as a small operator.
- Penalties, service disconnection, or OSP deregistration are possible for serious non-compliance.
- WFH is not a free pass: firms must be able to show connectivity and data controls if audited.
- Grey route international telephony (illegal bypass of licensed international gateways) is a serious matter under the Telegraph Act and DoT OSP terms.

## Process

1. Work out if you are domestic, international, or both from your end-customer and call path.
2. Register on the Saral Sanchar portal and open the OSP application for your company.
3. Fill in company, centre, network, and TSP connection information and upload the listed documents.
4. Submit the self-declaration on DoT OSP conditions and on not using illegal routing.
5. DoT reviews; many complete files are decided in a 15 to 30 working day style window (timelines are not fixed in law; treat as planning only).
6. Download the OSP registration certificate and share with TSPs and material clients.
7. File the annual self-declaration by the date in the then-current notice (commonly the end of March, confirm each year on the portal).

## Applicability

Call and contact centres, BPO, e-commerce and fintech support floors, and similar firms that use PSTN-class or bulk voice from TSPs for client service. A small in-house helpdesk that only uses normal office phone lines to support internal staff is a different use case. If the firm acts as a commercial OSP, register. Pan-India: one OSP with all sites disclosed.

## Validity

Under the 2020-style framework, OSP **registration** is not usually issued for a one-year only window; it stays on file while you file annual self-declaration and do not breach OSP terms. Deregistration or suspension can follow material breach or non-filing. Confirm wording on the certificate you download.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does a small in-house help desk need OSP?** If the desk only uses standard corporate phone lines to support your own employees, that is not the same as a commercial OSP. If you run a BPO, contract customer voice, or similar for external clients, OSP is in scope. Check your fact pattern and current DoT guidance.

**Is work-from-home allowed?** Yes, after the 2020 liberalisation, with VPN and reasonable compliance around logging and data security, not with uncontrolled consumer VOIP in place of licenced TSP access.

**What is a grey route?** A grey route is unlawful routing of international calls outside licensed international gateways, often to avoid rightful charges and oversight. It is illegal and a serious OSP and Telegraph Act problem.

**Five cities: do we need five OSPs?** No. One OSP for the company, with each centre and its address and design listed. Update the OSP when you add a centre.

**Our client says “OSP licence” but the portal says “registration.”** The certificate from successful OSP **registration** is what clients and TSPs treat as the OSP proof. The name “licence” is colloquial.
