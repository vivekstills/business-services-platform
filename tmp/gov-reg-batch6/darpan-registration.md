## DARPAN registration

### Overview

DARPAN (Database And Registration of Voluntary And Non-Profit Associations) is an online portal developed and maintained by NITI Aayog, Government of India, to create a verified and searchable national database of NGOs, voluntary organisations, and civil society organisations operating in India. The portal provides a Unique ID to registered organisations, which is increasingly becoming a prerequisite for NGOs and non-profit entities to access central government grants, foreign contributions, and government-funded welfare schemes.

The DARPAN portal, accessible at [ngodarpan.gov.in](https://ngodarpan.gov.in), serves as a central transparency and accountability mechanism. It enables the government to track the reach, activities, and funding of NGOs across India, and enables genuine civil society organisations to demonstrate their credibility, registration status, and work to government departments, foreign donors, and the general public.

DARPAN registration (obtaining the Unique ID on the NGO DARPAN portal) is essential for any NGO or voluntary organisation that seeks central government grants, files for FCRA (Foreign Contribution Regulation Act) registration or prior permission, applies for funds from the CSR corpus of companies, or seeks accreditation with international funding agencies operating in India.

### Key features

- **Unique DARPAN ID:** A unique identification number assigned to each registered organisation, serving as the NGO’s national identity.
- **Searchable public profile:** Registered NGOs get a public-facing profile on the NGO DARPAN portal showing registration details, key personnel, projects undertaken, and geographical reach.
- **Central government grant prerequisite:** Most central government ministries and departments require the DARPAN ID before processing any grant application.
- **FCRA prerequisite:** The Ministry of Home Affairs requires DARPAN ID for FCRA registration and prior permission applications.
- **CSR fund access:** Companies disbursing CSR funds are encouraged to channel them through DARPAN-registered NGOs for transparency.
- **Project tracking:** NGOs can update and track their projects and beneficiary data on the portal, improving accountability.
- **Multi-organisation type coverage:** Covers Societies, Trusts, Section 8 companies, and other voluntary non-profit entities.
- **Free registration:** Registration on DARPAN is free of cost.

## Benefits

- **Government grant eligibility:** DARPAN ID is a mandatory prerequisite for receiving grants from central government ministries, departments, and autonomous bodies under the General Financial Rules (GFR).
- **FCRA compliance:** Without DARPAN ID, an NGO cannot apply for FCRA registration (for foreign contributions) or FCRA prior permission, blocking access to international funding.
- **CSR fund access:** Corporate CSR teams prefer DARPAN-registered NGOs for fund disbursement as it provides a basic layer of verification and government recognition.
- **Credibility and transparency:** A DARPAN profile with updated project and beneficiary data signals operational credibility to donors, corporate partners, and government bodies.
- **National visibility:** DARPAN’s searchable database makes genuine NGOs discoverable by government agencies, CSR teams, and international organisations looking for implementation partners.
- **Reduced duplication:** DARPAN helps reduce shell NGOs and fraudulent organisations from accessing government resources by requiring verified documentation.
- **Digital identity:** The DARPAN ID serves as a digital identity for the NGO in government systems, simplifying application processes across multiple schemes.

## Limitations and caveats

- **Not recognition of quality:** DARPAN ID is a database entry, not a quality certification or government recognition of the NGO’s work, it only confirms existence and basic details.
- **Mandatory but not sufficient:** DARPAN registration alone does not make an NGO eligible for government grants; each ministry has additional eligibility and empanelment criteria.
- **Ongoing updates:** NGOs must keep their DARPAN profile updated (key personnel, annual returns, project updates); outdated profiles may affect grant applications.
- **Not a substitute for FCRA:** DARPAN is required before applying for FCRA, but FCRA registration is a separate and more rigorous process.
- **Central vs state focus:** DARPAN is primarily relevant for central government schemes; state grant applications may use separate state-level NGO portals and requirements.
- **No automatic grant:** A DARPAN ID does not guarantee any specific grant; it is the first step in a multi-stage grant process.

## Applicability

DARPAN registration is relevant for registered Societies, Charitable Trusts, Section 8 companies, and other voluntary non-profit organisations legally registered in India. It is **mandatory** in practice for entities that:

- apply for central government grants under any ministry or department;
- apply for FCRA registration or FCRA prior permission; or
- seek empanelment with government bodies as implementing agencies.

DARPAN is a **central** portal, applicable to NGOs across all states and UTs (Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, West Bengal, and others).

## Process

1. **Visit the DARPAN portal:** Go to [ngodarpan.gov.in](https://ngodarpan.gov.in). Use **Sign Up** to register a new account for the organisation.
2. **Organisation registration:** Enter the organisation’s basic details: name, type (Society, Trust, or Section 8), registration number, state, and PAN.
3. **Document upload:** Upload scanned copies of the registration certificate, PAN, governing body member details, and other required documents.
4. **Profile completion:** Complete the full DARPAN profile: mission and vision, sectors of work, geographical coverage, past projects, beneficiary data, and bank account details.
5. **Verification:** NITI Aayog reviews the submitted information and documents. The portal may send a verification email or OTP to confirm the registered email.
6. **Unique ID generation:** After successful registration and document verification, the system issues the **Unique DARPAN ID** for the organisation.
7. **Ongoing profile maintenance:** Update the DARPAN profile regularly with new projects, governance changes, and annual report uploads to keep the profile current.

## Validity and profile updates

DARPAN registration does not have a fixed expiry: once registered, the Unique ID is **permanent**. The profile must still be **kept updated**. Government departments may not consider applications from NGOs whose profiles have not been updated in the preceding one to two years. NITI Aayog periodically reviews the database and may flag or deactivate dormant or incomplete profiles.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is DARPAN registration mandatory for all NGOs in India?** DARPAN registration is mandatory only for NGOs seeking central government grants, FCRA registration, or FCRA prior permission. An NGO that operates purely on domestic private donations, membership fees, or state government grants may technically operate without DARPAN. However, DARPAN ID is increasingly expected by CSR donors and institutional funders as a basic credibility signal, so it is strongly advisable for all registered NGOs to obtain it.

**We are a newly registered Trust. Can we apply for DARPAN ID immediately after registration?** Yes. A Trust, Society, or Section 8 company can apply for DARPAN ID once it has the registration certificate. There is no minimum operational history. The “Projects and Beneficiaries” section may have limited data for a new organisation; you can update it as work progresses.

**Does DARPAN registration automatically enrol our NGO for government grants?** No. DARPAN gives the Unique ID that is often a **prerequisite** for grant applications, but it does not **automatically** make the NGO eligible or enrolled in any scheme. Each ministry has its own empanelment process, criteria (years of operation, minimum expenditure, audit, geography), and procedures. DARPAN is step one; empanelment is separate.

**Our NGO’s key functionaries have changed. Do we need to update DARPAN?** Yes, and promptly. The DARPAN profile must show current governing body members, Chairperson, Secretary, and Treasurer. Outdated data can cause mismatches during grant processing, FCRA verification, and CSR disbursement. Log in, update the governing body section (including Aadhaar and contact information). Update the Registrar of Societies, Trust, or MCA in parallel if required by law.

**Can a Section 8 company register on DARPAN? Is the process different from a Trust or Society?** Yes. Section 8 companies can and should register. The process is largely the same: registration on [ngodarpan.gov.in](https://ngodarpan.gov.in) with constitutional documents. For Section 8 companies, the registration certificate and Certificate of Incorporation from the RoC, plus MoA and AoA, are the key proofs. PAN, bank details, and governing body (board) information are required in the same way as for Trusts and Societies.
