# Barcode registration (GS1 India)

## Overview

A barcode is a machine-readable optical label that contains information about the product it is attached to, including the product’s origin, manufacturer, product category, and unique identification number. In India, barcode registration is managed by **GS1 India**, the national chapter of GS1, the global not-for-profit organisation that sets and administers barcode and supply chain standards used in over 150 countries.

GS1 India operates under the aegis of the **Ministry of Commerce and Industry**, Government of India, and the **Quality Council of India (QCI)**. When a manufacturer or brand registers with GS1 India, they receive a globally unique **GS1 Company Prefix**, a numeric code assigned exclusively to that company, from which they generate unique **GTINs (Global Trade Item Numbers)** for each of their product variants. These GTINs form the basis of the barcodes (EAN-13, UPC-A, QR Code, and others) printed on product packaging.

## Key features

- **GS1 Company Prefix:** A globally unique numeric prefix (6–9 digits) assigned exclusively to your company, the foundation of all your product barcodes.
- **GTIN generation:** Generate unique GTINs for each product and variant (size, flavour, colour, pack size).
- **Multiple formats:** EAN-13, EAN-8, UPC-A, ITF-14, GS1-128, QR Code.
- **Global recognition:** GS1 barcodes are recognised in retail and supply chain systems worldwide.
- **Product registry:** Access to the GS1 India Product Registry, a verified product database.
- **License model:** Annual license for the GS1 Company Prefix (subscription, not a one-time purchase).
- **Verification:** Barcode verification service to ensure print quality before retail.

## Benefits

- **Organised retail:** Modern trade often requires valid GS1 barcodes before listing.
- **E-commerce:** Marketplaces typically need GTINs for product listing and catalogue integrity.
- **Export:** International buyers often expect GS1 barcodes.
- **Supply chain:** Inventory, billing, and traceability.
- **Anti-counterfeiting:** Unique GTINs linked to a registry help verification.
- **Brand trust:** Professional labelling and compliance.
- **Regulatory context:** FSSAI, BIS, and some drug requirements reference GTIN-based traceability where applicable.

## Limitations

- **Annual renewal:** Non-renewal can affect rights to use the prefix and associated GTINs.
- **Not a government registration:** GS1 India is a private, not-for-profit body; it is a commercial or industry standard membership, not a statutory government registration in itself.
- **Non-transferable prefix:** M&A and brand sales may require the acquirer to obtain their own prefix and re-label.
- **Platform gate:** Many retailers and e-commerce channels expect valid GTINs.
- **Tiered cost:** Annual fees depend on how many GTINs you need.
- **Printing not included:** You receive numbers and data files; label printing is your cost.

## Process

1. **Count GTINs:** Count unique variants (size, flavour, colour, pack) to pick a membership tier.
2. **Apply on GS1 India:** Complete the online application at [gs1india.org](https://www.gs1india.org) with company and product details and GSTIN.
3. **Submit documents** for verification of identity, entity, and GSTIN.
4. **Pay the annual** membership fee for the selected tier.
5. **Receive** the **GS1 Company Prefix** (often within a few business days of approval).
6. **Generate GTINs** using the online GTIN Generator; download barcode image files (SVG, PNG, EPS) as provided.
7. **Verify** barcodes (GS1 India verification or a certified verifier) before mass printing.
8. **Upload to product registry** so retailers and platforms can use verified data.

## Applicability

Manufacturers and brands in FMCG, food, beverages, personal care, pharma, medical devices, electronics, apparel, and agriculture that sell through organised retail, e-commerce, or export, and need globally unique product identification. **Pan-India**; GS1 India has regional presence including Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai, Ahmedabad, Hyderabad, and Bengaluru.

## Validity

GS1 India membership and the **GS1 Company Prefix** are typically valid for **1 year** from registration. **Annual renewal** is required to keep using the prefix and services; confirm current terms on the GS1 India portal.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I use a free online barcode generator instead of GS1 India?** Free generators may draw random numbers. They do not give you a globally unique, GS1-allocated GTIN. Marketplaces and many retailers look for **GS1-issued** data; non-compliant numbers can cause listing and supply chain issues.

**Do 5 flavour variants need 5 GTINs?** **Yes.** Each consumer unit that is distinct (flavour, size, colour, pack type) should have its own **GTIN**. A multipack of 6 is a different GTIN from a single unit if both are sold.

**Can the GS1 prefix be transferred in an acquisition?** **No** transfer in the usual sense. If the legal entity and brand change, the parent or new company typically needs its own prefix and must plan relabelling. If the acquired company continues as the same legal entity, existing GTINs may continue under that entity’s prefix.

**Is GS1 registration mandatory for Amazon India?** Amazon India strongly prefers **valid GTINs**; many categories require them, with limited exemptions. Serious sellers should treat GS1 registration as part of go-to-market planning.

**Does a small home food business need GS1 immediately?** If you only sell D2C without marketplace or modern trade listing, you may defer. Once you list on large platforms or supply retail chains, **GTINs** are usually required. A starter tier is often a practical early step if you are scaling to those channels.
