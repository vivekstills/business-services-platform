# Digital signature certificate (Class 3 DSC)

## Overview

A **Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)** is the electronic equivalent of a physical signature, issued by a **Certifying Authority (CA)** licensed by the **Controller of Certifying Authorities (CCA)** under the **Information Technology Act, 2000**. A DSC authenticates the holder and enables secure, legally valid electronic signing of documents, forms, and transactions.

**Class 3** is the class issued in India for regulatory signing. **Class 1** and legacy **Class 2** (new issuance for signing discontinued from January 2021) are not suitable for most **MCA, GST,** and **e-tender** work. The private key is stored on a **FIPS 140-2** certified **USB** cryptographic token so it cannot be copied off the device in normal use.

## Key features

- Legally valid for filings under the **IT Act, 2000** when issued by a **CCA**-licensed **CA** (eMudhra, Sify, NeSL, (n)Code, NSDL, and others).
- **Class 3** is the standard for company and professional regulatory signing.
- **Video KYC** (live verification) is used for identity proof in the usual flow.
- **Individual** and **organisation**-tagged use cases, depending on product.
- **Signing only** or **signing and encryption** options.
- **Aadhaar eSign** is a separate cloud product; not every portal treats it the same as a **USB** token **DSC** for a given form.

## Benefits

- **MCA, income tax, GST, EPFO, ICEGATE,** FSSAI, **DGFT,** and **e-tender** (GeM, CPPP, state portals) often require or strongly expect **Class 3 DSC.**
- **Non-repudiation** and document integrity: tampering after signing is detectable.
- **Faster** than physical couriers and wet signatures for government channels.
- **Directors and professionals** can sign from a compliant workstation without being present in an office.
- **Audit** trails on registries and portals for compliance.

## Limitations

- If the **USB token** is **lost** or **stolen**, the certificate must be **revoked** and a **new** **DSC** obtained; the private key is not restorable.
- **Drivers** and browsers are still **Windows**-leaning; **Mac** and **Linux** can be clunky.
- **Renewal** every **1–2** years; a missed renewal can **block** filings mid-process.
- A **DSC** is **not transferable**; when a director changes, the new person needs a new **DSC.**
- **eSign** may have **transaction** or **category** limits and may not be accepted on **MCA** for director-class signing.

## Process

1. **Choose a CA** (price, support, token compatibility with your PC and the portals you use).
2. **Apply online** for **Class 3**, **signing** and/or **encryption**, **1** or **2** year validity, and a **new** or **reused** token.
3. **Upload** PAN, Aadhaar, photograph, and for organisation DSC the CoI, board resolution, and GST if the CA asks.
4. **Complete video KYC** (PAN and Aadhaar to camera) as the CA requires.
5. **Pay** the **CA** fee and **token** cost if applicable.
6. **Install** the token driver, **test** a sample signature and **MCA/portal** registration as needed.
7. **Register** the **DSC** on each portal (MCA, income tax, GST, EPFO, ICEGATE) per that portal’s steps.
8. **Set** reminders **60+** days before **expiry** to renew in time for statutory deadlines.

## Documents required

| Document | Individual DSC | Organisation or director DSC |
|----------|---------------|-----------------------------|
| PAN | Yes | Yes |
| Aadhaar | Yes | Yes |
| Photograph, email, mobile | Yes | Yes |
| Video KYC | Yes | Yes |
| Certificate of Incorporation, board resolution, GST (if needed) | No | Yes for designated org DSC / role |

Foreign directors can usually obtain an Indian **Class 3** **DSC** with **passport**-based KYC, overseas address, and **PAN** as required by the **CA**.

## Applicability

**Directors,** designated **partners,** **CAs, CS,** and **authorised** signatories for GST, **EPFO,** **ICEGATE,** e-tendering, and **EXIM** businesses. **Pan-India;** KYC is typically online. No state-wise restriction on issuance.

## Validity

**1** or **2** years from issue. Renew before **expiry**; on renewal, a new certificate is loaded (same or new token). If a filing hits **expiry** during submission, the filing may **fail,** so renew well ahead of year-end and statutory **due** dates.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can one USB token hold DSCs of multiple people?** Standard practice is one **DSC** (one person) per **token** for security and non-repudiation. Do not **share** tokens.

**What if the token is lost or stolen?** Contact the **CA** to **revoke** the certificate immediately, then apply for a **new** **DSC** on a **new** token.

**Can a foreign national director get an Indian DSC?** Yes, with **passport-** based **video** KYC, **PAN,** and documents the **CA** lists for **non-resident** applicants.

**Is Aadhaar eSign a substitute for USB token DSC?** eSign is valid for many uses, but **MCA** company filings typically require a **Class 3** **USB** **DSC.** **GSTN** and income tax have broader eSign acceptance. Check each **portal** before you rely on eSign.

**The DSC expired and I missed an MCA deadline. What now?** Renew or buy a new **DSC** as soon as possible, file with **late** fees as applicable to the form, and set **reminders** for the next **cycle**.
