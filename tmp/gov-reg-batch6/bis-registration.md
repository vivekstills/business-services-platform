# BIS registration (ISI, CRS, FMCS)

## Overview

**BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) Registration** is a mandatory certification requirement for specified product categories in India. The BIS is India’s national standards body under the **Bureau of Indian Standards Act, 2016**, in the **Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food and Public Distribution**. It ensures products meet relevant **Indian Standards (IS)**, including quality, safety, performance, and reliability.

BIS offers several schemes: **ISI Mark (product certification)** for many domestic goods, **Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS)** for electronics/IT under MeitY’s 2012 order, **FMCS (Foreign Manufacturer Certification Scheme)** for overseas plants exporting to India, and **hallmarking** for precious metals. Over **400** product categories are or can be in mandatory scope depending on current notifications. Selling non-certified products in mandatory categories can have serious legal consequences, including product seizure and penalties, subject to the law and facts of each case.

## Key features

- **ISI Mark:** A widely recognised mark for many mandatory product types (steel, cement, electricals, and others per notification).
- **CRS:** Mandatory for listed electronics/IT (phones, LED, power banks, and many more per current list).
- **Hallmarking:** Gold (and related) under BIS, including **HUID**-based processes where applicable.
- **Indian Standards:** BIS maintains a large set of **IS** standards that underpin certification.
- **BIS Care app:** Public verification of certified products and hallmark where supported.
- **FMCS:** Route for **foreign** manufacturers of covered products shipping to India.

## Benefits

- **Market access** for categories where certification is mandatory.
- **Consumer safety** through defined technical benchmarks.
- **Trust:** ISI and CRS build confidence with buyers and channels.
- **E-commerce** platforms may ask for BIS-CRS for listed electronics.
- **Government procurement** often requires BIS for covered products.
- **Export:** MRAs in some cases can help with other markets, subject to the agreement.
- **Jewellery:** Hallmarking helps demonstrate purity in line with the scheme.

## Limitations

- **Factory work:** ISI often needs factory inspection, upgrades, and ongoing surveillance.
- **Surveillance:** BIS and market testing can follow after grant.
- **Lab testing** at recognised labs, with time and cost.
- **Penalties** for misuse of marks or unlicensed sales in mandatory categories.
- **Import complexity:** Extra scrutiny and documentation for some supply chains, especially for FMCS/imports.
- **Tight standards** in some goods may need design or process changes for SMEs.

## Process (ISI Mark, outline)

1. **Identify the IS number** for your product on the BIS website.
2. **File Form V** on the BIS **MIS** portal with company and product details.
3. **Get tests** at a BIS-recognised or NABL lab; parameters must pass the standard.
4. **Factory inspection** by BIS for manufacturing alignment with the standard.
5. **License grant** with **CM/L** when satisfied; add the mark per licence rules.
6. **Ongoing** surveillance, testing, and compliance.

## Process (CRS, outline)

1. **Test** the product at a BIS-empanelled lab for the applicable **IS** (safety, EMI, etc.).
2. **Apply** on the **CRS** portal with model details and reports.
3. **BIS review;** on approval, receive the **CRS registration number** in a common **25–45** day class for straightforward files (timelines can vary).
4. **Mark** the **CRS** number on each unit or its packaging as required.

## Applicability

**ISI:** wires, cement, steel (TMT), LPG, helmets, drinking water, and other notified goods. **CRS:** smartphones, LED, power banks, many IT/consumer electronics. **FMCS:** foreign manufacturers in mandatory list. **Hallmarking:** gold jewellery in line with the scheme. BIS is **pan-India**; regional BIS offices support manufacturers across states and UTs.

## Frequently asked questions

**LED import from China: what is needed?** LED lamps in CRS. The overseas plant may use **FMCS** or the import route may be tied to a **CRS** application with a BIS-empanelled test report, depending on structure. **Customs** and port processes should align with a valid **CRS** registration; verify the live product list and forms.

**Are steel water bottles mandatory for ISI?** Often they are in **voluntary** unless notified. Voluntary BIS can still help for tenders and retail. Re-check the **BIS** mandatory list for your exact SKU.

**How long is ISI end-to-end?** Testing, inspection slots, and review often mean **4–6 months** in smooth cases, longer with failures or resubmissions. **CRS** is often faster (**25–45** days) for a complete, passing file.

**Can I use the ISI mark before the licence?** **No**. Unauthorised use is an offence under the BIS Act. Use only after grant and in the form required with **CM/L**.

**Minor product change: new CRS?** **Depends.** Cosmetic or firmware changes that do not affect safety/EMC may be documented; major electrical changes usually need new testing and an amended or new **CRS** registration. Take advice on BIS’s current guidance for your model family.
