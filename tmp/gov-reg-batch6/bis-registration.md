# Bis Registration

## Overview

BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) Registration is a mandatory certification requirement for specified categories of products sold in India. The Bureau of Indian Standards, established under the Bureau of Indian Standards Act, 2016, is India's national standards body operating under the Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food and Public Distribution. BIS certification ensures that products conform to relevant Indian Standards (IS standards) ,  covering quality, safety, performance, and reliability parameters. BIS offers three main product certification schemes: ISI Mark Scheme (Product Certification): For domestically manufactured products across categories like steel, cement, electrical goods, food products, and more. Products bearing the ISI Mark (IS certification mark) signal conformity to the relevant Indian Standard. Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS): For electronics and IT products (mobile phones, laptops, LED lights, power banks, set-top boxes, etc.) under the Electronics and Information Technology Goods (Requirement for Compulsory Registration) Order, 2012. CRS registration is mandatory and enforced by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY). Foreign Manufacturer Certification Scheme (FMCS): For overseas manufacturers exporting products to India that fall under mandatory BIS certification categories. BIS registration/certification is legally mandatory for over 400 product categories. Selling non-certified products in these categories is a cognisable offence attracting prosecution, product seizure, and heavy penalties.

## Features

IS Mark (ISI Mark): The IS Certification Mark ,  commonly called the ISI Mark ,  is one of India's most recognised quality certification symbols; it is mandatory for over 100 product categories under the ISI Mark Scheme Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS): Electronic products must be registered under CRS before being manufactured in or imported into India; covers 75+ product categories including smartphones, tablets, laptops, LED products Hallmarking: BIS administers the gold and silver hallmarking scheme ,  mandatory for gold jewellery sold in India (HUID-based hallmarking since 2021) Indian Standards: BIS publishes and maintains over 20,000 Indian Standards (IS standards) across sectors ,  the technical backbone of the certification scheme BIS Care App: Consumers can verify the authenticity of BIS-certified products (ISI mark, hallmark) through the BIS Care mobile app and BIS website FMCS for importers: Foreign manufacturers exporting BIS-mandatory categories to India must obtain FMCS certification before shipping; Indian importers must verify this compliance

## Benefits

Legal market access: Without mandatory BIS certification (ISI Mark or CRS), products in covered categories cannot be legally sold in India ,  certification is the licence to sell Consumer safety assurance: BIS-certified products meet defined safety and quality benchmarks ,  reducing product failure rates, consumer hazard risks, and product liability exposure Competitive advantage: The ISI Mark is widely trusted by Indian consumers ,  products bearing it command higher consumer confidence, better shelf placement, and pricing power E-commerce compliance: Amazon, Flipkart, and other platforms require BIS-CRS registration numbers for applicable electronics categories before listing approval Government procurement: Central and state government tenders for electrical goods, building materials, and other BIS-covered products require certified products ,  certification unlocks this massive procurement market Export support: BIS certification under MRAs (Mutual Recognition Agreements) with foreign standards bodies can facilitate market access in certain export markets Hallmarking for jewellery: Mandatory BIS hallmarking enables jewellers to demonstrate purity compliance ,  a significant trust factor in the Indian jewellery market

## Limitations

Factory inspection mandatory: BIS ISI Mark certification requires factory inspection by BIS officers ,  this involves preparation, potential disruptions, and additional costs for factory upgrades to meet IS standard requirements Ongoing surveillance: BIS conducts periodic (quarterly/half-yearly) surveillance inspections of certified factories and may conduct market surveillance (purchasing products from retail and testing them) ,  non-conformance during surveillance leads to license suspension Test report requirement: Products must be tested at BIS-recognised/NABL-accredited laboratories and must pass before certification is granted ,  testing costs and timelines add to the compliance burden High penalty for violations: Misuse of BIS certification mark or selling uncertified products in mandatory categories can result in imprisonment up to 2 years and fines up to ₹2 lakh (or both) under the BIS Act, 2016 Country-of-origin complexities: For CRS and FMCS, Chinese manufacturers face additional scrutiny and documentation requirements post 2020 trade tensions Category-specific challenges: Some IS standards are stringent and require significant product design or manufacturing process changes ,  especially for SME manufacturers

## Applicability

ISI Mark: Mandatory for products in categories including electrical wires and cables, switches, plugs, sockets, cement, steel (TMT bars), LPG cylinders, helmets, baby food, packaged drinking water, and many more CRS (Compulsory Registration): Mandatory for electronics/IT products including mobile phones, laptops, tablets, LED lights, power banks, smart watches, set-top boxes, printers, UPS, and 75+ more categories FMCS: Foreign manufacturers of products falling under ISI/CRS mandatory categories who export to India Hallmarking: Mandatory for gold jewellery manufacturers and retailers (HUID-based since June 2021) Voluntary certification: Manufacturers of non-mandatory products can voluntarily obtain BIS certification to signal quality ,  common in sectors like furniture, textiles, and toys State-wide applicability: BIS is a central government body with jurisdiction across all states and UTs. BIS has regional offices in New Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, Chandigarh, and other cities, serving manufacturers across India.

## Process

ISI Mark Certification: Step 1 ,  Identify Applicable IS Standard Identify the relevant Indian Standard (IS number) for your product category on the BIS website (bis.gov.in). Step 2 ,  Application on BIS MIS Portal Register and submit Form V application on the BIS MIS portal with all company and product details. Step 3 ,  Product Testing Get products tested at a BIS-recognised or NABL-accredited laboratory. Submit test reports (must pass all parameters of the relevant IS standard). Step 4 ,  BIS Factory Inspection BIS officers conduct an inspection of the manufacturing premises to verify production processes, quality control procedures, and infrastructure conformance with IS standard requirements. Step 5 ,  Grant of License Upon satisfactory inspection and test reports, BIS grants the IS Certification License with a unique CM/L number (Certification Mark/License number). The ISI mark can now be used on products. Step 6 ,  Ongoing Surveillance Maintain quality control procedures and cooperate with BIS for periodic surveillance inspections and market surveillance testing. CRS Registration Process: Step 1 ,  Product Testing Get the electronics product tested at a BIS-empanelled laboratory for conformance to applicable Indian Standard (safety/EMI-EMC parameters). Step 2 ,  Online CRS Application Apply on the BIS CRS portal (crsbis.in) with product details, test reports, and applicant information. Step 3 ,  BIS Review and Approval BIS reviews the application and test reports. Upon approval, the CRS Registration Number is issued within 25–45 days. Step 4 ,  Mark products with CRS number The CRS Registration Number must be marked on each unit of the product ,  physically on the device or its packaging.

## Frequently asked questions

1. My product is an LED bulb manufactured in China and I want to import and sell it in India. What BIS compliance do I need? LED lamps fall under the Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS). The Chinese manufacturer must either: (a) obtain FMCS (Foreign Manufacturer Certification Scheme) certification from BIS for the specific LED product models, or (b) the Indian importer can apply for CRS registration on behalf of the manufacturer with a test report from a BIS-empanelled lab. Without valid BIS-CRS registration, LED bulbs cannot be legally imported into or sold in India. Customs authorities check BIS compliance at the port of entry. 2. I'm a small manufacturer of steel water bottles. Is BIS certification mandatory? Steel water bottles are currently under voluntary BIS certification ,  they are not in the mandatory ISI Mark or CRS list. However, if you wish to supply to government institutions, large organised retailers, or export to certain markets, voluntary BIS certification (IS 14846 for stainless steel cookware/utensils) significantly boosts credibility. Monitor BIS notifications ,  product categories are regularly added to the mandatory list; having voluntary certification puts you ahead of compliance requirements. 3. How long does it typically take to get BIS ISI Mark certification? The timeline for ISI Mark certification varies: laboratory testing takes 4–8 weeks; BIS factory inspection scheduling takes 4–8 weeks after application; BIS review and grant of license takes an additional 4–6 weeks. In total, end-to-end ISI Mark certification typically takes 4–6 months for a well-prepared applicant. Delays in document submission, factory non-conformance during inspection, or product test failures extend this significantly. CRS registration for electronics is faster ,  typically 25–45 days from complete application submission. 4. Can I use the ISI Mark on my products before receiving the BIS license? Absolutely not. Using the ISI Mark without a valid BIS certification license is a cognisable offence under the Bureau of Indian Standards Act, 2016, punishable with imprisonment of up to 2 years and/or a fine of up to ₹2 lakh. BIS conducts regular market surveillance and can initiate prosecution for unauthorised mark usage. Manufacturers must wait for the official license grant before printing the ISI Mark (with the CM/L license number) on their products. 5. Our electronics product model has been updated with minor component changes. Do we need a fresh CRS registration? Whether a product update requires fresh CRS registration depends on the nature of the change. Minor changes (firmware updates, colour changes, cosmetic alterations) that do not affect electrical safety, EMI-EMC compliance, or the core specifications covered in the original test report may not require fresh registration ,  but a declaration of conformity for the modified model should be maintained. Significant changes (different chipset, changed power supply, altered circuit design) require fresh product testing and a new or amended CRS registration. BIS has issued guidance on this ,  consulting a BIS compliance expert is advisable before releasing an updated model.
