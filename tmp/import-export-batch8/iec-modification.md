# IEC modification

## Overview

**IEC modification** updates an existing **Import Export Code** on the **DGFT** portal when business facts change, or when you run the **mandatory annual IEC update** (usually **1 April to 30 June**). After **April 2021** integration, **IEC** is tied to **PAN** and **GSTN**; mismatches can block customs and scheme use. The **annual update** (confirm or change) is required even if nothing changed, or **IEC** may be **deactivated**, which blocks imports, exports, and many bank and scheme uses until fixed.

**IEC** is issued once per **PAN** in normal cases and remains the same 10 characters; the certificate reflects updated legal name, address, bank, directors, or branches.

## Why modification matters

- **Port and bank** checks compare **IEC** to **BoE** / **SB** and remittance data
- **GST** and **DGFT** alignment avoids cross-system friction
- **RCMC** and **schemes** (RoDTEP, AA, EPCG) need a live, accurate **IEC**
- **ICEGATE** profile must be updated **separately** after **IEC** changes, especially for bank and **ICEPAY**

## Applicability

**IEC modification** applies when:

- **Annual update** (April–June) for all holders, including “no change” confirmation
- **Address** of principal or branch
- **Bank** account / **IFSC** change
- **Directors** / **partners** / signatories
- **Legal name** after **RoC** / deed change
- **New** export or import **branches**
- **GSTIN** change, new registration, or cancellation in your structure
- **Proprietor** name change (gazette, **Aadhaar**, **PAN**)

**All India**, central **DGFT** process.

## Benefits

- Fewer **customs** mismatches and **BoE** / **SB** queries
- **Annual** confirmation avoids **deactivation** and a hard stop to trade
- **GSTN**-aligned **IEC** for clean **1**, **2B**, **3B** and **scheme** stories
- **Banks** and **FEMA**-style reconciliation on the declared account
- **RCMC** and **EPC** alignment when council records depend on **IEC** text
- **Legal** record matches **RoC** / deed reality

## Limitations

- **Annual** window is **mandatory**; missing it deactivates **IEC**
- **Reactivation** needs completing the overdue update, with possible gap risk for old shipments
- **PAN**-linked: fix **PAN** at **IT** first if the database name is wrong
- **New signatories** may need **DSC** / **Aadhaar** as the portal year requires
- **Dispute** or **suspension** on **IEC** can block modification until resolved
- **ICEGATE** does not auto-refresh; update profile after **DGFT** approval

## Process

1. **Login** at **dgft.gov.in**; during April–June, expect an **annual** prompt.
2. **Navigate** to **IEC** profile, **update** / **modify** as the menu shows for the year.
3. **Review** all fields: entity, address, bank, signatory, **GSTIN**, **directors** / **partners**.
4. **Edit** what changed, or **confirm** unchanged for **annual** compliance.
5. **Upload** proofs: address, bank certificate, **RoC** for name, resolution for signatory, etc., in **PDF** within size limits.
6. **Authenticate** with **Aadhaar** **OTP** and/or **DSC** as the portal requires for your entity type.
7. **Submit**; note **ARN**.
8. **DGFT** often clears simple changes in 1–3 days; name / complex cases can take longer.
9. **Download** updated **e-IEC**; number unchanged, details new.
10. **Update** **ICEGATE**, **RCMC**, and **AD bank** as needed.

## Use cases (short)

1. **Office moved:** Update **PPOB** before the next **SB** / **BoE** uses old address.
2. **Annual, no change:** **Confirm** in window to stay active.
3. **Funding, new directors:** **Modify** list and new **authorised** person **DSC** / **Aadhaar** path.
4. **Bank** switch: **One** primary account on **IEC**; change **IEC** before routing export proceeds to new bank.
5. **New** **Silvassa** **branch** export: **Map** extra branch in **IEC** so filings match place of business.

## Frequently asked questions

**Name change after RoC, new IEC?** **Modify**, do not re-issue; same **PAN**-linked **IEC** number, new legal name on certificate with **CoI** / **RoC** proof.

**Missed April–June, IEC deactivated?** Log in, complete the **annual** / overdue update; **reactivation** usually in a few days, no separate government fee, but check operations during the gap period on advice.

**Proprietor married, name change?** **Modify** with gazette, **Aadhaar**, **PAN** in one aligned name; **IEC** number stays the same.

**Add second bank, keep first?** **DGFT** link is typically **one** primary **IEC** account at a time; change to the account that will take export or import money under your **FEMA** plan.

**Merger, which IEC?** Surviving entity’s **IEC** continues, with post-merger **IEC** and entity updates; **absorbed** company’s **IEC** surrendered separately; **M&A** needs coordinated **DGFT**, **ICEGATE**, **RCMC**, and bank work.
