# TDS return filing

## Overview

TDS Return Filing is the quarterly statutory compliance requirement for all persons who have deducted Tax at Source (TDS) or collected Tax at Source (TCS) under the provisions of the Income Tax Act, 1961. Every TAN-holder (Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number holder) who has deducted TDS from payments made during the quarter must file the applicable TDS return statement with the Income Tax department through the TRACES/TIN portal by the prescribed due date.

TDS Returns are statements that consolidate all TDS deductions made during the quarter, payment-wise, deductee-wise, along with the corresponding tax deposits made via Challan 281. These returns form the basis for the automatic generation of Form 26AS for each deductee, which deductees use to claim TDS credit against their tax liability when filing ITRs.

**Types of TDS return forms:**

| Form | Nature of TDS/TCS | Filed by |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Form 24Q | TDS on Salary (Section 192) | Employers |
| Form 26Q | TDS on non-salary payments (domestic) | All deductors |
| Form 27Q | TDS on payments to non-residents | Persons making foreign payments |
| Form 27EQ | TCS (Tax Collected at Source) returns | TCS collectors |

The TDS return ecosystem has been significantly streamlined through the TRACES portal (TDSCPC.gov.in) and the RPU (Return Preparation Utility) provided free by the Income Tax department, enabling accurate TDS return preparation and online submission.

## Applicability

**TDS return filing is mandatory for:**

- All employers who deduct TDS on employee salaries (Form 24Q)
- All businesses and individuals (under tax audit) who deduct TDS on contractor payments, professional fees, rent, interest, commission, etc. (Form 26Q)
- All persons making payments to non-residents, foreign payments subject to TDS under Section 195 (Form 27Q)
- E-commerce operators, sellers of specified goods, lottery operators, and others collecting TCS (Form 27EQ)
- Banks and NBFCs deducting TDS on interest payments
- Companies deducting TDS on dividends paid

**NIL TDS returns:** If a TAN-holder made no TDS deductions in a quarter but has previously filed returns, a NIL return must still be filed to maintain compliance status.

Applicable pan-India, all states and UTs; TDS return filing is a central compliance on the TRACES portal.

## Key features

- Quarterly filing: TDS returns are filed four times a year, Q1 (April-June), Q2 (July-September), Q3 (October-December), Q4 (January-March)
- Deductee-wise data: Each TDS return contains transaction-level data, deductee PAN, payment amount, TDS rate, TDS amount, and challan number for each payment
- RPU (Return Preparation Utility): Free software from NSDL for preparing TDS returns in the .fvu (file validation utility) format required for submission
- TRACES portal filing: Online submission of prepared TDS return files on the TRACES portal
- Digital signature requirement: Companies and government deductors must submit TDS returns using DSC; others can use a physical signature on the verification form
- Form 26AS auto-update: Filed TDS returns directly feed into deductees' Form 26AS, enabling them to claim TDS credit in their ITR
- Correction statements: TDS returns can be corrected for PAN errors, amount errors, or challan mismatches through correction statements on TRACES
- Centralised Processing Cell (CPC-TDS): TRACES processes TDS returns centrally, generating demand notices for short deduction, late filing, or mismatch
- Form 16 and 16A generation: After filing TDS returns, Form 16 (for salary TDS) and Form 16A (for non-salary TDS) certificates are generated on TRACES and downloaded for issuance to deductees

## Benefits

- **Deductee tax credit facilitation:** TDS returns are the mechanism through which deductees receive credit for TDS deducted, without a filed return, the TDS doesn't appear in the deductee's Form 26AS and cannot be claimed
- **Employer compliance:** Timely Form 24Q filing and Form 16 issuance are legal obligations of every employer, compliance demonstrates responsible payroll management
- **Avoid interest and penalties:** Late filing attracts ₹200/day late fee under Section 234E and penalties up to ₹1 lakh under Section 271H, timely filing eliminates these costs
- **Vendor relationship management:** Vendors and service providers receiving TDS-deducted payments need TDS certificates (Form 16A), timely return filing enables timely certificate issuance, strengthening business relationships
- **TRACES analytics:** TRACES provides deductors with analytics on TDS credit matching, defaults, and pending corrections, enabling proactive compliance management
- **Regulatory credibility:** Companies with consistent TDS return filing history demonstrate tax compliance credibility, important during audits, due diligence, and government tenders

## Process

1. **Compile TDS data** Gather all payment data for the quarter, payments made to each deductee, TDS rate applied, TDS amount deducted, and the corresponding Challan 281 details (BSR code, date, serial number).
2. **Download and install RPU** Download the latest Return Preparation Utility (RPU) from the NSDL/TRACES website. Install on the computer. The RPU generates the .fvu file required for return submission.
3. **Prepare return in RPU** Open the RPU. Enter deductor details (TAN, PAN, name, address, financial year, quarter). Enter challan details (one row per challan). Enter deductee details (one row per deductee payment, PAN, name, payment amount, TDS rate, TDS amount). Validate the data, the RPU performs data validation.
4. **Generate FVU file** Run the FVU (File Validation Utility) on the prepared return. The FVU checks for errors, PAN validity, challan matching, rate correctness. Correct all errors. Generate the final .fvu file.
5. **Upload on TRACES portal** Log in to TRACES (tdscpc.gov.in) using TAN credentials. Navigate to Statements / Payments → Upload TDS. Upload the .fvu file along with the signed verification form (or DSC for electronic verification).
6. **Token number generation** TRACES generates a token number upon successful upload. Track the return processing status using this token.
7. **Return processing** TRACES processes the return, matching challans, validating PANs, and updating Form 26AS for all deductees. Any defaults (short deduction, PAN errors, challan mismatch) are flagged as demands.
8. **Generate and issue TDS certificates** After processing, log in to TRACES and download Form 16A for non-salary TDS or request Form 16 generation for salary TDS. Issue certificates to deductees within the prescribed deadline.

## Common errors

- Incorrect deductee PAN: The most common and costly error, TDS returns with wrong PANs result in TDS credit not reflecting in deductees' Form 26AS, leading to complaints, demand notices, and correction statement obligations. Always verify PANs before filing
- Challan mismatch: Entering wrong BSR code, challan date, or serial number in the TDS return, causes challan mismatch defaults on TRACES. Cross-verify challan details from the bank's Challan 281 acknowledgement before filing
- Short deduction: Deducting TDS at a lower rate than prescribed, attracts short deduction demand (deductor must pay the shortfall with interest)
- Late deposit of TDS after deduction: Deducting TDS in March but depositing in May, interest under Section 201(1A) at 1.5% per month for delay in deposit
- Not filing correction for PAN errors: Knowing that a deductee's PAN in the original return was wrong but not filing a correction, the deductee continues to not see the TDS credit, potentially filing complaints with the IT department against the deductor

## Frequently asked questions

**We deducted TDS from a vendor payment but the vendor says they don't see the credit in Form 26AS. What could be wrong?** The most likely reasons: (a) the TDS return has not been filed yet for that quarter, the vendor's Form 26AS updates only after the return is filed; (b) the PAN used in the TDS return for this vendor is incorrect, TDS credit is mapped to PAN; a wrong PAN means credit goes to someone else's account; (c) there is a challan mismatch in the filed return, the system couldn't reconcile the deduction with the payment, holding up Form 26AS update. Check the TRACES portal for the TDS return's processing status and any defaults. File a correction statement if there is a PAN error or challan mismatch.

**We missed the TDS return due date by 10 days. What is the exact penalty and can it be waived?** Under Section 234E, a late fee of ₹200 per day for the duration of delay is levied, for 10 days, this is ₹2,000 per return. This is a mandatory late fee, not a penalty, and typically cannot be waived. For egregious delays (beyond one year) or willful non-filing, an additional penalty of ₹10,000 to ₹1 lakh under Section 271H can be levied by the AO, but this requires a formal proceeding. The ₹200/day late fee is automatically assessed when the return is filed late. Pay the late fee before filing the delayed return, the TRACES system will prompt you for the fee payment.

**One of our employees left mid-year and later joined another company. How do we handle TDS for the period they worked with us?** For an employee who left mid-year, your TDS obligation (Form 24Q) covers the period they were in your payroll, from April 1 (or joining date if mid-year) to the date of leaving. File Form 24Q for Q4 including their salary for the portion of the year they were with you. Issue Form 16 (Part A and Part B) covering only the period of employment with your company. The new employer will collect their Form 12B (declaring prior employer salary) and consolidate the total salary for the year to compute the correct TDS from their salary. The employee then files their ITR combining salary from both employers and claiming TDS deducted by each under their respective Form 16s.

**We have deducted TDS at 10% from a contractor payment, but the contractor provided a lower TDS certificate from the IT department (Section 197). Can we revise the deduction retrospectively?** Section 197 certificates allow deductors to deduct TDS at a lower rate as specified in the certificate, but only from the date the certificate is received. If TDS was already deducted at the standard rate (10%) before the certificate was received, that deduction stands. Going forward (after receiving the certificate), deduct at the reduced rate specified. The contractor can claim a refund of the excess TDS in their ITR, the excess appears in their Form 26AS as deducted, and their lower final tax liability results in a refund. There is no mechanism to retrospectively revise the TDS deducted before the Section 197 certificate was obtained.

**We are a small company with only 3 employees, all with annual salary below ₹3 lakh (below tax threshold). Do we still need to file Form 24Q?** If no employee's salary is taxable (i.e., all three have total income below the basic exemption limit under the regime they chose), no TDS needs to be deducted under Section 192, and Form 24Q can be a NIL return. However, if you have previously filed Form 24Q for earlier quarters where TDS was deducted (from higher-earning employees), continue filing NIL returns to maintain continuity. If you have never filed Form 24Q before and have no TDS obligation, you may not technically be required to file, but obtaining a TAN and being prepared to file if salaries increase is advisable. Note: Other TDS obligations (contractor payments, professional fees, rent) may independently require Form 26Q filings even if salary TDS is NIL.
