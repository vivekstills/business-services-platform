# TDS return filing

## Overview

TDS returns are quarterly statements for deductors: 24Q salary, 26Q other than salary resident, 27Q non-resident, 27EQ TCS. They are prepared in RPU, validated to FVU, uploaded on TRACES, and drive Form 26AS for deductees.

## Deadlines (typical pattern, confirm year)

Q1 to July 31, Q2 to October 31, Q3 to January 31, Q4 to May 31. Form 16 by June 15 after year end; 16A fifteen days from each return due date for that quarter in law.

## Key features

- Deductee wise rows; challan match; late fee 234E per day per return; 271H in serious default.

## Documents

TAN, all deductee PAN, payment and rate, challan 281 BSR and serials, previous quarter files for continuity.

## Process

1. Reconcile books with TDS cut and deposited.
2. RPU, validate FVU, upload on TRACES, correct if needed, then 16A or 16 from TRACES or ERP.

## Fees (professional, excl. GST 18%)

| Return | Indicative per quarter (INR) |
|--------|-----------------------------|
| 24Q small | 2,000 to 5,000 |
| 26Q | 2,000 to 4,000 |
| 24Q plus 26Q mid size | 5,000 to 15,000 |
| 27Q | 3,000 to 8,000 |

## Common errors

- Wrong PAN; challan amount mismatch; short deduction; late deposit interest 201 1A.

## Frequently asked questions

**Credit not in 26AS?** Return not filed, wrong PAN, or processing default on TRACES.

**Late by ten days?** 200 per day under 234E in usual pattern; pay and file.

**Section 197 certificate?** Use rate from date of effect forward.
