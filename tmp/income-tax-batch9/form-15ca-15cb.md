# Form 15CA and 15CB filing

## Overview

Form 15CA and 15CB support compliance under Section 195 of the Income Tax Act, 1961, for remittances from India to non-residents or foreign companies. The remitter files 15CA on the Income Tax e-filing portal. Form 15CB is a Chartered Accountant certificate on the nature of payment, applicable TDS, the Act or DTAA, and compliance. The AD bank needs these before processing many foreign payments above the notified thresholds. Each distinct remittance generally needs its own 15CA; 15CB is required before Part C in the usual case.

## When 15CA parts apply (summary)

| Situation | 15CA part (typical) | 15CB |
|-----------|--------------------|------|
| Not taxable, small remittance (rules in force) | A | No |
| AO lower or nil TDS order | B | No |
| Taxable, CA certificate | C | Yes |
| Specified exempt list, Rule 37BB | D | No |

## Applicability

Required for: many payments chargeable in India; royalties, fees, interest, dividend, rent, salary to non-residents; FDI or group outflows; software and IP; import of services, where not excluded.

Often out of 15CA/15CB: entries in Rule 37BB(3) (examples include many current account and goods import style lines, travel, education, medical, with right proof); some personal remittances under Part D rules; cases covered by a specific nil or lower TDS order from the AO.

## Key features

- Four parts of 15CA: A, B, C, D as the facts and law match.
- 15CB: practising CA, UDIN, DTAA and rate.
- Filing on incometax.gov.in; bank submission before remittance in scope.
- TRC and Form 10F often material for DTAA in 15CB.
- 15CA may be withdrawable in a short window if remittance not made (check portal for the year).

## Benefits

- DTAA analysis can cut withholding versus high domestic rates when facts and TRC support it.
- Banks need clean 15CA/15CB to release funds.
- Section 271-I style penalty risk if default is in point (get advice on your case).

## Documents

| Item | Use |
|------|-----|
| Remitter and recipient ID, contract or invoice | Nature and amount |
| TRC, Form 10F if DTAA | Treaty rate |
| TDS challans if already paid | Reconciliation |
| AO order if Part B | Lower or nil TDS |
| CA details for 15CB | Filing and UDIN |

## Process

1. Classify the payment and pick Part A, B, C, or D.
2. For Part C, obtain 15CB from a CA, then TDS, deposit, then 15CA with 15CB ref.
3. File 15CA; download acknowledgement.
4. Give 15CA and 15CB to the AD bank; complete SWIFT or other remittance.
5. Issue 16A or other TDS certificate as per TDS law.

## Deadlines (pattern)

- TDS: time of credit or payment, earlier one.
- Deposit: 7th of next month (30 April for March).
- 15CA/15CB: before bank processes remittance.
- 27Q: quarterly, as notified.

## Common errors

- Wrong part (e.g. Part D for a clearly taxable line).
- DTAA without valid TRC where relied on.
- Part C without 15CB when required.
- Goods versus services label wrong for import.

## Frequently asked questions

**Monthly retainer to a US consultant, every month?** Each transfer is a separate 15CA in principle; 15CB may be annual or per event depending on your CA and facts.

**Singapore DTAA 10% royalty?** TRC, Form 10F, agreement, and CA 15CB at treaty rate; without TRC, domestic rates may apply.

**Missed 15CA before remittance?** File immediately, pay penalty risk as law may apply, fix process with bank and CA.

**Import goods from Germany?** Part D class for true goods import with BoE; mixed software as service needs careful characterisation.

**Service fully abroad, TDS?** Source and DTAA, PE, business profits versus royalty/FTS need CA review; do not assume nil without a memo.
