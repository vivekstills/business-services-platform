# PAN card application

## Overview

PAN is a ten character identifier from the Income Tax Department, issued via NSDL or UTI paths. It links Aadhaar for individuals. It is the key for ITR, TDS, banking, and large parts of Indian compliance. e-PAN and instant e-PAN options exist for many applicants.

## Applicability

All taxable persons and most entities; Rule 114B style quoting for many high value or prescribed transactions.

## Key features

- Entity type in fourth character; e-PAN; Aadhaar link to avoid inoperative PAN.

## Documents

For individuals: Aadhaar or other KYC, address, DOB proof. For companies: CoI, address, board resolution as needed. Form 49A or 49AA for foreigner.

## Process

1. File 49A or 49AA online; pay fee; e-KYC; track; receive card or e-PAN.

## Common errors

- Two PANs: surrender duplicate; fine risk.
- No Aadhaar link: inoperative after notified date; pay fee to link.
- Name DOB mismatch with bank KYC.

## Frequently asked questions

**Inoperative PAN?** Link Aadhaar with prescribed fee; follow portal steps.

**New company?** Apply soon after CoI; needed for bank and GST.

**Foreign employee in India?** 49AA and passport; employer TDS needs PAN.
