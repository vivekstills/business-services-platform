# ITR-1 form filing (Sahaj)

## Overview

ITR-1, or Sahaj, is for resident individuals with simple income: salary or pension, one house property, other sources such as interest and family pension, and small agricultural income within the limit the form allows. Total income must be within the ceiling the current ITR rules set. It is not for business, capital gains, foreign assets, director status, unlisted shares as the form bars, or non-residents.

## Applicability checklist (verify form for the year)

| Criterion | Typical ITR-1 rule |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Residency | Resident and ordinarily resident |
| Total income cap | Up to fifty lakh as per form |
| Salary | One or more employers ok if form allows |
| House property | One property only in ITR-1 |
| Other sources | Allowed types only |
| Agriculture | Up to five thousand |

## Key features

- Strong prefill from Form 16, AIS, TIS, 26AS.
- Old versus new regime choice where rules allow.
- Online on incometax.gov.in; JSON offline path exists.
- EVC: Aadhaar OTP, net banking, etc.

## Benefits

- Fast for simple salaried filers; quicker refunds when clean.
- Builds a clear compliance record for loans and visas.

## Documents

Form 16, 26AS, AIS, bank statements, HRA proofs, home loan certificate, 80C to 80U proofs as applicable, PAN, bank for refund.

## Process

1. Collect documents and reconcile 26AS with Form 16.
2. Login, pick assessment year, select ITR-1.
3. Check prefill, fix salary, one house, other income, TDS.
4. Choose regime and deductions allowed in that regime.
5. Pay balance tax if any before filing; file and verify within 30 days.

## Deadlines (typical pattern, confirm each year)

Original by 31 July for many non-audit individuals; belated and revised often by 31 December of the assessment year; late fee Section 234F if belated.

## Common errors

- Skipping savings interest in return of income.
- Using ITR-1 when capital gains exist, even small.
- HRA and metro limits wrong.
- 26AS TDS and Form 16 mismatch.

## Frequently asked questions

**Mutual fund redemption?** Capital gains: use ITR-2, not ITR-1.

**Two Form 16?** Combine salary; check total TDS and employer slabs.

**Side YouTube income?** Often ITR-3 or 4, not ITR-1, depending on character and presumption.

**Home loan self-occupied?** Interest up to the section limit in old regime; not in new for self-occupied as usually framed.

**Missed 31 July?** Belated return; then ITR-U only if the law path fits after December cut-off.
