# TAN registration

## Overview

TAN is the deductor or collector id for TDS and TCS. It is used on challan 281, TDS returns, and certificates. Banks need TAN for TDS credit to map correctly. Without valid TAN, compliance breaks for payroll and many vendor payments.

## Applicability

Employers, businesses paying rent above notified limits to residents in 194I style cases through prescribed processes, professional and contractor TDS in scope, and many TCS cases. Some individual transactions use Form 26QB or 26QC without a separate TAN; check the section.

## Key features

- Form 49B; online application; TRACES for returns and Form 16A; one TAN per normal deductor, branches may use more in large setups.

## Documents

PAN, address, incorporation for companies, signatory, contact.

## Process

1. Apply 49B on NSDL or UTI, pay small fee, get TAN letter.
2. Register on TRACES; map bank for 281.

## Fees

Government: about 65 rupees plus GST for application; professional five hundred to two thousand for help.

## Common errors

- Wrong TAN on challan; TAN without TRACES; duplicate TAN.

## Frequently asked questions

**First employee?** TAN before first salary TDS in scope.

**Freelancer paying others?** TDS 194J may need TAN if audit and threshold tests in law apply; many small payers do not cut TDS.
