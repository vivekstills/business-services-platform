# ITR-4 form filing (Sugam)

## Overview

ITR-4, Sugam, is for small taxpayers using presumptive schemes 44AD, 44ADA, or 44AE, plus often salary, one house, and other sources within the form limits. It avoids full books in eligible cases. Director, unlisted share, capital gain, or foreign assets rules generally bar ITR-4.

## Presumption snapshot (check limits in Act)

- 44AD: business turnover up to two crore, deemed profit percent on turnover, digital rate often lower.
- 44ADA: listed professions, gross up to fifty lakh, fifty percent of receipts unless opt for higher.
- 44AE: goods carriages, per vehicle per month method.

## Key features

- No full books in many presumptive cases; five year continuity mind-set for 44AD opt in and opt out; advance tax pattern simplified in many presumptive files.

## Benefits

- Lower compliance cost; no audit in many 44AD and 44ADA cases when profit at least at presumptive level.

## Documents

Turnover from GST and bank, Form 16, 26AS, vehicle list for 44AE, investment proofs for Chapter VIA in old regime.

## Process

1. Sum turnover; apply the right section.
2. Add salary and one house; pick regime; pay tax; file and verify.

## Fees (excl. GST 18%)

| Type | Band (INR) |
|------|------------|
| Simple | 1,500 to 4,000 |
| Salary and business | 2,500 to 6,000 |
| 44AE | 3,000 to 6,000 |

## Common errors

- Any capital gain: leave ITR-4.
- Turnover over section limits: use ITR-3.
- Declaring below presumption without following audit and opt out rules.

## Frequently asked questions

**Graphic design and 44ADA?** List of professions in rules must be checked each year.

**Ninety five lakh sales, 8% profit?** 44AD may allow; know opt out and five year effect.

**CA under 44ADA?** Common for sole practice under limit.

**Shop and engineering fees?** Both in one ITR-4 if total income and section tests met.
