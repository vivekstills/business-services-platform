# ITR-U filing (updated return)

## Overview

ITR-U is filed under Section 139(8A) to correct omissions or to file when no return was filed, inside the time limit the law allows from the end of the relevant assessment year. That limit has been amended by Finance Acts, so the exact end date for each assessment year must be read in the current Act. It is not for claiming a higher refund or lowering tax below the original return. Additional tax is charged as a deterrent at rates that step up the later you are within the window.

## Additional tax (pattern in law, verify in force)

| Filing from end of relevant AY | Additional tax on underpaid tax (verify exact wording) |
|--------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| First slab | 25% |
| Next slab | 50% |
| Next | 60% |
| Outermost | 70% |

Interest under Sections 234B and 234C may apply on shortfalls.

## Applicability

For: extra income not in original return, wrong head, belated true-up where revision is closed, subject to blockers below.

**Cannot** use ITR-U to: seek refund or lower tax from original, after covered assessment, during certain search and survey conditions as law lists, to cut reported income in ways the section forbids, or for years outside the ITR-U window the Act allows.

## Key features

- One updated return per year per assessee in the section’s sense, subject to current rules.
- Must pay tax, additional tax, and interest as computed before a valid file.
- Works with the correct ITR form type for the person.

## Benefits

- Voluntary clean-up can reduce harsher penalty paths if the department finds issues first, subject to facts.
- Clear numbers for past crypto, foreign, or unreported lines when law allows the route.

## Documents

Original ITR, Form 16 and 16A, bank and broker statements, sale deeds, foreign proofs, old challans, and payment for incremental tax for ITR-U.

## Process

1. Rebuild income, tax, and TDS for the year.
2. Check ITR-U window and blockers; decide revised return first if still open.
3. Compute incremental tax, additional tax, interest.
4. Pay via Challan 280 and keep proof.
5. File ITR-U on the portal with the linked ITR form, verify, keep acknowledgement.

## Deadlines (illustration only, verify each year)

The law ties ITR-U to a period from the end of the relevant assessment year. Do not use old web tables without cross-checking the Act for 2025-26 and later.

## Common errors

- Treating ITR-U as a revision for refund.
- Wrong slab for additional tax.
- Filing when assessment is already final as law defines.

## Frequently asked questions

**Crypto in FY 2021-22 not reported?** ITR-U may be an option in window; VDA tax rules differ by year. Use a CA for head and rate.

**Department found it first?** Voluntary ITR-U benefit may be lost; expect scrutiny and penalties the law allows.

**Revision still open?** Prefer revised return when allowed; ITR-U when revision window is gone.

**Depreciation error?** ITR-U only for patterns the section allows, usually upward income moves, not arbitrary loss increases.
