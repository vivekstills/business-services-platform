# Fssai Return Filing

## Overview

FSSAI Return Filing is a periodic statutory compliance requirement for specific categories of Food Business Operators (FBOs) under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006, and the Food Safety and Standards (Licensing and Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011. Certain FBOs ,  primarily manufacturers and importers ,  are required to file annual returns with FSSAI detailing the food products manufactured, imported, and sold during the financial year. There are two types of FSSAI returns relevant to most FBOs: Form D-1 (Annual Return): Filed annually by all food manufacturers and importers holding a State License or Central License. It captures details of food products manufactured/imported during the financial year. Form D-2 (Half-yearly Return): Filed half-yearly (April–September and October–March) specifically by milk and milk product manufacturers. This is a specialised return for dairy FBOs. FSSAI returns serve as a key data source for the food safety authority to monitor production volumes, product categories, and compliance levels across India's food industry. Non-filing or incorrect filing attracts penalties and can affect the license renewal process. While FSSAI Return Filing is often overlooked by small and medium food businesses, it is a non-negotiable statutory obligation for all licensed manufacturers and importers ,  and increasing enforcement action by FSSAI makes timely and accurate return filing more important than ever.

## Applicability

Form D-1 (Annual Return) ,  Mandatory for: All food manufacturers holding a State License or Central License All food importers holding a Central License FBOs engaged in both manufacturing and trading/distribution who manufacture any food product Form D-1 is NOT required for: Basic Registration holders (petty food business operators) Pure traders, distributors, and retailers who do not manufacture any food product Storage operators, transporters, and restaurants/caterers (unless they also manufacture) Form D-2 (Half-yearly Return) ,  Mandatory for: Licensed manufacturers of milk and milk products (dairy processors, chilling centres, processing plants) Applicable under both State and Central Licenses Filing Deadlines: Return Type Period Filing Deadline Form D-1 (Annual) April 1 – March 31 May 31 of the following year Form D-2 (Half-yearly) ,  H1 April – September October 31 Form D-2 (Half-yearly) ,  H2 October – March April 30

Pan-India applicability: Applies uniformly across all states and UTs to all licensed manufacturers and importers ,  Karnataka, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Gujarat, Rajasthan, West Bengal, Telangana, Kerala, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Haryana, Andhra Pradesh, and all others.

## Benefits

Legal compliance: Avoids penalties for non-filing which can range from ₹1 lakh to ₹5 lakh under the FSS Act Smooth license renewal: FSSAI licensing authorities verify return filing compliance at the time of license renewal ,  pending returns can stall or block renewals Data-driven credibility: Accurate return filing builds a verified production track record with FSSAI ,  useful for government procurement, export registrations, and B2B contracts Audit readiness: Filed FSSAI returns corroborate the production volumes and product categories declared to GST, income tax, and excise authorities ,  ensuring cross-compliance Investment and M&A readiness: Investors and acquirers verify FSSAI return compliance during due diligence ,  clean filing history is a mark of compliance maturity Export facilitation: APEDA, MPEDA, and other export promotion bodies often seek FSSAI compliance certificates; regular return filing supports export authorisation processes Market access: Certain organised retail chains, hypermarkets, and institutional buyers ask for FSSAI return filing compliance as part of supplier onboarding criteria

## Limitations

Manufacturing-only obligation: Pure traders and retailers are exempt from return filing ,  this creates confusion for businesses that have both manufacturing and trading activities (all must file for the manufacturing component) Data accuracy burden: Accurate compilation of product-wise manufacturing/import volumes, pack sizes, and sales figures across 12 months requires robust internal record-keeping systems that many SME food manufacturers lack No correction mechanism post-filing: Once filed, FSSAI returns cannot be easily amended. Errors in product volumes or category descriptions may create discrepancies with other regulatory filings Penalty for non-filing is significant: Fines of ₹1 lakh to ₹5 lakh for non-compliance with licensing conditions (which includes return filing) are disproportionate to the filing effort ,  making it a high-risk oversight Portal-based filing dependencies: The FoSCoS portal has historically been subject to technical issues around deadlines ,  last-minute filers often encounter portal downtime or login issues Awareness gap: A large proportion of State License holders in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities are unaware of the return filing obligation, leading to widespread non-compliance that is now attracting increased regulatory attention

## Process

Step 1 ,  Determine Applicability Confirm whether your FSSAI License category (State or Central) and business activity (manufacturing/importing) requires Form D-1 and/or Form D-2 filing. Step 2 ,  Data Collection and Compilation Gather product-wise manufacturing/import data for the entire financial year. This includes quantity produced, quantity imported, quantity sold, and closing stock ,  broken down by product category and product name. Step 3 ,  Cross-Reference with GST and Financial Records Reconcile FSSAI return data with GST returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B) and audited financials to ensure consistency. Discrepancies between FSSAI returns and GST/income tax filings can attract scrutiny from multiple regulators. Step 4 ,  FoSCoS Portal Login Log in to the FoSCoS portal and navigate to the "Annual Return" or "Half-Yearly Return" section under your License. Step 5 ,  Fill Return Form Online Complete the return form (D-1 or D-2) on the FoSCoS portal with accurate data for each product category. The portal auto-populates the FSSAI License details. Step 6 ,  Review and Validation Review all entries for accuracy before submission. Check that product categories match the categories mentioned in the FSSAI License. Validate totals and ensure no mandatory fields are blank. Step 7 ,  Submit Return Submit the return on the FoSCoS portal before the deadline. Download and save the submission acknowledgement with the unique submission reference number. Step 8 ,  Post-Filing Archival Maintain filed returns and supporting data for at least 2 years (for routine inspections) or 5 years (for legal/M&A purposes). FSSAI inspectors may request copies of filed returns during premises inspections.

## Validity

FSSAI Returns, once filed, are permanent statutory records. They do not have a "validity period" ,  they are historical compliance records for the period covered. However, their relevance is highest during: License renewal proceedings (authority checks for pending unfiled returns) Food Safety Officer inspections (inspector may request last 2 years' returns) M&A and investment due diligence (last 3–5 years' returns reviewed) Export registration and institutional buyer onboarding (last 1–2 years' returns may be required)

Renewal FSSAI Returns are a condition of license compliance. At the time of FSSAI License Renewal, the FoSCoS portal and the licensing authority verify whether all pending returns for the license validity period have been filed. If any annual or half-yearly returns are pending, the renewal application may be put on hold until the outstanding returns are filed. Best practice is to file all pending returns before initiating the renewal application to avoid delays in renewal approval.

## Frequently asked questions

1. I am a restaurant with a State License ,  do I need to file FSSAI Annual Returns? No. Restaurants, hotels, caterers, dhabas, and food service businesses that do not manufacture packaged food products are exempt from Form D-1 Annual Return filing. The return obligation applies specifically to licensed food manufacturers and importers. If, however, your restaurant also manufactures and sells packaged food products (e.g., bottled sauces, packed namkeen, branded pickles) under its FSSAI License, the manufacturing component would attract return filing obligations. 2. We manufacture in one state but sell across India. Do we file the FSSAI return with the Central authority or State authority? If you hold a State License (single manufacturing unit), the Annual Return is filed with the State Food Safety Department that issued your license ,  regardless of where your products are sold. If you hold a Central License (because you sell across states or your turnover exceeds ₹20 crore), the return is filed with FSSAI's Central Licensing Authority through the FoSCoS portal. The key factor is your license type, not your sales geography. 3. What details are required in the FSSAI Annual Return Form D-1? Form D-1 requires product-wise details including: the name of the food product, FSSAI food category code, quantity manufactured during the year (in MT, KL, or relevant unit), quantity imported (for importers), quantity sold during the year, and closing stock. The form also captures information about any product recalls during the year. The data must be broken down by food product category as per FSSAI's food categorisation system ,  making internal record-keeping in an organised, category-wise format essential throughout the year. 4. We are a small-scale manufacturer of organic jams and fruit preserves with a State License. Our accountant says we don't need to file FSSAI returns. Is this correct? Your accountant is incorrect. Any food manufacturer holding a State License or Central License ,  regardless of the size of the business or the nature of the product ,  is required to file Form D-1 Annual Return by May 31. The exemption from return filing applies only to Basic Registration holders and to non-manufacturing license holders (pure traders, retailers, transporters). As a licensed manufacturer, you must file the Annual Return every year. 5. Can FSSAI returns be revised or corrected after submission? The FoSCoS portal does not currently provide a straightforward revision mechanism for submitted FSSAI returns ,  once submitted, the return is treated as final. If you have made a material error (e.g., wrong quantity figures, wrong product category), you should immediately notify the licensing authority in writing with the correct data and seek guidance on the correction process. In practice, authorities have some discretion to accept corrigendum submissions in cases of bona fide errors. This underscores the importance of thorough review before submission ,  rushing to file at the last minute increases the risk of errors that cannot be easily corrected.
