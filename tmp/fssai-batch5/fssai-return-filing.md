# FSSAI return filing (Form D-1, Form D-2, FoSCoS)

## Overview

FSSAI return filing is a periodic statutory duty for certain food business operators under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006, and the Food Safety and Standards (Licensing and Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011. The returns that most licensed manufacturers and importers see are:

- Form D-1, annual: for every State or Central **manufacturer** and for **importers** on the correct licence, for the year April 1 to March 31, with a filing deadline in the next May, usually 31 May (use the FSSAI / FoSCoS notice in force for your year).
- Form D-2, half-yearly: for **milk and milk product** manufacturers, with two parts of the year and deadlines in the October and April window (again, confirm the live calendar on FoSCoS).

The authority uses returns to see volumes, product mix, and compliance. Non-filing or wrong filing can mean penalties, renewal holds, and inspection risk. Many SMEs only discover returns at renewal, enforcement is stricter, so the return should be in your annual compliance stack.

## Key features

| Return | Who (typical) | Period / deadline (confirm live) |
|--------|----------------|-----------------------------------|
| D-1 annual | State or Central **manufacturers** and **importers**; not Basic-only PFBO, not pure trade without own manufacture in the D-1 sense | Financial year, file by following May, often 31 May |
| D-2 half-yearly | Licensed **dairy** manufacturers | H1 about April to September, file by 31 Oct; H2 about October to March, file by 30 Apr |

> [!TIP] Keep product-wise, month-wise production and sales books so the return matches GST, stock, and FSSAI licence categories before you press submit, amendment on FoSCoS is not a simple one-click.

## Applicability

D-1 is mandatory in principle for licensed (State or Central) food **manufacturing** and for **importers** as the regulations apply. It is not in scope for Basic Registration; pure traders, retailers, and distributors with no in-house **manufacturing**; and storage, transport, and most restaurants and caterers that do not **manufacture**, unless a special rule applies. If you add in-house packaged product under your licence, the D-1 **manufacturing** obligation can attach.

**D-2** is for **milk and milk product** units as defined in the return rules.

**Pan-India** for every state and union territory where a covered licence is issued.

**Where to file** depends on the licence: State licence with the state authority path on FoSCoS; Central with the central path, not the geography of your sales per se.

## Benefits

- Stays on the right side of FSS Act penalties, often quoted in the 1 to 5 lakh range for some breaches.
- Smoother renewal: pending returns are a common hold on the file.
- Verifiable record for government, export, and B2B on-boarding.
- Aligns with GST and income-tax story when numbers are one consistent set.
- Due diligence: buyers like a clean D-1 history.
- Exporters: APEDA, MPEDA, and global buyers may expect FSSAI track record, returns support that.

## Limitations

- Traders and restaurants often think they need D-1, they do not, **unless** they manufacture, confusion causes wrong filings or false confidence.
- Small plants may lack MRP, batch, and category-wise data, the return is harder than it looks.
- FoSCoS does not make late correction easy; re-file or letter to the authority, case by case, so the first file must be right.
- Penalty for missing is large compared to the work of one annual compile.
- Portal stress on deadline day is real, file early in May.
- Tier 2 and 3 under-reporting is common, and enforcement is rising.

## Process

1. **Scope:** For your licence, decide D-1, D-2, or both (dairy and general manufacture in one business).
2. **Data:** Pull production, import, sell, and closing stock, by FSSAI product class and your licence list.
3. **Match:** Reconcile to GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, and financials, fix gaps before the authority sees them.
4. **FoSCoS:** Log in, open Annual or Half-yearly return, licence details usually auto-populate.
5. **Form:** Enter each line as FoSCoS asks, quantity in MT, KL, units, or the unit in the return for the year.
6. **Check:** No blank mandatory cell, product names match the licence.
7. **Submit:** Before the hard date, print or save the acknowledgement and reference.
8. **Store:** Keep workpapers and the filed return, many teams keep 5 years for tax and M&A, FSSAI inspection often asks 2+ years in practice.

## Validity and use of filed returns

Filed returns are a permanent record of that year; they are most used at renewal, FSO visit, and M&A. Keep them with your FSSAI dataroom. At renewal, open returns can block the next licence, file catch-up first.

## Frequently asked questions

**State licence restaurant, no factory pack. D-1?** No, if you only cook and serve and do not manufacture a packaged product under the licence, D-1 is for manufacture and importers, not a pure restaurant, if you add packed sauces or snacks under license, the manufacturing prong is in play.

**We manufacture in one state and sell in India, where we file?** The manufacturer files where the **licence** is: state licensing office path for a state licence, central for a central. Sales geography is not the trigger for the office, the licence type is.

**What is in D-1?** Product, FSSAI category, quantity made, import if any, sold, and stock, often in MT or KL, plus recall line if the form in your year has it, follow FoSCoS help text.

**Small organic jam unit, state licence. Accountant said no D-1.** FSSAI usually expects D-1 for licensed **manufacturing**, size is not a general exemption, unless you are only Basic, check your class.

**Change after file?** No easy re-open on FoSCoS; write the authority, attach evidence, in genuine error cases. Better to get it right before the clock runs in May.
