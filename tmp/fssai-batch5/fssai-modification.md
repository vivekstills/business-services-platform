# FSSAI modification (FoSCoS)

## Overview

FSSAI Modification updates an existing FSSAI Registration or License when the business changes, for example premises, product list, legal name, partners or directors, capacity, new unit, or activity type. Food business operators must keep the certificate true. Working on an old certificate after a move, a bigger range of products, or a new owner is non-compliance and can lead to cancellation and penalties.

Modification is filed on FoSCoS with evidence. Some changes are paper-based and quick; others need a Food Safety Officer visit. Good modification discipline helps with audits, marketplace checks, and investor or franchise due diligence.

## Key features

- One portal (FoSCoS) for Basic, State, and Central paths, with state and central officers as the rules set.
- Types of change: name, address, categories, capacity, partners, signatory, upgrade from Basic to State or State to Central, and more.
- Minor changes may avoid inspection; premises, capacity, or new units often trigger a site visit.
- Modified certificate keeps the same expiry date as the original in a simple modification, until you renew. A full upgrade can start a new validity window (see FoSCoS for the case you file).

> [!TIP] If the business is near renewal, your consultant may file modification and renewal together to avoid conflicting data in the FSSAI database.

## Applicability

Modification applies to any current FSSAI Basic, State, or Central holder when there is a change such as:

- Legal or trade name (map whether the change is only brand v. legal entity with your CA and FoSCoS).
- Address of manufacturing, warehouse, or retail.
- Add or remove food product categories.
- Change in proprietor, partners, or directors.
- Installed capacity or new manufacturing block.
- New manufacturing premises.
- Nature of business (for example retail to manufacturing).
- Contact, email, mobile, or authorised signatory.
- Upgrade of tier: Basic to State, State to Central.

All India: state-level files are with the State Food Safety Department; Central License with FSSAI Central Licensing Authority. FoSCoS is the common application front.

## Benefits

- Keeps the licence legally aligned with how you actually operate.
- Inspections and marketplace checks match the certificate, so fewer mismatch notices.
- You can add categories in a controlled way instead of informal production.
- Buyers and investors see an up-to-date FSSAI record.
- Timely address change can avoid the need for a separate fresh licence when one modification will do.
- Renewal is easier when the record is already correct.

## Limitations

- Premises and plant changes often mean inspection and longer timelines.
- Non-inspection paths are still often up to 30 days; inspection cases can run 30 to 60 days or more.
- FSSAI does not backdate; if you moved last year and only modify now, the gap can be a defect on the file.
- Big capacity steps can need a Central file instead of a small State amendment.
- Adding high-risk categories can force a higher licence class, not only a line add.
- Many changes in one form can slow review and increase query risk.

## Process

1. List what changed and whether FoSCoS treats it as minor, major, or upgrade.
2. Log in to FoSCoS and open Modification against the current FSSAI number.
3. Complete the modification form with accurate new data; wrong fields cause rejection or delay.
4. Upload supporting PDFs or images in the size and format FoSCoS asks for.
5. Pay the modification fee for your category and type of change.
6. Wait for review; attend inspection if scheduled and close observations.
7. Download the modified certificate and update packs, boards, marketplaces, and contracts with the new detail.
8. If the change is only part of a growth path, plan renewal and any upgrade in the same timeline.

## Validity and renewal

A simple modification does not by itself reset the end date. You renew on the same cycle unless you have upgraded the licence, in which case the new class may have a new validity. Prefer to clear modifications before a messy renewal, or file modification and renewal together with professional help so the database stays consistent.

## Frequently asked questions

**Only the brand name changed, not the company.** If the legal entity is unchanged, FSSAI may not need a full modification for a pure brand refresh. If the legal name on MCA and COI changed, you usually modify FSSAI with the new name and support papers.

**New category, for example we added dairy to bakery.** You need a modification to add the category, and you must not make the new line until the modified licence allows it, unless a temporary bridge is in law for your case (rare; treat as not allowed until cleared). The officer may visit.

**We moved within the same city.** File modification for address, expect inspection, and do not run the new site on the old address on the certificate.

**How long?** Many document-only files finish in about 30 days; inspection cases often need more. Track status on FoSCoS. Deemed rules may exist for some non-inspection cases if the authority is silent, subject to the FSSAI notice in your year.

**We want to add manufacturing on Basic.** Only if the full business still fits Basic and the new activity is not a restricted line. If you move into a high-risk or import path, you may need a new State or Central file instead of a light modification.
