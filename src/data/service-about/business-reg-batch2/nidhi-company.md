# Nidhi Company Registration

## Overview

A Nidhi Company is a type of Non-Banking Financial Company (NBFC) recognized under Section 406 of the Companies Act, 2013. It is formed for the purpose of borrowing and lending money among its members (known as "mutual benefit society"). Nidhi Companies promote thrift and savings habit among members and are governed by the Nidhi Rules, 2014. They do not require an RBI license but must comply with MCA regulations.

## Features

A Nidhi Company operates only with its members — deposits and loans are exclusively member-to-member. It requires a minimum of 7 shareholders and 3 directors at the time of incorporation. Minimum paid-up equity share capital of Rs. 10 lakh is mandatory. Within 120 days of incorporation, it must have at least 200 members, Rs. 20 lakh Net Owned Funds, and file Form NDH-1.

## Advantages

Nidhi Companies offer a simple structure for mutual savings and lending without needing RBI registration. They promote financial inclusion in rural and semi-urban areas. Interest rates for loans are typically lower than banks for members. Limited liability, separate legal entity, and perpetual succession are assured. Operational costs are lower than NBFCs.

## Limitations

Activities are restricted to members only — no business with non-members is permitted. They cannot deal in chit funds, hire purchase, insurance, or acquisition of securities. Advertising for deposits is prohibited. Branch opening is restricted. Interest rates on deposits are capped. Compliance is strict, and non-compliance can lead to license cancellation.

## Applicability

Nidhi Companies are popular in South India, especially Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, and Telangana. They are also present in Maharashtra, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, and other states. Nidhi concept does not apply to Jammu & Kashmir. They operate at local and district levels to promote thrift among specific communities.

## Documents

- **PAN of directors & shareholders** — Mandatory
- **Aadhaar / Voter ID / Passport** — ID proof
- **Address proof (bank statement / utility bill)** — Recent (2 months)
- **Photographs** — Passport-size
- **Registered office proof** — Rent agreement + NOC + utility bill
- **DSC of directors** — Class 3
- **DIN of directors** — Through SPICe+
- **MOA and AOA** — With "Nidhi" in objects
- **Declaration by promoters** — In INC-9

## Process

Obtain DSCs for all directors. Apply for name approval (name must end with "Nidhi Limited"). File SPICe+ with MOA, AOA, and declarations. Upon incorporation, receive COI, PAN, TAN. Within 120 days, achieve minimum criteria (200 members, Rs. 20 lakh NOF, 10% unencumbered deposits). File Form NDH-1 within 90 days of the end of the first financial year. Thereafter, file Form NDH-3 half-yearly.

## Timeline

Nidhi Company incorporation takes 15–20 working days. DSC: 1-2 days. Name approval: 2-3 days. SPICe+ approval: 7-10 days. Post-incorporation compliance (NDH-1) must be met within 120 days and filed within 90 days after the end of the first FY.

## FAQs

**Q1. Does a Nidhi Company require RBI approval?** No, Nidhi Companies are specifically exempted from RBI regulation as they deal only with their members. However, they are notified under Section 406 of the Companies Act.

**Q2. Can a Nidhi Company open branches anywhere?** A Nidhi Company can open up to 3 branches within the district only after earning profit for 3 consecutive financial years. Additional branches require Regional Director's approval.

**Q3. What is Net Owned Fund (NOF)?** NOF is the paid-up equity share capital plus free reserves minus accumulated losses and intangible assets. A minimum NOF of Rs. 20 lakh is mandatory within 120 days of incorporation.

**Q4. Can a Nidhi Company accept deposits from non-members?** No, a Nidhi Company can only accept deposits from its members and lend only to its members. Dealing with non-members is strictly prohibited.

**Q5. What is the interest rate limit for Nidhi deposits and loans?** The maximum interest rate on deposits cannot exceed the rate prescribed by RBI for NBFCs. Loan interest cannot exceed 7.5% above the highest deposit rate offered.
