# Section 8 Company Registration

## Overview

A Section 8 Company is a company incorporated under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013 for promoting charitable objects such as commerce, art, science, sports, education, research, social welfare, religion, charity, or protection of the environment. It operates as a non-profit entity — profits, if any, are applied towards promoting its objectives and cannot be distributed as dividends to members. It enjoys the credibility of a corporate structure combined with the purpose of a charitable organization.

## Features

Section 8 companies have no minimum capital requirement. Minimum 2 members and 2 directors (for private) or 7 members and 3 directors (for public) are required. The name can be without "Limited" or "Private Limited" suffix. Central Government license is mandatory before incorporation. Profits must be used for charitable purposes only. It enjoys higher credibility than trusts or societies.

## Advantages

Section 8 companies offer higher credibility and public trust compared to trusts and societies. Eligible for 12A and 80G tax exemptions under the Income Tax Act. Can receive CSR funds from corporates and foreign donations (with FCRA registration). Enjoys stamp duty exemptions in some states. Has a corporate structure for better governance and transparency. No minimum capital requirement.

## Limitations

Cannot distribute profits or pay dividends. Strict compliance with MCA, Income Tax, and possibly FCRA (for foreign donations). Alteration of MOA requires Central Government approval. Section 8 license can be revoked for misuse. Conversion into a regular company requires stringent approvals. Members do not receive any personal financial benefit from the company.

## Applicability

Section 8 companies can be registered across all Indian states and UTs. Major concentrations are in Maharashtra, Delhi, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Telangana, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, and Rajasthan. Registration is centralized through the MCA portal.

## Documents

- **PAN of all directors & subscribers** — Mandatory
- **Aadhaar of Indian directors** — ID proof
- **Passport (for foreigners)** — Apostilled
- **Address proof** — Recent utility bill
- **Photographs** — Passport-size
- **Registered office proof** — Utility bill + NOC + rent agreement
- **DSC of all directors** — Class 3
- **DIN for directors** — Through SPICe+
- **MOA (Form INC-13)** — Specific for Section 8
- **AOA** — Drafted
- **Declaration (INC-14 by CA/CS/CWA)** — Signed
- **Declaration (INC-15 by subscribers)** — Signed
- **Projected statements (3 years)** — Income-expenditure
- **Grounds for application** — Detailed note

## Process

Obtain DSC for all directors. Apply for name approval via SPICe+ Part A (name should reflect charitable purpose). File SPICe+ Part B along with MOA (INC-13), AOA, INC-14 (declaration by professional), INC-15 (declaration by subscribers), projected income-expenditure statement for 3 years, and grounds for application. The Registrar grants the Section 8 license and Certificate of Incorporation together. Apply for PAN, TAN, and subsequently 12A/80G registration.

## Timeline

Section 8 company registration takes 15–25 working days. DSC: 1-2 days. Name approval: 2-3 days. SPICe+ filing and license issuance: 10-15 days. 12A/80G application post-incorporation: 1-3 months. FCRA registration (if required): 3-6 months.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can a Section 8 company distribute profits?** No, Section 8 companies are strictly prohibited from distributing profits or paying dividends. All income must be used to promote the company's charitable objectives.

**Q2. Is 12A/80G registration mandatory for Section 8 companies?** While not mandatory for incorporation, 12A registration is essential for income tax exemption, and 80G registration is essential for donors to claim tax deductions. Both are highly recommended.

**Q3. Can foreign directors be appointed in a Section 8 company?** Yes, foreign nationals can be directors subject to obtaining DIN. However, at least one director must be an Indian resident. FCRA registration is required to accept foreign donations.

**Q4. Can a Section 8 company be converted into a regular company?** Yes, subject to strict conditions under Section 8(4)(ii) — including surrender of accumulated profits to another Section 8 entity, Central Government approval, and compliance with procedural requirements.

**Q5. How is a Section 8 company different from a trust?** A Section 8 company has higher credibility, structured corporate governance, and centralized MCA regulation, while a trust is regulated at the state level with less compliance but lower institutional credibility.
