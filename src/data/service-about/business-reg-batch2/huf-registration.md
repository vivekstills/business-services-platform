# HUF Registration

## Overview

A Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) is a unique legal entity recognized under Hindu law and the Income Tax Act, 1961. It consists of all persons lineally descended from a common ancestor, including wives and unmarried daughters. HUF is primarily used as a tax-saving vehicle, allowing families to enjoy a separate PAN, separate tax slab, and separate deductions under Section 80C, thereby reducing the overall family tax burden.

## Features

An HUF is created automatically upon marriage of a Hindu male, but for tax recognition, it needs a formal deed and PAN. It is managed by the eldest male member called the Karta, while other members are called coparceners. The HUF has a separate legal identity for taxation purposes. It continues in perpetuity and is not dissolved by the death of the Karta. Buddhists, Sikhs, and Jains are also eligible to form HUFs.

## Advantages

HUF enjoys a separate basic exemption limit of Rs. 2.5 lakh, separate Section 80C benefits of Rs. 1.5 lakh, and independent taxation of its income. Gifts received by the HUF from relatives are tax-free, and the family can split income across members to optimize tax. HUF is also useful for holding ancestral property and managing joint family businesses.

## Limitations

Partition of an HUF is complex and requires consent of all coparceners. Daughters, after the 2005 amendment, are equal coparceners, which can complicate succession. HUF income must be derived from joint family assets, not individual earnings. Once formed, HUF cannot be easily dissolved and attracts capital gains tax on partition. Non-Hindu families cannot form HUFs.

## Applicability

HUF is recognized throughout India — Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Odisha, and all Union Territories. However, Kerala abolished the Joint Hindu Family system via the Kerala Joint Hindu Family System (Abolition) Act, 1975, and no new HUFs can be formed there.

## Documents

- **HUF Deed** — Declaration of formation
- **PAN of Karta** — Identity of Karta
- **Aadhaar of Karta** — Address and ID proof
- **Address proof (utility bill / rent agreement)** — Registered address
- **Bank account details** — For HUF bank account
- **List of coparceners** — Members identification
- **Affidavit on stamp paper** — Legal declaration
- **Photographs of Karta and members** — Verification

## Process

Draft the HUF deed on stamp paper declaring the existence of the HUF, its name, Karta, and coparceners. Notarize the deed. Apply for a separate PAN for the HUF in Form 49A, mentioning the Karta as the authorized signatory. Open a bank account in the name of the HUF. Commence operations and file annual income tax returns in Form ITR-2.

## Timeline

The HUF deed can be drafted and notarized within 1–2 days. PAN issuance takes 7–15 working days. Bank account opening takes 3–7 days. The entire HUF setup is generally completed within 2–3 weeks.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can a female be the Karta of an HUF?** Yes, after the 2016 Delhi High Court ruling and the 2005 Hindu Succession Amendment, the eldest female coparcener can become the Karta of an HUF.

**Q2. Can a single person form an HUF?** No, an HUF requires at least two members — a Karta and at least one coparcener. However, an HUF can continue with a single member after partition until another member is added.

**Q3. Can an HUF own property and run a business?** Yes, an HUF can own movable and immovable property, operate a business, invest in securities, earn rental or interest income, and hold a separate bank account and demat account.

**Q4. Can NRIs form an HUF?** Yes, NRIs can form or be part of an HUF. The HUF's residential status depends on where the Karta controls and manages its affairs.

**Q5. How is HUF income taxed?** HUF income is taxed as a separate entity at individual tax slab rates, with a basic exemption of Rs. 2.5 lakh and benefits under Sections 80C, 80D, and others.
