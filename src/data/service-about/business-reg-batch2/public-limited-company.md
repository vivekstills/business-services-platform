# Public Limited Company Registration

## Overview

A Public Limited Company is a company that can raise capital from the public by issuing shares, which may be listed on a stock exchange. Governed by the Companies Act, 2013, public limited companies require higher minimum members, directors, and have stricter compliance norms. They are suited for large-scale businesses seeking public investment, listing on stock exchanges like BSE or NSE, or broader ownership.

## Features

Minimum 7 shareholders and 3 directors are required, with at least one resident director. There is no maximum limit on shareholders. The name ends with "Limited" (not "Private Limited"). Shares are freely transferable. A public limited company can issue a prospectus to the public, undertake IPOs, and list on stock exchanges. It must hold statutory, AGM, and board meetings regularly.

## Advantages

Public limited companies can raise massive capital through public offerings. Shares are freely transferable, enhancing liquidity. The company enjoys enhanced credibility, institutional trust, and brand recognition. Listing provides exit opportunities for early investors. Expansion and M&A activities are easier. Limited liability protection for shareholders remains intact.

## Limitations

Compliance is very high — including SEBI LODR (if listed), secretarial audit, independent directors, audit committee, CSR (if thresholds are met), quarterly disclosures, and corporate governance reports. Formation costs are higher. Takeover risks exist as shares are freely transferable. Detailed public disclosures are mandatory. Decision-making is slower due to larger boards and shareholder approvals.

## Applicability

Public limited company registration is available pan-India. Listed and unlisted public companies operate across Maharashtra (Mumbai being the financial capital), Karnataka, Delhi NCR, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, and other states. Listed entities also comply with BSE/NSE and SEBI regulations.

## Documents

- **PAN of all directors & subscribers** — Mandatory
- **Aadhaar / Passport** — ID proof
- **Address proof** — Recent utility bill or bank statement
- **Passport-size photographs** — All
- **Registered office proof** — Utility bill + NOC + rent agreement
- **DSC of all directors** — Class 3
- **DIN for directors** — Through SPICe+
- **MOA & AOA** — Drafted
- **INC-9 declaration** — Signed
- **Consent of directors (DIR-2)** — Signed

## Process

Obtain DSC for all proposed directors. Apply for name approval via SPICe+ Part A. File SPICe+ Part B with MOA, AOA, INC-9, DIR-2, AGILE-PRO, and other declarations. Once incorporated, obtain Certificate of Commencement of Business (INC-20A) before commencing operations. Comply with regulatory formalities including appointing auditors, holding statutory meeting, and optionally preparing for IPO.

## Timeline

Public limited company registration typically takes 15–25 working days. DSC: 1-2 days. Name approval: 2-3 days. SPICe+ filing and incorporation: 10-15 days. Commencement of Business Certificate (INC-20A): within 180 days of incorporation. Listing (if planned) takes several months of additional SEBI process.

## FAQs

**Q1. What is the minimum paid-up capital for a public limited company?** Under the Companies (Amendment) Act, 2015, the minimum paid-up capital requirement was removed. A public limited company can now be incorporated with any paid-up capital.

**Q2. Is INC-20A required for public limited companies?** Yes, every company (both public and private) must file INC-20A (Declaration for Commencement of Business) within 180 days of incorporation. Failure attracts Rs. 50,000 penalty on the company.

**Q3. Is a public limited company always listed?** No, public limited companies can be listed or unlisted. Listing on a stock exchange is optional and subject to SEBI listing requirements and eligibility.

**Q4. What is the role of independent directors?** Listed public companies must have at least 1/3rd of the board as independent directors. Specified unlisted public companies (with paid-up capital ≥ Rs. 10 crore, turnover ≥ Rs. 100 crore, or loans ≥ Rs. 50 crore) must have at least 2 independent directors.

**Q5. Can a public limited company be converted back to private?** Yes, through a special resolution, NCLT approval, and compliance with Section 14 of the Companies Act, 2013. The conversion triggers changes in the MOA, AOA, and name.
