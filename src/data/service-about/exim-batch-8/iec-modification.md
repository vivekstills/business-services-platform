# IEC Modification

## Overview

IEC Modification is the process of updating or amending the details of an existing Import Export Code (IEC) registered on the DGFT (Directorate General of Foreign Trade) portal. The IEC is the fundamental authorisation for conducting import and export activities in India — issued once and valid for the lifetime of the entity. However, businesses undergo changes over time — change of address, change in bank account, addition of new partners or directors, change in business name, addition of export/import branches — all of which must be reflected in the IEC by filing a modification application with DGFT.

IEC Modification became particularly important after DGFT's migration to the new, Aadhaar and PAN-integrated IEC system in April 2021. Under the updated framework:

IEC details are now directly linked with PAN data from the Income Tax database and GST data from the GSTN portal — any mismatch between IEC records and PAN/GST data triggers alerts and can block customs transactions

All IEC holders must mandatorily update (confirm or modify) their IEC details every year between April 1 and June 30 — the Annual IEC Updation — failing which the IEC is deactivated

IEC Modification is required for both material changes (where actual business information has changed) and for the mandatory annual update (where details are confirmed unchanged). Both are processed online through the DGFT portal at dgft.gov.in, are free of cost, and are processed within a few working days.

Outdated or incorrect IEC details create direct operational problems — customs port authorities cross-check IEC details for every consignment, and mismatches between the IEC record and the shipping documents cause clearance delays, Bill of Entry rejections, and in some cases, consignment holds at the port.

## Applicability

IEC Modification is applicable to:

- All IEC holders — for the mandatory Annual IEC Updation (April 1 to June 30 every year)
- IEC holders with changed registered address — whenever the principal place of business shifts
- IEC holders with changed bank account details — whenever the bank account linked to the IEC changes
- Companies with changed directors — addition or removal of directors due to resignation, appointment, or retirement
- Partnerships where partners have changed — induction or exit of partners
- IEC holders with changed business name — after a legal name change of the entity
- IEC holders adding new branch offices — for exporters/importers adding new export/import branches or warehouses
- IEC holders whose GST details have changed — new GSTIN, GSTIN cancellation, or GSTIN amendment
- Proprietorships where the proprietor has changed — succession, marriage name change, or legal change

Annual IEC Update (mandatory for all): Every IEC holder — regardless of whether any details have changed — must log in to the DGFT portal between April 1 and June 30 and either confirm that all details are current (no change update) or make the required modifications. Failure to complete this Annual Update results in automatic deactivation of the IEC. A deactivated IEC blocks all customs clearances and DGFT scheme applications.

Applicable pan-India — IEC and IEC Modification are central (DGFT) processes applicable uniformly across all states and UTs.

## Benefits

- Operational continuity: Updated IEC details prevent customs clearance rejections caused by document mismatches — particularly for address, bank account, and director/partner name fields that are routinely checked during port operations
- Annual reactivation: The Annual IEC Update (even if no changes are needed) prevents the IEC from being automatically deactivated — maintaining uninterrupted import/export capability
- GSTN alignment: IEC details must align with GSTIN records — modification ensures consistency between DGFT, Customs, and GST databases, preventing cross-system reconciliation issues
- Scheme benefit continuity: DGFT schemes (RoDTEP, Advance Authorisation, EPCG) require an active, accurate IEC — modifications maintain eligibility for these export promotion benefits
- Banking compliance: Banks process export/import transactions against the IEC — bank account changes must be reflected to prevent FEMA compliance issues in export proceeds realization
- RCMC alignment: RCMC (Registration-cum-Membership Certificate) with EPCs is linked to IEC details — IEC modifications may trigger corresponding RCMC updates for continued scheme eligibility
- Legal accuracy: The IEC is a legal document issued by the Government of India — inaccurate information creates regulatory risk, particularly in FEMA compliance and customs valuation contexts

## Limitations

- Annual update mandatory regardless of changes: Even if nothing has changed, the annual April–June update is mandatory — failure to complete it deactivates the IEC regardless of the entity's compliance history
- Deactivation if annual update is missed: An IEC deactivated due to missed annual update cannot be used for any import/export transaction — reactivation requires completing the delayed update and a waiting period
- PAN-linked constraints: Since IEC is now PAN-linked, any mismatch between the entity's PAN data and IEC data requires PAN correction first — DGFT cannot override PAN database records
- Director/partner changes require DSC of exiting/incoming persons: Modifications involving change of signatories require DSC of the new authorised signatory — which takes time to procure if the new director/partner doesn't have one
- No modification during dispute: If the IEC is under suspension or adjudication for a DGFT/customs violation, modifications may be restricted until the dispute is resolved
- ICEGATE update required separately: Modifying IEC on the DGFT portal does not automatically update ICEGATE — the importer/exporter must also update their ICEGATE profile separately, particularly for bank account changes linked to ICEPAY

## Process

1. Step 1 — Log In to DGFT Portal Go to dgft.gov.in. Log in using your IEC credentials (IEC number, registered email, and password). If it is the annual update period (April 1 to June 30), a mandatory update prompt will appear on the dashboard.
2. Step 2 — Navigate to IEC Modification Go to Services → IEC Profile Management → Update/Modify IEC Details. The current IEC details are displayed pre-populated from the DGFT database.
3. Step 3 — Identify Fields to Modify Review all fields — entity details, address, bank account, authorised signatory, directors/partners, and GSTIN. Identify all fields that need to be updated. For Annual Update with no changes: simply confirm all details are current.
4. Step 4 — Make Required Changes Update the relevant fields — address, bank account number and IFSC code, director/partner details, GSTIN, etc. The portal validates each field against PAN, GST, and other government databases in real time.
5. Step 5 — Upload Supporting Documents Upload the supporting documents for each changed field — address proof for address change, new bank certificate for bank account change, ROC documents for name change, etc. Documents must be in PDF format and within the specified file size limit.
6. Step 6 — Aadhaar Authentication / DSC Signing The modification application must be authenticated by the authorised signatory using either Aadhaar OTP (for proprietors and individuals) or DSC (for companies and LLPs). Sign the application digitally.
7. Step 7 — Submit the Modification Application Submit the completed modification application on the DGFT portal. An Application Reference Number (ARN) is generated immediately upon submission.
8. Step 8 — DGFT Processing and Approval DGFT processes the modification. For most standard modifications, approval is granted within 1–3 working days on a deemed basis (if PAN/GST validation is successful). For modifications requiring physical document verification or where there is a data mismatch, the processing may take longer.
9. Step 9 — Download Updated IEC Certificate Upon approval, the updated IEC certificate with the new details is available for download from the DGFT portal. The 10-digit IEC number remains the same — only the details change.
10. Step 10 — Update ICEGATE and Other Portals After IEC modification, update the new details on ICEGATE, RCMC with relevant EPCs, and any other portals where IEC details are registered.

## Use Cases

1. Company Relocated Its Office A Pune-based pharmaceutical exporter shifts its registered office from Aundh to Wakad. The IEC still shows the old Aundh address. Before the next export shipment, the company must modify the IEC to reflect the new address — as customs filings reference the IEC address, and a mismatch between the Shipping Bill and IEC record can cause clearance queries at the port.
2. Annual IEC Update (No Changes) A Delhi-based importer of electronic components has not changed any business details since last year. Between April 1 and June 30, they must log in to the DGFT portal and confirm all IEC details are unchanged — completing the mandatory Annual IEC Update. Missing this window results in IEC deactivation, which would block their imports at customs until the update is completed and the IEC is reactivated.
3. Director Change After Funding Round A Bengaluru-based tech startup that imports server hardware received Series A funding and onboarded two new investor-nominated directors, while one founding director exited. The IEC modification is required to update the director list — adding two new directors and removing the exiting director — along with the DSC of the new authorized signatory for future IEC-related filings.
4. Bank Account Change A Chennai-based seafood exporter changed their primary banking relationship from Canara Bank to HDFC Bank for better trade finance facilities. The IEC must be modified to reflect the new HDFC Bank account — as export proceeds realized in the HDFC account are reconciled against the IEC-linked bank account for FEMA compliance and DGFT scheme purposes.
5. Addition of New Export Branch A Surat-based textile exporter that primarily exports from Mumbai port opens a new manufacturing and export unit in Silvassa with its own dispatch and shipping operations. The IEC modification adds the Silvassa unit as an additional branch under the same IEC — enabling customs filings from Silvassa to reference the same IEC while correctly identifying the Silvassa facility as the place of export.

## Frequently asked questions

- **My company's name changed after RoC approval. Do I need a new IEC or just an IEC modification?** You need an IEC modification — not a new IEC. The IEC number (10 digits, linked to the company's PAN) remains the same even after a company name change. The modification updates the entity name on the IEC to reflect the new legal name as per the updated Certificate of Incorporation. Submit the IEC modification application on the DGFT portal with the MCA-issued Certificate of Incorporation showing the new name, the ROC approval order for the name change, and the company's updated PAN card (if the name on PAN has also been updated). Operating under the old IEC name after a legal name change creates compliance risk in customs and FEMA contexts.
- **We missed the Annual IEC Update window (April 1 to June 30). Our IEC has been deactivated. What do we do?** An IEC deactivated due to a missed annual update can be reactivated by completing the overdue Annual IEC Update — even after the June 30 deadline. Log in to the DGFT portal and complete the update (confirming or modifying details as applicable). Upon submission, the IEC is typically reactivated within 1–3 working days. There is no penalty fee for late annual update — but the period of deactivation means all imports and exports during that period were technically not covered by a valid IEC. Ensure any shipments during the deactivation period are documented carefully. Going forward, set a calendar reminder for April 1 each year.
- **Our proprietor got married and her name has legally changed. Does the IEC need to be modified?** Yes. Since the IEC for a proprietorship is issued in the name of the proprietor (for proprietorships) or the entity (for companies/firms), a legal name change of the proprietor must be reflected in the IEC. Submit a modification application with the name change proof — typically a gazette notification of name change, updated Aadhaar in the new name, and updated PAN in the new name (if already updated). All three identity documents should reflect the same name for the modification to be processed smoothly. The IEC number remains the same — only the proprietor name field is updated.
- **We want to add a new bank account to our IEC without removing the existing one. Is that possible?** The DGFT IEC system currently allows only one primary bank account to be linked to the IEC at any time — not multiple simultaneous bank accounts. If you want to use a new bank account for a specific transaction (e.g., export proceeds realization), you would need to modify the IEC to update the bank account to the new one. Export proceeds must be realized in the bank account linked to the IEC. If you anticipate switching between accounts frequently, discuss with your banker — some situations (e.g., different currencies in different accounts) may require specific advice on FEMA compliance alongside the IEC bank account registration.
- **We are merging with another company. The merged entity will use our IEC. How does IEC modification work in an M&A scenario?** Merger and amalgamation scenarios are among the most complex IEC situations. If your company (the surviving entity) is absorbing the other company, your existing IEC continues — but the IEC must be modified to reflect any changes in the surviving company's details post-merger (director changes, address changes, etc.). The absorbed company's IEC must be surrendered/cancelled separately. If the other company's IEC has more valuable export history (for scheme eligibility or licensing purposes), discuss with a DGFT consultant whether there is any mechanism to transfer scheme history — IEC numbers themselves are not transferable, but export turnover data may be consolidated for certain purposes. Post-merger, coordinate with DGFT, ICEGATE, EPCs (for RCMC), and the AD bank to ensure all systems reflect the post-merger entity structure.
