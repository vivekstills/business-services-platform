# One Year & Lifelong Permission for Foreign Liquor

## Detailed Overview

The One Year and Lifelong Personal Permission for Foreign Liquor (often called "FL-XII" in Maharashtra or similar codes in other states) is an individual-level liquor possession and consumption permit granted by state excise departments authorising a person to legally possess and consume IMFL within prescribed limits. This is a personal license for the individual — not a commercial license — and does not authorise commercial sale or service.

The license is most relevant in states like Maharashtra, where the Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 still technically requires individuals to have a permit to consume alcohol. While enforcement varies, the license provides legal protection for the holder against possession and consumption charges.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Legal authorisation for personal liquor possession
- Avoids penalties under prohibition acts (in states like Maharashtra)
- Permits possession within home for personal consumption
- Lifelong permission is one-time and never needs renewal
- Enables purchase from authorised retailers without legal questions

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Personal use only — cannot be used for commercial purposes
- Possession limits apply (typically 9 litres of IMFL or equivalent at home)
- License must be carried/produced when purchasing from retailers in some states
- Lifelong permission has higher one-time fee
- Not transferable

In most states (Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Delhi, etc.), personal consumption doesn't require an individual permit — the law allows possession within reasonable limits without specific permission.

## Features

- Individual permit (not entity-based)
- Personal consumption only
- One-year or lifelong validity options
- State-specific quantity limits

## Documents Required

- Application form — State excise form
- Identity proof — PAN, Aadhaar
- Address proof — Residence proof
- Photographs — 2–3 passport size
- Age proof — Confirming above legal drinking age
- Income certificate — Some states require this
- Affidavit — Stating personal use only

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Apply online or at Collector/Excise office
2. Step 2 — Submit documents and pay fees
3. Step 3 — Verification by excise officer
4. Step 4 — Permit issued (typically within 7–30 days)

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Is the FL-XII permit mandatory in Maharashtra to drink alcohol?** Technically yes under the Bombay Prohibition Act, but enforcement is selective. For legal protection during possession, transport, or any law enforcement encounter, holding a permit is advisable.
2. **Can I drink at a bar without an individual permit?** At licensed bars/restaurants, the establishment's license covers your consumption. The personal permit primarily applies to home possession and consumption.
3. **What is the home possession limit under this permit?** In Maharashtra, the limit is typically 9 litres of IMFL (12 bottles of 750ml). Beer limit may differ. Exceeding limits attracts excise penalties.
4. **Do tourists visiting Maharashtra need this permit?** Tourists can obtain temporary permits (one-day or one-month) at airports/major hotels in Maharashtra. The license helps avoid issues during stay.
5. **Can I transfer my lifelong permit to a family member?** No. Personal permits are non-transferable. Each individual must obtain their own permit.
