# One Day Permission for Foreign Liquor

## Detailed Overview

A One Day Permission for Foreign Liquor is a temporary license authorising the service of IMFL (Indian Made Foreign Liquor) — including whisky, rum, vodka, gin, brandy, beer, wine — at a specific event for a single day. This permission is the most commonly sought temporary liquor license in India for weddings, corporate events, parties, conferences, and private functions held at venues that don't have their own permanent liquor license.

The permission allows the host or event organiser to legally serve liquor procured from authorised state-licensed wholesalers or retailers at a specified venue for the duration of the event. It is governed by state excise rules and requires advance application typically 15–30 days before the event.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Legal authorisation for liquor service at private functions
- Suitable for weddings, corporate events, conferences, parties
- Quick processing (1–4 weeks)
- Allows full range of IMFL — beer, wine, spirits
- Avoids legal complications of unauthorised liquor service

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Single day validity only
- Quantity restrictions based on guest count
- Source restriction — must procure from authorised licensee
- Heavy penalties for non-compliance
- Cannot resell or transfer unused liquor

## Features

- Single-day validity
- Specific venue and event
- Quantity cap based on attendees
- Source restricted to authorised wholesalers
- Time-restricted (typically 11 AM to 11 PM, extendable on payment)

## Documents Required

- Application form — State-specific
- Event details — Date, time, venue, type of event
- Guest list / count estimate — Expected attendance
- Venue NOC — From property owner
- Local body permission — Municipal NOC
- Police clearance — Security arrangement details
- Applicant ID — PAN, Aadhaar
- Source license details — Of liquor wholesaler/retailer from whom liquor will be procured
- Fire NOC — If venue requires

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Apply 15–30 days in advance with documents and fees
2. Step 2 — Excise officer verifies application
3. Step 3 — Coordinate with police for clearance
4. Step 4 — Pay license fee and obtain permit
5. Step 5 — Procure liquor from authorised source on event day
6. Step 6 — Maintain consumption records and surrender unused stock

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **How far in advance should I apply for a wedding liquor permission?** Apply at least 30 days in advance. Wedding season (Nov-Feb) sees heavy application volumes — early application avoids last-minute rejections.
2. **Can I purchase liquor from outside the state for a one-day permission?** No. Liquor must be procured from authorised licensees within the state. Inter-state procurement requires separate import permits.
3. **What happens to leftover liquor after the event?** Most states require the licensee to either return unopened stock to the source vendor (with refund as per arrangement) or destroy it under excise supervision. Personal retention is not permitted.
4. **Can I serve liquor at my home for a private party under this permission?** Yes, residential premises permission is granted in many states for weddings and family functions. Address and capacity must be properly disclosed.
5. **Is a one-day permission required for serving liquor only to family members?** Most states exempt small private gatherings (under 25–50 persons). Larger family functions or events with non-family guests typically require permission.
