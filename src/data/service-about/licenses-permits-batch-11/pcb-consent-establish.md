# PCB Consent to Establish (CTE)

## Detailed Overview

PCB Consent to Establish (CTE) is a mandatory environmental clearance issued by the State Pollution Control Board (SPCB) under the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. CTE must be obtained before commencing construction or installation of any industrial facility, factory, or commercial establishment that may potentially pollute air, water, or soil.

CTE evaluates the proposed project's environmental impact based on category (Red, Orange, Green, White — categorised by pollution potential) and ensures pollution control measures are designed before construction begins.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Legal authorisation for project establishment
- Pre-construction environmental compliance
- Avoidance of post-construction modification costs
- Eligibility for environmental subsidies and schemes
- Bank loan eligibility (most lenders require CTE)
- Avoids prosecution for unauthorised construction

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Time-consuming approval process
- Extensive environmental documentation required
- Public hearing for certain project categories
- Conditions can be onerous for highly polluting industries
- Periodic compliance reporting

## Applicability Across India

Mandatory for all industries pan-India under SPCB authority of respective state:

- Maharashtra Pollution Control Board (MPCB)
- Karnataka State Pollution Control Board (KSPCB)
- Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board (TNPCB)
- Delhi Pollution Control Committee (DPCC)
- Other state PCBs

Industry categorisation:

- Red category: Highly polluting (chemicals, refineries, steel, cement)
- Orange category: Moderately polluting (food processing, textiles, plastics)
- Green category: Less polluting (assembly, repair)
- White category: Non-polluting (IT, software)

## Features

- Pre-construction approval mandatory
- Category-based applicability
- Project-specific
- Validity until project commissioning (typically 5 years)

## Documents Required

- Application Form 1/2 — SPCB prescribed
- Project report — Detailed project description
- Site plan — With surrounding land use
- Manufacturing process — Process flow with pollution sources
- Raw materials list — Including hazardous materials
- Effluent treatment plan — ETP design and capacity
- Air pollution control measures — Stack design, scrubbers
- Solid waste management plan
- Water consumption details — Source and quantity
- EIA report — For Red category large projects
- Land documents — Title or lease
- Local body NOC

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Determine industry category
2. Step 2 — Prepare environmental documentation
3. Step 3 — Submit CTE application to SPCB
4. Step 4 — Site inspection
5. Step 5 — Public hearing (for certain categories)
6. Step 6 — Technical scrutiny by SPCB committee
7. Step 7 — Conditions stipulation
8. Step 8 — CTE issuance

Timeline: 2–6 months (Green/Orange), 6–18 months (Red category with EIA).

- 5-year validity typically
- Extension possible with valid reasons
- Conversion to CTO (Consent to Operate) before commissioning

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Is CTE required for an IT/software office?** IT/software is White category — typically no CTE required, but State Pollution Control Boards may require notification or simple registration.
2. **Can construction begin while CTE is pending?** No. CTE must be obtained before any construction commences. Pre-CTE construction attracts stop-work orders and penalties.
3. **What if my project category changes during planning?** Apply for CTE based on the higher category. Subsequent downgrade can be applied for with revised documentation.
4. **Is Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) required for all projects?** EIA is required for Red category projects above specified thresholds. Most Green/Orange/small Red projects don't need EIA.
5. **Can CTE conditions be challenged?** Yes. Conditions can be appealed to the State Environment Appellate Authority and ultimately to the National Green Tribunal (NGT).
