# Wine Bar License

## Detailed Overview

A Wine Bar License is a specialised liquor license authorising restaurants and dedicated wine bars to serve wine (and sometimes mild liquor) for on-premises consumption with food. This license is distinct from a full bar license and caters to wine-focused establishments — wine bars, wine lounges, wine retail with tasting, vineyards' tasting rooms, and dedicated wine restaurants.

The license has gained popularity in metros (Mumbai, Bangalore, Pune, Delhi) with the growth of wine culture, wine tasting events, and premium dining.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Niche premium positioning
- Lower license fees than full bar in some states
- Aligns with sophisticated dining and tourism
- Less restrictive regulatory framework
- Supports wine tourism and vineyard tasting rooms
- Premium pricing power

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Limited to wine — narrower customer base
- Cannot serve beer or spirits (in pure wine bar license)
- Higher inventory costs (premium wines)
- Requires sommelier/wine expertise
- Staff training costs

## Applicability Across India

Particularly relevant in Maharashtra (wine country — Nashik, Sula, Fratelli vineyards), Karnataka, Delhi NCR, Bangalore, Mumbai, Pune, Hyderabad.

## Features

- On-premises wine service with food
- Specific premises designation
- Annual license validity
- FSSAI license mandatory

## Documents Required

Similar to Permit Room / Wine Bar combination — restaurant trade license, FSSAI, fire NOC, premises documents.

## Process / Procedure

Standard liquor license process. Timeline: 60–120 days.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I have wine tasting events under a wine bar license?** Yes, wine tasting events are typically permitted within the licensed premises. Off-premises tasting events may require additional permissions.
2. **Can I sell wine bottles for takeaway?** Wine bar licenses are typically for on-premises consumption only. Takeaway requires a wine retail license (Wine Shoppee).
3. **Are wine cocktails (sangria, spritzers) permitted?** Yes, cocktails using only wine as base are permitted.
4. **Can I serve cheese and small bites without full menu?** Most wine bar licenses require food service, but cheese boards, charcuterie, and tapas-style food typically qualify.
5. **Are wine education classes permitted at the wine bar?** Yes, wine education and certification programs (WSET, etc.) are commonly held at wine bars and don't require additional licenses if conducted within licensed premises.
