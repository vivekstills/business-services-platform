# RERA Registration for Agents

## Detailed Overview

RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority) Registration for Agents is mandatory under the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) for any person or entity facilitating sale, purchase, or transfer of real estate properties for consideration. Real estate agents — brokers, channel partners, sales consultants — must register with the State RERA Authority before facilitating any real estate transaction in projects covered under RERA.

The agent registration is a critical consumer protection mechanism — buyers can verify the agent's RERA registration before transacting and have recourse against registered agents in case of disputes.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Legal authorisation to operate as real estate agent
- Eligibility to deal in RERA-registered projects
- Consumer trust and credibility
- Protection from unauthorised practice penalties
- Access to RERA-registered project listings

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Annual fees and renewal
- Compliance with conduct rules
- Liability for misrepresentation under RERA
- State-specific registrations
- Disclosure requirements for past projects

## Applicability Across India

Mandatory in all states with active RERA Authority:

- Maharashtra (MahaRERA)
- Karnataka (K-RERA)
- Tamil Nadu (TNRERA)
- Telangana (TS-RERA)
- Delhi NCR (Delhi RERA, UP RERA, Haryana RERA)
- Gujarat (GujRERA)
- Rajasthan (RajRERA)
- All other states with active RERAs

## Features

- Individual or entity-level registration
- 5-year validity (typically)
- State-specific (separate registrations for multi-state operations)
- Online registration through state RERA portals

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I operate as an agent in multiple states under one RERA registration?** No. Each state requires separate RERA registration for the operating jurisdiction.
2. **Can an unregistered agent broker for RERA-registered projects?** No. Both project and agent must be registered. Unregistered agents face penalties up to 5% of project cost.
3. **Are property consultants required to register?** Yes — anyone facilitating real estate transactions for consideration, including consultants and channel partners.
4. **What's the penalty for unregistered operation?** Penalty up to 5% of estimated cost of property facilitated, plus potential criminal liability.
5. **Can sub-agents work under a registered agent without their own registration?** No. Each individual or entity dealing with consumers must have their own RERA registration.
