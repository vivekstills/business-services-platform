# PCB Consent to Operate (CTO)

## Detailed Overview

PCB Consent to Operate (CTO) is the post-construction environmental clearance issued by the State Pollution Control Board, authorising commencement of industrial operations after the facility has been constructed in compliance with CTE conditions. CTO is granted upon verification that pollution control infrastructure (ETP, scrubbers, stacks) is installed and operational as committed during CTE stage.

CTO is mandatory before any commercial production or operation commences. Operating without CTO attracts severe penalties under the Water Act and Air Act, including factory closure orders.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Legal authorisation to commence operations
- Continued compliance demonstration
- Eligibility for environmental certifications
- Required for many regulatory continuations (factory license renewal, etc.)
- Stakeholder credibility

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Periodic renewal required (1–5 years)
- Continuous monitoring obligations
- Heavy penalties for non-compliance
- Conditions can become more stringent over time
- Disclosure of emission data

## Applicability Across India

All operating industrial establishments must hold valid CTO from respective State PCB.

## Features

- Post-construction operating authorisation
- Periodic renewal cycle
- Conditions linked to monitoring data
- Stack/effluent monitoring obligations

## Documents Required

- Application Form 4 — SPCB prescribed
- CTE copy — Previous Consent to Establish
- Construction completion proof
- Pollution control equipment installation proof — ETP, APC equipment
- Stack details — Heights, monitoring points
- Monitoring data — Initial baseline data
- Hazardous waste management plan
- Authorization for hazardous wastes
- Site photographs — Of installed pollution control measures

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Complete construction per CTE conditions
2. Step 2 — Install all pollution control equipment
3. Step 3 — Apply for CTO with documentation
4. Step 4 — SPCB site verification
5. Step 5 — Compliance audit
6. Step 6 — CTO issuance with conditions

Timeline: 1–4 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I operate before CTO is granted?** No. Operations must wait for CTO. Trial runs are sometimes permitted with specific approval.
2. **What is the difference between CTE and CTO?** CTE is pre-construction authorisation; CTO is post-construction/pre-operation authorisation. Both are mandatory for industrial projects.
3. **Are smaller White category establishments exempt from CTO?** White category often has simplified intimation procedures rather than full CTO. Verify with state PCB.
4. **Can CTO be cancelled for violations?** Yes. Persistent violations can lead to CTO suspension or cancellation, requiring operations to cease.
5. **What's the consequence of operating without valid CTO?** Closure orders, electricity disconnection (in some states), heavy fines (₹10,000+ per day), and prosecution under Water/Air Acts.
