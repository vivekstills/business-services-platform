# Wholesale License for Wine

## Detailed Overview

A Wholesale License for Wine specifically authorises wholesale distribution of wines (still, sparkling, fortified) from licensed wineries and importers to retailers, hotels, and restaurants. With India's wine market growing rapidly (15-20% CAGR), this license category has gained importance, especially with the boom of Indian wine production in Maharashtra (Nashik, Pune), Karnataka (Bangalore), and import-driven markets.

Some states have separate wine wholesale categories with lower fees than IMFL wholesale, recognising wine as a low-alcohol, premium product distinct from spirits.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Growing wine consumption in India
- Premium pricing and margins
- Lower license fees than IMFL wholesale in some states
- Cleaner regulatory framework
- Aligns with wine tourism (Nashik wine country)
- Educated, premium customer base

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Niche market — smaller than beer/spirits
- High capital requirements for premium wine inventory
- Cold chain logistics needed
- Limited retail base for wine-specific sales
- Competing with beer/spirits in same outlets

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I wholesale only Indian wines under this license?** Most wine wholesale licenses cover both Indian and imported wines, provided imports come through licensed importers.
2. **Are champagne and sparkling wines covered?** Yes — sparkling wines, champagne, and prosecco are all covered under wine wholesale licenses.
3. **What are special storage requirements?** Temperature-controlled storage (12-18°C ideal), humidity control, and protection from sunlight. Premium wines need specialised cellar conditions.
4. **Can I supply wine to events directly?** Wholesale licenses are B2B only. Event supply requires either retail purchase by event organisers or specific event-licensee arrangements.
5. **Is wine wholesale more profitable than IMFL wholesale?** Per-bottle margins are higher, but absolute volumes are smaller. Profitability depends on portfolio mix and market access.
