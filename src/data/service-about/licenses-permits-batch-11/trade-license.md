# Trade License

## Detailed Overview

A Trade License is a municipal authorisation issued by the local municipal corporation/municipality permitting a person or entity to carry out a specified business/trade at a specified location. It is governed by state Municipal Corporation Acts and is one of the foundational compliance requirements for any business operating from a fixed premises within municipal limits.

The license ensures that businesses operate in zones permitted under municipal planning, comply with local trade norms, and contribute to municipal revenue. Different categories of trades have different license requirements — hazardous, non-hazardous, food, manufacturing, services.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Legal authorisation to operate business
- Required for various downstream registrations (GST, FSSAI, etc.)
- Required for bank account opening (current account)
- Tendering eligibility (government/PSU)
- Consumer trust
- Legal protection

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Multiple municipality registrations for multi-location operations
- Annual renewal
- Inspection-based renewal
- State and municipal variations
- Categorisation complexity for diverse businesses

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Is Trade License needed for online businesses with no physical premises?** If you have a registered business address (even a small office), Trade License is typically required. Pure home-based online businesses below thresholds may be exempt in some municipalities.
2. **Is Trade License same as Shops & Establishments License?** Different licenses with overlap. Shops Act covers labour aspects; Trade License covers business permission. Both often required.
3. **Can multiple businesses share one Trade License at same address?** Generally no. Each distinct trade typically requires its own license.
4. **What categories are typically prohibited from Trade Licenses in residential areas?** Hazardous trades, manufacturing units, large retail, and certain service businesses are typically prohibited in residential zones.
5. **What's the renewal grace period after expiry?** Most municipalities allow 30–60 days grace with late fee. Beyond this, fresh application may be needed.
