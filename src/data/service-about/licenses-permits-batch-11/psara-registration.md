# PSARA Registration

## Detailed Overview

PSARA (Private Security Agencies Regulation Act, 2005) Registration is a mandatory license for any person or entity operating a private security agency in India — providing security guards, bouncers, security personnel, and related services. The Act regulates the security industry to ensure professional standards, character verification of guards, and accountability in private security operations.

PSARA license is granted by the State Controlling Authority (typically the Home Department or Police Commissionerate) of each state where the agency operates.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Legal authorisation to operate security agency
- Eligibility for institutional security contracts
- Government and PSU tendering eligibility
- Professional credibility
- Insurance coverage facilitation

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- State-specific licenses (multi-state operation needs multiple licenses)
- Heavy compliance — guard training, verification, uniforms
- Background verification requirements
- Renewal compliance burden
- Initial capital and infrastructure requirements

## Applicability Across India

PSARA is mandatory in all states for any entity providing security services. Each state has its own PSARA Rules with state-specific procedures.

## Features

- State-level license under Central PSARA Act
- Mandatory for all private security agencies
- 5-year validity (typically)
- Renewable

## Documents Required

- Form I (Application) — PSARA prescribed form
- Affidavit — As per PSARA Rules
- Memorandum of Association — For company applicants
- PAN, GST — Entity tax registrations
- Office premises proof — Lease/ownership
- Director/proprietor verification — Police verification
- Training arrangement proof — MoU with training institute
- Bank guarantee — ₹25,000 – ₹1,00,000
- ITR (3 years) — Of entity/proprietor
- Insurance — Liability insurance

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Apply to State Controlling Authority with documents
2. Step 2 — Police verification of directors/proprietors
3. Step 3 — Office inspection
4. Step 4 — Training arrangement verification
5. Step 5 — Bank guarantee submission
6. Step 6 — License issuance

Timeline: 60–180 days.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I operate security in multiple states under one PSARA license?** No. Each state requires separate PSARA registration. Multi-state operations need multiple state licenses.
2. **Is training mandatory for security guards under PSARA?** Yes. All guards must undergo training (typically 100 hours) at recognised training institutes before deployment.
3. **Can ex-servicemen operate security agencies easily under PSARA?** PSARA gives some preference to ex-servicemen with reduced training requirements and facilitated registration.
4. **What is the bank guarantee for under PSARA?** The bank guarantee secures compliance and is invoked in case of violations. It's returned upon license closure with no pending liabilities.
5. **Are bouncers covered under PSARA?** Yes. Any private security personnel — guards, bouncers, security supervisors — fall under PSARA regulation.
