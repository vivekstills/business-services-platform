# Club License

## Detailed Overview

A Club License is a special category liquor license issued by state excise departments to registered private members' clubs — recreational clubs, sports clubs, social clubs, professional clubs (Rotary, Lions), and similar member-only establishments — authorising them to serve liquor (beer, wine, and IMFL) to bona fide members and their guests within the club premises.

The license is distinct from a regular bar/restaurant license because of the members-only nature — service is restricted to registered members and their accompanying guests, not to the general public. The club must be registered as a society, trust, or Section 8 Company under applicable laws and have a defined membership structure with admission criteria, membership fees, and proper governance.

Club Licenses are governed by state-specific excise acts and rules — with significant variations in eligibility, license fees, conditions, and operational restrictions. Premium clubs in major cities (Bombay Gymkhana, Madras Club, Bangalore Club, Delhi Gymkhana, etc.) operate under such licenses.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Captive customer base — registered members are loyal recurring customers
- Higher margins — clubs typically charge premium prices for liquor service
- Less competition — limited number of club licenses issued
- Opportunity to build prestigious membership base
- License fees generally lower than commercial bar licenses
- Less stringent operating hour restrictions in some states

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Restricted to members and guests — cannot serve walk-in public
- Requires established club infrastructure with adequate facilities
- Significant initial investment in club premises and amenities
- Member admission processes can be slow and selective
- Subject to excise audits and member register scrutiny
- Limited revenue scalability compared to public bars
- Compliance with club registration laws (Societies Act / Trusts Act / Companies Act)

## Features

- Issued only to registered clubs (not individual proprietorships)
- Members-only service (with limited guest privileges)
- Annual validity with renewal requirements
- Proper member register and guest register maintenance mandatory
- Service limited to club premises
- Typically permits all categories of liquor (beer, wine, IMFL)

## Documents Required

- Application form — State-specific club license form
- Club registration certificate — Society/Trust/Section 8 Company registration
- Memorandum and Rules — Club's MoA, AoA, or Rules and Regulations
- Member list — Current registered membership list with details
- Premises ownership / lease — Title deed or registered lease
- Premises plan — Layout showing dining, bar, and storage areas
- FSSAI License — For food service in the club
- Fire NOC — Mandatory safety clearance
- Local body NOC — Municipal approval
- GST Registration — Tax compliance
- Income Tax Returns — Club's last 3 years' ITRs
- Bank Statement — Demonstrating financial capacity
- Office bearer details — Names, PAN, Aadhaar of governing committee

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Application Submission: Submit application with all documents to State Excise Commissioner.
2. Step 2 — Documentary Verification: Excise department verifies club registration, membership structure, and premises documents.
3. Step 3 — Premises Inspection: Inspector verifies premises, dining facilities, bar area, storage, and overall infrastructure.
4. Step 4 — Public Notice (in some states): Publication of notice for objections.
5. Step 5 — License Fee Payment: Upon provisional approval, pay annual license fee and security deposit.
6. Step 6 — License Issuance: Club License issued by Excise Commissioner / authorised officer.
7. Step 7 — Stock Procurement: Obtain liquor stock from authorised wholesalers/state corporations.
8. Step 8 — Operations Commencement: Begin liquor service to members.

## Validity & Renewal

- Annual validity (April 1 to March 31)
- Renewal application 30–60 days before expiry
- Compliance audit and member register verification during renewal
- Late renewal fee: typically 50–100% additional

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can a club admit non-members for special events?** Most states allow guests of members but prohibit non-member entry for liquor service. For special public events, separate "One Day Function License" is typically required.
2. **Is there a minimum membership requirement for a Club License?** Most states require minimum 100–200 active members for a Club License application. Some states have higher thresholds for premium club categories.
3. **Can corporate entities purchase club memberships?** Corporate memberships are permitted in most clubs and most state laws. Each corporate member typically gets nominee privileges as defined in club rules.
4. **Can the club host wedding receptions and serve liquor to wedding guests?** For wedding receptions where non-members attend, an additional One Day Function License is required for liquor service. The Club License alone doesn't cover such public events.
5. **Are office bearers personally liable for club license violations?** Yes. Office bearers (President, Secretary, Treasurer) named in the license can be held personally liable for excise violations, including underage service, after-hours service, or sale to non-members.
