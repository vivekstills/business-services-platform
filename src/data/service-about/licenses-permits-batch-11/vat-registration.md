# VAT Registration

## Detailed Overview

VAT (Value Added Tax) Registration was mandatory until July 1, 2017 for businesses involved in sale of goods within a state under State VAT Acts. With the implementation of GST on July 1, 2017, VAT was largely subsumed into GST — however, certain products remain outside GST and continue to be governed by state VAT laws:

- Petroleum products (petrol, diesel, ATF, crude oil, natural gas)
- Alcoholic liquor for human consumption
- Electricity (in some contexts)

Businesses dealing in these excluded goods continue to require VAT Registration in the relevant state.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Legal authorisation to deal in VAT-able goods
- Input VAT credit on purchases (where applicable)
- Eligibility for B2B transactions
- Tax compliance credibility

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Limited to VAT-excluded products only
- State-specific compliance burden
- Multiple state registrations for multi-state operations
- Complex documentation

## Applicability Across India

Required in any state where business deals in VAT-able goods:

- Petroleum dealers (petrol pumps, oil distributors)
- Liquor wholesalers and retailers
- Aviation fuel suppliers
- Electricity distributors (in specific contexts)

## Features

- State-level registration
- Product-specific applicability
- Periodic VAT returns
- Input credit mechanism (where allowed)

## Documents Required

Standard registration documents — entity proof, premises proof, identity proofs, financial documents, product licenses (e.g., excise license for liquor, oil marketing company authorisation for fuel).

## Process / Procedure

State-specific online registration on respective state commercial taxes department portal. Timeline: 15–60 days.

- Typically permanent registration with annual return filing requirements.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Is VAT still applicable after GST implementation?** Only for specific products (petroleum, alcohol, electricity) that remain outside GST.
2. **Can I claim input VAT credit on regular business purchases under State VAT?** Only on VAT-able product purchases. Regular business inputs are under GST regime.
3. **Do liquor retailers need both VAT registration and Excise License?** Yes. Excise License is for liquor licensing; VAT Registration is for sales tax compliance on liquor.
4. **Are petrol pump dealers required to register for VAT?** Yes. Petrol pumps must register for VAT in their state of operation.
5. **Can VAT registration be cancelled if business shifts to only GST goods?** Yes. If business no longer deals in VAT-able goods, registration can be surrendered with proper procedures.
