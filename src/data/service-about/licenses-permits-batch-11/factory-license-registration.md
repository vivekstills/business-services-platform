# Factory License Registration

## Detailed Overview

A Factory License is a mandatory registration under the Factories Act, 1948, required for any premises where manufacturing activities involve the use of power and 10 or more workers, or 20 or more workers without power. The license is issued by the Chief Inspector of Factories or Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health (DISH) in the respective state.

The Factory License framework regulates worker safety, working conditions, hazardous processes, welfare facilities, and statutory compliance for industrial establishments. It is one of the foundational compliances for any manufacturing business in India.

## Advantages / Benefits

- Legal authorisation for manufacturing operations
- Worker safety framework compliance
- Eligibility for industrial subsidies and schemes
- Required for institutional bank loans
- Supports export licensing and ESI/EPF registrations
- Enables tendering for government contracts

## Disadvantages / Limitations

- Stringent compliance requirements
- Periodic renewal and inspection
- Detailed safety infrastructure mandatory
- High penalty for non-compliance
- Worker welfare facility costs

## Applicability Across India

## Features

- Mandatory under Factories Act, 1948
- Premises-specific
- Annual or multi-year validity
- Periodic inspection and renewal
- Worker headcount-based applicability

## Documents Required

- Application Form 2 — Factories Act prescribed form
- Factory site plan — Approved by competent authority
- Building plan — Architectural drawings, approved by local authority
- Machinery layout — Detailed machinery placement plan
- Manufacturing process — Process flow with hazards
- List of raw materials — Including hazardous chemicals
- Workers' details — Number, classification, demographics
- Welfare facility plan — Toilets, canteen, restroom, drinking water
- Fire NOC — From Fire Services
- PCB Consent — Environmental clearance (if applicable)
- Factory Manager appointment — With qualifications
- Stability certificate — For building structure
- Power load sanction — From electricity board

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Submit application with complete documents to DISH/Factory Inspector
2. Step 2 — Plan approval by Chief Inspector
3. Step 3 — Site inspection
4. Step 4 — Compliance verification
5. Step 5 — License fee payment
6. Step 6 — Factory License issuance

Timeline: 3–6 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Is a Factory License required for a small unit with 8 workers?** Below the 10-worker threshold (with power) or 20-worker threshold (without power), Factory License under the Factories Act is not mandatory. However, Shops and Establishments Act registration would still apply.
2. **Can I add more machinery without amending the Factory License?** Significant changes in machinery, manufacturing process, or worker strength require License amendment. Minor modifications need notification.
3. **What is the role of a Factory Manager?** A qualified Factory Manager (with prescribed qualifications) must be appointed and named in the License. The Manager is statutorily responsible for compliance.
4. **Are home-based manufacturing units required to have Factory Licenses?** If they meet the worker threshold and use power, yes. Most home-based units below 10 workers are exempt.
5. **What are the consequences of operating without a Factory License?** Criminal prosecution under the Factories Act with fines up to ₹2 lakh and/or imprisonment up to 2 years for the occupier and manager.
