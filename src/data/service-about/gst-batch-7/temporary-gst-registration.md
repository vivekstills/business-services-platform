# Temporary GST Registration (Casual Taxable Person)

## Overview

Temporary GST Registration refers to the registration obtained by a Casual Taxable Person (CTP) under Section 24 of the CGST Act, 2017. A Casual Taxable Person is defined under Section 2(20) of the CGST Act as a person who occasionally undertakes transactions involving supply of goods or services in a state or Union Territory where they do not have a fixed place of business.
Common situations requiring Casual Taxable Person registration include:
Businesses participating in trade fairs or exhibitions in a state where they are not otherwise registered
Seasonal businesses operating in a specific state only during a festival or tourist season (e.g., a handicraft seller from Rajasthan selling at a Delhi craft fair)
Contract workers and freelancers executing a short-term project in a different state from where they are registered
Event management companies managing events in states outside their registration state
Film production companies shooting in a state where they don't have a permanent establishment
Unlike the regular taxpayer who obtains permanent GST registration, the CTP registration is temporary — valid for a maximum of 90 days (extendable by another 90 days). The CTP cannot claim ITC from inter-state purchases in most cases, and must make an advance deposit of estimated GST liability before registration is granted.

## Applicability

Casual Taxable Person registration is required for:

- Any person making occasional taxable supplies of goods or services in a state where they do not have a fixed place of business
- Person attending trade exhibitions, fairs, or expos in a different state
- Seasonal business operators entering a new state temporarily
- Businesses executing a one-time project or contract in a state outside their normal registration

CTP is NOT applicable if:

- The person has a permanent business establishment in the state — they must register as a regular taxpayer
- The supply is entirely exempt from GST — no registration required for exempt supplies
- The person is a Non-Resident (foreign national/entity) making supplies in India — they register as NRTP, not CTP

Applicable pan-India — CTP registration is available in all states and UTs.

## Features

- Registration in Form GST REG-01: CTP applies using the same form as regular registration, but selects "Casual Taxable Person" as the taxpayer type
- Mandatory advance deposit: Before the CTP GSTIN is issued, the applicant must make an advance tax deposit equal to the estimated GST liability for the period of supply
- 90-day validity: CTP registration is valid for up to 90 days from the effective date — extendable by another 90 days maximum
- GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B filing: CTPs file regular GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B returns during their registration period — these must be filed before applying for extension
- Final GSTR-3B: CTPs file a final return after the supply period ends
- Refund of excess advance deposit: If actual GST liability is less than the advance deposit, the excess is refundable
- No ITC on inter-state purchases for CTPs in many situations: This limitation makes the advance deposit model more conservative
- Existing regular GSTIN in home state: A CTP can simultaneously hold their regular GSTIN in their home state — the CTP GSTIN in the new state is separate and temporary

## Benefits

- Legal supply across state boundaries: Enables businesses to make taxable supplies in states where they are not permanently registered — without having to go through the full permanent registration process
- Short-term and hassle-free: For genuinely occasional supplies, CTP registration is less burdensome than permanent multi-state registration
- Refund mechanism: Excess advance deposit is refundable — the advance deposit does not represent a cost, only a temporary cash outflow
- Tax invoice issuance: CTPs can issue valid GST tax invoices — enabling their B2B buyers to claim ITC
- Compliance simplicity: For a defined short-term supply period, CTP returns are straightforward compared to managing permanent registrations in multiple states

## Process

1. Step 1 — Estimate GST Liability Estimate the total turnover expected during the supply period and compute the expected GST liability (applying the correct GST rate to the estimated turnover).
1. Step 2 — File GST REG-01 as CTP File Form GST REG-01 on the GST portal — select the state where the temporary supply will be made and choose "Casual Taxable Person" as the registration type.
1. Step 3 — Make Advance Deposit Make the advance tax deposit on the GST portal (the system requests the deposit before granting the GSTIN). Deposit into the Electronic Cash Ledger via net banking/UPI.
1. Step 4 — Receive CTP GSTIN Upon deposit confirmation and document verification, the GST officer grants the CTP GSTIN. The certificate specifies the validity period (up to 90 days).
1. Step 5 — Conduct Business Use the CTP GSTIN for all supplies made during the registration period. Issue tax invoices with the CTP GSTIN.
1. Step 6 — File GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B File returns for each return period within the CTP registration validity. All returns must be filed before the registration expires.
1. Step 7 — Apply for Extension (if needed) If the supply period needs to be extended beyond 90 days, apply for extension before the CTP registration expires — with an additional advance deposit for the extended period. Maximum total period: 180 days.
1. Step 8 — File Final Returns and Claim Refund After the supply period, file the final GSTR-3B. Apply for a refund of any excess advance deposit through Form RFD-01.

## Compliance

- Apply for CTP registration and make advance deposit before commencing supply — not on the day of the event
- File all GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B returns within the registration period
- Do not exceed the 90-day (or 180-day with extension) validity without a fresh registration
- Maintain all tax invoices, stock records, and return copies for 6 years even for the CTP period
- Refund applications must be filed after the final GSTR-3B — within 2 years of the relevant date

## Common Mistakes

- Starting supply without CTP registration: Beginning to sell at an exhibition without a CTP GSTIN — all sales are non-compliant and GST collected (if any) cannot be legally retained
- Underestimating advance deposit: If actual GST exceeds the advance deposit, the shortfall must be paid immediately — compute conservatively to avoid cash crunch
- Not filing returns within the validity period: CTP returns must be filed within the 90-day period — expired CTP registrations cannot be reactivated; a fresh registration must be obtained
- Using home state GSTIN for supplies in another state: Issuing invoices from a home-state GSTIN for supplies made at an event in another state — this is an incorrect place of supply treatment and creates ITC issues for buyers in the other state
- Forgetting to claim refund: The advance deposit refund is not automatic — businesses often forget to file RFD-01 after completing the supply period

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **We participate in 4 trade fairs per year in different states. Do we need a fresh CTP registration for each fair?** Yes — each state requires a separate CTP registration for the duration of the fair in that state. If you attend a fair in Delhi, another in Mumbai, one in Chennai, and one in Kolkata, you need 4 separate CTP registrations — one for each state, covering only the duration of the fair in that state. Each CTP registration requires a separate advance deposit for the estimated GST liability in that state for that period. If you regularly attend trade fairs in the same states every year, it may be more efficient to obtain permanent GST registrations in those states instead.
- **The trade fair where I'm participating starts in 3 days. Is it too late to get a CTP GST registration?** The standard GST registration processing timeline is 7 working days (for Aadhaar-authenticated applications) — which may be too long for a fair starting in 3 days. However, the GST portal processes CTP applications with some urgency given the time-bound nature. Additionally, since the portal has a deemed approval mechanism (if the officer does not act within 7 working days, registration is deemed granted), you can often commence operations with the ARN pending. That said, waiting until 3 days before is inadvisable — apply for CTP registration at least 10–15 days before the event to ensure the GSTIN is active and the advance deposit is processed.
- **I am registered in Karnataka. I am providing professional consulting services for a 2-month project in Hyderabad (Telangana). Do I need a CTP registration in Telangana?** This depends on whether you have a fixed place of business in Telangana. If you are working remotely from Karnataka or staying in a hotel in Hyderabad (temporary accommodation is not a fixed establishment), the place of supply for services is typically the location of the service recipient (Telangana). You would charge IGST using your Karnataka GSTIN — no CTP registration in Telangana is needed for most service supplies. However, if the nature of your engagement requires physical presence and operations from Telangana over an extended period with significant infrastructure, CTP registration may be more appropriate. Get a professional opinion on the specific facts.
- **What is the difference between CTP (Casual Taxable Person) and NRTP (Non-Resident Taxable Person)?** Both are temporary GST registrations, but they differ in applicability: CTP is for Indian residents (individuals or businesses) making occasional supplies in a state outside their home registration. NRTP is for foreign nationals or foreign entities (without a fixed establishment in India) making occasional supplies anywhere in India. The application forms differ — CTP uses GST REG-01 (same form as regular taxpayers, with CTP type selected); NRTP uses GST REG-09 (a separate form designed for non-residents). Both require advance deposits and have 90-day maximum validity. The return form also differs — CTPs file GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B; NRTPs file GSTR-5.
- **Our CTP registration for a 2-month fair in Delhi has expired but we still have 3 weeks of the fair remaining. What do we do?** If the CTP registration expires before the supply period ends, you have two options: (a) if you are still within the first 90-day period and haven't yet applied for extension — apply immediately for the 90-day extension (maximum total period is 180 days); (b) if the maximum 180-day period has already been exhausted — apply for a fresh CTP registration for the remaining period, with a new advance deposit. In the interim period between expiry and fresh registration, technically you cannot make taxable supplies. Minimise this gap by tracking expiry dates carefully and applying for extension or fresh registration well before the current registration expires.
