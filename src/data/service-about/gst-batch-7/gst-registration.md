# GST Registration

## Overview

GST Registration is the process by which a business obtains a unique GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number) — a 15-digit alphanumeric code — from the GST portal, authorizing it to collect GST from customers, claim Input Tax Credit, and file GST returns. GST Registration is the foundation of GST compliance in India.
Under the GST law (CGST Act, 2017), registration is mandatory for every supplier whose aggregate annual turnover exceeds the prescribed threshold, or who is otherwise required to register (regardless of turnover) under specific categories.
The GSTIN structure is: State code (2 digits) + PAN (10 digits) + Entity number in state (2 digits) + Check digit (1 digit).

| Registration Type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Regular Taxpayer | Standard GST registration for ongoing business |
| Composition Scheme | Simplified compliance for small businesses (turnover limits apply) |
| Casual Taxable Person | For temporary/occasional taxable supply in a state |
| Non-Resident Taxable Person | For foreign entities supplying in India |
| TDS Deductor | Government entities deducting TDS under GST |
| TCS Collector | E-commerce operators collecting TCS |
| SEZ Unit / Developer | Suppliers or developers in SEZs |
| Input Service Distributor | For distributing ITC from head office to branches |

## Applicability

| Threshold / Category | Threshold |
| --- | --- |

| Service providers (non-special category states) | Annual turnover above ₹20 lakh |
| Special category states (NE states, Uttarakhand, etc.) | Goods: ₹20 lakh; Services: ₹10 lakh |
| Interstate supply (goods) | Mandatory regardless of turnover |
| E-commerce sellers | Mandatory regardless of turnover |
| E-commerce operators (TCS) | Mandatory regardless of turnover |

| Casual taxable persons | Mandatory before commencing supply |
| Non-resident taxable persons | Mandatory before commencing supply |

Applicable pan-India — 37 jurisdictions (28 states + 8 UTs + 1 central jurisdiction for central taxpayers).

## Features

- 15-digit GSTIN: Unique identifier for every registered taxpayer — state-wise
- PAN-based: GST registration is linked to PAN — one PAN can have multiple GSTINs (one per state/UT where business is conducted)
- Online application: Entirely online on gst.gov.in — Form GST REG-01
- ARN-based tracking: Application Reference Number generated on submission — allows tracking of application status
- Deemed approval: If the GST officer does not act on an application within the prescribed period (7 working days for Aadhaar-authenticated applications), registration is deemed granted
- State-wise registration: A separate GSTIN is required for each state/UT where the business has operations or makes taxable supplies
- Multiple registrations possible: A business with operations in 5 states needs 5 separate GSTINs
- Composition option: Small businesses (goods up to ₹1.5 crore turnover, restaurants up to ₹1.5 crore, service providers up to ₹50 lakh) can opt for simplified composition scheme at registration

## Benefits

- Legal compliance: Mandatory for applicable businesses — operating without GST registration attracts a penalty of ₹10,000 or the tax amount evaded, whichever is higher
- ITC eligibility: Only GST-registered businesses can claim Input Tax Credit — unregistered businesses bear GST as a cost
- B2B business enablement: Most businesses transact only with GST-registered vendors — registration is a commercial prerequisite for B2B operations
- Credibility: A GSTIN-displaying business is perceived as formal, compliant, and credible — essential for enterprise clients, government contracts, and institutional buyers
- E-commerce marketplace access: Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, and other platforms require GST registration before onboarding sellers — registration is a gating condition
- Export benefits: GST registration is required for LUT filing, IGST refund, and ITC refund — all critical for exporters
- Interstate supply: Registered businesses can make inter-state supplies freely; unregistered businesses cannot make inter-state supplies of goods at all

## Process

1. Step 1 — Determine Registration Need Confirm whether mandatory registration is required or voluntary registration is being sought. Identify all states where registration is needed.
1. Step 2 — Aadhaar Authentication The primary signatory's Aadhaar must be linked with the mobile number for OTP-based authentication during the registration application.
1. Step 3 — Form GST REG-01 Filing Visit gst.gov.in. Click on "New Registration." Select taxpayer type, state, and district. Fill Part A — PAN, mobile, and email verification. Then complete Part B — business details, promoter/partner details, business address, bank details, and upload documents.
1. Step 4 — Application Submission Submit Form GST REG-01 using DSC (for companies/LLPs) or EVC (OTP-based for proprietors and partnerships). The system generates an ARN.
1. Step 5 — Aadhaar Authentication / Biometric Verification For Aadhaar-authenticated applications: complete the Aadhaar OTP verification. For states requiring biometric verification (or for applicants who flag Aadhaar authentication): complete biometric verification at the GST Suvidha Kendra.
1. Step 6 — GST Officer Review The GST officer reviews the application within 7 working days. For applications where Aadhaar authentication is complete — if no action in 7 working days, registration is deemed granted. For non-Aadhaar/biometric cases — 30 working days.
1. Step 7 — GSTIN Issuance Upon approval, Form GST REG-06 (Certificate of Registration) is issued — containing the 15-digit GSTIN. Download the certificate from the GST portal.
1. Step 8 — Post-Registration Setup Set up the GST return filing schedule, configure accounting software with GSTIN, update invoicing templates with GSTIN, and link bank account for refund credits.

## Compliance

- File GST returns (GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B) from the first return period after registration
- Mandatory to display GSTIN on signboard at principal place of business
- GSTIN must be mentioned on all tax invoices issued
- File NIL returns even if no business activity in a month — penalties apply for non-filing
- Apply for additional place of business registration or new state GSTIN if business expands geographically

## Common Mistakes

- Registering under wrong business type: Selecting wrong entity type (e.g., "Proprietor" instead of "Private Limited Company") causes significant compliance complications
- Wrong address on application: Using a virtual office or shared workspace address without proper NOC — leads to rejection or subsequent cancellation
- Not registering in all relevant states: Operating in a state without a GSTIN for that state is a violation — particularly common for service companies with clients across states
- Delayed registration: Crossing the threshold and not registering within 30 days of exceeding it — the 30-day window is the legal requirement

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **We are a freelancer providing design services with an annual income of ₹12 lakh. Do we need GST registration?** No — not mandatory. Service providers with annual turnover below ₹20 lakh (₹10 lakh in special category states) are below the GST registration threshold. However, voluntary GST registration may be beneficial if your clients are GST-registered businesses — they can then claim ITC on your invoices, making your services more cost-effective for them and potentially allowing you to charge more. Weigh the compliance burden against the commercial benefit before deciding.
- **I have an e-commerce business selling handmade products on Flipkart. My revenue is only ₹8 lakh. Do I need GST?** Yes. E-commerce sellers are mandatorily required to register for GST regardless of their turnover — the ₹20/40 lakh threshold exemption does not apply to e-commerce sellers. Both Amazon and Flipkart require a GSTIN before you can list products. Additionally, e-commerce operators (Flipkart, Amazon) collect TCS (Tax Collected at Source) at 1% from registered sellers' payments — which can be claimed back in GSTR-3B. Register for GST before starting your marketplace selling journey.
- **We are a Bengaluru-based company providing IT services to a client in Mumbai. Do we need a Maharashtra GST registration?** Not necessarily. For supply of services, the place of supply is generally the location of the service recipient — i.e., Maharashtra. However, a service provider does not need a GST registration in every state where their clients are located. Your Bengaluru GSTIN can be used to supply services interstate — charge IGST on the invoice. A Maharashtra GSTIN would only be needed if you have a physical office/branch in Maharashtra that constitutes a "fixed establishment." For pure remote service delivery, your Karnataka GSTIN suffices for pan-India service clients.
- **My GST registration application was rejected. What are the common reasons and how do I reapply?** Common rejection reasons include: mismatch between PAN name and business name; address proof not matching the declared principal place of business; NOC from property owner not submitted or not properly executed; Aadhaar authentication failure; and incomplete or unclear document uploads. Review the rejection order carefully — it specifies the grounds. Address each ground in the reapplication. File a fresh Form GST REG-01 with corrected details and proper documentation. Alternatively, file a reply to the Show Cause Notice (GST REG-23) if the rejection was preceded by an SCN, which allows you to clarify without a fresh application.
- **We want to register for GST voluntarily even though our turnover is below the threshold. Is voluntary registration advisable?** Voluntary GST registration makes commercial sense if: (a) you supply to GST-registered B2B clients who need ITC on your invoices; (b) you want to be eligible for ITC on your own purchases; (c) you plan to export and need GST registration for LUT and refund claims; or (d) your turnover is approaching the threshold and you want to avoid the disruption of a mid-year mandatory registration. The downside is the ongoing compliance burden — monthly/quarterly GST returns. Weigh these factors against your specific business situation before opting for voluntary registration.
