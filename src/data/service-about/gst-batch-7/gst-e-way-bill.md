# GST E-Way Bill

## Overview

The E-Way Bill (Electronic Way Bill) is a compliance document generated on the GST E-Way Bill portal (ewaybillgst.gov.in) for the movement of goods valued above ₹50,000 within India. Introduced under Rule 138 of the CGST Rules, 2017, the E-Way Bill is a digital document that must accompany the physical movement of goods — whether by road, rail, air, or ship — from the point of supply to the point of delivery.
An E-Way Bill consists of two parts: Part A containing supply details (GSTIN of supplier/recipient, place of supply, invoice number, value, HSN code) and Part B containing vehicle details (transporter ID, vehicle number, mode of transport). The bill contains a unique E-Way Bill Number (EBN) that can be verified by GST enforcement officers at checkposts and during transit.
The E-Way Bill system was introduced to replace the paper-based way bill/transit pass system that existed under the pre-GST state VAT regimes, and to create a real-time digital trail of goods movement across India — enabling enforcement authorities to cross-verify consignments with GST returns and detect tax evasion in the supply chain.
Failure to generate an E-Way Bill for applicable consignments — or transportation of goods with an expired, incorrect, or invalid E-Way Bill — attracts seizure of goods and vehicles plus a penalty of ₹10,000 or tax payable (whichever is higher) under Section 129 of the CGST Act.

## Applicability

E-Way Bill is required for:

- Movement of goods valued above ₹50,000 (consignment value including GST) across or within India — inter-state or intra-state
- All modes of transport: Road (own vehicle, hired vehicle), rail, air, ship
- Both taxable and zero-rated supplies: Exports, SEZ supplies, job work, and bill-to-ship-to scenarios all require E-Way Bill if value exceeds ₹50,000
- Unregistered persons: Even if the supplier is unregistered, E-Way Bill is required for consignments above ₹50,000

Exemptions from E-Way Bill:

| --- | --- |
| Exempted goods | 153 categories of goods exempted by CBIC notification |

State-specific thresholds: Some states have set higher intra-state E-Way Bill thresholds (e.g., ₹1 lakh for certain states). Check state-specific notifications.

## Features

- Single portal: ewaybillgst.gov.in — centralized E-Way Bill generation for all of India with real-time validation
- Unique EBN: Each E-Way Bill has a unique 12-digit Electronic Way Bill Number
- Validity period: Based on distance — 1 day for every 200 km (road), with extension possible for exceptional circumstances
- Part A and Part B: Part A is supply details; Part B is transporter/vehicle details. Part A can be generated without Part B — Part B must be updated before goods move
- Multiple generation methods: Web interface, SMS, mobile app, API integration with ERP/billing systems
- Consolidated E-Way Bill: For multiple invoices in a single vehicle, a consolidated E-Way Bill (CEWB) covering multiple EBNs can be generated
- E-Way Bill cancellation: Can be cancelled within 24 hours of generation if goods are not transported
- RFID integration: Vehicles registered on the E-Way Bill portal with RFID tags can be tracked through RFID scanners at checkposts — reducing manual verification delays
- Alert system: SMS and email alerts to supplier, recipient, and transporter when E-Way Bill is generated, updated, or expires

## Benefits

- Legal movement of goods: E-Way Bill is the legal authorisation for goods movement — without it, vehicles can be intercepted, goods seized, and penalties imposed
- Digital compliance trail: E-Way Bill data is cross-referenced with GSTR-1 filings — consistent data reduces the risk of scrutiny and demand notices
- Reduced checkpost delays: The E-Way Bill system eliminated the multi-state checkpost system that caused significant transit delays — goods now move more freely with digital verification
- Real-time tracking: Businesses can track their own consignments using the E-Way Bill portal — improving supply chain visibility
- Dispute prevention: A valid E-Way Bill with correct consignment details protects against unjustified detention by enforcement officers — the document is the legal proof of compliance
- GSTIN verification: The E-Way Bill system validates GSTIN of supplier and recipient — catching registration issues before goods move
- Business intelligence: E-Way Bill data provides real-time intelligence on goods movement patterns — useful for supply chain analysis

## Process

1. Step 1 — E-Way Bill Portal Registration Register on ewaybillgst.gov.in using GSTIN and OTP verification. Suppliers, recipients, and transporters can all register.
1. Step 2 — Generate E-Way Bill (Part A) Log in to the portal. Select "Generate E-Way Bill." Enter: transaction type (outward/inward/job work/etc.), document type (invoice/delivery challan), document details, supplier/recipient GSTIN, consignment value, HSN code, and quantity.
1. Step 3 — Enter Transporter Details (Part B) Enter the vehicle number and transporter GSTIN (or transporter ID). Part B can be updated by the transporter if the supplier has not entered it.
1. Step 4 — Generate and Download EBN The portal generates a unique 12-digit EBN. Download and print (or share digitally) the E-Way Bill document. The driver/transporter must carry a copy (physical or digital).
1. Step 5 — Monitor Validity Track E-Way Bill validity — 1 day per 200 km. For long-distance shipments, plan movements to ensure delivery within validity. In case of exceptional delay (accident, natural calamity, transhipment), extend validity on the portal before expiry.
1. Step 6 — Update Part B (if vehicle changes mid-route) If goods are transhipped from one vehicle to another, the transporter must update Part B on the portal with the new vehicle number.
1. Step 7 — Cancellation (if required) If goods are not transported after E-Way Bill generation, cancel the EBN within 24 hours on the portal.

## Compliance

- Generate E-Way Bill before goods commence movement — not after
- Ensure Part B (vehicle details) is filled before the goods physically move
- Monitor expiry and extend validity if goods are delayed in transit
- Cancel E-Way Bills for consignments that don't move within 24 hours of generation
- Maintain all E-Way Bill records for 6 years
- Cross-verify E-Way Bill data with GSTR-1 before filing — EBN data is cross-checked by the system

## Common Mistakes

- Generating E-Way Bill after goods have moved: EBN must precede movement — generating it post-movement is a violation even if the value is correct
- Incorrect HSN code in E-Way Bill: Mismatch between invoice HSN and E-Way Bill HSN attracts detention — use the same HSN in both documents
- Not updating vehicle details (Part B): Moving goods with only Part A filled and no vehicle number is non-compliant — enforcement officers will detain the consignment
- Allowing E-Way Bill to expire during transit: Without a valid extension, an expired E-Way Bill is treated the same as no E-Way Bill — goods are subject to detention and penalty
- Not generating E-Way Bill for branch transfers: Transfers between two GST registrations of the same company are taxable supplies — E-Way Bill is required above ₹50,000 even for intra-company movements

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Our goods are valued at ₹45,000 on the invoice. Do we still need an E-Way Bill if we add GST, making the total ₹53,100?** The consignment value for E-Way Bill threshold purposes is the invoice value including GST (taxable value + CGST + SGST/IGST + cess). If the total invoice value including GST exceeds ₹50,000, an E-Way Bill is required regardless of whether the pre-tax value was below ₹50,000. In your example, ₹53,100 (total with GST) exceeds the ₹50,000 threshold — an E-Way Bill is required.
- **We sell through a marketplace model (seller ships to customer directly). Who generates the E-Way Bill — us, the marketplace, or the customer?** In a marketplace model, E-Way Bill can be generated by the supplier (seller), the recipient, or the transporter — whoever initiates the movement. In bill-to-ship-to scenarios (where the marketplace invoices the end customer but the supplier ships to the customer), both the supplier's invoice and the marketplace's invoice are involved. CBIC has clarified that a single E-Way Bill referencing either invoice is acceptable. In practice, most marketplace platforms have integrated E-Way Bill generation into their systems — sellers should confirm the specific arrangement with the marketplace.
- **What happens if our driver is stopped at a check-point and the E-Way Bill is valid but the vehicle number is different from what's on the E-Way Bill?** This is a common situation during transhipment or vehicle breakdown. If the vehicle number on the E-Way Bill doesn't match the actual vehicle, the officer can detain the goods. To avoid this, update Part B on the E-Way Bill portal with the new vehicle number before the replacement vehicle commences the journey. In case of an unplanned vehicle change (breakdown), update the vehicle number on the portal as soon as possible and show the update to the officer — this demonstrates compliance and typically resolves the issue without penalty.
- **Is an E-Way Bill required for goods sent for job work?** Yes. Movement of goods for job work (from principal to job worker and back) requires an E-Way Bill if the value of goods exceeds ₹50,000. The document used is a delivery challan (not a tax invoice, since this is not a supply). The E-Way Bill is generated based on the delivery challan. One exception: for intra-state movement between a principal and a registered job worker within 50 km, some states have exempted the E-Way Bill requirement — check your specific state's notification.
- **Our E-Way Bill expired while the goods were in transit due to a flood in the route. What do we do?** Exceptional circumstances like natural calamities, floods, and accidents are grounds for E-Way Bill extension. The generator or transporter can extend the E-Way Bill validity through the portal — the extension must be initiated within 8 hours of expiry in case of force majeure circumstances. In practice, if the portal extension was not done in time due to the emergency, the transporter should carry evidence of the natural calamity (news reports, government advisories, route blockage certificates) and present them to the enforcement officer. The officer has discretion to consider such circumstances — and a DRC-01 representation can be filed if goods are detained unfairly.
