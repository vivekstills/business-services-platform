# GST Annual Return GSTR-10 Filing (Final Return)

## Overview

GSTR-10, commonly known as the Final Return, is a one-time GST return that must be filed by every taxpayer whose GST registration has been cancelled or surrendered. It is the last formal compliance requirement under a cancelled GSTIN — and it finalises all GST obligations, particularly the reversal of Input Tax Credit on closing stock of goods held as on the effective date of cancellation.
GSTR-10 is filed on the GST portal and must be submitted within 3 months from the date of the cancellation order or the date of cancellation — whichever is earlier. For example, if GST registration is cancelled with effective date October 31 and the cancellation order is issued on November 10, the GSTR-10 must be filed by February 10 (3 months from November 10).
GSTR-10 is applicable for:
All regular taxpayers whose registration has been cancelled voluntarily (Form GST REG-16) or suo motu by the GST officer
All composition scheme dealers whose registration has been cancelled

GSTR-10 is NOT applicable for:
Input Service Distributors
Non-Resident Taxable Persons (they file final GSTR-5)
Casual Taxable Persons

The GSTR-10 captures the closing stock details, ITC attributable to the closing stock (which must be reversed and paid), and the total tax payable. It is a critical compliance document — non-filing or late filing attracts significant penalties.

## Applicability

GSTR-10 (Final Return) is mandatory for all regular and composition GST-registered taxpayers who have:

- Voluntarily applied for cancellation of GST registration (Form GST REG-16)
- Had their GST registration cancelled by the GST officer (suo motu cancellation under Section 29(2))
- Had their registration cancelled pursuant to a court or tribunal order
- Undergone business transfer, merger, or dissolution requiring GSTIN cancellation

Applicable pan-India — all states and UTs.

## Features

- One-time filing: GSTR-10 is filed exactly once — after the GST registration is cancelled; it is not a periodic return
- Closing stock ITC reversal: The core of GSTR-10 — declaring the value of closing stock (inputs, semi-finished, finished goods, capital goods) as on the date of cancellation and reversing the ITC thereon
- ITC reversal computation: The amount to be paid = ITC in respect of inputs held in stock, or the output tax payable on such goods (at the applicable GST rate) — whichever is higher
- Late fee structure: If GSTR-10 is not filed within 3 months of the cancellation date, CBIC issues a notice in Form GSTR-10 directing filing within 15 days. Failure leads to assessment and best judgment determination of ITC reversal. Late fee: ₹200/day (₹100 CGST + ₹100 SGST), subject to a maximum of ₹10,000
- Pre-populated with cancellation data: The GSTR-10 form on the GST portal pre-populates the taxpayer's details and effective cancellation date from the cancellation order
- All pending GSTR-3Bs must be filed first: GSTR-10 cannot be filed if there are pending GSTR-3B returns — all regular returns up to the cancellation date must be filed before GSTR-10

## Benefits

- Clean GST closure: Filing GSTR-10 formally closes the taxpayer's GST obligations — no further liability or compliance requirement under the cancelled GSTIN
- Prevents officer-initiated assessment: If GSTR-10 is not filed within 3 months, the GST officer may make a best-judgment assessment of the ITC reversal liability — which could be higher than the actual liability computed by the taxpayer
- Legal finality: GSTR-10 creates a legal record of all closing stock and ITC reversal — protecting against future demands on the cancelled GSTIN
- Refund eligibility: If after ITC reversal there is still an excess balance in the Electronic Credit Ledger, it can be claimed as a refund after filing GSTR-10
- Unblocks portal for related entities: In group company structures, a cancelled GSTIN with unfiled GSTR-10 can sometimes cause compliance complications for related GSTINs — filing GSTR-10 resolves this

## Process

1. Step 1 — Complete All Pending GSTR-3B Filings Ensure GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B are filed for all return periods up to the effective date of GST registration cancellation. GSTR-10 cannot be submitted if there are pending GSTR-3Bs.
1. Step 2 — Identify Closing Stock Prepare a detailed inventory of all goods (inputs, semi-finished, finished, capital goods) held as on the effective date of cancellation. Include quantity, value, and HSN code.
1. Step 3 — Compute ITC Reversal For each item in closing stock: compute the ITC availed when the goods were purchased (from purchase invoices). Also compute the output tax payable on the closing stock at the applicable GST rate. The ITC reversal = higher of ITC on closing stock OR output tax on closing stock.
1. Step 4 — Log In and Access GSTR-10 Log in to the GST portal. Navigate to Returns → Final Return → GSTR-10.
1. Step 5 — Fill GSTR-10 The system auto-populates ARN of the cancellation order and the effective date. Enter details of closing stock (Table 7A — goods, Table 7B — capital goods), the ITC to be reversed, and the output tax payable.
1. Step 6 — Pay the ITC Reversal Amount If the Electronic Credit Ledger has sufficient balance, the reversal is debited from the credit ledger. If insufficient, pay the balance through the Electronic Cash Ledger (NEFT/RTGS/online payment on GST portal).
1. Step 7 — Submit and File GSTR-10 Submit the GSTR-10. The system requires DSC or EVC for filing. An ARN is generated on successful submission.
1. Step 8 — Claim Refund (if applicable) If post-reversal there is an excess credit balance in the Electronic Credit Ledger, file Form RFD-01 for refund of the excess.

## Compliance

- File GSTR-10 within 3 months of the cancellation date or order — whichever is earlier
- File all pending GSTR-3Bs before submitting GSTR-10
- Pay the ITC reversal amount before or at the time of filing GSTR-10
- Respond to GSTR-10 notices (issued by officer if not filed within 3 months) within the 15-day response window
- Archive all GSTR-10 records and supporting stock valuation documents for 6 years

## Common Mistakes

- Filing GSTR-10 before all GSTR-3Bs are filed: The portal will not allow GSTR-10 submission if there are pending GSTR-3B returns
- Ignoring GSTR-10 entirely: The most common mistake — businesses that cancel their GSTIN consider compliance done after the REG-16 application and completely overlook the mandatory GSTR-10 filing
- Incorrect closing stock valuation: Under-declaring closing stock to reduce ITC reversal liability — this is a compliance risk and can be detected in a GST audit
- Not claiming excess credit ledger refund: After ITC reversal in GSTR-10, if there's still excess credit balance, businesses often leave it unclaimed — this money is a legitimate refund
- Missing the 3-month deadline: The 3-month window from the cancellation date or order runs quickly — businesses that don't track this deadline end up with late fees of ₹200/day

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Our company's GST was cancelled by the officer (compulsory cancellation) because we didn't file returns for 6 months. We want to revoke the cancellation. Do we still need to file GSTR-10?** No — if you are applying for revocation of cancellation (to resume your GST registration), you should NOT file GSTR-10. GSTR-10 is the final return filed when you permanently close the GSTIN. To revoke a compulsorily cancelled registration, file all pending returns first and then file the revocation application (Form GST REG-21). GSTR-10 is only for when the cancellation is permanent and intended. Filing GSTR-10 when seeking revocation would permanently close the GSTIN, defeating the purpose.
- **We have no closing stock on the date of cancellation. Do we still need to file GSTR-10?** Yes. GSTR-10 is mandatory for all cancellation situations — even if you have zero closing stock. In this case, GSTR-10 is filed with NIL closing stock details — the ITC reversal will be zero. The filing is still a required legal compliance step to formally close the GST obligations under the cancelled GSTIN. Many businesses with zero stock skip GSTR-10, leading to late fee notices from the GST department.
- **We cancelled our GST registration 5 months ago and completely forgot to file GSTR-10. What should we do now?** File GSTR-10 immediately. The late fee will be ₹200/day for the days beyond the 3-month deadline (₹100 CGST + ₹100 SGST) — up to a maximum of ₹10,000. Also check if the GST officer has issued a best-judgment assessment notice (under Section 62 of the CGST Act) — if a notice has been received, respond and file GSTR-10 within the notice period. Continuing to ignore GSTR-10 after the 3-month deadline makes the officer's best-judgment assessment final — which may result in a higher ITC reversal demand than your actual liability.
- **Our GST was cancelled because we merged with another company. The merged entity's GSTIN is active. Who files GSTR-10 for the old entity?** The old entity (the merged/absorbed company) must file GSTR-10 for its cancelled GSTIN. This is typically the responsibility of the authorised signatory of the old entity — who continues to have access to the old GSTIN's GST portal credentials until GSTR-10 is filed and the cancellation is formally completed. In practice, the CFO or Company Secretary of the merged company's post-merger management handles this closure filing. Ensure GSTR-10 is filed as part of the merger completion checklist — it is often overlooked in M&A integration.
- **What happens to the unclaimed ITC in my Electronic Credit Ledger after GST cancellation?** The ITC balance in the Electronic Credit Ledger at the time of cancellation is first used to offset the ITC reversal liability (on closing stock). If the ITC reversal amount is less than the credit ledger balance, the remaining credit balance can be claimed as a refund through Form RFD-01 after filing GSTR-10. If the credit ledger balance is insufficient to cover the ITC reversal, the shortfall must be paid through the Electronic Cash Ledger. In no circumstance does the remaining ITC "lapse" without either being used for reversal or refunded — but you must actively file the refund claim; it is not automatically credited.
