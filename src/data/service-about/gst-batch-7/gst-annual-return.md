# GST Annual Return Filing (GSTR-9)

## Overview

GSTR-9 is the Annual Return that every regular GST-registered taxpayer is required to file for each financial year. It consolidates all the monthly/quarterly GST returns (GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B) filed during the year into a comprehensive annual summary — providing a complete picture of the taxpayer's outward supplies, inward supplies, ITC availed, taxes paid, and adjustments made during the financial year.
GSTR-9 is filed on the GST portal (gst.gov.in) and is due by December 31 of the year following the financial year for which it is filed. For example, GSTR-9 for FY 2024-25 is due by December 31, 2025.
The GST Council has provided various exemptions and simplifications for GSTR-9 over the years:

| Taxpayer Category | GSTR-9 Status |
| --- | --- |
| Turnover up to ₹2 crore | Exempt from GSTR-9 (optional filing) |
| Turnover above ₹2 crore | Mandatory GSTR-9 |
| Turnover above ₹5 crore | GSTR-9 + GSTR-9C (Self-Certified Reconciliation Statement) mandatory |
| Composition taxpayers | File GSTR-9A (separate annual return for composition dealers) |
| Input Service Distributors | GSTR-9 not applicable |
| Non-resident taxable persons | GSTR-9 not applicable |

GSTR-9C (previously requiring CA/CMA certification, now self-certified for turnover up to ₹5 crore, and requiring reconciliation for above ₹5 crore) is a reconciliation statement that compares the GSTR-9 data with the audited annual accounts.

## Applicability

GSTR-9 annual return is mandatory for:

- All regular GST-registered taxpayers with annual aggregate turnover above ₹2 crore
- Taxpayers registered as regular taxable persons throughout the financial year
- Taxpayers who were registered for part of the year (pro-rata period covered)

GSTR-9C (Reconciliation Statement) is additionally mandatory for:

- Taxpayers with annual aggregate turnover above ₹5 crore

GSTR-9 is NOT applicable for:

- Taxpayers with turnover up to ₹2 crore (optional)
- Composition scheme dealers (they file GSTR-9A)
- Input Service Distributors
- Casual taxable persons
- Non-resident taxable persons
- TDS deductors and TCS collectors

Applicable pan-India — all GST-registered businesses across all states and UTs must comply with GSTR-9 filing requirements.

## Features

- Consolidated annual summary: Aggregates all GSTR-1 (outward supply) and GSTR-3B (tax payment) data filed during the year into one comprehensive return
- ITC reconciliation: Part of GSTR-9 specifically captures ITC availed, ITC reversed, and ineligible ITC — providing a full-year ITC picture
- Amendment window: GSTR-9 provides an opportunity to declare and pay tax on any amendments, corrections, or additional liabilities identified after the regular returns were filed
- GSTR-9C reconciliation: The reconciliation statement compares GSTR-9 data with audited financials — surfacing discrepancies between accounting records and GST returns
- Pre-populated data: The GST portal pre-populates GSTR-9 with data from GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, and GSTR-2B — reducing data entry effort; pre-populated data can be edited where required
- Late fee structure: Late filing attracts ₹200 per day (₹100 CGST + ₹100 SGST), subject to a maximum of 0.25% of turnover in the state/UT
- Financial year based: One GSTR-9 per GSTIN per financial year — businesses with multiple GSTINs must file separate returns for each

## Benefits

- Statutory compliance: Avoids late fees, notices, and potential scrutiny from GST authorities for non-filing
- Error correction opportunity: Discrepancies identified between GSTR-1/3B and actual business data can be corrected in GSTR-9 by paying additional tax — preventing future demand notices
- ITC health check: The process of preparing GSTR-9 forces a comprehensive review of the year's ITC position — helping identify over-claimed or under-claimed credits
- Financial statement alignment: GSTR-9C preparation aligns GST returns with audited financials — improving overall accounting accuracy and audit readiness
- Litigation prevention: Accurate GSTR-9 filings reduce the scope for GST department to raise annual turnover or ITC discrepancy notices
- Banking and lending: Lenders increasingly cross-verify GSTR-9 data with ITR and financial statements for loan sanctions — a clean GSTR-9 supports creditworthiness
- Clean compliance record: A complete, accurate GSTR-9 filing history supports the business during M&A due diligence, IPO readiness, and large contract bidding

## Process

1. Step 1 — Data Compilation Compile all GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B filed during the FY. Download GSTR-2B for all months. Extract data from the accounting system — sales, purchases, ITC register.
1. Step 2 — Reconciliation Reconcile GSTR-1 data with the sales register; GSTR-3B ITC data with GSTR-2B and purchase register. Identify and explain all differences.
1. Step 3 — GSTR-9 Preparation Prepare the GSTR-9 return table-by-table — outward supplies (Tables 4 & 5), ITC availed (Table 6), ITC reversed (Table 7), other ITC (Table 8), tax paid (Table 9), and previous year adjustments (Tables 10 & 11).
1. Step 4 — GSTR-9C Preparation (if turnover above ₹5 crore) Prepare the GSTR-9C reconciliation statement — comparing GSTR-9 turnover with audited P&L turnover, identifying and explaining differences (deductions, non-GST supplies, advances, etc.).
1. Step 5 — Review and Finalisation Review the GSTR-9 (and GSTR-9C) for accuracy. Identify any additional tax liability and compute the interest (if applicable).
1. Step 6 — Additional Tax Payment (if required) Pay any additional tax liability identified during GSTR-9 preparation — through DRC-03 (voluntary payment form) or directly in GSTR-9.
1. Step 7 — Filing on GST Portal File GSTR-9 (and GSTR-9C) on the GST portal before December 31. Download and archive the ARN (Acknowledgement Reference Number).

## Compliance

- File GSTR-9 by December 31 of the succeeding financial year
- Turnover above ₹5 crore must also file GSTR-9C
- All GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B for the year must be filed before GSTR-9 can be submitted
- Pay any additional liability identified during GSTR-9 preparation to avoid interest
- Maintain all reconciliation workings and supporting data for at least 6 years
- GSTR-9 once filed cannot be revised — accuracy before submission is critical

## Common Mistakes

- Not reconciling GSTR-1 with sales register before GSTR-9 filing: Leads to incorrect turnover reporting in GSTR-9 that conflicts with audited financials
- Incorrect ITC reversal reporting: Missing ITC reversals under Rule 42 or Section 17(5) in GSTR-9 creates exposure for demand notices
- Ignoring previous year's adjustments: Amendments made in the current year relating to the previous year must be separately reported in Tables 10 and 11 — commonly omitted
- Filing GSTR-9 before all GSTR-3Bs are filed: GSTR-9 cannot capture accurate data if all monthly returns haven't been filed — always file all pending GSTR-3Bs first
- Not paying additional liability before filing: Reporting additional liability in GSTR-9 without paying it immediately attracts interest from the original due date

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Our turnover is ₹1.8 crore. Is GSTR-9 optional for us and should we still file it?** Yes, GSTR-9 is optional for taxpayers with annual aggregate turnover up to ₹2 crore. However, we recommend filing voluntarily for several reasons: (a) it creates a complete compliance record; (b) banks and NBFC lenders increasingly cross-check GSTR-9 data; (c) it provides an opportunity to identify and correct discrepancies between monthly returns and actual business data before they surface in departmental scrutiny; (d) as your business grows beyond ₹2 crore, a prior filing history avoids the first-year complexity of filing GSTR-9 without practice.
- **We discovered during GSTR-9 preparation that we over-claimed ₹3 lakh in ITC last year. How do we handle this in GSTR-9?** The over-claimed ITC must be reversed. Report the reversal in Table 7 of GSTR-9 (ITC Reversed). The additional tax liability arising from the reversal (plus interest at 18% p.a. from the date of original incorrect claim) should be paid through DRC-03 before or at the time of filing GSTR-9. Proactive disclosure and payment in GSTR-9 demonstrates good faith compliance and typically results in lower exposure than waiting for the department to detect and demand the reversal.
- **Is GSTR-9C still required to be certified by a Chartered Accountant?** For taxpayers with turnover above ₹5 crore, GSTR-9C is required as a self-certified reconciliation statement — meaning the taxpayer or their authorised signatory certifies the statement. CA/CMA certification is no longer mandatory for GSTR-9C (this change was made effective from FY 2020-21 onwards). However, engaging a CA or GST practitioner to prepare GSTR-9C is strongly advisable — the reconciliation between GST returns and audited accounts requires accounting expertise and errors in GSTR-9C can attract notices.
- **We have 5 GSTINs across 5 states. How many GSTR-9 returns do we need to file?** One GSTR-9 per GSTIN per financial year. If your business has 5 GSTINs (registered in 5 states), you must file 5 separate GSTR-9 returns — one for each GSTIN, reporting only the transactions of that GSTIN. The turnover threshold (₹2 crore for mandatory filing, ₹5 crore for GSTR-9C) is based on the aggregate turnover across all GSTINs of the PAN — not the individual GSTIN's turnover. So even if one GSTIN has a turnover of ₹80 lakh, if the total PAN-level turnover exceeds ₹2 crore, all GSTINs must file GSTR-9.
- **Can we amend or revise GSTR-9 after filing?** No. Once GSTR-9 is submitted and filed, it cannot be revised or amended. This is why thorough review before filing is absolutely critical. If you discover an error after filing, the recourse is limited — material errors (particularly underreporting of tax liability) should be disclosed and paid through DRC-03 proactively before the GST department detects them through scrutiny. For genuine errors, a representation can be made to the GST officer with supporting documentation during any subsequent scrutiny or audit.
