# GST LUT Filing

## Overview

A Letter of Undertaking (LUT) is a declaration filed by eligible exporters under GST, allowing them to make zero-rated supplies (exports of goods/services and supplies to Special Economic Zones — SEZs) without paying Integrated GST (IGST) at the time of export. Instead of paying IGST and then claiming a refund, exporters who have filed a valid LUT can export under bond/LUT and collect the refund of Input Tax Credit (ITC) accumulated due to zero-rated exports — a far more cash-flow friendly mechanism.
LUT filing is governed by Section 16 of the IGST Act, 2017 read with Rule 96A of the CGST Rules. The LUT is filed at the beginning of each financial year on the GST portal, and remains valid for the entire financial year. It was previously filed in a physical form (RFD-11) with the jurisdictional GST commissionerate — but since FY 2019-20, it has been entirely online on the GST portal.
Without a valid LUT, exporters must either:
Pay IGST on exports and claim a refund (refunds take time and block working capital), or
Obtain a bond (with bank guarantee) instead of LUT — a more cumbersome and costly option

LUT is the most practical and commonly used route for Indian exporters of goods and services.

## Applicability

LUT filing is available to:

- All GST-registered exporters of goods
- All GST-registered service exporters (IT companies, ITES/BPO, consultants, professionals, financial services)
- Suppliers making zero-rated supplies to Special Economic Zones (SEZs) — both supply of goods and services

LUT is NOT available to:

- Exporters who have been prosecuted for tax evasion involving an amount of ₹2.5 crore or above under the CGST Act or IGST Act. Such exporters must furnish a bond with bank guarantee instead of LUT

Who must file LUT:

- Every exporter/SEZ supplier who wants to export without paying GST must file LUT for each financial year — the LUT from the previous year is not carried forward

## Features

- Annual filing: LUT must be filed at the start of each financial year — valid for the entire FY (April 1 to March 31)
- Online filing: Completely online on the GST portal — no physical submission to the tax office
- No bank guarantee required: Unlike bonds, LUT does not require a bank guarantee — making it zero-cost and zero-collateral
- One LUT covers all exports: A single LUT filed covers all exports of goods and services — no separate LUT for each shipment or customer
- Acknowledged by system: GST portal generates an ARN (Acknowledgement Reference Number) upon LUT submission — this serves as the LUT certificate
- Export without IGST payment: Once LUT is filed, all exports and SEZ supplies can be made without charging or paying IGST
- ITC refund remains available: Accumulated ITC on inputs used for zero-rated exports can be claimed as a refund under the ITC refund route even with LUT
- E-commerce exports: Exporters selling through e-commerce platforms (Amazon Global, eBay, Etsy) can also use LUT for their export shipments

## Benefits

- Working capital efficiency: Eliminates the need to pay IGST on exports (often 18% on services — a significant cash outflow) and wait months for refunds — the LUT route keeps working capital within the business
- Zero cost compliance: LUT has no government fee and no bank guarantee requirement — it is the most cost-effective export compliance mechanism under GST
- Simplified export process: Customs clearance is smoother when exports are under LUT — shipping bills reference the LUT and IGST is marked as "NIL" — reducing documentation complexity
- Refund of accumulated ITC: Exporters with LUT can claim refund of ITC accumulated due to zero-rated exports — ensuring no ITC gets permanently stranded
- SEZ supply benefits: Indian companies supplying to SEZ customers can use LUT to supply without IGST — making them cost-competitive for SEZ procurement
- Cash flow for IT/service exporters: For IT and services companies with large foreign currency revenue, avoiding 18% IGST payment (even temporarily) saves crores in working capital — the LUT route is essential
- Hassle-free export documents: Shipping bills, invoices, and logistics documents are simpler without IGST — fewer reconciliations and corrections needed

## Process

1. Step 1 — GST Portal Login Log in to the GST portal (gst.gov.in) with the GSTIN credentials.
1. Step 2 — Navigate to LUT Section Go to Services → User Services → Furnish Letter of Undertaking (LUT).
1. Step 3 — Select Financial Year Select the financial year for which the LUT is being filed (LUT is valid for one FY — file at the start of each FY, ideally before March 31 of the prior FY or on April 1 of the new FY).
1. Step 4 — Fill LUT Form (RFD-11) Complete the online LUT form — confirm compliance with the conditions, provide details of the authorized person executing the LUT, and enter witness details (2 witnesses required).
1. Step 5 — Self-Declaration Confirm the self-declaration that the entity has not been prosecuted for GST tax evasion above ₹2.5 crore.
1. Step 6 — Sign and Submit Sign the LUT using DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) or EVC (Electronic Verification Code — OTP-based). Submit the form.
1. Step 7 — Download ARN The GST portal generates an ARN (Acknowledgement Reference Number) confirming the LUT filing. Download and save the ARN acknowledgement — this serves as the LUT certificate. Share the ARN with your AD bank (for foreign remittance compliance) and customs broker (for shipping bill preparation).

## Compliance

- File LUT for every financial year — the previous year's LUT does not automatically carry forward
- File LUT before commencing exports in the new FY — ideally on or before April 1
- If LUT is not filed and exports happen, IGST becomes payable on those exports — though the LUT can be filed retrospectively in practice with departmental permission
- Maintain all export documents (shipping bills, FIRC, bank realisation certificates) for 6 years
- File GSTR-1 correctly marking all exports as zero-rated — references to the LUT and shipping bill numbers in GSTR-1 are essential for refund claims
- Refund claims for accumulated ITC (under LUT route) must be filed in Form RFD-01 within 2 years of the relevant date

## Common Mistakes

- Not renewing LUT annually: Exporting without a valid LUT (using the previous year's LUT after March 31) — all such exports are technically without LUT, making IGST payable on them
- Using LUT without IEC for goods exports: LUT for goods export requires a valid IEC — exporters without IEC cannot export goods even with LUT
- Not referencing LUT ARN in shipping bills: Customs requires the LUT ARN to be referenced in shipping bills for zero-rated exports — missing this causes clearance issues and refund complications
- Claiming IGST refund route instead of ITC refund route: Exporters with LUT should claim the ITC refund (accumulated ITC refund) route — not the IGST refund route (which is for those who paid IGST on exports). Mixing up the two creates reconciliation problems
- Exporting to SEZ without LUT: SEZ supplies are zero-rated only if LUT is filed — supplying to SEZ without LUT means IGST must be paid and a refund claimed (cash flow impact)

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Our IT company provides software services to US clients. Do we need to file an LUT even though we don't export physical goods?** Yes, absolutely. Export of services (including software services, IT services, ITES, consulting) is zero-rated under GST — and to export services without paying IGST, an LUT must be filed. Without LUT, IGST at 18% would be payable on your foreign client invoices, and you would need to claim a refund — a cash flow burden of 18% on your export turnover. Filing LUT is one of the most impactful compliance steps for service exporters and is strongly recommended at the start of every financial year.
- **We forgot to file LUT for this FY and have already invoiced ₹50 lakh in exports. What do we do now?** File the LUT immediately on the GST portal for the current FY. While the GST law technically requires the LUT to be filed before exports commence, the GST department has in practice accepted retrospective LUT filings where the taxpayer demonstrates bona fide intention to comply. For the exports already invoiced without LUT, IGST is technically payable — but many exporters in this situation file LUT and continue exports going forward, maintaining a position that the earlier exports were in the spirit of zero-rated treatment. For material amounts, seek professional advice on whether to pay IGST on prior exports and claim a refund, or take a defended position with LUT retrospective filing.
- **Can we use both the LUT route and the IGST payment route for exports in the same year?** Technically, once LUT is filed, the exporter is expected to use the LUT route (zero IGST on exports, claim accumulated ITC refund). However, there is no bar on using the IGST payment route for some shipments in the same year — different shipping bills can reference either IGST payment or LUT. In practice, mixing routes creates reconciliation complexity in GSTR-1 and refund applications. It is cleaner and simpler to consistently use one route throughout the financial year.
- **What is the difference between an LUT and a Bond under GST export compliance?** Both LUT and Bond allow exporters to export without paying IGST. The difference is: (a) LUT requires no bank guarantee — it is a simple self-declaration by the exporter undertaking to fulfill export obligations; (b) Bond requires a bank guarantee (typically 15% of the bond amount) — making it more costly and administratively complex. LUT is available to all regular exporters who have not been prosecuted for tax evasion above ₹2.5 crore. Bond is for exporters who don't qualify for LUT. Virtually all exporters prefer LUT — bonds are rarely used.
- **LUT was filed in April but we stopped exporting by June and cancelled our GST registration in September. Do we need to do anything about the LUT?** If your GST registration is cancelled, the LUT automatically becomes irrelevant — you cannot export without a GST registration. No formal cancellation of the LUT is required. Ensure that your final GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B correctly reflect all exports made under LUT during the period it was active, and file any pending refund claims for accumulated ITC from those zero-rated exports before your final return is filed under the GST registration cancellation process.
