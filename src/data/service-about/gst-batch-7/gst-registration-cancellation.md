# GST Registration Cancellation

## Overview

GST Registration Cancellation is the formal process of surrendering a GSTIN — either voluntarily by the taxpayer or compulsorily by the GST officer. Upon cancellation, the taxpayer is no longer required to file GST returns or pay GST going forward. However, the cancellation process involves important closing obligations — including filing pending returns, reversing ITC on remaining stock, and paying the tax on closing inventory.
Voluntary Cancellation (initiated by the taxpayer) is done in Form GST REG-16 and is applicable when:
The business is closed, shut down, or transferred
The business has been deregistered or amalgamated with another entity
The taxpayer's turnover has permanently fallen below the registration threshold
The business has changed its constitution (e.g., sole proprietor converting to company — old GSTIN is cancelled, new one registered)
Compulsory Cancellation (initiated by the GST officer) under Section 29(2) of the CGST Act occurs when:
The taxpayer has not filed returns for 6 consecutive months (regular taxpayer)
The taxpayer has not filed returns for 3 consecutive quarters (composition taxpayer)
The taxpayer obtained registration by fraud, misrepresentation, or suppression of facts
The taxpayer does not conduct business from the declared place of business
After cancellation, if the taxpayer wishes to resume business and GST compliance, they can apply for Revocation of Cancellation (covered in a separate service page).

## Applicability

GST Registration Cancellation applies to:

- Businesses shutting down or winding up operations
- Businesses transferred through slump sale, merger, or demerger (seller's GSTIN is cancelled)
- Proprietorships where the proprietor has died and business is not being continued
- Businesses whose turnover has permanently fallen below the GST threshold
- Entities changing their legal form (e.g., proprietor to company — old GSTIN cancelled, new registered)
- Businesses that registered voluntarily but no longer wish to remain registered

Applicable pan-India — all GST-registered entities across all states and UTs.

## Features

- Voluntary cancellation via GST REG-16: Taxpayer-initiated cancellation application filed on the GST portal
- 30-day processing: GST officer must process the cancellation application within 30 days of receipt
- Final return filing: Upon cancellation, the taxpayer must file GSTR-10 (Final Return) within 3 months of the cancellation effective date or order date
- ITC reversal on closing stock: At the time of cancellation, the taxpayer must pay an amount equal to ITC on closing stock of inputs, semi-finished goods, and finished goods (or output tax thereon — whichever is higher) — this is a critical and often overlooked obligation
- Cancellation effective date: Voluntary cancellations can be effective from a future date chosen by the taxpayer or the date of filing the application — the officer determines the effective date
- Suspension during proceedings: The GST officer may first suspend the registration (GST REG-17) and issue a show-cause notice before compulsory cancellation
- Interest on pending liability: Any GST liability pending at the time of cancellation must be paid with interest before or at the time of filing the final return

## Benefits

- End of compliance obligations: Once cancelled, no further GST returns need to be filed — reducing administrative burden and professional fees for businesses that have ceased operations
- Clean closure: A formally cancelled GSTIN creates a clean regulatory record — important for legal closure of the business entity
- Avoid penalties for non-filing: If a business has ceased operations but the GSTIN remains active, non-filing of returns attracts penalties — cancellation stops this penalty accumulation
- Financial clarity: The ITC reversal and final return process creates a clear accounting of all GST obligations at closure — preventing future demands
- No GST liability post-cancellation: After effective cancellation date, GST is neither charged on supplies nor is return filing required — legal and financial certainty

## Process

1. Step 1 — File All Pending Returns Before applying for cancellation, ensure all pending GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B returns are filed up to the period immediately before the desired cancellation date.
1. Step 2 — Compute ITC Reversal on Closing Stock Calculate the ITC reversal obligation on the closing stock of inputs, semi-finished goods, and finished goods as on the cancellation date. The reversal amount is the ITC attributable to closing stock or output tax thereon — whichever is higher.
1. Step 3 — File GST REG-16 Log in to the GST portal. Go to Registration → Application for Cancellation. Complete Form GST REG-16 — select reason, enter cancellation effective date, provide closing stock details, and the ITC reversal amount.
1. Step 4 — Pay ITC Reversal Amount If the ITC reversal amount is payable (i.e., credit ledger balance is insufficient), pay the amount through DRC-03 or in GSTR-3B for the final period.
1. Step 5 — GST Officer Processing The officer reviews the cancellation application within 30 days. If satisfied, issues Form GST REG-19 (Cancellation Order) specifying the effective date of cancellation.
1. Step 6 — File GSTR-10 (Final Return) After cancellation order is issued, file GSTR-10 (Final Return) within 3 months of the cancellation date or order date. GSTR-10 confirms the ITC reversal on closing stock and finalises all GST obligations.
1. Step 7 — Archive GSTIN Records Maintain all GST records (returns, invoices, ITC registers) for 6 years from the date of cancellation — GST authorities can still audit past periods.

## Compliance

- File GSTR-10 (Final Return) within 3 months of the effective date of cancellation — non-filing attracts penalties
- All GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B must be filed before the cancellation application
- ITC reversal on closing stock is a mandatory obligation — not optional
- After cancellation, do not collect GST from customers — issuing a tax invoice after the effective date of cancellation and collecting GST is an offence
- Maintain all records for 6 years from the date of the last return filed

## Common Mistakes

- Applying for cancellation without filing all pending returns: The GST portal will not process the cancellation application if there are pending returns
- Not computing ITC reversal on closing stock: Cancelling without reversing ITC on closing stock leaves a pending liability that the department can demand with interest later
- Forgetting to file GSTR-10: The final return (GSTR-10) is separate from the regular returns and is a mandatory post-cancellation filing — widely overlooked by businesses
- Collecting GST after the effective cancellation date: After cancellation, any GST collected from customers must still be deposited with the government — but continuing to charge GST without a valid GSTIN is an offence
- Not maintaining records after cancellation: GST records must be maintained for 6 years even after the GSTIN is cancelled — audits of past periods are possible

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Our company is being liquidated under the Companies Act. When and how should we cancel our GSTIN?** In a voluntary liquidation, the company's GST registration should be cancelled once the decision to wind up is taken and all business operations have ceased. The liquidator is responsible for filing the cancellation application (GST REG-16) on behalf of the company, filing all pending GST returns, paying all outstanding GST liabilities, reversing ITC on closing stock, and filing GSTR-10. The GST cancellation should ideally be coordinated with the final settlement of assets and liabilities under the liquidation — as the ITC reversal on closing stock depends on the inventory position at the time of cancellation.
- **We have ₹8 lakh in the Electronic Credit Ledger. Can we get a refund of this ITC credit before cancellation?** Yes. If you have excess ITC in the Electronic Credit Ledger that exceeds your outstanding GST liabilities, you can apply for a refund of the excess credit under Section 54(1) of the CGST Act — "refund of balance in the electronic credit ledger." File Form RFD-01 for this refund before applying for cancellation. Alternatively, apply for refund and cancellation simultaneously — the refund will be processed as part of the cancellation settlement. Do not cancel first and then try to claim the refund — it becomes significantly more complex after cancellation.
- **We transferred our business to a new company. Do we need to cancel the old GSTIN?** Yes. When a business is transferred — through slump sale, demerger, or any other mechanism — the transferor's GSTIN must be cancelled as the business entity ceases to operate under the old structure. The transferee (new company) must either register for a new GSTIN or include the transferred business in its existing GST registration. In the cancellation application (GST REG-16), the reason cited would be "Transfer of business" and the details of the transferee entity should be provided. ITC balances can be transferred to the transferee under the GST ITC transfer provisions on merger/demerger.
- **Can I cancel GST registration on a specific future date?** Yes. In Form GST REG-16, the applicant can specify the desired date of cancellation — which can be a future date. The GST officer, upon processing, may accept this date or fix a different effective date. Common scenarios for future-dated cancellation include: businesses that want to complete all current orders/contracts under the existing GSTIN before closure; businesses that want to ensure all annual returns (GSTR-9) are filed before the GSTIN is cancelled; and businesses timing the cancellation to align with their financial year-end for cleaner accounting.
- **If my GSTIN is cancelled, can I still claim ITC on invoices received before the cancellation date?** The right to claim ITC on invoices received before the cancellation date depends on whether those invoices were reflected in GSTR-3B before cancellation. Generally, ITC must be claimed in the GSTR-3B for the period in which the supply was received — so ITC on pre-cancellation invoices should have been claimed in the regular GSTR-3Bs filed before the cancellation. After the final return (GSTR-10) is filed, there is no mechanism to claim additional ITC. This is another reason to ensure all pending ITC is captured in the last GSTR-3B before the cancellation effective date.
