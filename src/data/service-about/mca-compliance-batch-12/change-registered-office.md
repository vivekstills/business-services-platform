# Change Registered Office of Company

## Detailed Overview

The Registered Office of a company is its official address for receiving statutory communications, government notices, legal documents, and stakeholder correspondence. Change of registered office is a common corporate action — driven by office relocations, business expansion, or strategic reasons.

The procedure under Section 12 and 13 of the Companies Act, 2013 varies based on the scope of change:

- Within the same city — simple board resolution
- Within the same state but different city — board + shareholder approval
- From one state to another — RD approval, public notice, creditor consents

## Applicability

Applicable to all companies — private, public, OPC, Section 8, etc. Pan-India.

## Compliance Requirements

| Change Type | Approval Required | Form |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Within same city/town | Board resolution | INC-22 (within 30 days) |
| Within same state, different ROC jurisdiction | Special resolution | MGT-14 + INC-22 |
| One state to another | Special resolution + RD approval | MGT-14 + INC-23 + INC-22 |

## Benefits

- Operational alignment with business location
- Cost optimisation (relocating from expensive to affordable areas)
- Better tax/regulatory environment in some states
- Strategic positioning

## Process / Procedure

### Within same city

- Board resolution approving change
- File INC-22 within 30 days

### Within same state but different RoC

- Board resolution + EGM approval (special resolution)
- File MGT-14
- File INC-22 within 30 days

### State to state change

- Special resolution
- File MGT-14
- Public notice in newspapers
- Apply to RD via INC-23 with creditor and member consent
- RD approval (typically 30–60 days)
- File INC-22 with new RoC
- Update company records

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can registered office be at a residential address?** Yes, particularly common for startups and small companies. Society NOC and proper utility bill documentation are needed. Some commercial activities may not be permitted at residential addresses.
2. **Can the registered office be a virtual office or coworking space?** Yes — if the virtual office provider issues proper NOC and utility bills, MCA accepts coworking and virtual office addresses. This is common practice in tech and consulting sectors.
3. **What's the difference between registered office and corporate office?** Registered office is the legal address for MCA/government communications. Corporate office is the operational headquarters where business is conducted. They can be the same or different.
4. **How long does state-to-state change take and why?** 60–180 days typically. The process involves: special resolution, public notice (30 days for objections), RD approval, and final filing — multiple stages requiring sequential clearances.
5. **Does change of registered office require updating GST registration?** Yes. Change of registered office affects GST records. File GST amendment (Form REG-14) within 15 days of address change.
