# MSME-1 Form Filing

## Detailed Overview

Form MSME-1 is the half-yearly return required to be filed by every "specified company" (defined as a company that receives goods or services from a Micro or Small Enterprise) under Section 405 of the Companies Act, 2013 read with Companies (Furnishing of Information about Payment to Micro and Small Enterprises) Order, 2019.

The form captures details of payments due to MSME suppliers exceeding 45 days from acceptance — protecting MSMEs from delayed payments by larger companies and ensuring compliance with the MSMED Act, 2006.

## Applicability

| Filer | Trigger |
| --- | --- |
| All companies | Receiving supplies from MSMEs |
| Particularly applicable to | Companies with significant procurement from small vendors |

Companies must file even if there are no outstanding dues to MSMEs (NIL return).

## Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Form | MSME-1 |
| Frequency | Half-yearly |
| Due dates | April 30 (for October–March period); October 31 (for April–September period) |
| Information | Outstanding dues to MSMEs > 45 days as on the period-end |
| Director DSC | For signing |

## Benefits

- Compliance with MSME protection regulations
- Avoids penalties under Companies Act
- Demonstrates ethical procurement practices
- Aligns with government MSME support agenda

## Process / Procedure

- Identify all MSME suppliers (verify Udyam registrations)
- Identify outstanding payments > 45 days as on period end
- Compile list with invoice details and outstanding amounts
- Prepare Form MSME-1
- File on MCA portal by deadline

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **How do we identify MSME suppliers?** Suppliers should provide their Udyam Registration Number. Without Udyam registration, suppliers don't qualify as MSMEs and aren't tracked in MSME-1.
2. **What if there are no outstanding payments to MSMEs?** A NIL Form MSME-1 should be filed. Filing is mandatory regardless of whether dues exist.
3. **Are dues to medium enterprises (above small) tracked?** No. MSME-1 is specifically for Micro and Small enterprise dues. Medium enterprise dues are not tracked.
4. **What if an MSME supplier hasn't claimed Udyam registration but qualifies?** The MSMED Act protections apply only to registered MSMEs. Without Udyam, supplier doesn't get statutory protections — but commercial best practice is to track and pay all suppliers timely.
5. **Can interest be charged on MSME dues > 45 days?** Yes, the MSMED Act allows MSMEs to claim interest at 3x the bank rate (compounding) on overdue payments. Significant cost to defaulting buyers.
