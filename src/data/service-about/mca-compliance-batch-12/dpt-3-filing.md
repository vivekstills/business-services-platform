# DPT-3 Form Filing

## Detailed Overview

Form DPT-3 is the prescribed form under Section 73 of the Companies Act, 2013 read with Rule 16 of the Companies (Acceptance of Deposits) Rules, 2014, for filing the annual return of deposits (and exempt deposits) by every company. The form captures details of deposits, money received but not considered as deposit (loans from directors, share application money pending allotment, advances from customers, etc.), and outstanding amounts.

DPT-3 is a critical compliance for tracking unsecured loans, deposits, and other monies received by companies — preventing illegal deposit acceptance and ensuring transparency in company finances.

## Applicability

Mandatory for all companies (except government companies) that have received money from any source other than:

- Issue of shares/bonds
- Loans from banks/NBFCs

Including loans from directors, related parties, customers (advances), employees, etc.

## Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Form | DPT-3 |
| Due date | June 30 each year (annual return) |
| Information period | Money received during the FY |
| Outstanding amounts | As on March 31 |
| Auditor certification | Required |
| Director DSC | For form signing |

## Benefits

- Statutory compliance with deposit regulations
- Transparency in financial dealings
- Prevention of disguised illegal deposit acceptance
- Required for clean compliance record

## Risks / Penalties

| Default | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Non-filing | ₹100 per day late fee |
| Inaccurate information | Penalty on company and officers in default |
| Continued default | Up to ₹5 crore on company; ₹2 lakh on officers |

| Customer advance details | If significant |
| Employee deposit details | If applicable |

## Process / Procedure

- Compile all amounts received during the year (other than from share issue and bank loans)
- Categorise — deposits, exempt deposits, loans from directors, customer advances
- Get auditor's certificate
- Prepare Form DPT-3
- Director DSC signing
- File with MCA by June 30

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Are loans from directors considered deposits requiring DPT-3?** Loans from directors are exempted deposits, but still must be reported in DPT-3. They are listed under "money received not considered as deposits."
2. **Are customer advances reported in DPT-3?** Yes, customer advances received that have been outstanding beyond 365 days (or specific timelines) are reported. Short-term advances tied to specific contracts are typically exempt.
3. **Does an inactive company need to file DPT-3?** If the company has any outstanding loans/deposits as on March 31, yes. NIL DPT-3 is required if no such amounts exist but the company is otherwise active.
4. **Can DPT-3 be filed without an auditor's certificate?** No. The auditor's certificate confirming the deposit information is mandatory for DPT-3 filing.
5. **What happens if a company illegally accepts deposits?** Penalty up to ₹5 crore, repayment of deposits with interest, and possible criminal proceedings. Director's personal liability can also arise. Strict compliance with deposit rules is essential.
