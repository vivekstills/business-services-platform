# Close a Company

## Detailed Overview

Closing a company in India can be done through multiple legal pathways depending on the company's status, financial position, and reason for closure:

- Strike-off (Voluntary) — Section 248(2) under Companies Act for defunct companies
- Winding up — Section 270 onwards (judicial process)
- Insolvency proceedings — IBC, 2016 for insolvent companies
- Fast-track exit — for companies meeting specific criteria

The most common method for clean, debt-free, non-operational companies is voluntary strike-off via Form STK-2.

## Applicability

Applicable to all companies wishing to legally close operations. The chosen pathway depends on:

- Operational status (active vs. dormant)
- Financial health (solvent vs. insolvent)
- Asset and liability position
- Stakeholder consent

## Compliance Requirements

| Closure Method | Pre-conditions |
| --- | --- |
| Strike-off (STK-2) | No business activity for 2 years; no liabilities; all returns filed; no pending litigation |
| Winding up | Various grounds; Tribunal involvement |
| IBC | Insolvent; CIRP/Liquidation process |
| Fast-track exit | Operational up to 1 year only; minimal activity |

## Benefits

- Legal closure with no future liabilities
- End of MCA compliance obligations
- Director freed from company-related responsibilities
- Recovery of any frozen funds

## Risks / Penalties

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Pending compliance creates issues | Clear all defaults before closure |
| Pending litigation | Complete or settle before strike-off |
| Director disqualification (Section 164) | Ensure all filings done before strike-off application |
| Asset distribution issues | Properly distribute remaining assets |

## Documents Required (For Strike-off)

| Document | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Form STK-2 | Strike-off application |
| Indemnity bond by directors | Protection against future claims |
| Affidavit by directors | Confirmation of inactive status |
| Statement of accounts | Recent financials showing no liabilities |
| Special resolution | Shareholder approval (if applicable) |
| All pending returns | AOC-4, MGT-7 must be up to date |
| NOC from regulators | If applicable (banks, etc.) |

## Process / Procedure (Strike-off process)

- Verify eligibility (no activity for 2 years, no liabilities, all filings done)
- Board resolution approving closure
- Special resolution (where required)
- Settle all liabilities
- Distribute remaining assets to shareholders
- File Form STK-2 with all attachments
- RoC publishes notice in Gazette
- Public objections invited (30 days)
- RoC strike-off order if no objections
- Company name struck from register

## Timeline

4–6 months typically for strike-off. Winding up: 1–3 years. IBC: 6–24 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can a company with assets be closed via strike-off?** The company should distribute all assets before strike-off. Any assets remaining at strike-off vest with the government. Cleanly distribute assets to shareholders before applying.
2. **What happens to a struck-off company's bank accounts?** They are frozen. Banks close the accounts upon notification of strike-off. Distribute funds before strike-off.
3. **Can a struck-off company be revived?** Yes — Section 252 of Companies Act allows revival application within 20 years through NCLT, on grounds like wrongful strike-off or having continued business. Process is complex.
4. **Are directors of struck-off companies disqualified?** If the company was struck off due to non-filing of MCA forms for 3+ years, directors are disqualified under Section 164(2) for 5 years. Voluntary strike-off doesn't lead to disqualification.
5. **What's the difference between strike-off and winding up?** Strike-off is administrative — RoC removes the company name from register; suitable for clean, defunct companies. Winding up is judicial — Tribunal-supervised liquidation; needed for companies with disputes, liabilities, or contested closures.
