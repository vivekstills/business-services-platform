# Auditor Appointment (Form ADT-1 Filing)

## Detailed Overview

The appointment of an auditor is a mandatory annual compliance under Section 139 of the Companies Act, 2013, requiring every company to appoint an auditor for conducting the statutory audit of its financial statements. The first auditor is appointed by the Board within 30 days of incorporation; subsequent auditors are appointed by shareholders at each AGM for a 5-year term (typically).

Form ADT-1 is the prescribed MCA form used to intimate the auditor's appointment to the Registrar of Companies. Filing ADT-1 is the formal acknowledgement of the audit engagement and is a foundational compliance for every company.

## Applicability

Applicable to all companies — private limited, public limited, Section 8, OPC, producer company. Pan-India.

## Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| First auditor | Appointed by board within 30 days of incorporation |
| Subsequent auditors | Appointed at AGM for 5 years |
| Form ADT-1 | Filed within 15 days of appointment |
| Auditor consent | Form ADT-2 (auditor's consent) |
| Auditor eligibility | Section 141 — independence, qualifications |
| Rotation | Mandatory for listed companies and large unlisted (5/10 year cycle) |

## Benefits

- Statutory compliance with audit requirements
- Independent verification of financial statements
- Lender/investor confidence
- Detection and prevention of frauds
- Tax compliance and credibility

## Risks / Penalties

| Default | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Late ADT-1 filing | ₹100 per day |
| No auditor appointed | Company in default; financial statements unaudited; cannot file AOC-4 |
| Wrong appointment (ineligible auditor) | Appointment void; reappointment required |

## Documents Required

| Document | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Auditor's consent (ADT-2) | Auditor's willingness to accept appointment |
| Auditor's eligibility certificate | Confirming Section 141 compliance |
| Board resolution / Shareholder resolution | Approving appointment |
| AGM minutes | For shareholder appointments |
| Auditor's PAN and FRN | Firm Registration Number for CA firms |
| DSC of director | For ADT-1 signing |

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Auditor Identification: Identify CA/CA firm meeting Section 141 eligibility (independence, no conflicts).
2. Step 2 — Auditor Consent: Obtain Form ADT-2 from auditor — consent and eligibility certificate.
3. Step 3 — Board/Shareholder Approval: First auditor by board (within 30 days of incorporation); subsequent at AGM by shareholders.
4. Step 4 — File ADT-1: Within 15 days of appointment, file ADT-1 with MCA.
5. Step 5 — Audit Engagement: Auditor commences audit work.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can the same auditor be reappointed indefinitely?** For listed companies and certain unlisted public companies, mandatory rotation applies — individual auditor maximum 5 years; firm maximum 10 years. After this, a cooling-off period of 5 years applies. For other companies, reappointment without rotation is permitted.
2. **What is Form ADT-2 vs ADT-1?** ADT-2 is the auditor's written consent and eligibility certificate (auditor's document). ADT-1 is the company's intimation of appointment to MCA. Both are required.
3. **Can a company appoint the same auditor for both statutory audit and tax audit?** Yes. There's no statutory bar on the same CA/firm conducting both statutory audit (under Companies Act) and tax audit (under Income Tax Act). However, independence considerations should be evaluated.
4. **What happens if a company doesn't appoint an auditor at AGM?** The Comptroller and Auditor General (C&AG) for government companies, or the Tribunal in some cases, may appoint an auditor. The company is in default. Practical resolution is to convene EGM and appoint promptly.
5. **Can an auditor resign mid-term and what's the process?** Yes. Auditor can resign by filing Form ADT-3 within 30 days of resignation. The company must appoint a new auditor within 30 days of receiving the resignation notice — through a board meeting and confirmation at the next general meeting.
