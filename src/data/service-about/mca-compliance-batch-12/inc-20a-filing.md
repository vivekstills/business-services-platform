# Commencement Form Filing (INC-20A)

## Detailed Overview

Form INC-20A is the Declaration for Commencement of Business filed by every company incorporated on or after November 2, 2018, under Section 10A of the Companies Act, 2013. The form is a declaration that the company has received subscription money from all subscribers and has commenced business activities.

INC-20A must be filed within 180 days of the date of incorporation. Until INC-20A is filed, the company cannot:

- Commence business activities
- Borrow money
- Exercise any borrowing powers

## Applicability

Mandatory for:

- All companies incorporated on or after November 2, 2018
- Companies with share capital (private and public limited)
- Section 8 companies (with conditions)

Not required for:

- OPC with sole subscriber (subscription is upfront)
- Companies incorporated before November 2, 2018

## Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Filing deadline | Within 180 days of incorporation |
| Subscription verification | All subscribers to MoA must have paid subscription money |
| Bank statement | Showing receipt of share subscription |
| Board declaration | Confirming commencement |
| Form INC-20A | Filed with MCA |

## Benefits

- Authorisation to commence business
- Enables borrowing and financial transactions
- Required for opening of bank current account in some banks
- Compliance for GST registration in some cases
- Critical for fundraising and operations

## Risks / Penalties

| Default | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Non-filing within 180 days | Company cannot commence business; ₹50,000 penalty on company |
| Continued non-compliance | ₹1,000 per day on directors (max ₹1 lakh) |
| Strike-off risk | After 180 days of non-compliance, RoC may strike off |

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Open company bank account.
2. Step 2 — All subscribers deposit their subscription amount.
3. Step 3 — Auditor verifies and issues certificate.
4. Step 4 — Board passes resolution declaring commencement.
5. Step 5 — File INC-20A within 180 days of incorporation.
6. Step 6 — Receive acknowledgement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can a company operate without filing INC-20A?** Legally no. Until INC-20A is filed, the company is restricted from commencing business or borrowing. Continuing operations without filing is non-compliant and risks penalties.
2. **What if subscribers haven't paid subscription within 180 days?** The company should follow up with subscribers. Without subscription, INC-20A cannot be filed. After 180 days, the company faces strike-off risk. Subscription delays are a serious compliance issue.
3. **Is INC-20A required for OPC?** For OPC with single subscriber, the subscription is typically paid at incorporation, making INC-20A largely redundant. However, technical filing may still be required — check current MCA position.
4. **Can subscription be in form of assets instead of cash?** Yes, but with proper valuation and AOA provisions. Asset-in-kind subscription is common in startup founder share allotments. Auditor verification is critical.
5. **What if directors face the ₹1,000 per day penalty?** The penalty accumulates from day 181. Directors face personal liability up to ₹1 lakh maximum. Filing INC-20A immediately stops the accumulation.
