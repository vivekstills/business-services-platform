# AOA Amendment

## Detailed Overview

The Articles of Association (AOA) is the foundational document of a company that defines the internal rules of management — governance structure, share capital provisions, director powers, meetings, dividends, transfer of shares, and operational rules. Amendment of AOA is governed by Section 14 of the Companies Act, 2013, and requires shareholder approval through a special resolution (75% majority).

AOA amendments are common during company life cycle events — fundraising rounds (introducing investor protection clauses), conversion from private to public, modifying share transfer restrictions, changing borrowing limits, and altering management provisions.

## Applicability

Applicable to all companies — private limited, public limited, Section 8, OPC. Pan-India.

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Board Approval: Board passes resolution approving the proposed AOA amendment and convening EGM.
2. Step 2 — Notice of EGM: Issue 21 clear days notice to all shareholders with explanatory statement explaining the proposed changes.
3. Step 3 — EGM: Convene EGM. Special resolution passed with 75%+ majority.
4. Step 4 — Amended AOA: Prepare new AOA incorporating the amendments.
5. Step 5 — File MGT-14: Within 30 days of resolution, file Form MGT-14 with MCA, attaching the special resolution and amended AOA.
6. Step 6 — Register Update: Update company's statutory register and AOA copy at registered office.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can AOA be amended without shareholder approval?** No. AOA amendment requires special resolution of shareholders (75% majority). Board cannot unilaterally amend AOA — fundamental governance principle.
2. **Are there any AOA clauses that cannot be amended?** Most clauses can be amended through special resolution. However, certain alterations affecting class rights of shareholders (e.g., preference shareholders) require additional approvals — class meetings and consent of the affected class.
3. **Does AOA amendment require RoC approval?** No prior approval required from RoC. AOA amendments are filed with RoC for record purposes (via MGT-14). However, certain amendments (like change of name or capital alteration) require additional MCA approvals.
4. **Can a new investor in a Series A round require AOA amendments?** Yes. Investor protection rights (anti-dilution, drag-along, tag-along, liquidation preference, board representation) are typically incorporated through AOA amendments alongside the Shareholders' Agreement (SHA). This is standard in startup fundraising.
5. **What happens if MGT-14 is not filed for an AOA amendment?** The amendment doesn't become legally effective until filed with MCA. The company faces penalties and the amendment cannot be relied upon in legal proceedings or subsequent corporate actions.
