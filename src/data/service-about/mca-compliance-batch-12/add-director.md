# Add Directors

## Detailed Overview

Adding a director to a company involves the formal appointment of a new director through compliance with provisions of the Companies Act, 2013 — primarily Sections 149 to 167 — and filing the necessary forms with the Registrar of Companies. Adding directors is a routine corporate action that companies undertake as they grow, raise funding, induct independent directors, or restructure their boards.

The process involves identifying the candidate, obtaining DIN (Director Identification Number) and DSC (Digital Signature Certificate), passing board/shareholder resolutions, and filing Form DIR-12 with the MCA within 30 days of appointment.

## Applicability

Director addition is applicable to all companies — private limited, public limited, OPC (with restrictions on number of directors), Section 8, and producer companies. Applicable pan-India.

| Company Type | Director Limits |
| --- | --- |
| Private Limited | Min 2, Max 15 |
| Public Limited | Min 3, Max 15 |
| OPC | Min 1, Max 15 (only one nominated by sole member) |
| Section 8 | Min 2 (or as per Articles) |

Beyond 15, special resolution required.

## Process / Procedure

1. Step 1 — Candidate Identification and Background Check: Identify candidate, verify they meet eligibility under Section 164 (no disqualifications).
2. Step 2 — Obtain DIN: If candidate doesn't have DIN, apply via Form DIR-3.
3. Step 3 — Obtain DSC: Class 3 DSC from a Certifying Authority (eMudhra, Sify, etc.).
4. Step 4 — Director Consent: Candidate signs Form DIR-2 (consent to act as director).
5. Step 5 — Board Meeting: Convene board meeting to approve appointment. Pass board resolution.
6. Step 6 — Shareholder Approval (if required): For appointment beyond 15 directors, additional directors, or independent directors.
7. Step 7 — File DIR-12: Within 30 days of appointment, file Form DIR-12 with MCA.
8. Step 8 — Update Company Records: Update statutory register of directors, MCA portal records.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can a foreign national be added as director of an Indian company?** Yes. Foreign nationals can serve as directors of Indian companies. They need to obtain Indian DIN, DSC, and ensure that at least one director on the board is a resident Indian (i.e., has stayed in India for 182+ days in the previous calendar year) — this is a statutory requirement under Section 149(3).
2. **What is the difference between an Independent Director and a regular director?** Independent Directors are non-executive directors who don't have a material/pecuniary relationship with the company beyond directorship — they provide objective oversight. Mandatory for listed companies and certain unlisted public companies. Regular directors can be promoters, executives, or shareholders' nominees.
3. **Can a person who is a director in 20 other companies be appointed as director?** The Companies Act limits public company directorships to 10 (Section 165). Total directorships (including private companies) cannot exceed 20. Exceeding these limits disqualifies the candidate from appointment.
4. **Does adding a director require shareholder approval?** Shareholder approval through ordinary resolution is generally required for: (a) appointment of additional directors as per Articles; (b) appointment beyond 15 directors (special resolution); (c) appointment of independent directors. Routine board-appointed directors may not need immediate shareholder approval but require regularisation at the next general meeting.
5. **What happens if the appointed director's DIN is later disqualified?** Disqualification under Section 164(2) (e.g., due to default in MCA filings of other companies) makes the director ineligible to act. The company must immediately remove such a director and notify MCA via DIR-12.
