# Company incorporation in USA

## Overview

The United States is the world's largest economy and the most important destination for Indian startups, technology companies, and entrepreneurs seeking global scale. For Indian IT firms, SaaS startups, e-commerce businesses, and investors, a US company — typically a Delaware C-Corporation or an LLC — is not just a legal entity but a strategic asset that enables US client relationships, access to US venture capital, and global payment acceptance.

Delaware is the most popular state for incorporation due to its business-friendly corporate laws, Court of Chancery (specialised business court), and widespread acceptance among US investors and venture capitalists. Wyoming is popular for LLCs. Indian founders can incorporate in the US without visiting, entirely remotely.

## Key Benefits

- Delaware C-Corp: the standard for US VC-backed startups — accepted by Y Combinator, Sequoia, and all major US investors
- 100% foreign ownership — no US citizenship or residency required
- World's largest B2B and B2C market — credibility with US enterprise clients
- Access to US dollar payment rails (Stripe, PayPal, Braintree)
- Path to US banking and credit infrastructure
- LLC structure allows pass-through taxation — no entity-level US tax for non-US members
- US trademark and IP protection
- US visa options — E-2, L-1, O-1, EB-5 for business owners

## Limitations

- Annual franchise tax in Delaware (minimum USD 175–400 for LLC; minimum USD 400 for C-Corp) even if no US revenue
- C-Corp double taxation — federal corporate tax (21%) + state tax + dividend withholding on repatriation
- US tax filing obligations arise even for foreign-owned entities with US operations
- Complex state-by-state sales tax compliance (for e-commerce)
- US payroll, benefits, and employment law compliance if US employees hired
- Maintaining a US registered agent is mandatory
- India-US DTAA is relatively old and does not cover capital gains comprehensively

## Documents Required

For Indian nationals incorporating a Delaware C-Corp or LLC:

- Passport copy of all shareholders/officers/members
- US Registered Agent details (mandatory in Delaware)
- Certificate of Incorporation (filed with Delaware Secretary of State) — prepared by registered agent
- Employer Identification Number (EIN) application — SS-4 form to IRS (takes 4–8 weeks by mail; same-day if US phone number available)
- Bank account: ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) may be needed for personal account; Mercury, Brex, Relay accept EIN-only for business banking
- Operating Agreement (LLC) or Bylaws + Shareholders' Agreement (C-Corp)
- For Indian corporate parent: apostilled Certificate of Incorporation, Board Resolution

## Step-by-Step Process

1. Step 1 — Choose Structure: C-Corp (Delaware) for VC fundraising and institutional credibility; LLC (Wyoming or Delaware) for pass-through taxation, consulting, and simple structures.
2. Step 2 — Choose Registered Agent: Mandatory in every US state. Services like Northwest Registered Agent, Incfile, or Stripe Atlas provide US registered agent services.
3. Step 3 — File Certificate of Incorporation / Articles of Organisation: Filed with the Delaware Secretary of State (or chosen state). Online filing takes 1–3 business days (expedited: same-day).
4. Step 4 — Obtain EIN from IRS: Employer Identification Number — the equivalent of India's PAN for a company. Apply using Form SS-4. Non-residents apply by fax or mail (takes 4–6 weeks) unless using a US phone number (same-day).
5. Step 5 — Open US Business Bank Account: Mercury, Brex, and Relay allow non-resident founders to open accounts online with Certificate of Incorporation + EIN. Traditional banks (Chase, Bank of America) require in-person visits.
6. Step 6 — Adopt Bylaws and Issue Shares (C-Corp): Issue founder shares, adopt company bylaws, and hold an organisational meeting (can be done remotely).
7. Step 7 — Register in Operating States: If conducting business in states beyond Delaware (e.g., California, New York), "foreign qualification" registration in those states is required.
8. Step 8 — Annual Compliance: Delaware franchise tax (annual), registered agent fee (annual), federal and state income tax returns.

## Tax Implications for Indian Founders

C-Corp: Subject to 21% federal corporate income tax. State taxes apply additionally (Delaware itself has 0% corporate tax on companies not operating in Delaware). Dividends to Indian shareholders attract 15% withholding tax under India-US DTAA.
LLC (single-member, foreign-owned): A single-member LLC owned by a foreign individual/entity is treated as a "disregarded entity" for US tax — meaning the LLC itself pays no US income tax on non-US source income. This is a significant tax benefit for Indian founders using a US LLC purely for international billing.
US tax filing: C-Corps file Form 1120. Foreign-owned LLCs must file Form 5472 and Form 1120 (even if no US operations) — non-compliance penalty is USD 25,000.
FEMA ODI compliance: Indian residents investing in US entities must report to RBI under the ODI route.
Schedule FA in ITR: Mandatory disclosure of US entity ownership by Indian tax residents.
India-US DTAA: Covers income from business, dividends, interest, royalties, and provides PE provisions — but capital gains coverage is limited.

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Why do Indian startups prefer Delaware C-Corp over other US states?** Delaware has the most sophisticated corporate law in the US, administered by the Court of Chancery — a specialist business court with no jury trials. US venture capital firms, accelerators (Y Combinator, Techstars), and institutional investors are trained to invest in Delaware C-Corps and typically refuse to invest in entities incorporated elsewhere. Delaware also allows flexible share structures (Series A/B preferred shares, SAFEs, convertible notes) that are standard in US venture investing.
- **What is the difference between a C-Corp and an S-Corp for Indian founders?** An S-Corp has pass-through taxation (like an LLC) but restricts shareholders to US citizens or resident aliens. Indian nationals residing in India are ineligible to hold S-Corp shares. C-Corp is the only corporate structure available for foreign-owned US companies.
- **Can a US LLC owned by an Indian company pay zero US tax?** A US LLC wholly owned by an Indian company (single-member LLC) is a "disregarded entity" — it is ignored for US federal income tax purposes. The Indian company reports the LLC's income in India. If the LLC's income is entirely from non-US sources (e.g., selling software to European clients), no US income tax applies. However, Form 5472 must still be filed with the IRS each year.
- **What is Delaware Annual Franchise Tax and why is it high for startups?** Delaware franchise tax for C-Corps is calculated using either the Authorised Shares Method or the Assumed Par Value Capital Method (APVCA). If a startup authorises millions of shares at low par value (common practice for VC fundraising), the Authorised Shares Method can produce a huge tax bill. Startups should use the APVCA method — which is based on the company's actual capital — to dramatically reduce the tax liability, often to the minimum of USD 400.
- **Which Indian sectors most commonly use US entities?** SaaS and software companies (Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune), IT services companies targeting US enterprise clients (pan-India), e-commerce businesses, digital marketing agencies, and pharmaceutical companies seeking US FDA approvals are the highest users of US entity structures. The majority of Indian-founded unicorns (Freshworks, Zoho, BrowserStack, Postman) maintain or have maintained Delaware holding structures.
