# Company incorporation in Dubai (UAE)

## Overview

Dubai and the broader United Arab Emirates (UAE) is one of the top destinations globally for Indian entrepreneurs, traders, and professionals looking to establish an international business presence. With zero personal income tax, zero corporate tax on qualifying free zone entities (prior to the new regime), world-class infrastructure, and a large Indian diaspora, the UAE is India's single most popular offshore incorporation destination.

Businesses in Dubai can be set up in three jurisdictions: Mainland UAE, Free Zones (like DMCC, JAFZA, IFZA, DIFC), or Offshore (RAK ICC, JAFZA Offshore). Each structure serves different business purposes and comes with distinct rules on local trading, ownership, and banking.

The UAE introduced a 9% Corporate Tax effective June 2023 for taxable income above AED 375,000, a significant shift from the earlier zero-tax environment. Free zone entities meeting qualifying conditions continue to enjoy 0% tax on qualifying income.

## Key Benefits

- 0% personal income tax — no tax on salaries or personal wealth
- Free zone entities: 0% corporate tax on qualifying income
- 100% foreign ownership in free zones and (since 2021) in most mainland activities
- Strategic location bridging India, Middle East, Africa, and Europe
- India-UAE CEPA (Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement) in force since 2022
- Strong banking ecosystem — ease of multi-currency accounts
- Residency visa eligibility for founders/shareholders
- No forex restrictions — full profit repatriation
- Large Indian business community — strong network

## Limitations

- New 9% UAE Corporate Tax (effective June 2023) for mainland and non-qualifying free zone income
- Free zone companies cannot directly trade on UAE mainland without a distributor
- Substance requirements: must have genuine economic activity for 0% tax benefit
- Annual license renewal costs
- Physical office lease mandatory for most free zones
- Indian RBI ODI/LRS reporting required for investing in UAE entities
- UAE banking KYC for Indian nationals has become stricter post-FATF grey-listing (India removed from grey list in 2023)

## Documents Required

For Indian nationals incorporating in a Dubai free zone:

- Passport copy (attested)
- UAE residence visa or entry stamp (if applicable)
- No Objection Certificate (NOC) from employer (if employed in UAE)
- Business plan (required by some free zones like DIFC)
- Bank reference letter
- Proof of address (utility bill / bank statement — less than 3 months old)
- Passport-size photograph
- Shareholder / director KYC documents
- For Indian company as shareholder: Certificate of Incorporation, MoA/AoA (apostilled), board resolution

## Step-by-Step Process

1. Step 1 — Choose Jurisdiction: Decide between Mainland, Free Zone, or Offshore based on your business model (local trade, international trade, or holding).
2. Step 2 — Select Free Zone & Business Activity: Each free zone permits specific activities. Confirm your business activity is permitted.
3. Step 3 — Reserve Company Name: Submit name for approval — must follow UAE naming conventions (no religious/political terms; no names resembling government entities).
4. Step 4 — Submit Application & Documents: KYC documents, passport copies, business plan (if required).
5. Step 5 — Initial Approval & License Issuance: Trade license issued by the free zone authority.
6. Step 6 — Lease Office Space: Flexi-desk, shared office, or physical office depending on free zone requirements and visa needs.
7. Step 7 — Company Stamp & MoA: Obtain company seal and execute Memorandum of Association.
8. Step 8 — Open Business Bank Account: UAE banks for Indian nationals include Emirates NBD, Mashreq, RAKBank, ADCB. Non-resident banking options also available.
9. Step 9 — Apply for Residency Visas: Founders can apply for UAE investor visas (2-year or 10-year Golden Visa depending on investment amount).
10. Step 10 — Annual Renewal: Trade licenses must be renewed annually.

## Tax Implications for Indian Founders

UAE Corporate Tax (9%) applies on taxable income above AED 375,000 for both mainland and non-qualifying free zone income (from June 2023).
Qualifying Free Zone Persons (QFZPs) retain 0% on qualifying income if they meet substance, nexus, and non-excluded income conditions.
India-UAE DTAA applies — withholding tax on dividends is 10% (reduced rate).
Indian residents investing in UAE companies must comply with FEMA ODI regulations — RBI reporting mandatory.
Profits repatriated to India as salary, consulting fees, or dividends must be declared in Indian ITR.
Schedule FA in ITR mandates disclosure of UAE company ownership for Indian tax residents.
Risk of UAE company being treated as a tax resident of India if "Place of Effective Management" (POEM) is in India — ensure genuine UAE substance.

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Has the UAE Corporate Tax changed the attractiveness of Dubai for Indian businesses?** Yes, partially. The new 9% corporate tax (June 2023) reduces but does not eliminate Dubai's tax advantage over India. Free zone entities meeting qualifying conditions still enjoy 0% tax on qualifying income. For international trade, holding structures, and global businesses, Dubai remains highly competitive. For pure India-focused operations, the tax benefit is now less compelling.
- **What is the difference between a free zone company and a mainland company in Dubai?** A free zone company (e.g., DMCC, IFZA) enjoys 100% foreign ownership and 0% tax on qualifying income but cannot directly trade on the UAE mainland without appointing a local distributor or establishing a mainland branch. A mainland company can trade freely across the UAE and hold government contracts — 100% foreign ownership is now allowed in most sectors post-2021 Commercial Companies Law reform.
- **Can a Dubai company operate in India without being taxed in India?** A Dubai company can have Indian customers, but if its management and control is effectively exercised from India (POEM test), the Indian tax authorities can deem it a tax resident of India and tax it at 40% (foreign company rate). Genuine UAE substance — office, staff, board meetings — is critical to avoid this.
- **Is an Indian resident eligible for a UAE Golden Visa through company incorporation?** Yes. UAE Golden Visa (10-year residency) is available to investors who invest a minimum of AED 2 million in a UAE company or property. For business owners, a 5-year or 10-year visa is available under specified criteria. This gives UAE residency without needing employer sponsorship.
- **Which Indian cities have the highest volume of Dubai company incorporations?** Mumbai, Delhi/NCR, Ahmedabad, Surat, and Bengaluru account for the majority of Dubai incorporations by Indians. Gujarat-origin traders (diamonds, textiles, commodities) dominate DMCC registrations, while tech and consulting founders from Bengaluru and Hyderabad prefer IFZA and Meydan.
