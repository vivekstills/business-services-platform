# Company incorporation in Singapore

## Overview

Singapore is the undisputed number one destination for Indian entrepreneurs, startups, and mid-market businesses seeking an Asia-Pacific base. With the world's easiest incorporation process, a 17% flat corporate tax rate, an extensive DTAA network, strong rule of law, and a world-class financial ecosystem, Singapore has been the preferred offshore jurisdiction for Indian founders for decades.

The standard structure is the Private Limited Company (Pte. Ltd.), which can be incorporated in as little as one day online. ACRA (Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority) manages all company registrations through its BizFile+ portal.

The India-Singapore DTAA (revised 2017) provides efficient tax planning options. Singapore's startup ecosystem — with access to government grants (Startup SG, EDB incentives), venture capital, and talent — makes it especially attractive for high-growth tech ventures.

## Key Benefits

- 17% flat corporate tax rate (one of Asia's lowest)
- Startup Tax Exemption: 75% exemption on first SGD 100,000 and 50% on next SGD 100,000 for qualifying new startups (first 3 years)
- Extensive DTAA network (90+ treaties)
- 100% foreign ownership allowed — no local shareholder needed
- No capital gains tax, no dividend tax at shareholder level (one-tier tax system)
- World-class banking — DBS, OCBC, UOB, HSBC, Standard Chartered
- Global financial centre — access to venture capital, private equity, family offices
- Fastest incorporation in Asia — 1–3 days
- India-Singapore DTAA (2017) — dividends, capital gains coverage

## Limitations

- At least one locally resident director required (Singaporean, PR, or holder of Employment/EntrePass)
- Annual compliance costs higher than Malaysia
- Singapore does not allow pure holding companies for treaty benefits under 2017 DTAA revisions
- High cost of living — operational costs (office, talent) significant
- Nominee director arrangements add ongoing cost
- IRAS actively monitors economic substance and transfer pricing

## Documents Required

For Indian nationals incorporating a Singapore Pte Ltd:

- Passport copy (all shareholders and directors)
- Proof of residential address (utility bill or bank statement — within 3 months)
- Proposed company name
- Details of shareholder structure and shareholding percentage
- Registered office address in Singapore (agent address acceptable initially)
- Particulars of the local resident director
- ACRA SingPass account (foreigners can register via MyInfo Business or Corppass — with agent assistance)

## Step-by-Step Process

1. Step 1 — Reserve Company Name: Check and reserve on BizFile+ (ACRA portal). Approval usually within 15 minutes to 1 hour.
2. Step 2 — Appoint a Local Resident Director: At least one director must be ordinarily resident in Singapore. Indian founders can engage a nominee director service provider until they obtain an Employment Pass or EntrePass.
3. Step 3 — Submit Incorporation Application: File online via BizFile+. Requires particulars of all directors, shareholders, registered office address, and share capital.
4. Step 4 — Receive UEN (Unique Entity Number): Issued immediately upon incorporation. This is the company's primary identifier in Singapore.
5. Step 5 — Open Corporate Bank Account: Singapore banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB) have strong KYC but support non-resident account opening. Neobanks like Airwallex and Aspire are alternatives for digital banking.
6. Step 6 — Register for GST: Mandatory if annual taxable turnover exceeds SGD 1 million. Optional voluntary registration.
7. Step 7 — Engage a Company Secretary: Must be appointed within 6 months of incorporation. Must be a Singapore-resident individual or licensed corporate secretary.
8. Step 8 — EntrePass / Employment Pass: Indian founders who want to relocate to Singapore can apply for an EntrePass (for startup founders) or Employment Pass (for employed directors) to establish residency.

## Tax Implications for Indian Founders

Singapore Corporate Tax is 17% on chargeable income. Effective rate for startups (first 3 years) can be as low as 4.25% on the first SGD 100,000 after exemptions.
One-tier tax system: Dividends paid to shareholders (including Indian founders) are exempt from further tax in Singapore. No withholding tax on dividends under Singapore domestic law.
India-Singapore DTAA (revised 2017): Capital gains on shares held in Singapore companies are now taxable in India (grandfathering provisions apply for pre-April 2017 investments). Dividends are covered under the treaty.
Indian residents investing in Singapore must report under Schedule FA in ITR and comply with FEMA ODI regulations.
Singapore has mandatory transfer pricing documentation for related-party transactions.
IRAS (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore) actively enforces economic substance requirements — nominee-only structures without genuine operations face scrutiny.

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Is a Singapore company still useful for Indian startups after the 2017 India-Singapore DTAA revision?** Yes, significantly. While the 2017 DTAA revision removed capital gains exemption (previously a popular tax planning tool), Singapore remains attractive for operational reasons — clean corporate structure, access to global VC, 17% flat tax, ease of fundraising, and credibility with global investors. Many India-headquartered startups (Razorpay, Zoho, Grab had Indian roots) maintain Singapore holding companies for international operations.
- **What is the "flip structure" used by Indian startups and is it still viable?** A flip structure involves a Singapore holding company owning an Indian subsidiary, allowing fundraising from global investors via the Singapore entity. SEBI and RBI have tightened norms around such structures, and the income-tax implications (deemed dividend, Sec 9 sourcing rules) need careful structuring. It is still viable when executed with proper tax advice, but the era of friction-free flipping for tax arbitrage is largely over.
- **Can an Indian founder hold 100% shares in a Singapore Pte Ltd?** Yes. There is no restriction on foreign shareholding in Singapore private limited companies. An Indian national can hold 100% shares. The only requirement is having at least one locally resident director — which can be met through a nominee director service.
- **Does Singapore require audited financial statements for all companies?** No. Singapore introduced the "small company" audit exemption — companies with annual revenue below SGD 10 million, total assets below SGD 10 million, and fewer than 50 employees are exempt from statutory audit. Most Indian founder-owned Singapore entities qualify for this exemption, significantly reducing compliance cost.
- **How do Indian tech companies from Bengaluru and Hyderabad typically use Singapore entities?** IT and SaaS companies use Singapore as the entity for international client contracts and invoicing, especially for North America, Europe, and Southeast Asia clients. The Indian subsidiary (often a wholly owned subsidiary) handles R&D, development, and back-office work and raises invoices to the Singapore parent under a transfer pricing arrangement. This separates IP ownership and revenue recognition at the Singapore level while keeping operational costs in India.
