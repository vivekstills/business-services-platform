# Company incorporation in Australia

## Overview

Australia is one of the most business-friendly and transparent economies in the world, consistently ranking in the top tier of global ease-of-doing-business indices. For Indian entrepreneurs, exporters, IT firms, and consultants, incorporating in Australia offers access to a stable English-speaking market, strong IP protection laws, and preferential trade ties with Asia-Pacific countries. The most common structure for foreign founders is the Proprietary Limited Company (Pty Ltd), which offers limited liability and is straightforward to set up.

Australia has a strong rule of law, a highly skilled workforce, and a market of 26+ million with high per capita income — making it attractive for B2B services, fintech, education technology, and professional services.

## Key Benefits

- Stable, transparent legal and regulatory framework
- English-speaking market — minimal language/legal barrier for Indian founders
- Access to CPTPP and Australia-India ECTA (free trade agreement) benefits
- Strong IP and trademark protection under Australian law
- Easy banking and financial infrastructure
- No requirement for a local director in some structures (though recommended)
- Australia-India Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement (DTAA) in place
- Path to business migration visas for founders

## Limitations

- Annual compliance costs are relatively high (ASIC fees, audits, tax filings)
- Australian market is smaller than USA or China in absolute size
- Payroll tax and Goods and Services Tax (GST) apply with strict compliance
- Directors must meet fit-and-proper person requirements
- Distance and time zone difference from India may create operational challenges
- Minimum one local director required for most Pty Ltd companies

## Documents Required

For the company registration process with ASIC (Australian Securities and Investments Commission):

- Proposed company name (check ASIC availability)
- Details of directors and shareholders (passport copies for foreign nationals)
- Registered office address in Australia
- Constitution or adoption of replaceable rules
- ACN (Australian Company Number) application
- TFN (Tax File Number) application post-registration
- ABN (Australian Business Number) registration
- For Indian residents: Indian address proof, PAN card, notarised passport copy

## Step-by-Step Process

1. Step 1 — Name Search & Reservation: Check name availability on the ASIC register. Reserve if needed.
2. Step 2 — Choose Company Structure: Most foreign founders choose Pty Ltd (Proprietary Limited). Public companies have more disclosure obligations.
3. Step 3 — Appoint Directors & Shareholders: Minimum one director must ordinarily reside in Australia. Foreign founders can be shareholders but typically need a local director.
4. Step 4 — Register with ASIC: Submit Form 201 (Application for Registration as an Australian Company) online or through a registered agent.
5. Step 5 — Obtain ACN: Issued immediately upon approval by ASIC.
6. Step 6 — Apply for ABN and TFN: Through the Australian Business Register (ABR) — required for tax and GST.
7. Step 7 — Open Business Bank Account: Major banks include Commonwealth Bank, ANZ, Westpac, NAB. Many support non-resident business accounts.
8. Step 8 — Register for GST: Mandatory if turnover exceeds AUD 75,000.
9. Step 9 — PAYG and Payroll Setup: Required if hiring Australian employees.
10. Step 10 — Annual Compliance: Annual review with ASIC, tax returns with ATO, financial statements if required.

## Tax Implications for Indian Founders

Australia levies 25% corporate tax on small businesses (turnover below AUD 50 million) and 30% on larger entities.
Dividend repatriation to India attracts withholding tax — typically 15% under the India-Australia DTAA (reduced from 30%).
Royalties and interest also benefit from reduced DTAA rates.
Indian founders must report foreign company ownership to the RBI under FEMA (Overseas Direct Investment — ODI route) if their Indian entity holds shares.
Transfer pricing rules apply if transactions occur between the Australian company and the Indian parent/associate.
Controlled Foreign Company (CFC) rules do not currently apply in India but CBDT reporting of foreign assets is mandatory for resident Indians under Schedule FA in ITR.

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Can an Indian resident incorporate a company in Australia without visiting?** Yes. You can register remotely through a registered agent or business formation service. However, you must appoint at least one director who ordinarily resides in Australia. Many services provide a nominee director facility.
- **Is there a minimum capital requirement to start an Australian Pty Ltd?** No. There is no minimum paid-up capital requirement. You can start with as little as AUD 1 in share capital, making it accessible for bootstrapped startups.
- **What is the India-Australia ECTA and how does it benefit Indian businesses?** The India-Australia Economic Cooperation and Trade Agreement (ECTA), effective from December 2022, reduces or eliminates tariffs on goods and improves market access for services. Indian IT and professional services companies incorporated in Australia benefit from reduced trade friction.
- **Do I need to file tax returns in both India and Australia?** If you are an Indian tax resident holding shares in an Australian company, you must report the foreign asset in your Indian ITR (Schedule FA). The Australian company files its own tax returns with the ATO. Taxes paid in Australia can be claimed as foreign tax credit in India under DTAA provisions.
- **Which Indian states commonly have businesses expanding to Australia?** Businesses from Maharashtra (Mumbai), Karnataka (Bengaluru), Tamil Nadu (Chennai), Delhi NCR, and Telangana (Hyderabad) — particularly in IT, pharma, and professional services — are the most active in Australia incorporations.
