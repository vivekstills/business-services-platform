# OPC to Public Limited Company Conversion

## Overview

An OPC can be converted into a Public Limited Company when the entrepreneur intends to raise public capital, list on a stock exchange, or expand ownership significantly beyond the private circle. This conversion involves a two-step process — the OPC first converts into a Private Limited Company (via INC-6) and then into a Public Limited Company (via INC-27 or alteration of articles). Alternatively, a direct route via INC-6 to public company status is followed in some cases.

## When Needed

This conversion is pursued when the OPC plans to conduct an Initial Public Offering (IPO) or raise capital from the public; when government or institutional tenders require public company status; when the company wants to issue shares to more than 200 shareholders or the general public; or when the business has grown significantly and the founders seek partial exit through public markets.

## Benefits

- Conversion to a public limited company enables the entity to access public capital markets, issue shares to the general public, list on BSE or NSE, have unlimited shareholders, freely transfer shares, attract larger institutional investors, and enjoy enhanced corporate governance recognition.
- Brand visibility and trust with customers, lenders, and partners improve substantially.

## Limitations

- The compliance burden post-conversion is the highest of any structure — mandatory independent directors, secretarial audit, CSR compliance (if thresholds are met), SEBI LODR compliance (if listed), corporate governance reports, and quarterly disclosures.
- A minimum of 7 shareholders and 3 directors are required.
- The listing process itself takes 6–18 months and involves SEBI scrutiny, DRHP filing, and merchant bankers.

## Applicability

This conversion route is available for OPCs registered across India — Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Telangana, and other states. Listing is available on BSE and NSE with presence across India. Relevant RoC jurisdiction applies for conversion filings.

## Documents

- **Certificate of Incorporation (OPC)** — Existing
- **MOA & AOA (OPC)** — Existing
- **Board resolution for conversion** — Signed
- **Altered MOA & AOA** — For public limited
- **INC-6 (OPC to Pvt Ltd first)** — MCA filing
- **INC-27 (Pvt to Public)** — MCA filing
- **MGT-14 (special resolution)** — After shareholder approval
- **Financial statements (latest audited)** — Certified CA
- **DSC of all directors** — Class 3
- **NOC from creditors** — Signed
- **Consent of new directors (DIR-2)** — Minimum 3 required
- **PAN, Aadhaar of new directors** — KYC

## Process

1. Step 1 — Convert OPC to Private Limited Company by filing INC-6 with altered MOA and AOA (minimum 2 shareholders and 2 directors).
1. Step 2 — Once the Certificate of Incorporation as a Private Limited Company is received, file MGT-14 with the special resolution to convert to a Public Limited Company.
1. File INC-27 with further altered MOA and AOA.
1. Appoint minimum 7 shareholders and 3 directors.
1. The RoC issues a fresh Certificate of Incorporation as a Public Limited Company.
1. Obtain INC-20A (Commencement of Business) and commence operations.

## Tax Impact

The conversion from OPC to Public Limited Company is tax-neutral as the same legal entity (PAN, CIN) continues through both conversions. No deemed transfer, no capital gains, and no GST implications arise. All accumulated tax benefits, losses, depreciation, and credits carry forward seamlessly. If the company subsequently lists and conducts an IPO, the IPO proceeds received by the company are not taxable. Founders selling existing shares during IPO are liable for capital gains tax. Shares issued to employees via ESOPs post-listing attract perquisite tax at exercise and capital gains tax on subsequent sale.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can an OPC directly convert to a public limited company without first becoming a private limited company?** Technically, the procedural route under INC-6 allows direct conversion, but in practice, most RoCs require the OPC to first transition to a private limited company before conversion to a public limited company, making it a two-step process.

**Q2. Does the company need 7 shareholders at the time of OPC to Public conversion?** Yes, a public limited company requires a minimum of 7 shareholders and 3 directors before or at the time of conversion. These must be in place before the RoC issues the public company certificate.

**Q3. Is SEBI registration required immediately after becoming a public limited company?** No, SEBI registration is required only for securities-related activities. SEBI's LODR regulations apply only to listed public companies. An unlisted public limited company has no direct SEBI compliance obligations.

**Q4. Can the name change along with conversion?** Yes, the name change can be effected simultaneously. The word "Private" is removed from the name, and if the company chooses to alter the rest of the name, an additional name-change process under Form INC-24 is required.

**Q5. What is the minimum capital needed for an IPO after conversion?** SEBI does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital for SME IPOs (SME platform of BSE/NSE), but the post-issue paid-up capital should be between Rs. 3 crore and Rs. 25 crore for SME boards, and Rs. 10 crore+ for main board listing.
