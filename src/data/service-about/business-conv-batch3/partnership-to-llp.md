# Partnership to LLP Conversion

## Overview

A registered partnership firm can be converted into a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) under Section 55 of the LLP Act, 2008 read with the Second Schedule of the Act. This is a popular route for professional firms — law firms, chartered accountants, architects, and consultancies — that want to retain the partnership structure while gaining limited liability and separate legal identity without the full compliance burden of a company.

## When Needed

This conversion is ideal when partners wish to protect personal assets from business liabilities, when the firm needs a more structured governance framework, when the business wants to attract NRI partners (who can be LLP partners), when the firm needs to formalize operations for bank lending or government contracts, or when partners want to separate the firm's legal identity from their personal identities while avoiding the high compliance of a private limited company.

## Benefits

- Conversion to LLP gives the firm a separate legal identity, limited liability for all partners, perpetual succession, and a more credible legal standing.
- LLP compliance is simpler than a private limited company.
- Partners' share of profit remains tax-free in their hands.
- Audit is not required unless turnover exceeds Rs. 40 lakh.
- The LLP can have NRI partners and expand the partner base more flexibly.

## Limitations

- Only registered partnership firms can convert into an LLP — unregistered firms cannot avail this route.
- All partners must become partners of the LLP.
- LLPs cannot raise equity capital or issue shares.
- FDI in LLPs is restricted to sectors with 100% automatic route FDI.
- Investors who prefer equity structures may not accept LLP form.
- Conversion involves moderate procedural compliance.

## Applicability

Conversion of partnership to LLP is available across all states — Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Telangana, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, and others. The partnership must be registered with the respective state's Registrar of Firms. The LLP is then registered centrally with MCA.

## Documents

- **Certificate of registration of partnership** — From Registrar of Firms
- **Partnership Deed** — Latest version
- **Consent of all partners** — Written resolution
- **NOC from secured creditors** — Original signed
- **Statement of assets & liabilities** — Certified by CA
- **PAN, Aadhaar of all partners** — KYC
- **DSC of designated partners** — Class 3
- **DPIN application** — For all partners
- **Form 17 (Application for conversion)** — Filed with MCA
- **Form FiLLiP** — For LLP incorporation
- **LLP Agreement (draft)** — On stamp paper
- **Proof of registered office** — Utility bill + NOC + rent agreement

## Process

1. Pass a resolution of all partners consenting to conversion.
1. Obtain NOC from secured creditors.
1. Prepare the Statement of Assets and Liabilities certified by a CA.
1. File Form 17 (application for conversion) along with Form FiLLiP (LLP incorporation) on the MCA portal.
1. Submit the LLP Agreement (to be filed within 30 days via Form 3).
1. On approval, the MCA issues a Certificate of Incorporation of the LLP.
1. The partnership firm is deemed dissolved from this date.
1. Update PAN, GSTIN, bank accounts, and other registrations.

## Tax Impact

Under Section 47(xii) of the Income Tax Act, the conversion of a registered partnership firm into an LLP is tax-neutral, subject to conditions: all assets and liabilities of the firm become those of the LLP, all partners become partners of the LLP in the same proportion, and no consideration other than partnership rights is paid. Additionally, if the firm was subject to taxation as a partnership (30% flat), the LLP will continue under the same tax regime. Partners' profit share remains tax-free. Accumulated losses and depreciation of the firm can be carried forward by the LLP under Section 72A conditions. If conditions under Section 47(xii) are breached within 5 years, the gains are taxed in the year of breach. GST: conversion as a going concern is exempt.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can an unregistered partnership firm convert into an LLP?** No, the Second Schedule of the LLP Act, 2008 specifically requires the firm to be a registered partnership firm for conversion. An unregistered firm must first register with the Registrar of Firms before proceeding.

**Q2. Is it mandatory for all partners of the firm to become partners of the LLP?** Yes, all partners of the partnership firm must become partners of the LLP at the time of conversion. New partners can be added after conversion through the LLP Agreement.

**Q3. Does the LLP get the firm's registration numbers, PAN, and GSTIN?** No. The LLP is a new entity with a new LLPIN and fresh PAN and GSTIN. The firm's PAN is surrendered. ITC balance can be transferred via Form GST ITC-02.

**Q4. How long does the conversion process take?** The process typically takes 15–25 working days after all documents are ready. The most time-consuming step is obtaining NOC from secured creditors and preparation of the certified statement of accounts.

**Q5. Can the LLP use the same name as the partnership firm?** Yes, the LLP can adopt the same name as the partnership firm (with "LLP" added as a suffix), subject to MCA name availability checks. The name must not be identical or similar to an existing company or LLP.
