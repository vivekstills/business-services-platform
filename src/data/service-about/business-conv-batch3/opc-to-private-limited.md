# OPC to Private Limited Company Conversion

## Overview

A One Person Company (OPC) can be converted into a Private Limited Company either voluntarily or mandatorily under the Companies Act, 2013. The conversion process is governed by Section 18 of the Companies Act and the Companies (Incorporation) Rules, 2014. Post-conversion, the entity expands its membership, invites new investors, and takes on the full compliance framework of a private limited company. The existing CIN of the OPC continues with appropriate amendments.

## When Needed

Conversion becomes necessary or desirable when the entrepreneur wants to bring in co-founders, angel investors, or venture capital; when the business requires more than one director or shareholder; when the company crosses the mandatory conversion thresholds (though the strict capital threshold was relaxed in 2021); when participation in tenders, government contracts, or export opportunities requires a multi-member structure; or when the OPC seeks startup recognition under DPIIT, which may require additional directors.

## Benefits

- Conversion allows induction of new shareholders and directors, enabling equity fundraising and structured ownership.
- The company can issue ESOPs, enter into more complex agreements, and access a larger credit facility.
- The business gains enhanced credibility and governance structure.
- It continues the same PAN, CIN, and business history without any break in continuity.

## Limitations

- After conversion, compliance burden increases significantly — mandatory board meetings (minimum 4), AGM, additional MCA filings, and audit requirements.
- Share transfer restrictions apply unless MOA/AOA allow flexibility.
- A second director must be appointed before or at the time of conversion.
- The conversion cannot be reversed — a private limited company cannot be converted back to an OPC.

## Applicability

This conversion is available across all Indian states and Union Territories for OPCs registered under the Companies Act, 2013. The application is filed with the concerned Registrar of Companies (RoC) — jurisdictions include RoC Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi-NCR, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Rajasthan, and others.

## Documents

- **Certificate of Incorporation (OPC)** — Existing
- **MOA & AOA (OPC)** — Existing
- **Board resolution for conversion** — Signed
- **Altered MOA & AOA** — For private limited company
- **NOC from creditors (if any)** — Signed
- **Financial statements (latest)** — Audited
- **INC-6 (application for conversion)** — Filed online
- **PAN, Aadhaar, address proof** — For new directors/members
- **DSC of directors** — Class 3
- **DIR-2 from new directors** — Consent form
- **List of shareholders** — With shareholding details

## Process

1. Hold a board meeting and pass a resolution authorizing conversion.
1. Alter the MOA and AOA to suit a private limited company structure.
1. Appoint at least one additional director (and shareholder) to meet the minimum requirement of 2 each.
1. File Form INC-6 with the RoC along with amended MOA, AOA, and prescribed attachments.
1. Pay the applicable stamp duty and fees.
1. On approval, the RoC issues a fresh Certificate of Incorporation as a Private Limited Company bearing the same CIN.

## Tax Impact

The conversion from OPC to Private Limited Company is generally tax-neutral as it is a change of form, not a change of entity. The PAN remains the same, and there is no deemed transfer of assets. Accumulated losses, depreciation, and tax credits continue without interruption. No capital gains are triggered. GST registration details and GSTIN remain the same. The company simply updates its registration details with the GST department. Existing tax holiday or startup benefits under DPIIT recognition continue for the converted entity subject to original eligibility conditions.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can an OPC convert voluntarily before crossing any threshold?** Yes, as per the Companies (Incorporation) Amendment Rules, 2021, an OPC can voluntarily convert into a private limited company at any time after 2 years from the date of incorporation, without any restriction on capital or turnover.

**Q2. Does the company need a new PAN after conversion?** No, the PAN of the OPC continues as it is. Only the company name, MOA, and AOA are amended. The tax records, ITR history, and registration details continue uninterrupted.

**Q3. How many directors and shareholders are needed post-conversion?** A minimum of 2 shareholders and 2 directors are required for a private limited company. At least one director must be a resident of India.

**Q4. Does conversion affect existing contracts and licenses?** Generally, contracts and licenses continue as the legal entity (CIN) remains the same. However, some licenses (GST, import-export code, MSME) may require an update to reflect the new company name and structure.

**Q5. Is the nominee's consent required for conversion?** Yes, the nominee must be informed of the conversion. Since the nominee's role ceases to exist after the OPC becomes a private limited company (which no longer requires a nominee), their NOC is advisable and may be required by some RoCs.
