# Private to Public Company Conversion

## Overview

A Private Limited Company can be converted into a Public Limited Company by altering its MOA and AOA under Section 14 of the Companies Act, 2013. This conversion is typically driven by plans to go public (IPO), accept broader ownership, issue shares to more than 200 shareholders, or access public capital markets. The conversion involves expanding the minimum membership and directorship requirements.

## When Needed

Conversion is pursued when the company plans an IPO or wants to be listed on BSE or NSE; when it needs to issue shares to more than 200 shareholders; when large government projects or institutional contracts require public company status; when the company seeks to raise capital from the general public through a prospectus; or when the business reaches a stage where broad-based ownership and governance are desirable.

## Benefits

- A public limited company can issue a prospectus and raise funds from the general public.
- Shares are freely transferable without restrictions, enabling better liquidity for investors.
- The company can list on stock exchanges, enabling exits for early investors.
- Enhanced credibility and transparency attracts top-tier institutional investors, international funds, and strategic partners.

## Limitations

- Compliance increases substantially — minimum 7 shareholders, 3 directors, independent directors, audit committees, secretarial audit, quarterly reporting, and CSR compliance (if thresholds are met).
- Shares are freely transferable, increasing the risk of hostile takeovers.
- Extensive public disclosures are mandatory.
- The listing process (for those wishing to list) involves SEBI scrutiny and is time-consuming and expensive.

## Applicability

This conversion is available for private limited companies registered under the Companies Act, 2013 across all Indian states including Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi NCR, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Telangana, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, and Union Territories. The filing is made with the relevant RoC.

## Documents

- **Certificate of Incorporation (Pvt Ltd)** — Existing
- **MOA & AOA (Pvt Ltd)** — Existing
- **Altered MOA & AOA (Public)** — Drafted
- **Special resolution (EGM)** — Passed
- **MGT-14 (filing of resolution)** — MCA filing
- **INC-27 (application for conversion)** — MCA filing
- **List of shareholders (min 7)** — Updated
- **Consent of new directors (DIR-2)** — Min 3 directors
- **DIR-12 (appointment of new directors)** — If new directors added
- **Financial statements (audited)** — Latest year
- **PAN, Aadhaar of new directors** — KYC
- **DSC of all directors** — Class 3

## Process

1. Hold an EGM and pass a special resolution authorizing the conversion and alteration of MOA and AOA.
1. File MGT-14 within 30 days of the resolution.
1. File INC-27 (application for conversion) with altered MOA and AOA.
1. Appoint at least 3 directors and 7 shareholders before or simultaneously.
1. Remove the word "Private" from the company name.
1. The RoC issues a fresh Certificate of Incorporation as a Public Limited Company bearing the same CIN.
1. Obtain INC-20A if required.
1. If seeking listing, initiate SEBI/exchange registration processes.

## Tax Impact

The conversion from Private to Public Limited Company is completely tax-neutral — the same PAN and CIN continue, no deemed transfer occurs, and no capital gains are triggered. All tax losses, depreciation, exemptions, and credits continue uninterrupted. The tax rate structure remains the same (22% under Section 115BAA or 15% for new manufacturing under 115BAB, if applicable). Post-listing, the company becomes subject to STT (Securities Transaction Tax) on share trading. ESOPs issued to employees attract perquisite tax at exercise and capital gains at sale. IPO proceeds received by the company are not taxable.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can a private limited company convert to a public company without planning an IPO?** Yes, the conversion to public limited company status and the decision to list on a stock exchange are two independent actions. A company can be a public limited company without being listed.

**Q2. How many shareholders and directors are required after conversion?** A minimum of 7 shareholders and 3 directors are required. For listed public companies, at least 1/3rd of the board must comprise independent directors.

**Q3. Does the company need to change its name?** Yes, the word "Private" must be removed from the company name. For example, "ABC Private Limited" becomes "ABC Limited". The rest of the name remains unchanged unless a separate name-change process is initiated.

**Q4. Is shareholder approval mandatory for this conversion?** Yes, a special resolution (passed by at least 75% of votes) at an EGM is mandatory. The resolution must authorize both the conversion and the consequent alteration of MOA and AOA.

**Q5. Can a public limited company be delisted and converted back to private?** Yes, listed public companies can be delisted through SEBI's delisting regulations, and thereafter the company can apply for conversion back to private limited status through NCLT approval and special resolution.
