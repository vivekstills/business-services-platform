# FSSAI Modification

## Overview

FSSAI Modification refers to the process of amending an existing FSSAI Registration or License to reflect changes in the food business's details — such as a change in premises, addition of new food product categories, change in business name, change in proprietor/partners/directors, addition of a new manufacturing unit, or change in the installed capacity of machinery.
Food Business Operators (FBOs) are legally required to inform FSSAI of any significant change in business details within the prescribed timeline. Operating under an FSSAI certificate that no longer accurately reflects the actual business — different address, expanded product range, or changed ownership — constitutes non-compliance and can result in cancellation of the license and penalties.
FSSAI Modification is processed through the FoSCoS portal and requires submission of the modification application along with supporting documents evidencing the change. Depending on the nature of the modification, it may be approved without inspection or may trigger a physical inspection of the premises by a Food Safety Officer.
Proactive modification management is a best practice for all scaling food businesses — it keeps the FSSAI certificate current and avoids regulatory complications during audits, marketplace verifications, or due diligence for investment.

## Applicability

FSSAI Modification is applicable to all existing FSSAI certificate holders — Basic Registration, State License, and Central License — when any of the following changes occur:

- Change in business name — rebranding or legal name change of the FBO
- Change in business address — shifting of manufacturing unit, warehouse, or retail outlet
- Addition of new food product categories — expanding into a new food segment not covered in the original certificate
- Removal of food product categories — discontinuing certain product lines
- Change in proprietor/partners/directors — due to retirement, death, resignation, or induction of new partners/directors
- Change in installed capacity — upgrading manufacturing machinery or increasing production capacity
- Addition of new manufacturing premises — adding a new factory or production facility
- Change in nature of business — e.g., from retail to manufacturing
- Change in contact details — mobile number, email, or authorised signatory
- Upgrade of license category — from Basic Registration to State License, or from State License to Central License

Across India: Applicable in all states and UTs. State-level modifications are processed by the respective State Food Safety Department; Central License modifications by FSSAI's Central Licensing Authority. The FoSCoS portal is the unified application platform across all jurisdictions.

## Benefits

- Legal accuracy: Keeps the FSSAI certificate current and legally valid — prevents cancellation due to mismatched details
- Audit protection: Food Safety Officers conducting inspections verify premises, products, and capacity against the FSSAI certificate; mismatches result in notices and penalties
- Marketplace compliance: E-commerce platforms verify FSSAI certificate details against the seller's profile; outdated certificates trigger delisting
- Business continuity: Addition of new product categories through modification allows the FBO to expand legally without interruption or fresh licensing
- Investor and buyer confidence: In M&A, franchise, or investment transactions, buyers conduct FSSAI due diligence; updated certificates signal good compliance culture
- Avoids double registration: Timely modification (e.g., for address change) avoids the need to apply for a fresh registration/license, saving time and money
- Seamless renewal: Renewals are smoother when all details on the existing certificate are accurate and current

## Limitations

- Inspection risk: Modifications involving change of premises or addition of manufacturing units trigger mandatory physical inspection by a Food Safety Officer, which can cause delays
- Timeline uncertainty: While the FoSCoS portal has deemed approval mechanisms, modifications involving inspections can take 30–60 days or longer
- Retrospective modification not available: FSSAI does not allow backdating of modifications; if a change was made without modifying the certificate, the FBO is technically in violation for the intervening period
- Capacity upgrade limitations: Certain capacity expansions may require the business to upgrade from State to Central License rather than simply modifying the existing State License
- Category additions — risk of downgrade trigger: Adding regulated food categories (e.g., meat, infant foods) may trigger enhanced compliance requirements or license category change
- Multiple changes in one application: While multiple changes can be clubbed in a single modification application, it increases document complexity and processing time

## Documents Required

- **Existing FSSAI Certificate (copy)** — Reference for current certificate details
- **Application Form (Modification) on FoSCoS** — Filled modification application with changed details
- **Proof of new address** — Electricity bill, rent agreement, or property documents (for address change)
- **NOC from new premises owner** — If the new premises is rented
- **Updated list of food products** — For addition/removal of food categories
- **New partnership deed / MoA / board resolution** — For change in partners/directors
- **Affidavit from outgoing/incoming partner/director** — Confirming change in management
- **Updated layout plan of premises** — For new premises or capacity addition
- **List and details of machinery** — For capacity enhancement modifications
- **CA certificate for turnover** — If modification triggers license category upgrade
- **Photo of new authorised signatory** — For change in authorised signatory
- **Government ID proof of new partner/director** — PAN, Aadhaar of incoming management personnel

## Process

1. Step 1 — Identify Nature of Modification Determine the specific change(s) to be incorporated into the FSSAI certificate and assess whether the modification requires inspection or is document-based only.
1. Step 2 — FoSCoS Portal Login Log in to the FoSCoS portal using existing credentials. Navigate to the "Modification" section against the relevant FSSAI Registration/License number.
1. Step 3 — Fill Modification Application Complete the modification application online with accurate details of the proposed changes. Ensure all fields are filled correctly — errors will cause rejection or delays.
1. Step 4 — Document Upload Upload all supporting documents in the prescribed format (PDF, JPEG). Ensure document quality is clear and legible.
1. Step 5 — Fee Payment Pay the applicable modification fee online. The fee varies based on the nature and type of modification and the category of FSSAI authorisation.
1. Step 6 — Application Review by Authority The Registering/Licensing Authority reviews the application. For document-based modifications (e.g., contact details, name change), approval is typically granted within 30 days. For inspection-required modifications, a Food Safety Officer conducts a premises inspection.
1. Step 7 — Grant of Modified Certificate Upon approval, the modified FSSAI Registration/License Certificate is generated digitally on the FoSCoS portal. The FBO downloads and uses the updated certificate.
1. Step 8 — Update Business Materials Update the FSSAI number and certificate details on food packaging, signage, e-commerce profiles, and marketing materials to reflect the modified certificate.

## Validity

A modification does not reset or extend the validity of the existing FSSAI Registration or License. The modified certificate retains the same expiry date as the original certificate. The FBO must apply for renewal separately before the expiry date. In cases where the modification results in a license category upgrade (e.g., Basic to State), the new license is issued with a fresh validity period chosen at the time of the upgrade application.

## Renewal

Renewal of an FSSAI certificate with pending modifications is discouraged. Best practice is to:

- Complete all modifications first and ensure the certificate is current
- Then file the renewal application on FoSCoS with the updated details reflected in the modified certificate
- Filing renewal with outdated details (i.e., applying for renewal of a certificate that already has changes not yet modified) creates inconsistencies in the FSSAI database and can complicate future compliance

If the certificate is close to expiry and a modification is pending, it is advisable to submit both the modification and renewal applications simultaneously to avoid any gap in authorisation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. I have renamed my food brand. Do I need to modify my FSSAI License or get a fresh one?** If only your brand/trade name has changed but your legal business entity name remains the same, you may not need a formal modification for the FSSAI license (since FSSAI licenses are issued to the legal entity, not the brand). However, if your legal entity name has also changed — for example, your company was renamed through MCA — you must file a modification application with FSSAI with the updated MoA and certificate of incorporation. Operating under a certificate with a different legal name than your current registered name is a compliance risk.

**2. We've started manufacturing a new product category (e.g., we're adding dairy products to our existing bakery license). Do we need a fresh license or a modification?** You need a modification to add the new food category to your existing FSSAI License. The modification application should specify the new category of food products being added. Depending on the risk level of the new category, this may trigger a physical inspection by a Food Safety Officer. You should not manufacture the new category before the modified license reflecting the addition is issued — doing so constitutes unauthorised manufacturing under FSSAI.

**3. Our food manufacturing unit has shifted to a new location within the same city. Is FSSAI modification sufficient or do we need a fresh license?** A modification application for change of address/premises is the correct approach — not a fresh license. You must file the modification before shifting operations to the new premises (or immediately upon shifting). The modification will typically trigger a physical inspection of the new premises to verify food safety compliance before the updated certificate is issued. Manufacturing from the new premises without an updated FSSAI certificate reflecting the new address is a violation.

**4. How long does FSSAI take to process a modification application?** Under the FoSCoS system, document-based modifications (name change, contact details, partner change) are typically processed within 30 days. Modifications requiring physical inspection (new premises, capacity enhancement) can take 30–60 days depending on the workload of the Food Safety Officer in your jurisdiction. If the authority does not respond within 30 days for non-inspection modifications, deemed approval applies. Tracking the application status on FoSCoS is recommended.

**5. Can I modify my Basic FSSAI Registration to add manufacturing activities that were not part of the original application?** Yes, provided the added manufacturing activity and the overall business still falls within the Basic Registration eligibility criteria (annual turnover below ₹12 lakh and no restricted food categories). If the new manufacturing activity places you in a restricted or high-risk food category (e.g., proprietary foods, infant foods, meat processing), you will need to upgrade to a State License — modification of the Basic Registration alone will not be sufficient. Our team can advise on whether a modification or a fresh license application is the appropriate path.
