# Partnership Firm Annual Compliance

## Overview

A Partnership Firm is governed by the Indian Partnership Act, 1932 and is one of the simplest forms of business structure in India. Unlike companies or LLPs, partnership firms are not required to file annual returns with the MCA. However, they must comply with income tax regulations, GST laws (if applicable), TDS requirements, and state-specific registrations.

## Applicability

Partnership firm compliance applies across India. Registration of partnership firms is done at the state level under the Registrar of Firms, with each state (Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Delhi, West Bengal, Kerala, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, etc.) having its own registration authority. Compliance requirements are mostly uniform across India except for state-specific professional tax and Shops & Establishment rules.

## Compliance Requirements

| Compliance | Form | Authority | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Income Tax Return | ITR-5 | IT Dept | Annual |
| Tax Audit | Form 3CB-3CD | IT Dept | If turnover > Rs. 1 crore |
| GST Returns | GSTR-1, 3B, 9 | GST Dept | Monthly/Annual |
| TDS Returns | 24Q, 26Q, 27Q | IT Dept | Quarterly |
| Professional Tax | PT Return | State | Monthly/Annual |
| ESI/PF Returns | As applicable | EPFO/ESIC | Monthly |
| Shops & Establishment | Renewal | State | As per state law |
| Partnership Deed | Update (if needed) | State Registrar | As needed |

## Benefits

Regular compliance helps partnership firms maintain legal standing, claim tax deductions on partners' salary and interest on capital, avoid interest and penalties, participate in government tenders, and establish creditworthiness for bank loans. It also ensures smooth dissolution or conversion in the future.

## Penalties / Risks

| Default | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Late ITR filing | Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000 |
| Tax audit non-compliance | 0.5% of turnover (max Rs. 1.5 lakh) |
| GST return delay | Rs. 50/day (Rs. 20 for nil) + 18% interest |
| TDS default | 1% to 1.5% per month interest + penalty |
| No PAN for firm | Rs. 10,000 |
| Partnership deed not registered (in some states) | Cannot file suits |

## Documents Required

- Partnership Deed
- PAN of firm
- PAN of all partners
- Bank statements
- Books of accounts (ledger, cash book, journal)
- GST returns
- TDS challans
- Sales and purchase invoices
- Salary records
- Rent agreements
- Proof of expenses

## Process / Timeline

Partnership firms follow a simpler annual compliance cycle. Books of accounts are closed at FY end. Tax audit (if applicable) is completed by September. Income tax return is filed by July 31 (non-audit) or October 31 (audit). GST annual return is filed by December 31. TDS returns are filed quarterly.

## FAQs

**Q1. Do partnership firms need to file returns with ROC?** No, registered or unregistered partnership firms do not file returns with the MCA/ROC. Their compliance is limited to tax and labour laws.

**Q2. Is registration of a partnership firm mandatory?** Registration with the Registrar of Firms is not mandatory under the Indian Partnership Act, 1932, but an unregistered firm cannot file suits in court against third parties or partners.

**Q3. What tax rate applies to partnership firms?** Partnership firms are taxed at a flat rate of 30% plus surcharge (if applicable) and 4% health and education cess, regardless of income level.

**Q4. Can partners draw salary from the firm?** Yes, working partners can draw salary and interest on capital, subject to the limits prescribed in Section 40(b) of the Income Tax Act and as per the partnership deed.

**Q5. Is GST registration mandatory for partnership firms?** GST registration is required only if turnover exceeds Rs. 20 lakh for services or Rs. 40 lakh for goods, or if the firm is engaged in interstate supply or e-commerce.
