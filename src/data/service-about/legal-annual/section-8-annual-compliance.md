# Section 8 Company Annual Compliance

## Overview

A Section 8 Company is a non-profit organization formed for promoting commerce, art, science, sports, education, research, social welfare, religion, charity, or environmental protection. It is registered under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013. Profits, if any, must be used solely to promote the company's objectives and cannot be distributed as dividends. Section 8 companies have strict annual compliance obligations akin to private/public limited companies, along with additional NGO-specific filings.

## Applicability

Section 8 company compliance applies uniformly across India in states like Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Gujarat, West Bengal, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Odisha, and Union Territories. It also applies to Section 8 companies receiving foreign contributions under FCRA.

## Benefits

Proper compliance ensures continuation of 12A and 80G tax exemptions, eligibility to receive CSR funds from companies, uninterrupted FCRA registration for foreign donations, public trust and donor confidence, access to grants from government bodies, and avoidance of penalties or license revocation by the Central Government.

## Penalties / Risks

| Default | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| AOC-4/MGT-7 delay | Rs. 100/day |
| Revocation of Section 8 license | Company ceases to exist as Sec 8 |
| CG fine (serious default) | Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 1 crore |
| Director liability | Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 25 lakh |
| Loss of 12A/80G | Donors lose deduction, taxed as AOP |
| FCRA violation | Up to 5x of amount + license cancellation |

## Process / Timeline

Books are closed at FY end. Statutory audit is conducted by a CA. AGM is held within 6 months of FY end. AOC-4, MGT-7, and ITR-7 are filed in prescribed timelines. FCRA return (FC-4) is filed by December 31 for the preceding FY. Section 8 companies must file FC-4 online even if no foreign contribution is received.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can a Section 8 company distribute profits?** No, a Section 8 company is strictly prohibited from distributing profits or dividends to its members. All income must be applied towards the company's objectives.

**Q2. Is 12A and 80G registration mandatory?** These are not mandatory for incorporation but essential for income tax exemption (12A) and donor deduction benefits (80G). Without them, donations are taxable in the company's hands.

**Q3. What is FCRA registration and when is it needed?** FCRA registration is required to receive foreign contributions. A Section 8 company must have FCRA registration (or prior permission) and three years of existence with Rs. 10 lakh spent on objectives.

**Q4. Can a Section 8 company be converted into a private limited company?** Yes, conversion is permitted under Section 8(4)(ii) with Central Government approval, subject to strict conditions including surrender of accumulated profits to a similar Section 8 entity.

**Q5. Do Section 8 companies need to pay income tax?** Section 8 companies with valid 12A registration are exempt on income applied for charitable purposes. However, income not applied or accumulated beyond prescribed limits becomes taxable.
