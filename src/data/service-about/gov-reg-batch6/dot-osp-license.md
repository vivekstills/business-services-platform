# DOT/OSP License

## Overview

A DOT (Department of Telecommunications) OSP (Other Service Provider) License is a registration required by companies that provide certain telecom-based services in India using the public switched telephone network (PSTN), ISDN, or similar telecom resources — without owning their own telecom infrastructure. OSP registration is regulated by the Department of Telecommunications, Ministry of Communications, Government of India.
OSPs typically use telecom infrastructure (telephone connections, internet bandwidth) provided by licensed telecom operators (TSPs — Telecom Service Providers) to deliver services to customers. Common OSP services include:

- Call centre services (domestic and international BPOs)
- IT-enabled services (ITES) that use voice/data telecom connections
- Tele-banking, tele-medicine, and tele-education services
- E-commerce helpdesk and customer support services
- Network Operation Centres (NOCs)
- IT/ITES companies with home-based agents (work from home)

The DoT significantly liberalised the OSP framework in November 2020 — removing many restrictions on interconnection between domestic and international OSPs, relaxing infrastructure requirements, and expanding the scope for work-from-home agents. Understanding the revised OSP framework is critical for IT/BPO companies structuring their operations and telecom infrastructure.

## Features

- Two categories: Domestic OSP (serves only Indian customers) and International OSP (serves overseas customers)
- Online registration: OSP registration is done online through the DoT's Saral Sanchar portal (dot.gov.in)
- No license fee: DoT does not charge any license fee for OSP registration — it is a free registration
- Multi-location registration: A single OSP company can register multiple centres under the same OSP registration
- Work-from-home agents allowed: Post-2020 liberalisation, OSPs can deploy agents working from home without specific DoT approval — with some compliance requirements
- EPABX/auto-dialler regulation: OSP registered companies must ensure their EPABX (Electronic Private Automatic Branch Exchange) systems are compliant with DoT specifications
- Interconnection flexibility: Post-2020, restrictions on interconnection between domestic OSPs have been significantly relaxed
- Annual compliance: OSPs must submit an annual self-declaration of compliance to DoT

## Benefits

- Legal authorisation for BPO/call centre operations: OSP registration is a legal prerequisite for operating a call centre or BPO in India — without it, the company risks DoT enforcement action, including disconnection of telecom services
- Credibility with telecom operators: TSPs (BSNL, Airtel, Jio, etc.) require OSP registration proof before providing bulk telephone lines, leased circuits, and internet bandwidth to BPO/call centre companies
- Client confidence: International clients (US, UK, Australian companies) outsourcing to Indian BPOs require proof of OSP registration as part of their vendor due diligence and compliance requirements
- Work-from-home flexibility: Post-2020, OSP companies can deploy home-based agents legally — enabling hybrid work models, business continuity, and talent access beyond metro cities
- Access to international calling infrastructure: International OSPs can provide international calling services legally — without OSP registration, using international VOIP in a commercial call centre setting is a violation
- Audit-ready compliance: Proper OSP registration and annual compliance submissions protect companies against DoT audits and enforcement actions

## Limitations

- Penalties for non-compliance: Operating as an OSP without registration or violating OSP conditions (e.g., unauthorised interconnections) can result in disconnection of all telecom services and financial penalties
- Telecom infrastructure constraints: OSPs must use telecom services from licensed TSPs — they cannot use unlicensed VOIP or grey market telephone connections
- Work-from-home compliance requirements: While WFH agents are permitted, OSPs must maintain logs of agent connectivity, VPN usage, and data security compliance — adding operational overhead
- No resale of telecom capacity: OSPs cannot resell excess telecom capacity to other businesses — the telecom connections must be used only for the OSP's own service delivery
- International OSP — routing restrictions: International OSPs must route international calls through licensed ISPs and TSPs — using unauthorised international gateways is a serious violation attracting criminal prosecution
- EPABX compliance: OSPs must use DoT-approved EPABX configurations — non-compliant EPABX setups (common in older BPO infrastructure) must be upgraded

## Applicability

OSP registration is required by:

- Domestic and international call centres and BPOs
- IT/ITES companies with customer-facing voice operations
- Tele-medicine platforms using patient voice/video consultation at scale
- Online education platforms with tutoring call centre operations
- E-commerce and fintech companies with large customer support voice operations
- Insurance companies and banks operating tele-sales call centres (through third-party BPOs)
- Companies providing home-based call centre agent services

Applicable pan-India — OSP registration is with the DoT's Central office; the registration covers operations across all states. However, companies with centres in multiple states must register each centre under the same OSP license.

## Documents Required

- **Certificate of Incorporation** — Company's registration with RoC
- **PAN card of the company** — Corporate PAN
- **GST registration certificate** — GSTIN of the OSP entity
- **List of directors/partners** — With DIN/PAN details
- **Address proof of OSP centre(s)** — Lease agreement or ownership documents for each centre
- **Network diagram** — EPABX configuration, network topology showing TSP connectivity
- **Declaration on OSP compliance** — Self-declaration regarding non-usage of grey routes
- **Board resolution** — Authorising OSP registration and naming authorised signatory
- **Details of TSP connections** — Telecom service provider, type of connection, number of lines
- **Site plan of OSP centre** — Floor plan showing agent workstations, EPABX location

## Process

1. Step 1 — Determine OSP Category Identify whether registration is required as a Domestic OSP, International OSP, or both — based on the customer geography of the services being delivered.
1. Step 2 — Saral Sanchar Portal Registration Register on the DoT's Saral Sanchar portal (dot.gov.in/saral-sanchar) as an OSP applicant.
1. Step 3 — Application Submission Complete the OSP registration application with company details, centre address(es), nature of OSP services, network/EPABX details, and TSP information.
1. Step 4 — Document Upload Upload all required documents — Incorporation certificate, PAN, GST certificate, address proof of centre(s), network diagram, and board resolution.
1. Step 5 — Self-Declaration Submission Submit the self-declaration of compliance with DoT OSP conditions — confirming that grey routes will not be used and that EPABX infrastructure meets DoT specifications.
1. Step 6 — DoT Review and Grant of Registration DoT reviews the application. OSP registration is typically granted within 15–30 working days for complete applications.
1. Step 7 — Obtain Registration Certificate Download the OSP Registration Certificate from the Saral Sanchar portal. Share with TSPs as proof of OSP status for procuring bulk telecom connections.
1. Step 8 — Annual Compliance File the annual self-declaration of compliance on the Saral Sanchar portal by March 31 each year.

## Validity

OSP registration under the revised 2020 framework does not have a fixed expiry period — it is a perpetual registration subject to annual compliance filings and adherence to DoT conditions. Non-filing of annual self-declaration or violation of OSP conditions can lead to suspension or cancellation of the registration.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Does a software company with a small internal help desk need OSP registration?** Not necessarily. OSP registration is required only when a company uses PSTN/ISDN-based telecom resources to provide services to external clients or customers. An internal IT help desk serving only the company's own employees using standard corporate telephone connections does not require OSP registration. However, if the company operates a customer-facing call centre (even if small) and uses bulk telephone lines or EPABX systems to handle customer calls as a commercial service, OSP registration is required.

**2. Can home-based call centre agents be deployed legally after the 2020 OSP liberalisation?** Yes. The DoT's November 2020 OSP liberalisation explicitly permits OSPs to deploy agents working from home (WFH/remote agents) without requiring specific DoT approval. WFH agents must connect to the OSP's systems via VPN, maintain data security as per the company's policy, and use only TSP-provided connections (not grey market VOIP). The OSP must maintain logs of WFH agent connectivity and ensure compliance with the OSP conditions. This change has been a significant enabler for India's BPO industry's hybrid work models.

**3. What is a "grey route" in the context of OSP compliance and why is it illegal?** A grey route refers to the use of unauthorized or unlicensed channels to route international telephone calls — typically using illegal VOIP gateways that bypass licensed TSPs and evade call charges and regulatory oversight. Using grey routes for international calls is a serious violation under both the Indian Telegraph Act and DoT OSP conditions — it deprives the government of revenues, undermines licensed TSPs, and exposes the OSP to criminal prosecution, disconnection of all telecom services, and heavy penalties. All international calls from OSP centres must be routed exclusively through DoT-licensed international gateways operated by TSPs.

**4. We have call centres in 5 cities. Do we need separate OSP registrations for each?** No. A single OSP registration covers all of the company's OSP centres across India. However, the registration application must list all centres (with addresses and infrastructure details for each) under the same company. If a new centre is added after the initial registration, the company must update its OSP registration to include the new centre before commencing OSP operations from it. Each centre's EPABX configuration and TSP connections must comply with DoT OSP conditions.

**5. Our BPO client (a US company) is asking for our OSP license. Is OSP registration the same as a license?** In common parlance (and in many international client contracts), "OSP License" and "OSP Registration" are used interchangeably to refer to the DoT OSP registration certificate. Technically, OSP is a "registration" under the current DoT framework (not a license that requires approval in the traditional sense). The Registration Certificate issued by DoT upon successful OSP registration is the document you provide to clients and TSPs as proof of your authorised OSP status. Most international BPO clients understand this and accept the DoT OSP Registration Certificate as the required "OSP license."
