# DARPAN Registration

## Overview

DARPAN (Database And Registration of Voluntary And Non-Profit Associations) is an online portal developed and maintained by NITI Aayog, Government of India, to create a verified and searchable national database of NGOs, voluntary organisations, and civil society organisations operating in India. The portal provides a Unique ID to registered organisations, which is increasingly becoming a prerequisite for NGOs and non-profit entities to access central government grants, foreign contributions, and government-funded welfare schemes.
The DARPAN portal — accessible at ngodarpan.gov.in — serves as a central transparency and accountability mechanism. It enables the government to track the reach, activities, and funding of NGOs across India, and enables genuine civil society organisations to demonstrate their credibility, registration status, and work to government departments, foreign donors, and the general public.
DARPAN Registration (obtaining the Unique ID on the NGO DARPAN portal) has become essential for any NGO or voluntary organisation that:

- Seeks central government grants
- Files for FCRA (Foreign Contribution Regulation Act) registration or prior permission
- Applies for funds from the CSR corpus of companies
- Seeks accreditation with international funding agencies operating in India

## Features

- Unique DARPAN ID: A unique identification number assigned to each registered organisation — serves as the NGO's national identity
- Searchable public profile: Registered NGOs get a public-facing profile on the NGO DARPAN portal showing registration details, key personnel, projects undertaken, and geographical reach
- Central government grant prerequisite: Most central government ministries and departments require the DARPAN ID before processing any grant application
- FCRA prerequisite: The Ministry of Home Affairs requires DARPAN ID for FCRA registration and prior permission applications
- CSR fund access: Companies disbursing CSR funds are encouraged to channel them through DARPAN-registered NGOs for transparency
- Project tracking: NGOs can update and track their projects and beneficiary data on the portal — improving accountability
- Multi-organisation type coverage: Covers Societies, Trusts, Section 8 Companies, and other voluntary non-profit entities
- Free registration: Registration on DARPAN is free of cost

## Benefits

- Government grant eligibility: DARPAN ID is a mandatory prerequisite for receiving grants from central government ministries, departments, and autonomous bodies under the General Financial Rules (GFR)
- FCRA compliance: Without DARPAN ID, an NGO cannot apply for FCRA registration (for foreign contributions) or FCRA prior permission — blocking access to international funding
- CSR fund access: Corporate CSR teams prefer DARPAN-registered NGOs for fund disbursement as it provides a basic layer of verification and government recognition
- Credibility and transparency: A DARPAN profile with updated project and beneficiary data signals operational credibility to donors, corporate partners, and government bodies
- National visibility: DARPAN's searchable database makes genuine NGOs discoverable by government agencies, CSR teams, and international organisations looking for implementation partners
- Reduced duplication: DARPAN helps eliminate shell NGOs and fraudulent organisations from accessing government resources by requiring verified documentation
- Digital identity: The DARPAN ID serves as a digital identity for the NGO in government systems — simplifying application processes across multiple schemes

## Limitations

- Registration is not recognition: DARPAN ID is a database entry, not a quality certification or government recognition of the NGO's work — it merely confirms existence and basic details
- Mandatory but not sufficient: DARPAN registration alone does not make an NGO eligible for government grants — each ministry has additional eligibility and empanelment criteria
- Ongoing update burden: NGOs must keep their DARPAN profile updated (key personnel changes, annual returns, project updates) — outdated profiles may affect grant applications
- Not a substitute for FCRA: DARPAN registration is required before applying for FCRA, but the FCRA registration itself is a separate and far more rigorous process
- Limited to central government: DARPAN is primarily relevant for central government schemes; state government grant applications may have their own state-level NGO portals and requirements
- No automatic grant: Obtaining a DARPAN ID does not guarantee access to any specific grant; it is merely the first step in a multi-stage grant application process

## Applicability

DARPAN Registration is applicable to:

- Registered Societies (under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 or state equivalents)
- Charitable Trusts (registered under the Indian Trusts Act, 1882 or state trust legislation)
- Section 8 Companies (non-profit companies under the Companies Act, 2013)
- Other voluntary non-profit organisations legally registered in India

DARPAN registration is mandatory for any of the above entities that:

- Apply for central government grants under any ministry or department
- Apply for FCRA registration or FCRA prior permission
- Seek empanelment with government bodies as implementing agencies

State-wide applicability: DARPAN is a central portal applicable to NGOs across all states and UTs — Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, West Bengal, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Kerala, Bihar, Odisha, and all others.

## Documents Required

- **Registration certificate of the organisation** — Society registration / Trust deed / Section 8 certificate
- **PAN card of the organisation** — Organisation's PAN
- **Aadhaar card of key functionaries** — Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer
- **Bank account details of the organisation** — Cancelled cheque / bank passbook
- **List of governing body members** — Name, designation, address, contact details
- **Memorandum of Association / Trust Deed** — Constitutional document of the organisation
- **Annual report (last year)** — If the organisation has been operational
- **Details of projects/activities undertaken** — Brief description of work areas and beneficiaries
- **FCRA registration number (if already registered)** — For organisations with existing FCRA
- **Contact details** — Registered office address, phone, email

## Process

1. Step 1 — Visit DARPAN Portal Go to ngodarpan.gov.in. Click on "Sign Up" to register a new account for the organisation.
1. Step 2 — Organisation Registration Enter the organisation's basic details — name, type (Society/Trust/Section 8), registration number, state, and PAN.
1. Step 3 — Document Upload Upload scanned copies of the registration certificate, PAN, governing body member details, and other required documents.
1. Step 4 — Profile Completion Complete the full DARPAN profile — mission and vision, sectors of work, geographical coverage, past projects, beneficiary data, and bank account details.
1. Step 5 — Verification NITI Aayog reviews the submitted information and documents. The portal may send a verification email/OTP to confirm the registered email.
1. Step 6 — Unique ID Generation Upon successful registration and document verification, the system generates the Unique DARPAN ID for the organisation.
1. Step 7 — Ongoing Profile Maintenance Update the DARPAN profile annually with new projects, governance changes, and annual report uploads to keep the profile active and current.

## Validity

DARPAN registration does not have a fixed expiry date — once registered, the Unique ID is permanent. However, the profile must be kept updated. Government departments may not consider grant applications from NGOs with profiles that have not been updated in the preceding 1–2 years. NITI Aayog periodically reviews the database and may flag or deactivate dormant or incomplete profiles.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Is DARPAN registration mandatory for all NGOs in India?** DARPAN registration is mandatory only for NGOs seeking central government grants, FCRA registration, or FCRA prior permission. An NGO that operates purely on domestic private donations, membership fees, or state government grants may technically operate without DARPAN registration. However, given that DARPAN ID is becoming increasingly expected by CSR donors and institutional funders as a basic credibility indicator, it is strongly advisable for all registered NGOs to obtain it.

**2. We are a newly registered Trust. Can we apply for DARPAN ID immediately after registration?** Yes. A Trust (or Society or Section 8 Company) can apply for DARPAN ID immediately after obtaining the registration certificate. There is no minimum operational history requirement for DARPAN registration. However, the portal's "Projects and Beneficiaries" section may have limited data for a new organisation — this can be updated as the organisation undertakes work.

**3. Does DARPAN registration automatically enrol our NGO for government grants?** No. DARPAN registration provides the Unique ID that is a prerequisite for grant applications — but it does not automatically make your NGO eligible for or enrolled in any government grant scheme. Each central ministry and department has its own empanelment process, eligibility criteria (years of operation, minimum annual expenditure, audit requirements, geographic focus), and application procedures. DARPAN ID is step one; grant empanelment is a separate process for each ministry.

**4. Our NGO's key functionaries have changed. Do we need to update DARPAN?** Yes, and promptly. The DARPAN profile must reflect the current governing body members, Chairperson, Secretary, and Treasurer. Outdated personnel information on DARPAN can cause mismatches during grant processing, FCRA verification, and CSR fund disbursement. Log in to the DARPAN portal and update the governing body section with the new members' details, Aadhaar, and contact information. Changes in key personnel should also be updated with the Registrar of Societies/Trust/MCA simultaneously.

**5. Can a Section 8 Company register on DARPAN? Is there any difference in the process compared to a Trust or Society?** Yes, Section 8 Companies can and should register on DARPAN. The process is largely the same — registration on ngodarpan.gov.in with the organisation's constitutional documents. For Section 8 Companies, the registration certificate issued by the Registrar of Companies (RoC) under the Companies Act, 2013 serves as the registration proof, along with the Certificate of Incorporation and MoA/AoA. The Section 8 Company's PAN, bank details, and governing body (board of directors) details are required — the same categories of information as for Trusts and Societies.
