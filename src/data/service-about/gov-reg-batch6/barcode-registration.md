# Bar Code Registration

## Overview

A Barcode is a machine-readable optical label that contains information about the product it is attached to — including the product's origin, manufacturer, product category, and unique identification number. In India, barcode registration is managed by GS1 India, the national chapter of GS1 — the global not-for-profit organisation that sets and administers barcode and supply chain standards used in over 150 countries.
GS1 India operates under the aegis of the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India, and the Quality Council of India (QCI). When a manufacturer or brand registers with GS1 India, they receive a globally unique GS1 Company Prefix — a numeric code assigned exclusively to that company — from which they generate unique GTINs (Global Trade Item Numbers) for each of their product variants. These GTINs form the basis of the barcodes (EAN-13, UPC-A, QR Code, etc.) printed on product packaging.

Barcode registration is mandatory for:

- Products sold in modern retail (supermarkets, hypermarkets, organised retail chains)
- Products listed on e-commerce marketplaces (Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, BigBasket)
- Products exported to international markets
- Healthcare and pharmaceutical products tracked in supply chains

Beyond retail, barcodes underpin logistics, inventory management, traceability, anti-counterfeiting, and supply chain automation across industries.

## Features

- GS1 Company Prefix: A globally unique numeric prefix (6–9 digits) assigned exclusively to your company — the foundation of all your product barcodes
- GTIN generation: Generate unique GTINs for each of your products and product variants (size, flavour, colour, pack size)
- Multiple barcode formats supported: EAN-13 (retail shelf), EAN-8 (small packs), UPC-A (US market), ITF-14 (logistics/cartons), GS1-128 (shipments), QR Code (extended information)
- Global recognition: GS1 barcodes are recognised and scannable in retail and supply chain systems worldwide — no separate registration needed for export markets
- Online product registry: Enrolment with GS1 India gives access to the GS1 India Product Registry — a verified product database accessible to retailers and e-commerce platforms
- License-based model: GS1 India issues annual licenses for use of the GS1 Company Prefix — the prefix cannot be permanently purchased; it is an annual subscription
- Verification service: GS1 India provides a barcode verification service to ensure your printed barcodes are scan-compliant before they reach retail shelves

## Benefits

- Mandatory for organised retail: Modern trade retailers (DMart, Reliance Retail, BigBazaar, Spencer's) require valid GS1 barcodes on all products before they will stock them
- E-commerce marketplace access: Amazon, Flipkart, and other major platforms require GTIN/barcode for product listing; valid GS1 barcodes prevent listing rejections and ASIN creation issues
- Export readiness: International buyers and retailers require GS1 barcodes; Indian products with proper GS1 barcodes are immediately retail-shelf ready in global markets
- Supply chain efficiency: Barcodes enable automated inventory management, faster billing, reduced human error, and real-time stock tracking across warehouses and retail points
- Anti-counterfeiting: Unique GTINs linked to the GS1 registry make it difficult for counterfeiters to replicate products with valid, verifiable barcodes
- Brand credibility: A properly barcoded product signals professionalism and compliance — important for B2B sales, institutional procurement, and investor presentations
- Regulatory compliance: FSSAI, BIS, and certain drug regulatory requirements reference barcode/GTIN-based traceability for food, consumer goods, and medical devices

## Limitations

- Annual renewal mandatory: GS1 Company Prefix is an annual license — non-renewal means the company loses the right to use its prefix and all associated GTINs, causing retail and supply chain disruptions
- Not a government registration: GS1 India is a private, not-for-profit body; its registration is a commercial/industry standard membership, not a statutory government registration
- Prefix cannot be transferred: The GS1 Company Prefix is non-transferable — in M&A and brand acquisition transactions, the acquirer must obtain their own prefix and re-label products
- No barcode = no marketplace listing: Without valid GTINs, products cannot be listed on most organised retail and e-commerce platforms — this is an absolute gate, not a suggestion
- Cost scales with prefix capacity: Fees are tiered based on the number of products (GTINs) you need to generate — businesses with large product catalogs pay significantly higher annual fees
- Barcode printing not included: GS1 India provides the GTIN numbers and barcode data files — the physical printing of barcodes on packaging is the company's responsibility and cost

## Applicability

- Pharmaceutical companies and medical device manufacturers
- Electronics and consumer durables manufacturers
- Textile and apparel brands

## Process

1. Step 1 — Determine GTIN Requirement Count the number of unique product variants (each size, flavour, colour, and packaging combination is a separate GTIN) to determine the appropriate GS1 membership tier.
1. Step 2 — Online Application on GS1 India Portal Visit gs1india.org and complete the online membership application with company details, product information, and GSTIN.
1. Step 3 — Document Submission Upload required documents. GS1 India verifies the applicant's identity, legal entity status, and GSTIN.
1. Step 4 — Fee Payment Pay the annual membership/license fee based on the selected GTIN capacity tier via online payment.
1. Step 5 — GS1 Company Prefix Allotment Upon approval (typically within 3–7 working days), GS1 India issues the unique GS1 Company Prefix.
1. Step 6 — GTIN Generation Use the GS1 India online GTIN Generator to assign unique GTINs to each product variant. GS1 India provides barcode image files (SVG, PNG, EPS) for each GTIN.
1. Step 7 — Barcode Verification Before printing on packaging, verify barcode quality using GS1 India's verification service or a certified barcode verifier to ensure scan-compliance.
1. Step 8 — GS1 India Product Registry Upload product data (images, descriptions, attributes) to the GS1 India Product Registry — this makes your products discoverable by retailers and e-commerce platforms.

## Validity

GS1 India membership and the associated GS1 Company Prefix are valid for 1 year from the date of registration. Annual renewal is mandatory to continue using the prefix and associated GTINs. If the membership lapses, the company technically loses the right to use its prefix — and GS1 India may reassign the prefix to another company after a suitable dormancy period, potentially creating conflicts. Products already in circulation with the old GTIN continue to be scannable (the barcode data doesn't disappear), but the company cannot generate new GTINs or access GS1 India services.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Can I use free barcode generators available online instead of registering with GS1 India?** Free online barcode generators create the visual barcode image but do not assign a globally unique GTIN. Barcodes generated from free tools use random numbers that may conflict with GTINs legitimately assigned to other products globally. Amazon, Flipkart, and most organised retailers specifically require GS1-issued GTINs and can detect non-GS1 barcodes during listing. Using non-GS1 barcodes on products can lead to listing rejection, account suspension, and supply chain failures.

**2. I have 5 product variants of the same product (different flavours). Do I need 5 separate GTINs?** Yes. Each unique product variant — differentiated by flavour, size, colour, packaging type, or any attribute that makes it a distinct consumer unit — requires its own unique GTIN. A 250g mango juice and a 500ml mango juice from the same brand are two different GTINs. A mango flavour and an orange flavour are two more. Additionally, if you sell the product individually (single unit) and also in a pack of 6, the pack of 6 is another GTIN. Plan your GTIN requirements carefully before choosing a membership tier.

**3. My company was acquired by a larger group. Can we transfer the GS1 prefix to the new parent company?** No. GS1 Company Prefixes are company-specific and non-transferable. In an acquisition, the existing prefix and GTINs can continue to be used by the acquired company if it continues to operate as a separate legal entity. If the brand/products are absorbed into the parent company, new GTINs must be generated under the parent company's prefix, and product packaging must be updated. This is an important practical consideration in M&A transactions involving product companies.

**4. Is GS1 barcode registration mandatory for selling on Amazon India?** Amazon India strongly prefers GS1-issued GTINs for product listings. While Amazon has a "GTIN exemption" process for certain categories (handmade products, private labels without barcodes), most categories require valid GTINs for listing. Amazon uses GTIN data to maintain catalogue integrity and prevent duplicate listings. Sellers using non-GS1 barcodes risk listing rejections, ASIN suppression, and account health warnings. For any serious e-commerce business, GS1 registration is effectively mandatory.

**5. We are a small home-based food business. Do we really need GS1 barcode registration?** If you are selling exclusively through your own social media, WhatsApp, or direct-to-consumer without listing on organised retail platforms or e-commerce marketplaces, you may not immediately need GS1 registration. However, the moment you want to list on Swiggy Instamart, BigBasket, Amazon Fresh, or supply to retail stores, GS1 barcodes become mandatory. Given the low cost of GS1 Starter membership (10 GTINs for approximately ₹5,000–₹8,000 per year), it is advisable to register early — especially as FSSAI also increasingly references GTIN-based traceability in its food safety framework.
