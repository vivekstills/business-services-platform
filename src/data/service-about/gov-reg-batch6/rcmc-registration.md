# RCMC Registration

## Overview

RCMC (Registration-cum-Membership Certificate) is a certificate issued by Export Promotion Councils (EPCs) or Commodity Boards to Indian exporters. It serves the dual purpose of registering the exporter with the relevant EPC/Commodity Board and granting them membership — hence "Registration-cum-Membership Certificate."
RCMC is mandatory for Indian exporters who wish to claim benefits under India's Foreign Trade Policy (FTP) — including export incentive schemes like the Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products (RoDTEP), Advance Authorisation, EPCG (Export Promotion Capital Goods) Scheme, and duty drawback. The RCMC is issued by the EPC or Commodity Board whose mandate covers the product category being exported by the applicant.
India has approximately 26 Export Promotion Councils and 10 Commodity Boards, each covering specific product categories:

## Features

- Dual-purpose certificate: Simultaneously registers the exporter with the EPC and grants membership — all in one certificate
- Product-specific: RCMC is issued for specific product categories (HS codes) covered by the issuing EPC/Commodity Board; exporters dealing in multiple product categories may need RCMCs from multiple bodies
- DGFT integration: RCMC must be linked to the exporter's IEC (Import Export Code) on the DGFT portal — this linkage enables claim of FTP benefits
- 5-year validity: RCMC is typically valid for 5 years (April 1 to March 31 of the 5th year) with annual renewal of membership
- EPC services access: RCMC holders get access to EPC services — buyer-seller meets, export fairs, market development assistance, trade delegations, and export intelligence
- Digital RCMC: DGFT has introduced e-RCMC — digital RCMC integrated with the DGFT portal for seamless FTP benefit claims
- Mandatory for FTP schemes: RCMC is a prerequisite for filing applications for RoDTEP, Advance Authorisation, EPCG, and other FTP schemes with DGFT

## Benefits

- FTP incentive scheme access: Without RCMC, exporters cannot claim benefits under RoDTEP, Advance Authorisation, EPCG, Merchandise Exports from India Scheme (if still applicable), and other DGFT-administered export promotion schemes
- APEDA/MPEDA certification for food exports: For agricultural and marine product exports, APEDA and MPEDA RCMC is mandatory for customs clearance — not just for incentive claims
- EPC networking: EPC membership provides access to government-supported international trade fairs, buyer-seller meets, and market intelligence — helping exporters identify new markets
- Brand India promotion: EPCs collectively promote Indian products in global markets — RCMC holders benefit from these promotional efforts as representatives of Indian export industries
- Market development assistance: Many EPCs provide Market Development Assistance (MDA) grants to RCMC holders for international trade fair participation and export market development
- Legal standing in export disputes: RCMC validates the exporter's registration status — useful in commercial and legal disputes with overseas buyers
- Certificate of Origin facilitation: Some EPCs assist RCMC holders in obtaining Certificates of Origin for preferential trade under FTAs — relevant for exports to ASEAN, Japan, Korea, UAE, etc.

## Limitations

- Multiple RCMCs may be needed: Exporters dealing in diverse product categories (e.g., both engineering goods and chemicals) may need RCMCs from multiple EPCs — adding administrative and fee burden
- Annual membership fee: While the RCMC itself has 5-year validity, EPC membership fees are typically annual — non-payment of annual fees can affect RCMC status
- IEC prerequisite: RCMC can only be obtained after obtaining an IEC (Import Export Code) from DGFT — IEC is therefore the first step
- Product category accuracy: Exporters must correctly identify and apply under the appropriate EPC for their product HS code — misclassification can cause FTP benefit rejections
- Renewal complexity: When RCMC expires after 5 years, renewal requires fresh application with updated documents
- APEDA registration separate: For certain food product categories, APEDA registration (separate from RCMC) may be required for specific export certifications

## Applicability

RCMC is required for any Indian entity that:

- Exports goods or services and wishes to claim benefits under India's Foreign Trade Policy
- Exports agricultural, processed food, marine, or horticultural products (APEDA/MPEDA RCMC mandatory for customs clearance)
- Exports under an Advance Authorisation or EPCG scheme
- Is a manufacturer-exporter or merchant-exporter with an IEC

Applicable pan-India — exporters across all states (Maharashtra, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Rajasthan, UP, West Bengal, Telangana, Kerala, Punjab, etc.) can obtain RCMC from the relevant EPC.

## Documents Required

- **IEC (Import Export Code)** — Mandatory prerequisite — from DGFT
- **Application form of the relevant EPC** — EPC-specific application
- **Certificate of Incorporation / Partnership Deed** — Legal entity documents
- **PAN card** — Company/individual PAN
- **GST Registration Certificate** — GSTIN
- **Bank certificate / Banker's letter** — Certifying banking relationship
- **Export performance data (if any)** — Prior year's export turnover (for renewal or some EPCs)
- **MSME/Udyam Registration (if applicable)** — For MSME fee category
- **DSC of authorised signatory** — For digital/online application
- **Photograph of authorised signatory** — For EPC records

## Process

1. Step 1 — Obtain IEC Apply for and obtain Import Export Code (IEC) from DGFT (dgft.gov.in) — mandatory before applying for RCMC.
1. Step 2 — Identify Relevant EPC Based on your export product category and HS codes, identify the appropriate Export Promotion Council or Commodity Board.
1. Step 3 — Application Submission Apply on the EPC's online portal (most major EPCs now have online application systems) or submit a physical application to the EPC's regional office.
1. Step 4 — Document Submission and Fee Payment Submit all required documents and pay the membership/RCMC fee online or via demand draft.
1. Step 5 — EPC Verification The EPC verifies documents and IEC details. Some EPCs may request additional information.
1. Step 6 — RCMC Issuance Upon verification, the EPC issues the RCMC — typically within 7–15 working days of complete application.
1. Step 7 — DGFT Portal Linkage Link the issued RCMC with the IEC on the DGFT portal to enable FTP scheme benefit claims.
1. Step 8 — Annual Membership Renewal Renew EPC membership annually by paying the annual membership fee — maintains RCMC validity throughout the 5-year period.

## Validity

RCMC is typically valid for 5 years (April 1 of the year of issue to March 31 of the 5th year). Annual EPC membership fees must be paid within the 5-year period to maintain active RCMC status. At the end of 5 years, a fresh RCMC application is required. Some EPCs issue certificates co-terminus with the calendar year — verify the specific EPC's validity structure at the time of application.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Can I export without RCMC if I'm not claiming any FTP incentives?** For most product categories, yes — exporters who are not claiming any DGFT-administered FTP benefits are not legally required to hold RCMC. However, for specific categories like agricultural and marine products, APEDA and MPEDA RCMC is mandatory for customs clearance regardless of whether FTP incentives are claimed. Additionally, many overseas buyers, particularly institutional and government buyers, require proof of EPC membership (RCMC) as part of supplier qualification.

**2. My company exports both garments and engineering goods. Do I need two separate RCMCs?** Yes. Garments fall under AEPC (Apparel Export Promotion Council) and engineering goods fall under EEPC India. Since different product categories are governed by different EPCs, you need separate RCMC from each relevant EPC. Some exporters with highly diverse product portfolios hold 3–4 RCMCs from different EPCs. The IEC is common across all RCMCs — it is the single identifier on the DGFT portal that links all your RCMCs.

**3. Is RCMC the same as IEC? What's the difference?** No, they are different. IEC (Import Export Code) is a 10-digit code issued by DGFT that is the basic licence to import or export — every exporter/importer must have it. RCMC is a registration with a specific Export Promotion Council that is required to claim FTP scheme benefits. IEC is always needed first; RCMC is a second-layer registration built on top of IEC. IEC is from DGFT (government); RCMC is from private-sector EPCs/Commodity Boards (though they operate under the Ministry of Commerce framework).

**4. How do I link my RCMC with my IEC on the DGFT portal?** Log in to the DGFT portal (dgft.gov.in) with your IEC credentials. Navigate to the "Services" section and find "RCMC Management." Enter your RCMC details — issuing EPC, certificate number, validity dates, and product HS codes covered. The portal validates the RCMC details against the EPC's records (if the EPC is integrated with DGFT's e-RCMC system). Once linked, your RCMC is visible in your DGFT profile and FTP scheme applications automatically reference it.

**5. Our RCMC expired 2 months ago and we didn't renew it. Can we still claim RoDTEP on exports done during the valid period?** For exports made during the period when the RCMC was valid, you can claim RoDTEP provided all other eligibility conditions are met and the claim is filed within the permissible filing window. DGFT's RoDTEP claim portal checks RCMC validity as of the export date (shipping bill date) — not the date of claim filing. If your RCMC was valid when the exports were made, the claim should be processable. However, you must renew the RCMC immediately for future exports to continue being eligible for FTP benefits.
