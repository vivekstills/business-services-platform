# Startup India Registration

## Overview

Startup India Registration refers to the process of getting your startup officially recognised by DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade) under the Startup India initiative. This is the same DPIIT Recognition covered in our DPIIT Registration service — however, many users specifically search for "Startup India Registration" as the entry point to the Startup India ecosystem.
The Startup India initiative was launched on January 16, 2016 by the Government of India with the vision of building a strong ecosystem for nurturing innovation and startups in India that will drive sustainable economic growth and generate large-scale employment opportunities.
Startup India Registration (DPIIT Recognition) is the foundational step in accessing the full suite of Startup India benefits — ranging from tax exemptions and self-certification of labour law compliance to IPR fee concessions, government procurement preference, and access to the Fund of Funds.
The registration is done on the Startup India portal (startupindia.gov.in), is completely free, and is processed within a few working days for eligible entities.
For a complete coverage of eligibility, features, process, and FAQs, please refer to our DPIIT Registration service page. This page focuses on the Startup India programme context and the specific benefits ecosystem.

## Features

- Free and online: Startup India Registration is completely free and done online at startupindia.gov.in
- DPIIT Recognition Certificate: The output is the DPIIT Recognition Certificate with unique recognition number
- Access to Startup India Hub: Registered startups get access to the Startup India Hub — a knowledge, mentoring, and networking platform
- Tax exemption gateway: Gateway to applying for Section 80-IAC income tax exemption and Section 54GB capital gains exemption
- Self-certification of compliance: 5-year self-certification under 6 labour laws and 3 environmental laws
- IPR fee concessions: 80% rebate on patent fees and 50% on trademark fees
- Startup India showcase: Profile on Startup India portal — discoverable by investors, incubators, and government stakeholders
- Access to government schemes: Access to Startup India-specific schemes — Seed Fund Scheme (up to ₹20 lakh for prototype development), Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS — up to ₹50 lakh), and AIM (Atal Innovation Mission) programmes
- Incubator network: Access to DPIIT-recognised incubators and accelerators across India

## Benefits

- Seed funding access: The Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS) provides up to ₹50 lakh to DPIIT-recognised startups for proof of concept, prototype development, product trials, market entry, and commercialisation — disbursed through DPIIT-recognised incubators
- Income tax holiday: After IMB approval, 100% income tax exemption for 3 years out of the first 10 years — significantly improving early-stage unit economics
- Capital gains exemption: Exemption from capital gains tax under Section 54GB when proceeds from residential property sale are invested in eligible startup equity
- Angel tax exemption: DPIIT-recognised startups are exempt from Section 56(2)(viib) — the "angel tax" — on issue of shares at a premium (with conditions and investment value limits)
- Government procurement: Participation in government procurement without turnover and prior experience requirements — access to a ₹70,000+ crore annual government procurement market
- Regulatory sandbox: DPIIT facilitates access to regulatory sandboxes with SEBI, RBI, IRDAI, and other regulators — allowing startups to test innovative financial, fintech, and insurance products under relaxed regulatory conditions
- Easy exit: IBC fast-track winding up (within 90 days) for registered startups classified as fast-track companies

## Limitations

- 10-year and ₹100 crore cap: Recognition lapses if the startup crosses 10 years from incorporation or ₹100 crore in annual turnover
- Innovation requirement: Generic or conventional businesses do not qualify; innovation, technology, or IP must be demonstrable
- Tax exemption requires IMB approval: DPIIT Recognition alone does not grant income tax exemption — a separate Inter-Ministerial Board (IMB) application is needed
- Angel tax exemption — investment cap: Angel tax exemption for DPIIT-recognised startups applies only for investments up to the fair market value of shares — investments above FMV are still taxable
- SISFS fund is limited: The seed fund is channelled through DPIIT-recognised incubators who have their own selection criteria — recognition does not guarantee seed fund access
- Not all benefits are central: State-specific startup benefits (state seed funds, state innovation schemes) are separate from Startup India and have their own eligibility criteria

## Applicability

| Criterion | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Entity type | Private Limited Company, LLP, or Registered Partnership Firm |
| Age | Incorporated/registered within the past 10 years |
| Annual turnover | Below ₹100 crore in any year since incorporation |
| Nature | Working towards innovation/development or scalable business model |
| Restriction | Not formed by splitting/restructuring existing business |

Applicable across all states and UTs — Bengaluru, Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune, Ahmedabad, Jaipur, Kolkata, Kochi, and all Tier 2 and Tier 3 startup ecosystems.

## Documents Required

- **Certificate of Incorporation / Registration** — For Pvt Ltd, LLP, or registered partnership
- **PAN of the entity** — Business PAN
- **Innovation description** — 500-word description of product/service and innovation
- **Pitch deck or demo link (optional)** — Supports innovation description
- **Founders' details** — Name, DIN/DPIN, PAN, Aadhaar
- **Revenue details (if any)** — Confirming sub-₹100 crore threshold

## Process

1. Step 1 — startupindia.gov.in Portal Register on the Startup India portal with the company's email. Create an entity profile.
1. Step 2 — Application Details Fill in incorporation details, founders' information, entity type, and industry sector.
1. Step 3 — Innovation Description Write a compelling description of the startup's innovation or scalable business model. This is the most reviewed section.
1. Step 4 — Document Upload Upload the Certificate of Incorporation.
1. Step 5 — Self-Declaration Submit the self-declaration of eligibility.
1. Step 6 — DPIIT Review Review and recognition within 2–10 working days typically.
1. Step 7 — Recognition Certificate Download the DPIIT Recognition Certificate from the Startup India portal.

## Validity

DPIIT Recognition (Startup India Registration) is valid until the entity crosses 10 years from incorporation date or ₹100 crore in annual turnover — whichever occurs first. No periodic renewal is required. The certificate does not expire within the 10-year eligibility window.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Can a Partnership Firm (not LLP) get Startup India Registration?** Yes. Registered Partnership Firms are eligible for DPIIT Recognition under the Startup India framework — provided they meet all other eligibility criteria (age, turnover, and innovation requirement). The partner applying for recognition must be a registered partner of the firm. Note that Registered Partnership Firms have certain limitations compared to Pvt Ltd companies and LLPs — particularly in raising equity investment and issuing shares — which are important considerations for startups planning to raise external funding.

**2. We are a 4-year-old edtech startup with ₹15 crore revenue. Are we eligible for Startup India Registration?** Yes, based on the stated facts. You meet the age criterion (within 10 years) and the turnover criterion (below ₹100 crore). Eligibility depends additionally on demonstrating the innovation/technology/IP element. An edtech startup developing proprietary learning technology, adaptive AI, or innovative pedagogy would typically qualify. A pure content aggregation or tutoring marketplace business model without a technology-driven innovation element might face more scrutiny in the recognition review.

**3. Our startup was rejected for DPIIT Recognition. What should we do?** If DPIIT rejects a recognition application, the applicant can reapply with a strengthened innovation description. The most common reason for rejection is an insufficient or unclear description of the innovation element. Revise the innovation write-up to clearly articulate: (a) what the product/service is; (b) what specific problem it solves; (c) how it uses technology, IP, or an innovative business model; (d) its scalability potential and employment/wealth creation impact. If the rejection reason is entity-type or eligibility-related, address the structural issue first before reapplying.

**4. After getting DPIIT Recognition, how do we apply for the Section 80-IAC income tax exemption?** Section 80-IAC income tax exemption requires a separate application to the Inter-Ministerial Board (IMB) — constituted by DPIIT — through the Startup India portal. The IMB evaluates whether the startup is engaged in genuine innovation and meets additional criteria for tax exemption. The process involves submitting detailed information about the business model, technology, product, team, financials, and innovation. IMB applications are reviewed rigorously — not all DPIIT-recognised startups are granted 80-IAC exemption. Professional support for the IMB application is highly advisable given its complexity and financial impact.

**5. Can an existing profitable business spin off a startup subsidiary and get DPIIT Recognition for the subsidiary?** Yes, provided the subsidiary is independently incorporated (as a new Pvt Ltd or LLP), meets the age criterion, has its own turnover below ₹100 crore, and is genuinely working on innovation. However, the restriction that the entity should "not be formed by splitting up or reconstruction of an existing business" must be carefully navigated — if the subsidiary is merely taking over an existing business segment (rather than developing a new, innovative product/service), DPIIT may deny recognition. The subsidiary must demonstrate independent innovation, not just a restructured version of the parent's existing operations.
