# ITR-U filing (updated return)

## Overview

ITR-U (Updated Return) is a special provision introduced under Section 139(8A) of the Income Tax Act, 1961 by the Finance Act, 2022, that allows taxpayers to file or update their Income Tax Return within 2 years from the end of the relevant assessment year — even after the standard deadline for filing or revising a return has passed. This was a landmark reform aimed at encouraging voluntary tax compliance by providing taxpayers a mechanism to correct past omissions or errors without waiting for a notice from the Income Tax department.
The Finance Act, 2025 extended the ITR-U filing window from 2 years to 4 years from the end of the relevant assessment year — significantly expanding the opportunity for voluntary compliance.
ITR-U can be filed to:

- Declare income that was omitted or under-reported in the original return
- Correct errors in the original return (wrong head of income, incorrect exemption claims)
- File a fresh return where no return was originally filed
- Reduce carried forward losses or unabsorbed depreciation (only to reduce a previously over-claimed loss)
Key financial deterrent: ITR-U is not penalty-free — an additional tax is levied as a deterrent against using it as a routine filing tool:
| Filing Period | Additional Tax on Underpaid Tax |
| --- | --- |
| Within 1 year from end of relevant AY | 25% additional tax |
| Between 1–2 years from end of relevant AY | 50% additional tax |
| Between 2–3 years from end of relevant AY | 60% additional tax |
| Between 3–4 years from end of relevant AY | 70% additional tax |


## Applicability

ITR-U can be filed by:

- Individuals who missed the original filing deadline (July 31) and the belated filing deadline (December 31)
- Individuals/entities who filed an original return but omitted certain income (salary, rental income, capital gains, freelance income)
- Companies and firms who did not report certain transactions in their original return
- Taxpayers who incorrectly claimed deductions, exemptions, or credits not entitled to them
- Taxpayers who want to switch the head of income (e.g., income incorrectly reported as business income instead of capital gains)
**ITR-U CANNOT be filed to:**

Reduce tax liability compared to the original return
Claim a refund or increase the refund amount
File if a search or survey has been conducted on the taxpayer and proceedings are pending
Reduce the amount of income reported in the original return (you can only increase income)
File if assessment, reassessment, or revision proceedings have been completed for the year
File for AY 2020-21 and earlier (ITR-U is applicable from AY 2020-21 onwards)

## Features

Extended window: Up to 4 years from the end of the relevant assessment year (after Finance Act, 2025 amendment)
Voluntary compliance mechanism: Allows self-correction without waiting for or responding to an income tax notice
Additional tax as deterrent: Graduated additional tax (25% to 70%) discourages using ITR-U as a routine delay mechanism
No refund claims: ITR-U cannot result in a refund — only additional tax payment scenarios are covered
One ITR-U per assessment year per taxpayer: Only one updated return can be filed per assessment year — it cannot be revised again after filing
Form ITR-U: A separate form (ITR-U) is used — it captures the reason for updating and the additional income being declared
Works alongside original ITR form: ITR-U is filed in conjunction with the relevant ITR form (ITR-1 through ITR-7) that would have been applicable to the taxpayer
Available for all taxpayer categories: Individuals, HUFs, firms, companies, AOPs, BOIs, and other entities can file ITR-U

## Benefits

Avoid income tax notice and scrutiny: Voluntarily declaring under-reported income through ITR-U significantly reduces the risk of a formal notice, reassessment, and the associated penalty (200% of tax for concealment under Section 271(1)(c))
Penalty protection: By filing ITR-U and paying the additional tax, taxpayers avoid the stringent penalty provisions for under-reporting (50% of tax on under-reported income) and mis-reporting (200% of tax) under Sections 270A
Prosecution risk reduction: Voluntary disclosure through ITR-U is treated favourably by the Income Tax department — reducing prosecution risk under Section 276C for willful tax evasion
Clean compliance record: A corrected return through ITR-U helps in maintaining a clean compliance record — important for loan applications, visa applications, and other KYC-linked processes
Regularises past omissions: Particularly useful for taxpayers who received foreign income, cryptocurrency gains, or freelance payments without reporting them — ITR-U provides a structured path to regularise
Business transactions regularisation: Companies and firms can correct discrepancies between their books and returns — aligning tax filings with audited financials

## Process

1. Step 1 — Identify the Under-Reported Income Review all income sources — salary, house property, business/profession, capital gains, other sources — and identify any income not declared in the original return or declared under the wrong head.
2. Step 2 — Determine Applicable Assessment Year Identify the relevant Assessment Year for which ITR-U is being filed. Confirm the year is within the permissible window (currently up to AY 2020-21 onwards, within 4 years of AY end).
3. Step 3 — Compute Additional Tax Calculate the additional income, the tax thereon (including surcharge and cess), and the additional tax payable (25%/50%/60%/70% depending on timing). Also compute interest under Section 234B and 234C on the shortfall.
4. Step 4 — Pay Additional Tax Pay the computed tax, interest, and additional tax through Challan 280 on the Income Tax portal or at an authorised bank. Note the challan details (BSR code, date of deposit, serial number).
5. Step 5 — Log In to Income Tax Portal Log in to incometax.gov.in. Navigate to e-File → Income Tax Returns → File Income Tax Return → Select Assessment Year → Select ITR-U.
6. Step 6 — Fill ITR-U Form Complete the ITR-U form — state the reason for updating (income omitted, wrong head of income, etc.), provide the additional income details, reconcile with the original return, and enter tax payment challan details.
7. Step 7 — Verify the Return Verify the ITR-U using Aadhaar OTP, net banking EVC, or physically sending signed ITR-V to CPC Bengaluru within 30 days.
8. Step 8 — Download Acknowledgement Download the ITR-U acknowledgement (ITR-V) from the portal after successful verification.

## Errors

- Attempting to use ITR-U to claim refund: ITR-U cannot result in a refund — attempting to file ITR-U to increase a refund or reduce tax liability is not permitted
- Filing ITR-U when assessment is already completed: If the AO has completed assessment under Sections 143(3), 144, 147, or 153A for the year, ITR-U cannot be filed for that year
- Computing additional tax incorrectly: The 25%/50%/60%/70% additional tax is computed on the incremental tax (additional income's tax plus interest) — computing it on the total tax liability is a common error
- Not paying the full additional tax before filing: ITR-U must be accompanied by full payment of the tax due — filing ITR-U without complete payment makes it defective
- Disclosing reduction in income: Attempting to reduce income or increase loss through ITR-U — the law only permits updating to increase income/reduce losses


## Frequently asked questions

- **I received cryptocurrency gains in FY 2021-22 and didn't report them. Can I use ITR-U to report these now?** Yes — unreported cryptocurrency gains are one of the most common reasons for ITR-U filing. Cryptocurrency income (categorised as Virtual Digital Assets — VDA — taxable at 30% flat under Section 115BBH from AY 2022-23 onwards) that was not declared can be reported through ITR-U. For AY 2022-23 onwards, VDA income is taxed at 30% without any deduction (except cost of acquisition). For AY 2021-22 (before the VDA-specific provisions), crypto gains were taxable under the applicable head (business income or capital gains). File ITR-U for the relevant AY, pay the additional tax, and maintain all transaction records from your exchange as supporting documentation.
- **What is the penalty if the Income Tax department discovers the under-reported income before I file ITR-U?** If the Income Tax department detects the under-reported income through their data analytics (AIS mismatch, SFT data, bank information exchange) before you voluntarily file ITR-U, the voluntary disclosure benefit is lost. The department will initiate scrutiny assessment or reassessment — and can levy: (a) tax on under-reported income; (b) interest under Sections 234A, 234B, 234C; (c) penalty of 50% of tax on under-reported income under Section 270A; (d) penalty of 200% for mis-reporting/concealment under Section 270A(9). The department also has the option of prosecution for willful tax evasion. Filing ITR-U proactively, even with the additional tax cost, is significantly less expensive than facing assessment with penalties.
- **I filed my ITR-1 for AY 2023-24 but forgot to include rental income of ₹3 lakh. Can I file ITR-U or should I revise my return?** If you are within the revision deadline — i.e., the assessment year is not yet completed and the December 31 deadline for revised return has not passed — you should file a Revised Return under Section 139(5) instead of ITR-U. A revised return has no additional tax implication. ITR-U should only be used when the revision window has closed. Since AY 2023-24 revision deadline was December 31, 2023, and if you are approaching this after that date, ITR-U (with additional tax) would be the appropriate remedy. For AY 2024-25 (FY 2023-24), if the revision window (December 31, 2024) is still open, file a Revised Return.
- **Can a company file ITR-U to correct a mistake in its depreciation calculation?** Yes, a company can file ITR-U to correct errors in depreciation calculation — such as claiming depreciation at the wrong rate or missing depreciation on a newly acquired asset. However, there is a critical constraint: ITR-U can only be used to increase income or reduce losses, not to decrease income or increase losses. If the depreciation correction results in additional income (because the original return over-claimed depreciation), ITR-U is the right mechanism. If the correction would decrease income (because depreciation was under-claimed in the original return, resulting in lower income than actuals), the provision permitting ITR-U does not allow this — as it would reduce tax liability.
- **We missed ITR-U for a year where we also had TDS already deducted. How do we compute the additional tax?** The additional tax under ITR-U is computed on the "net tax payable" after considering TDS already deducted. The computation is: (Total tax on total income including the new additional income) minus (Tax on income as per original return) minus (TDS already deducted and deposited) = Net incremental tax. The additional tax (25%/50%/60%/70%) is applied to this net incremental tax plus any interest under Sections 234B/234C on the shortfall. Engaging a CA for this computation is strongly advisable — errors in the additional tax calculation cause processing issues with the filed ITR-U.
