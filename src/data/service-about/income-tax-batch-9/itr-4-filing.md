# ITR-4 form filing (Sugam)

## Overview

ITR-4, popularly known as Sugam (meaning "easy" in Hindi), is the simplified Income Tax Return form for individuals, HUFs (Hindu Undivided Families), and firms (other than LLPs) who have opted for the presumptive taxation scheme under Sections 44AD, 44ADA, or 44AE of the Income Tax Act. It is designed to reduce the compliance burden for small business owners, freelancers, and transport operators by allowing them to declare a percentage of turnover as profit — without maintaining detailed books of accounts.
The presumptive taxation scheme provides three tracks:
Section
Applicable To
Presumptive Income
44AD
Business persons with turnover up to ₹2 crore
8% of turnover (6% for digital receipts)
44ADA
Specified professionals (doctors, lawyers, CAs, architects, etc.) with gross receipts up to ₹50 lakh
50% of gross receipts
44AE
Goods transport operators with up to 10 vehicles
Fixed amount per vehicle per month

ITR-4 is one of the most widely filed return forms in India — making it accessible to lakhs of small shopkeepers, traders, consultants, and professionals who otherwise would need to maintain extensive accounting records.


## Applicability

ITR-4 (Sugam) is applicable for:
Individuals, HUFs, and Partnership Firms (not LLP) with:
Business income eligible for Section 44AD (turnover up to ₹2 crore)
Professional income eligible for Section 44ADA (gross receipts up to ₹50 lakh)
Income from goods transport vehicles under Section 44AE
Additionally having income from salary/pension, one house property, and other sources (interest, family pension)
Total income up to ₹50 lakh
**ITR-4 CANNOT be used if:**

The person is a director in a company
Unlisted equity shares are held
Income from capital gains exists
The person has foreign assets or foreign income
Agricultural income exceeds ₹5,000
Income from speculative business exists
The person is an NRI or RNOR
The taxpayer opts out of presumptive scheme (must use ITR-3 instead)

## Features

No books of accounts required: The prime benefit of ITR-4 — taxpayers under presumptive scheme need not maintain day-to-day accounts, cash books, or ledgers
Simplified income computation: Income is computed as a fixed percentage of turnover/receipts — no complex P&L preparation
Pre-filled data: Bank interest, TDS, and other pre-filled data from AIS/TIS is auto-populated
Five-year commitment: Taxpayers opting for 44AD must continue for 5 consecutive years; opting out in any year requires switching to ITR-3 and paying regular tax for the next 5 years
Advance tax simplification: Under Section 44AD/44ADA, advance tax is payable in a single installment of 100% by March 15 (instead of four quarterly installments)
Suitable for GST composition scheme dealers: Businesses under GST composition scheme (which also has turnover limits) typically align well with the presumptive taxation track in ITR-4

## Benefits

No tax audit: Businesses under 44AD/44ADA are exempt from tax audit under Section 44AB as long as they declare income at or above the presumptive rate
Simplified compliance: No need for professional accountants to maintain detailed books — reducing accounting costs significantly for small businesses
Time efficiency: ITR-4 preparation takes a fraction of the time compared to ITR-3 with full financial statements
Lower effective tax: For professionals with low actual expenses (where actual profit > 50% of receipts), 44ADA reduces the declared profit to exactly 50%, lowering the tax base
Digital transaction incentive: 44AD offers a lower presumptive rate of 6% (vs. 8%) for digital receipts — incentivising cashless transactions
Advance tax simplicity: Single-installment advance tax by March 15 is simpler to manage than four quarterly installments

## Process

1. Step 1 — Compute Presumptive Income Calculate total business turnover or professional gross receipts. Apply the applicable percentage (8%/6% for 44AD; 50% for 44ADA; fixed per vehicle for 44AE) to arrive at presumptive income.
2. Step 2 — Add Other Income Add salary (from Form 16), house property income, and other sources income to the presumptive business income.
3. Step 3 — Apply Deductions Under old regime: claim eligible deductions — 80C, 80D, 80CCD, etc. Note: business deductions are not separately claimed under presumptive scheme — the percentage already accounts for all expenses.
4. Step 4 — File ITR-4 Online Log in to incometax.gov.in. Select ITR-4. Review pre-filled data. Enter presumptive income details — select the applicable section (44AD/44ADA/44AE), enter turnover, and the system computes presumptive income automatically.
5. Step 5 — Pay Self-Assessment Tax Compute and pay any balance tax. Enter challan details.
6. Step 6 — Submit and Verify Submit and verify using Aadhaar OTP or EVC.

## Errors

- Opting for ITR-4 when capital gains exist: Even ₹1 of capital gain disqualifies ITR-4 — use ITR-3
- Turnover exceeding the limit: Filing ITR-4 with business turnover above ₹2 crore (44AD limit) or professional receipts above ₹50 lakh (44ADA) — invalid filing; ITR-3 is required
- Declaring income below presumptive rate without audit: Declaring business income below 8%/6% (44AD) or 50% (44ADA) without getting a tax audit done — attracts penalty under Section 271B
- Not maintaining the 5-year continuity for 44AD: Opting out of 44AD before completing 5 years — triggers loss of presumptive benefits and mandatory tax audit for the following years


## Frequently asked questions

- **I am a freelance graphic designer earning ₹18 lakh annually. Can I use ITR-4?** Graphic design is not specifically listed among the eligible professions under Section 44ADA (which covers medicine, law, engineering, architecture, accountancy, technical consultancy, interior decoration, and other notified professions). The CBDT has expanded the list of eligible professions under 44ADA over time — verify whether graphic design/creative services fall under "other notified professions." If your profession qualifies under 44ADA, you can file ITR-4 declaring 50% of ₹18 lakh (₹9 lakh) as income. If not qualified, file ITR-3 with actual P&L. Many digital professionals (content creators, designers, developers) face this ambiguity — consulting a CA is advisable.
- **My shop's annual sales are ₹95 lakh. Can I use ITR-4 under Section 44AD and declare 8% as income (₹7.6 lakh) even if my actual profit is ₹15 lakh?** Technically, yes — under Section 44AD, declaring 8% of turnover as income is permissible regardless of actual profit. The section provides a "deemed" income computation — no obligation to prove actual accounts. If your actual profit is ₹15 lakh but you declare ₹7.6 lakh (8% of ₹95 lakh) under 44AD, the difference is your benefit of the presumptive scheme — you pay tax only on ₹7.6 lakh. This is legally valid. However, if the Income Tax department has reason to believe your actual profit is significantly higher and you have suppressed income (not just using the presumptive scheme), there is a risk of scrutiny. Using the presumptive scheme appropriately — with legitimate business activity at a reasonable profit margin — is the intended use.
- **Can a CA (Chartered Accountant) file under ITR-4 using Section 44ADA?** Yes — Chartered Accountants are specifically listed as eligible professionals under Section 44ADA. A CA with gross professional receipts up to ₹50 lakh can file ITR-4, declaring 50% of gross receipts as professional income — without maintaining detailed books. This is widely used by sole-practitioner CAs and small CA firms (where the firm itself is not an LLP — LLPs file ITR-5). Note that the 44ADA limit of ₹50 lakh applies to gross receipts — if you receive referral fees, consulting income, or any other professional income, all must be included in the ₹50 lakh computation.
- **If I declare presumptive income under 44AD this year but want to switch to regular taxation next year (because I want to claim more expenses), can I?** Opting out of Section 44AD before completing 5 consecutive years has consequences. If you opt for 44AD for one year and then opt out (i.e., declare income below 8% of turnover) in any of the 5 subsequent years, you lose the right to claim presumptive taxation under Section 44AD for the following 5 years. During those 5 years, you must file ITR-3 with regular books and may require a tax audit if income falls below the basic exemption limit. This 5-year lock-in is a key consideration before initially opting for 44AD — plan your tax strategy carefully.
- **I have both a small retail shop (turnover ₹45 lakh) and a professional practice as an engineer (gross receipts ₹18 lakh). Can I combine both in ITR-4?** This is an interesting dual-income scenario. Section 44AD applies to business income and Section 44ADA applies to professional income — they are separate provisions. In principle, you can claim both in a single ITR-4 — reporting your shop's income under 44AD (6%/8% of ₹45 lakh) and your engineering fees under 44ADA (50% of ₹18 lakh). However, the total income (combined business + professional + other sources) must remain within the ₹50 lakh overall income limit for ITR-4 eligibility. Verify the aggregate income — if it exceeds ₹50 lakh, you must file ITR-3 instead.
