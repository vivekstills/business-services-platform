# PAN card application

## Overview

A Permanent Account Number (PAN) is a unique 10-character alphanumeric identifier issued by the Income Tax Department of India under the authority of the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT), Government of India. PAN is assigned to every taxpaying entity in India — individuals, Hindu Undivided Families, firms, companies, trusts, AOPs, BOIs, and other entities — and serves as the primary identifier for all financial and tax-related transactions in the country.
PAN is issued in Form 49A (for Indian citizens and entities) or Form 49AA (for foreign citizens and entities). The PAN application process is managed through two authorised PAN service agencies: NSDL (now Protean e-Gov Technologies Limited) and UTIITSL (UTI Infrastructure Technology and Services Limited).
Since 2021, the IEC (Import Export Code) has been mapped to PAN, and since 2023, PAN is linked with Aadhaar — making Aadhaar-PAN linkage mandatory for individual taxpayers. Failure to link Aadhaar with PAN results in the PAN becoming inoperative — rendering it invalid for financial transactions.
PAN is required for virtually every significant financial transaction in India — bank account opening, income tax filing, property purchase, vehicle purchase, FD investment above ₹50,000, cash deposits above ₹50,000, foreign travel (forex purchase), and hundreds of other transactions where quoting PAN is legally mandated under Rule 114B of the Income Tax Rules.

## Features

Unique 10-digit alphanumeric code: Format: AAAPL1234C — first 3 letters (random), 4th letter (entity type), 5th letter (first letter of surname/name), 4 numbers (sequential), 1 check digit
Lifetime validity: PAN once issued is permanent — no expiry, no renewal required
Entity-type indicator: The 4th character indicates the entity type — 'P' for individual, 'C' for company, 'H' for HUF, 'F' for firm, 'A' for AOP, 'T' for trust, 'B' for BOI, 'L' for local authority, 'J' for artificial juridical person, 'G' for government
Physical PAN card: Issued as a laminated credit-card-sized physical card with photo (for individuals) — valid as a photo identity document for KYC purposes
e-PAN: Electronic PAN is immediately issued digitally (PDF) upon application — valid for all legal and financial purposes
Aadhaar-PAN linkage: Mandatory for individual Indian citizens — linked PAN is "active"; unlinked PAN is "inoperative"
PAN database on portal: PAN details can be verified on the Income Tax portal and through the NSDL/UTIITSL portals
Instant e-PAN: For Aadhaar-holding individuals, the Income Tax portal issues Instant e-PAN in minutes — free of cost, automatically populated from Aadhaar data

## Benefits

Universal financial identity: PAN is accepted as KYC proof for bank accounts, mutual funds, insurance, brokerage accounts, and hundreds of other financial services — one document for all
Tax compliance foundation: PAN is the identifier for income tax filing, TDS credit, advance tax payment, and all tax department communications — without PAN, tax compliance is impossible
Photo identity document: PAN card (physical) is a widely accepted government-issued photo ID — valid for KYC across sectors
Business transactions: Companies and firms need PAN for corporate banking, statutory compliance, and regulatory registrations — GST registration, FSSAI, drug licenses, and most government registrations require PAN
Import-export: PAN is the basis of IEC — without PAN, no IEC can be obtained, and no import/export can be conducted
Investment access: All investment accounts — mutual funds, stocks, bonds, bank FDs — require PAN for KYC compliance under SEBI and RBI guidelines
Digital transaction facilitation: UPI, mobile banking, and internet banking accounts above certain thresholds require PAN-linked bank accounts

## Process

Standard PAN Application (Form 49A):
Step 1 — Choose Application Mode Choose online application (NSDL at onlineservices.nsdl.com or UTIITSL at utiitsl.com) or offline application through authorised PAN centres.
Step 2 — Fill Form 49A Online Complete the online Form 49A — personal details, address, father's name, date of birth, contact details, and status (individual, company, firm, etc.).
Step 3 — Upload Documents Upload photo (for individuals), proof of identity, proof of address, and proof of date of birth (for individuals) in the prescribed format.
Step 4 — Payment Pay the PAN application fee online. Physical PAN card delivery within India: ₹107 (inclusive of GST). Outside India: ₹1,017 (with dispatch to foreign address).
Step 5 — Biometric / Document Verification For online applications: Aadhaar OTP-based verification. For applications requiring physical verification: visit a PAN centre with original documents.
Step 6 — Application Status Tracking Track the PAN application status using the 15-digit acknowledgement number on the NSDL/UTIITSL portal.
Step 7 — Receive PAN Physical PAN card is dispatched within 15–20 working days. e-PAN is emailed within 2–3 working days of application.
Instant e-PAN (for Aadhaar-holding individuals): Available free of cost on the Income Tax portal (incometax.gov.in) — generates e-PAN instantly using Aadhaar data, without physical documents.

## Errors

- Multiple PAN cards: Applying for a second PAN (deliberately or by mistake) is a violation of the Income Tax Act — punishable with a fine of ₹10,000 under Section 272B. Surrender the duplicate PAN immediately at an Income Tax office
- Not linking Aadhaar with PAN: An inoperative PAN (due to Aadhaar non-linkage) disqualifies the taxpayer from receiving tax refunds, having TDS credited, and making financial transactions — link immediately with ₹1,000 fee
- Wrong date of birth or name in PAN application: Errors in PAN records lead to KYC mismatches in banks, financial institutions, and government databases — apply for PAN correction immediately if errors are found
- Using another person's PAN: Quoting another individual's or entity's PAN in financial transactions is a criminal offence — attracts prosecution under Section 277A


## Frequently asked questions

- **My PAN is showing as "Inoperative" on the Income Tax portal. What does this mean and how do I fix it?** Your PAN has become inoperative because it has not been linked with your Aadhaar by the deadline of March 31, 2023. An inoperative PAN means: (a) you cannot receive income tax refunds; (b) TDS will be deducted at higher rates (as if you did not furnish PAN); (c) you cannot make certain financial transactions. To reactivate, link your Aadhaar with PAN by paying a fee of ₹1,000 on the Income Tax portal under "e-Pay Tax" (Service Request → Aadhaar-PAN linking fee). After payment and Aadhaar OTP verification, the PAN is reactivated within 30 days. The inoperative period does not disqualify past ITR filings — only the current period is affected until reactivation.
- **Our startup company was just incorporated. When should we apply for PAN and what documents do we need?** Apply for PAN immediately after receiving the Certificate of Incorporation (CoI) from the MCA portal. Most companies apply for PAN within 1–7 days of incorporation. Required documents for a company's PAN: Certificate of Incorporation, company's registered address proof (electricity bill, NOC from owner), and the PAN application (Form 49A). The company's PAN is required for GST registration, opening a corporate bank account, employee TDS compliance, and all regulatory filings — making it a Day 1 priority after incorporation. The NSDL online application typically delivers e-PAN within 2–3 working days of a complete application.
- **I am a foreign national working in India on an employment visa. Do I need a PAN and what documents are required?** Yes — a foreign national working in India should obtain a PAN if they have taxable income in India (which they do, as an employee). For foreigners, the application is made in Form 49AA (not 49A). Documents required: passport (mandatory — serves as identity, address, and date of birth proof), visa copy, employment letter (for India address), and a recent photograph. The PAN is essential for your employer to deduct TDS from your salary, for you to file an ITR (if applicable), and for opening an Indian bank account. Apply through NSDL or UTIITSL — physical verification at a PAN centre may be required for foreign nationals.
- **I have two PAN cards — one obtained many years ago and another obtained recently (perhaps due to a mistake). What should I do?** Possessing two PAN cards is illegal under Section 272B of the Income Tax Act and is punishable with a fine of ₹10,000. Surrender the duplicate PAN immediately — submit a written application to your jurisdictional Assessing Officer or the nearest Income Tax office, surrendering the duplicate PAN and requesting it to be cancelled. Include both PAN card copies, your Aadhaar, and a cover letter explaining the duplication. After surrender, verify on the Income Tax portal that only one PAN (the original/correct one) remains active for your PAN. Do not ignore this situation — using one PAN for some transactions and the other for different transactions is a serious compliance violation.
- **Can a HUF have a PAN separate from the Karta's individual PAN?** Yes. A Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) is a separate taxable entity under the Income Tax Act — distinct from the Karta (head of the HUF) as an individual. The HUF must have its own separate PAN (Form 49A — status: HUF). The Karta's individual PAN is different from the HUF's PAN. Income earned by the HUF (agricultural income, rental income from HUF property, business income from HUF business) is assessed in the name of the HUF using the HUF's PAN. The Karta files his own individual ITR under his personal PAN and also files the HUF's ITR under the HUF's PAN. Tax planning through HUF (income splitting, deductions) requires separate PAN and compliance for the HUF entity.
