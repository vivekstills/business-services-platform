# How to Register a Private Limited Company in India

When entrepreneurs, investors, and startups think about incorporating a formal business entity in India, the Private Limited Company (Pvt. Ltd.) consistently tops the list. Its combination of limited liability protection, credibility with investors and banks, ability to issue equity shares, and perpetual existence makes it the default choice for serious business ventures.

This article walks you through every step of registering a private limited company in India under the Companies Act, 2013, managed through the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) portal.

## What defines a private limited company?

Under Section 2(68) of the Companies Act, 2013, a private limited company:

- Restricts the right to transfer shares
- Limits its members to a maximum of 200 (excluding current and former employees who are members)
- Prohibits any invitation to the public to subscribe to its securities

| Feature | Private limited company |
| --- | --- |
| Minimum directors | 2 |
| Maximum directors | 15 (more with special resolution) |
| Minimum shareholders | 2 |
| Maximum shareholders | 200 |
| Minimum paid-up capital | No minimum (removed by Companies Amendment Act 2015) |
| Liability of members | Limited to unpaid share capital |
| Separate legal entity | Yes |
| Perpetual succession | Yes |
| Can accept public deposits | No |
| IPO or public listing | Not directly (must convert to public company first) |

## Prerequisites before you begin

Before starting the MCA registration process, ensure you have:

| Requirement | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Directors | Minimum 2; at least 1 must be a resident Indian (stayed in India for 182 days in previous calendar year) |
| DIN | Director Identification Number required for all directors |
| DSC | Digital Signature Certificate required for the subscriber to the MoA and the proposed directors |
| Proposed name | 2 to 3 name options in order of preference |
| Registered office address | A physical address in India (can be a rented premises) |
| Share capital structure | Who owns how many shares (shareholding pattern) |

## Step-by-step registration process

### Step 1: Obtain digital signature certificates (DSC)

All proposed directors and subscribers to the Memorandum of Association (MoA) need a Class 3 DSC. DSC is obtained from certified agencies such as eMudhra, Sify Technologies, or National Informatics Centre (NIC). Documents required for DSC:

- PAN card
- Aadhaar card
- Photo and email ID verification

DSC is issued within 1 to 2 days and is valid for 2 years.

### Step 2: Apply for director identification number (DIN)

DIN can be obtained directly through the SPICe+ (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically Plus) form during company registration. First-time directors do not need to apply separately. Those already having a DIN from a previous directorship should use the existing one.

### Step 3: Name availability check and reservation

Use the MCA portal's RUN (Reserve Unique Name) service or the integrated SPICe+ Part A to reserve a company name. Guidelines for name approval:

- Must end with Private Limited (Pvt. Ltd.)
- Must not be identical or similar to an existing company or LLP name
- Must not use restricted words (bank, insurance, government, etc.) without regulatory approval
- Should ideally indicate the line of business

Upon approval, the name is reserved for 20 days (SPICe+ Part A) or 90 days (RUN).

### Step 4: Draft the memorandum of association (MoA) and articles of association (AoA)

The MoA contains the fundamental conditions upon which the company is registered. The AoA contains the rules for internal management.

Key clauses in MoA:

- Name clause
- Registered office clause
- Objects clause (what the company will do)
- Liability clause (limited by shares)
- Capital clause

Draft these carefully with the help of a CA or CS. The Companies Act provides Table F as a model AoA that companies can adopt wholly or with modifications.

### Step 5: File SPICe+ (INC-32) with MCA

SPICe+ is the integrated incorporation form available at `https://www.mca.gov.in`. It handles multiple applications simultaneously:

| Service | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| SPICe+ Part A | Name reservation |
| SPICe+ Part B | Company incorporation |
| AGILE-PRO-S | GST registration, EPFO, ESIC, PTAX (Maharashtra) registration |
| INC-33 | e-MoA (for up to 7 subscribers) |
| INC-34 | e-AoA |

Documents to be attached in SPICe+:

- Proof of registered office (utility bill plus NOC from owner or rent agreement)
- Identity and address proof of all directors and subscribers
- Consent to act as director (Form DIR-2)
- Self-declaration by proposed directors (Form INC-9)

### Step 6: Pay MCA registration fees

Government fee structure (as of 2024):

| Authorised capital | Registration fee |
| --- | --- |
| Up to Rs. 1,00,000 | Rs. 0 (nominal fee only for filing) |
| Rs. 1,00,001 to Rs. 5,00,000 | Rs. 4,000 |
| Rs. 5,00,001 to Rs. 50 lakhs | Slab-based |
| Above Rs. 50 lakhs | Slab-based |

Stamp duty varies by state. Most states allow e-stamping through the portal.

### Step 7: Certificate of incorporation

Once MCA approves the application (typically within 2 to 7 working days), the Registrar of Companies (RoC) issues:

- Certificate of Incorporation (CoI)
- Company Identification Number (CIN)
- PAN and TAN of the company (automatically applied for through SPICe+)

## Post-incorporation compliance

Getting incorporated is just the beginning. Private limited companies must comply with ongoing requirements:

| Requirement | Timeline |
| --- | --- |
| Open a current bank account | Within 30 days of incorporation |
| Deposit initial share capital | Within 30 days of incorporation |
| Obtain statutory registers | Immediately after incorporation |
| First board meeting | Within 30 days of incorporation |
| File INC-20A (business commencement declaration) | Within 180 days of incorporation |
| Appoint statutory auditor | At first AGM or within 30 days of incorporation |
| Annual return (MGT-7A / MGT-7) | Within 60 days of AGM |
| Financial statements (AOC-4) | Within 30 days of AGM |
| Annual general meeting | Within 6 months of financial year end |

## Penalties for non-compliance

| Default | Consequence |
| --- | --- |
| Not filing INC-20A (commencement) | Rs. 50,000 for company; Rs. 1,000 per day for officers |
| Not holding AGM | Rs. 1 lakh fine plus Rs. 5,000 per day continuing default |
| Not filing annual return | Rs. 50,000 plus Rs. 100 per day |
| Not appointing auditor | Rs. 25,000 for company; Rs. 5,000 for each officer |

## Frequently asked questions

**Q1. Can a single person start a private limited company, or is there a minimum of two people required?**

A Private Limited Company requires a minimum of two directors and two shareholders. If you want to be the sole promoter, you have two options: (1) involve a family member or trusted person as the second director or shareholder (even with 1 share), or (2) incorporate a One Person Company (OPC) instead, which allows a single member. However, OPCs have their own restrictions, including a turnover cap of Rs. 2 crores before mandatory conversion.

**Q2. Does the registered office need to be a commercial property, or can it be a residential address?**

A residential address can be used as the registered office for a private limited company. Many startups use the founder's home address initially. The only requirement is that the address is in India and documents served there will reach the company. An NOC from the property owner and a utility bill as proof are required. The company can change the registered office later as it grows.

**Q3. How long does it take to register a private limited company end-to-end?**

With all documents ready and correct, the process typically takes 7 to 15 working days from DSC application to Certificate of Incorporation. DSC takes 1 to 2 days, name reservation under SPICe+ Part A takes 1 to 5 days, and the final incorporation approval takes 2 to 7 days. Delays occur when documents are incomplete or the proposed name is rejected. Engaging a CA or CS speeds up the process considerably.

**Q4. Can a foreign national be a director or shareholder in an Indian private limited company?**

Yes. Foreign nationals can be directors (subject to visa and KYC requirements) and shareholders in an Indian private limited company. However, at least one director must be a resident Indian. Foreign shareholding must comply with the Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) policy and sector-specific caps. Reporting requirements under Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA) and RBI regulations also apply when foreign capital is brought in.

**Q5. What is the difference between authorized capital and paid-up capital in a private limited company?**

Authorized capital is the maximum amount of share capital a company is permitted to issue as per its MoA. Paid-up capital is the actual capital contributed by shareholders for shares already issued and paid for. For example, a company may have Rs. 10 lakh authorized capital but issue shares worth Rs. 2 lakhs initially: that is the paid-up capital. Increasing authorized capital requires an MCA filing (SH-7) and an additional fee. There is no minimum paid-up capital requirement for private limited companies in India since the 2015 amendment.
