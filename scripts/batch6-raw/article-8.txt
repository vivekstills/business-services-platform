ARTICLE SUMMARY 
The Income Tax Department provides multiple channels for taxpayer assistance: phone 
helplines, email support, the Aaykar Sampark Kendra, the online IT portal grievance 
system, and the Centralised Processing Centre (CPC) for ITR processing queries. This 
article consolidates all current contact details, the best channel for each type of query, the 
Aaykar Seva Kendra network, how to raise a grievance on the portal, and tips for resolving 
common problems efficiently.

How to Raise a Grievance on the IT Portal 
For unresolved issues that cannot be handled through the helpline, taxpayers can file a formal 
grievance on the IT portal: 
12. Log in at incometax.gov.in using your PAN and password. 
13. Navigate to: e-Nivaran (Grievance) or the Helpdesk option in the top menu. 
14. Select the grievance category (Refund, ITR Processing, PAN, TDS, Demand, etc.). 
15. Describe the issue, attach supporting documents (acknowledgement number, Form 
26AS, bank statement for refund issues), and submit. 
16. A grievance reference number (GRN) is generated. Track the status using this GRN. 
17. Grievances are typically resolved within 30 to 45 days. Escalation is possible if not 
resolved within the timeframe.

received, or outstanding demand after ITR filing, the CPC helpline and email are the right

Aaykar Seva Kendra: In-Person Assistance 
Aaykar Seva Kendras (ASKs) are physical income tax offices set up in all major cities to provide 
in-person assistance to taxpayers. Services available at ASK offices include: PAN card 
application and corrections, TAN-related queries, submission of physical documents for pending 
assessments, collection of tax demand challans, and general income tax guidance. All income 
tax offices at the ward and range level also have taxpayer service counters. Visiting the ASK 
with your PAN card, Aadhaar, and relevant documents typically allows same-day resolution of 
PAN and TAN issues. 
 
Pro Tip 
When calling the helpline for an ITR processing or refund query, keep ready: your PAN number, 
the ITR acknowledgement number (from the ITR-V), the assessment year, and the bank account 
number linked for refund. The helpline officer can check the processing status and refund 
dispatch status in real time with these details. 
 
KEY TAKEAWAYS 
1.  Primary helpline for general tax queries: 1800 180 1961 (Aaykar Sampark Kendra); for portal 
technical issues: 1800 103 0025. 
2.  CPC Bengaluru helpline 1800 103 4455 is the right channel for ITR processing status, 
Section 143(1) intimation queries, and refund delays. 
3.  Online grievances can be filed through the e-Nivaran system on the IT portal for formal 
escalation with a trackable reference number. 
4.  Email support: ask@incometax.gov.in for general queries; helpdesk@incometax.gov.in for 
portal technical issues. 
5.  In-person assistance is available at Aaykar Seva Kendras in all major cities for PAN, TAN, 
and general tax guidance. 
6.  For Section 143(1) demand response, use the 'Response to Outstanding Demand' module on 
the IT portal rather than calling. 
7.  All helplines are available Monday to Saturday from 8 AM to 8 PM; government holidays are 
excluded. 
 
Frequently Asked Questions 
Q1. I filed my ITR in July 2025 for AY 2025-26 but have not received my refund. What 
should I do? 
First, check the refund status on the IT portal under 'My Account > Refund/Demand Status' 
or on the TIN NSDL website using your PAN. Verify that your bank account is pre-validated 
and ECS-enabled on the portal. If the status shows 'Refund Sent' but the amount has not 
been credited, check with your bank or contact the CPC helpline at 1800 103 4455 with your 
PAN and acknowledgement number. If the ITR is still under processing after 90 days, raise 
a grievance on the portal. 
Q2. I received a Section 143(1) intimation with a demand. The helpline says my case is 
with the CPC. What is the process? 
Respond to the Section 143(1) demand online within 30 days of the intimation date. Log in 
to the IT portal, go to 'Pending Actions > Response to Outstanding Demand', select the 
demand, and indicate whether you agree or disagree with the adjustment. If you disagree, 
provide a reason and supporting documents. If you agree, pay the demanded amount using 
Challan 280. The CPC helpline can explain the basis of the adjustment but the formal 
response must be filed through the portal. 
Q3. My PAN was incorrectly printed on my PAN card. How do I get it corrected? 
For PAN card corrections (name, date of birth, address, photo), apply online through the 
NSDL portal at onlineservices.nsdl.com or UTITSL portal at myutiitsl.com. Submit the 
correction form with supporting documents (Aadhaar, passport, birth certificate). A corrected 
PAN card is issued within 15 to 20 days. For corrections related to income tax records linked 
to the PAN, contact the jurisdictional Aaykar Seva Kendra with the corrected PAN card. 
Q4. The IT portal shows an outstanding demand from AY 2018-19. I have paid this. What 
should I do? 
If you have paid the demand but the portal still shows it as outstanding, raise a grievance on 
the portal specifying the demand details and attaching the challan/payment proof. 
Alternatively, contact the Aaykar Sampark Kendra helpline. For older demands (before e-
filing era), you may need to visit your jurisdictional income tax ward with the payment challan 
and request correction in the demand register. Most old demands that have been paid can 
be resolved by submitting the challan copy. 
Q5. I lost my ITR-V acknowledgement for a return filed 3 years ago. Can I get a copy? 
Yes. Log in to the IT portal and navigate to e-File > View Filed Returns. Select the relevant 
assessment year and download the ITR-V acknowledgement (PDF format). The IT portal 
retains ITR-V copies for all years that were filed electronically. If the return was paper-filed 
(before e-filing was mandatory), contact your jurisdictional income tax officer for a copy.
