# Income Tax Surcharge Rates and Marginal Relief for AY 2026-27

## Article summary

Income tax surcharge is an additional tax levied on the income tax payable by taxpayers whose income exceeds specified thresholds. For individuals, surcharge rates range from 10% to 25% (or 37% under old regime for income above Rs. 5 crore). Marginal relief ensures that the extra tax payable due to crossing a surcharge slab does not exceed the marginal income that caused the slab to be breached. This article covers all surcharge rates for individuals, HUFs, firms, and companies under both regimes, the marginal relief mechanism with worked examples, and the Budget 2023 cap on surcharge under the new regime.

## Surcharge rates for individuals and HUFs (AY 2026-27)

| Total income range | Surcharge rate - Old regime | Surcharge rate - New regime (115BAC) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Up to Rs. 50 lakh | Nil | Nil |
| Above Rs. 50 lakh to Rs. 1 crore | 10% on income tax | 10% on income tax |
| Above Rs. 1 crore to Rs. 2 crore | 15% on income tax | 15% on income tax |
| Above Rs. 2 crore to Rs. 5 crore | 25% on income tax | 25% on income tax |
| Above Rs. 5 crore | 37% on income tax (old regime) | 25% on income tax (new regime cap - Budget 2023) |
 

## New regime surcharge cap at 25% Budget 2023 reduced the maximum surcharge rate under the new tax regime (Section 115BAC) from 37% to 25% for income above Rs. 5 crore. This means the maximum effective tax rate under the new regime is lower than under the old regime for very high incomes. For a taxpayer with income above Rs. 5 crore, the new regime surcharge cap is one of the key reasons why the new regime may be more attractive despite the loss of deductions.

 ## Marginal relief: what it is and how it works Marginal relief is a provision that ensures a taxpayer does not pay more additional tax on account of surcharge than the amount by which their income exceeds the surcharge threshold. Without marginal relief, a taxpayer earning Rs. 50,00,001 (just Rs. 1 above the Rs. 50 lakh surcharge threshold) would pay 10% surcharge on their entire tax, resulting in a disproportionately large jump in total tax for just Rs. 1 extra income. The marginal relief formula ensures: (Total tax with surcharge at income X) minus (Total tax without surcharge at the threshold) must not exceed (Income X minus the threshold income). ### Example: Marginal relief worked example Taxpayer income: Rs. 50,10,000 (just Rs. 10,000 above Rs. 50 lakh threshold) New Tax Regime | AY 2026-27

STEP 1: Tax on Rs. 50,10,000 (before surcharge): Using new regime slabs (approx): Rs. 9,00,000 Surcharge @ 10% on Rs. 9,00,000: Rs. 90,000 Total Tax with surcharge: Rs. 9,90,000 Add: Cess 4%: Rs. 39,600 TOTAL TAX SCENARIO A: Rs. 10,29,600

STEP 2: Tax on Rs. 50,00,000 (threshold, no surcharge): Tax on Rs. 50 lakh (approx): Rs. 8,97,500 Add: Cess 4%: Rs. 35,900 TOTAL TAX SCENARIO B: Rs. 9,33,400

STEP 3: Without marginal relief, extra tax = Rs. 10,29,600 - Rs. 9,33,400 = Rs. 96,200 STEP 4: Marginal income = Rs. 50,10,000 - Rs. 50,00,000 = Rs. 10,000

Since Rs. 96,200 > Rs. 10,000, marginal relief applies. Marginal Relief = Rs. 96,200 - Rs. 10,000 = Rs. 86,200 Actual surcharge payable after marginal relief = Rs. 90,000 - Rs. 86,200 = Rs. 3,800

## Surcharge on capital gains with special tax rates For capital gains that are subject to special tax rates (such as LTCG at 12.5% on equity or STCG at 20%), the surcharge rate is capped at 15% regardless of the total income of the taxpayer. This cap applies to: • LTCG under Section 112A (equity shares and equity mutual funds) • STCG under Section 111A (equity shares and equity mutual funds) • LTCG under Section 112 (other than equity) This means that even a very high income taxpayer with income above Rs. 5 crore pays only 15% surcharge (not 25% or 37%) on their LTCG and STCG from listed equity transactions.

## Pro tip Taxpayers with income near a surcharge threshold should carefully time income receipts across financial years. For example, a business owner expecting Rs. 1.05 crore total income (crossing the Rs. 1 crore threshold for 15% surcharge) might consider deferring Rs. 6 lakh of income to the next financial year by invoicing in April instead of March. However, this must be done genuinely and must reflect actual transaction timing, not just paper deferral.
