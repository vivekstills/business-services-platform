# Tax Audit Report Form 3CD: Key Clauses Explained

## Article summary

Tax Audit Report Form 3CD: Key Clauses Explained

Form 3CD is the Statement of Particulars to be furnished under Section 44AB of the Income Tax Act as part of the tax audit report. It contains 44 clauses (as amended) covering virtually every aspect of the taxpayer's business or profession: method of accounting, depreciation, loans and advances, related party transactions, deductions under various sections, compliance with TDS obligations, and more. This article explains the most important and complex clauses in Form 3CD and what auditors and taxpayers must disclose.

## What is Form 3CD and how it fits into the tax audit Form 3CD is the annexure to either Form 3CA (when accounts are audited under another law) or Form 3CB (when accounts are audited only for income tax purposes). It is a detailed factual statement prepared by the Chartered Accountant based on the books of account, financial statements, and other documents of the taxpayer. Form 3CD does not express an opinion - it discloses facts and particulars in a standardised format that allows the Income Tax Department to efficiently assess the return. Form 3CD has undergone significant revisions. Clause 30C on permissible deductions under GAAR (General Anti-Avoidance Rules) and Clause 44 on GST turnover reconciliation were added in recent amendments. The current Form 3CD has 44 clauses numbered from 1 to 44.

## Key clauses grouped by category ### Business information and accounting method (Clauses 1-8)

| Clause | Disclosure required |
| --- | --- |
| 1 and 2 | Name, PAN, and address of the taxpayer; nature of business or profession |
| 3 | Whether accounts are maintained on mercantile (accrual) or cash basis; any change in method from prior year must be disclosed and the effect quantified |
| 4 | Whether there is any change in the method of valuation of closing stock from the prior year; if yes, the impact on profit or loss |
| 5 | Name and address of the firm, address of principal place of business |
| 6 | Whether the report relates to a partnership firm; if yes, whether the partnership deed authorises remuneration and interest to partners (critical for Section 40(b) deductibility) |
 

### Related party and loan transactions (Clauses 19-24)

| Clause | Disclosure required |
| --- | --- |
| 19 | Amounts not credited to the P&L account: items of a capital nature, items exempt under Section 10, items covered under Sections 28 to 43D, and other items - full details required |
| 21 | Amounts debited to P&L but disallowable under Sections 40, 40A, 43B, etc. - each disallowance must be separately listed with the applicable section and amount |
| 22 | Amount of interest inadmissible under Section 23 of the Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Development Act 2006 (interest on delayed payments to MSMEs) |
| 23 | Whether any payment to related persons (Section 40A(2)(b)) exceeds the fair market value; excess amount to be disclosed |
| 24 | Amounts covered by Section 43B: PF, ESI, bonus, interest on government loans, leave encashment, etc. - both the amount payable and the amount actually paid before the ITR due date must be disclosed separately |
 

## Depreciation (Clause 18) Clause 18 requires disclosure of depreciation computed under the Income Tax Act (Sections 32 and 35) versus depreciation debited to the P&L account as per the companies or accounting standards. Key sub-clauses include: • 18(a): Depreciation under Section 32 - separately for each asset block, rate applied, opening WDV, additions and disposals, closing WDV, and depreciation amount. • 18(b): Amounts admissible under Sections 33AB (tea/coffee/rubber development), 33ABA (site restoration fund), 35 (scientific research), 35ABB (telecom licence fee), 35AC, 35AD, 35CCC, 35CCD. • Auditors must ensure that the asset additions are supported by invoices, that disposals are correctly computed, and that the correct block rates are applied.

## TDS compliance (Clause 34) Clause 34 is one of the most detailed and scrutinised clauses. It requires disclosure of: • Whether TDS has been deducted at correct rates on all payments liable to TDS (salary, rent, interest, professional fees, contractor payments, etc.). • Whether TDS deducted has been paid on time to the government. • Whether TDS returns (Form 24Q, 26Q, 27Q, 27EQ) have been filed on time. • Details of any TDS deducted but not paid, or any payment made without deduction of TDS where TDS was required. • Details of interest paid or payable under Section 201 for late TDS payment or late filing of TDS returns. The IT Department cross-references Clause 34 disclosures with the TRACES TDS database. Any mismatch between what is declared in Clause 34 and the TRACES records triggers scrutiny notices.

## GAAR and international transactions (Clauses 30C and 30D) Clause 30C (added by the 2018 amendment) requires the tax auditor to report whether any impermissible avoidance arrangement within the meaning of Section 95 (GAAR) has been identified in the books of the taxpayer. If the auditor identifies any arrangement, its nature, parties involved, and the claimed tax benefit must be disclosed. Clause 30D requires disclosure of whether the taxpayer has entered into specified domestic transactions under Section 92BA or international transactions under Section 92B, and whether transfer pricing documentation under Section 92D has been maintained.

## GST reconciliation (Clause 44) Clause 44, added by a recent amendment, requires the tax auditor to provide a reconciliation between the total turnover or gross receipts reported in the income tax return and the total turnover reported in GST returns (GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B) for the year. This reconciliation must explain all differences arising from: advance receipts, exempt supplies, zero-rated exports, non- GST supplies, and any other reconciling items. Clause 44 has become one of the most labour-intensive clauses for tax auditors since it requires co-ordination with the GST consultant and reconciliation of two different tax returns prepared under two different accounting systems.

## Scrutiny risk indicators

| Form 3CD clause | What triggers scrutiny risk |
| --- | --- |
| | Clause 3 (change in accounting method) Change from cash to mercantile or vice versa without disclosure can lead to income escaping tax Clause 18 (depreciation) Incorrect block rates, additions claimed without invoice support, WDV mismatch with prior year return Clause 21 (disallowances) Large amounts disallowable under 40A(3) (cash payments above Rs. 10,000), or 43B non-compliance Clause 34 (TDS) Non-deduction of TDS on large contractor or professional fee payments leads to 30% disallowance under Section 40(a)(ia) Clause 40 (SFT reporting) Non-reporting of specified financial transactions (SFT) above threshold amounts

## Pro tip Taxpayers being audited for the first time should prepare a detailed workings file for each clause of Form 3CD before meeting the CA. Organise supporting documents clause by clause: a depreciation schedule for Clause 18, a TDS payment register for Clause 34, a list of cash payments above Rs. 10,000 for Clause 21, and the GST turnover reconciliation for Clause 44. A well-organised client file reduces audit time, reduces errors in the report, and reduces the risk of incorrect disclosures.
