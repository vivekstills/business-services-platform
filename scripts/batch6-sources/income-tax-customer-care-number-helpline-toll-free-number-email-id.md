# Income Tax Customer Care Number and Helpline: All Contact Details

## Article summary

The Income Tax Department provides multiple channels for taxpayer assistance: phone helplines, email support, the Aaykar Sampark Kendra, the online IT portal grievance system, and the Centralised Processing Centre (CPC) for ITR processing queries. This article consolidates all current contact details, the best channel for each type of query, the Aaykar Seva Kendra network, how to raise a grievance on the portal, and tips for resolving common problems efficiently.

## How to raise a grievance on the IT portal

For unresolved issues that cannot be handled through the helpline, taxpayers can file a formal grievance on the IT portal:

### Steps

1. Log in at incometax.gov.in using your PAN and password.

2. Navigate to: e-Nivaran (Grievance) or the Helpdesk option in the top menu.

3. Select the grievance category (Refund, ITR Processing, PAN, TDS, Demand, etc.).

4. Describe the issue, attach supporting documents (acknowledgement number, Form 26AS, bank statement for refund issues), and submit.

5. A grievance reference number (GRN) is generated. Track the status using this GRN.

6. Grievances are typically resolved within 30 to 45 days. Escalation is possible if not resolved within the timeframe.

## CPC Bengaluru: for ITR processing and refund issues

| CPC query type | Resolution channel |
| --- | --- |
| ITR still showing as 'Processing' after 90 days | Call 1800 103 4455 or email cpc.bng@incometax.gov.in with PAN and acknowledgement number |
| Refund not credited despite intimation showing 'Refund Determined' | Call CPC helpline; also check that bank account is correctly linked and pre-validated on portal |
| Section 143(1) intimation shows additional demand | File online response through 'Pending Actions > Response to Outstanding Demand' on portal |
| ITR returns to 'Defective Return' status | File a revised return correcting the defect within the time allowed in the notice |
| Received Notice under Section 139(9) for defective return | Respond on portal within 15 days or file a revised return |
 The Centralised Processing Centre (CPC) in Bengaluru processes all electronically filed ITRs. For queries specifically related to ITR processing status, Section 143(1) intimation, refund not received, or outstanding demand after ITR filing, the CPC helpline and email are the right channels. ## Aaykar Seva Kendra: in-person assistance

 Aaykar Seva Kendras (ASKs) are physical income tax offices set up in all major cities to provide in-person assistance to taxpayers. Services available at ASK offices include: PAN card application and corrections, TAN-related queries, submission of physical documents for pending assessments, collection of tax demand challans, and general income tax guidance. All income tax offices at the ward and range level also have taxpayer service counters. Visiting the ASK with your PAN card, Aadhaar, and relevant documents typically allows same-day resolution of PAN and TAN issues.

## Pro tip

When calling the helpline for an ITR processing or refund query, keep ready: your PAN number, the ITR acknowledgement number (from the ITR-V), the assessment year, and the bank account number linked for refund. The helpline officer can check the processing status and refund dispatch status in real time with these details.
