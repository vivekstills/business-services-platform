# Form 67 and Foreign Tax Credit: How to Claim Relief on Taxes Paid Abroad

## Article summary

Indian residents who earn income from foreign sources (salary, dividends, interest, capital gains, or business income) may have tax withheld by the foreign country on that income. To avoid double taxation, the Income Tax Act under Section 90 or 91 allows a credit for foreign taxes paid against the Indian tax liability. The claim is made by filing Form 67 on the IT portal on or before the ITR filing due date. This article explains the foreign tax credit framework, the eligible foreign taxes, the Form 67 filing procedure, computation rules, and the consequences of late filing.

## Legal basis for foreign tax credit

The foreign tax credit (FTC) framework in India is governed by Section 90 (for countries with which India has a Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement - DTAA) and Section 91 (for countries with which India does not have a DTAA). Rule 128 of the Income Tax Rules, 1962 provides the detailed computation rules and the Form 67 requirement. The FTC rules were substantially revised in 2016 (effective AY 2017-18) to provide a more structured framework.

## Who can claim foreign tax credit?

| Condition | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Eligible claimant | Resident and Ordinarily Resident (ROR) individuals, companies, and other entities in India |
| RNOR and NRI | Not eligible for FTC since their foreign income is generally not taxable in India |
| Income type | Foreign income that is included in the Indian tax computation and also taxed abroad |
| Foreign tax eligible | Tax paid in a foreign country on income that is also taxed in India; must be an income tax (not sales tax, VAT, customs, or other non-income taxes) |
| DTAA countries | Credit under Section 90; generally limited to the lower of Indian tax or foreign tax on the same income |
| Non-DTAA countries | Credit under Section 91; limited to the lower of Indian tax rate or foreign tax rate on the doubly taxed income |

## Foreign tax credit computation

The FTC is computed separately for each country and for each type or source of income. The credit for any given income is limited to the LOWER of:

- The actual foreign tax paid on that income, converted to Indian Rupees at the telegraphic transfer buying rate (TTBR) of the State Bank of India on the last day of the month preceding the month of tax payment.

- The Indian tax payable on that foreign income (i.e., the Indian tax attributable to that specific income item). This ensures that FTC cannot exceed the Indian tax on the same income and does not result in a net refund.

### Example: Foreign tax credit calculation

Resident individual: Salary from US employer in FY 2025-26 Gross US salary (converted to INR): Rs. 30,00,000 US Federal Income Tax withheld (converted): Rs. 4,50,000

Indian tax computation: Total income including US salary: Rs. 45,00,000 Indian tax on total income (new regime): Rs. 6,75,000 (approx) Indian tax attributable to US salary: = Rs. 6,75,000 x (Rs. 30L / Rs. 45L) = Rs. 4,50,000

FTC = LOWER of: (a) US tax paid: Rs. 4,50,000 (b) Indian tax on US income: Rs. 4,50,000 FTC = Rs. 4,50,000

Indian tax payable after FTC: Rs. 6,75,000 - Rs. 4,50,000 = Rs. 2,25,000 (on remaining Indian income)

## Form 67: Filing requirement and procedure

Rule 128(9) mandates that any taxpayer claiming FTC must file Form 67 on the IT portal on or before the due date of filing the income tax return. Form 67 is filed separately from the ITR itself.

### Steps

1. Log in to the IT portal at incometax.gov.in.

2. Navigate to: e-File > Income Tax Forms > File Income Tax Forms > Form 67.

3. Select the relevant Assessment Year and fill in: the country where tax was paid, the nature of income (salary, dividends, interest, etc.), the amount of foreign income in the foreign currency and INR, the amount of foreign tax paid in foreign currency and INR, and the exchange rate used.

4. Upload supporting documents: Tax assessment order or withholding tax certificate from the foreign country showing the tax paid (must be in the foreign country's official language; if not in English, attach a certified translation).

5. Submit and e-verify Form 67 using Aadhaar OTP or digital signature.

6. Then proceed to file the ITR, which will reference the Form 67 claim.

## Important

Form 67 must be filed ON OR BEFORE the ITR due date. If Form 67 is filed after the ITR due date (even a day late), the FTC claim is denied entirely. This is one of the most common and costly mistakes made by taxpayers with foreign income. The Delhi High Court has upheld the denial of FTC for late Form 67 filing in multiple cases.

## Documents required for Form 67
- Tax Residency Certificate (if claiming DTAA benefits along with FTC) • Foreign tax payment proof: withholding certificate issued by the foreign employer or payer (e.g., W-2 form from US, P60 from UK, or equivalent) • Foreign tax assessment order or foreign ITR showing taxes paid (for countries where the taxpayer files their own return abroad) • Bank statement showing remittance of tax to the foreign government (if self-assessed) • Exchange rate certificate from SBI or authorised dealer bank for the conversion rate used

## Pro tip

NRIs returning to India who become ROR in their first or second year back should track foreign income and foreign taxes paid from day one. Once ROR status is attained, global income becomes taxable in India. Set up the Form 67 filing as a recurring calendar reminder well before July 31 each year to ensure it is never filed late.
