Filing the wrong ITR form is not a minor clerical error. It results in a defective return notice from the Income Tax Department, forcing you to refile — sometimes with a late penalty attached. The Indian ITR system has seven distinct forms, each designed for a specific taxpayer profile. This guide helps you identify the right one in minutes.

## Overview of All ITR Forms

| Form | For | Cannot be used if |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ITR-1 (Sahaj) | Resident individuals: salary/pension ≤ Rs. 50L, 1 house property, other sources (interest, dividends) | You have capital gains, >1 house property, foreign assets, business income, or income >Rs. 50L |
| ITR-2 | Individuals and HUFs: income >Rs. 50L, capital gains, multiple house properties, foreign income/assets, NRIs | You have business or professional income |
| ITR-3 | Individuals and HUFs with business or professional income, partners in a firm | You opt for presumptive taxation (use ITR-4 instead) |
| ITR-4 (Sugam) | Individuals, HUFs, firms (not LLP) opting for presumptive taxation under 44AD, 44ADA, or 44AE | Income >Rs. 50L, or you have capital gains, or you are an NRI |
| ITR-5 | Firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs, co-operative societies | Not for individuals or companies |
| ITR-6 | Companies (other than those claiming exemption u/s 11) | Charitable trusts and NGOs |
| ITR-7 | Trusts, political parties, institutions under Sections 139(4A) to 139(4F) | Not for regular individuals or businesses |

## Detailed Eligibility: Key Decision Points

### ITR-1 (Sahaj) — Simplest Form, Most Restrictions

You can use ITR-1 if ALL of the following are true:
- You are a resident individual (not NRI or RNOR)
- Total income does not exceed Rs. 50 lakh
- Income is from salary or pension, one house property, and/or other sources (interest, family pension)
- You do not have capital gains of any kind
- You do not have foreign assets or income
- You are not a director in a company or an unlisted company shareholder

**Common mistake:** Many salaried employees with mutual fund redemptions or stock sales assume ITR-1 is fine. Any capital gain — even small STCG from one fund redemption — disqualifies you from ITR-1.

### ITR-2 — For Investors and HNIs

Use ITR-2 if you are an individual or HUF and:
- You have capital gains (equity, property, gold, mutual funds)
- You have more than one house property
- Your income exceeds Rs. 50 lakh
- You are an NRI with Indian income
- You hold foreign assets or have foreign income
- You are a company director (even if salary is below Rs. 50L)

### ITR-3 — For Business Owners and Professionals

Use ITR-3 if you are an individual or HUF with:
- Income from a proprietary business
- Professional income (doctor, architect, CA, consultant) where you are NOT opting for presumptive taxation
- You are a partner in a partnership firm (you report your share of profit here)

Also use ITR-3 if you have business/professional income in addition to salary and capital gains.

### ITR-4 (Sugam) — Presumptive Taxation Made Simple

Use ITR-4 if:
- You are an individual, HUF, or firm (not LLP) opting for presumptive taxation
- Business income under Section 44AD (turnover ≤ Rs. 3 crore): declare 8% (or 6% for digital receipts) as profit
- Professional income under Section 44ADA (receipts ≤ Rs. 75 lakh): declare 50% as profit
- Transport business under Section 44AE

Restriction: If total income exceeds Rs. 50 lakh, or if you have capital gains, you must use ITR-3 instead.

## Examples: Who Should File Which Form

**Example 1: Salaried employee with mutual fund gains**
Amit earns Rs. 12L salary and sold ELSS funds for Rs. 30,000 LTCG. He also has a savings account interest of Rs. 8,000.
→ Cannot use ITR-1 (capital gains disqualify). **Must use ITR-2.**

**Example 2: Freelance content writer**
Pooja earns Rs. 8L from freelancing and opts for 50% presumptive deduction under 44ADA.
→ Uses **ITR-4** (income under Rs. 50L, no capital gains, opts for 44ADA).

**Example 3: Small business owner**
Ravi runs a kirana store with Rs. 80L annual turnover, wants to opt for 44AD presumptive taxation.
→ Uses **ITR-4** (turnover under Rs. 3 crore, opts for 44AD).

**Example 4: Doctor with clinic + investments**
Dr. Sharma earns Rs. 35L from her clinic (does not opt for presumptive taxation) plus Rs. 1.2L in LTCG from stocks.
→ Cannot use ITR-4 (has capital gains + does not opt for presumptive). **Must use ITR-3.**

## Quick Elimination Checklist

Answer yes/no to find your form:

1. Do you have business or professional income? → If YES: ITR-3 or ITR-4
2. Do you opt for presumptive taxation (44AD/44ADA/44AE) AND income < Rs. 50L AND no capital gains? → ITR-4
3. Do you have capital gains? → If YES: ITR-2 or ITR-3 (depending on whether you also have business income)
4. Are you an NRI or hold foreign assets? → ITR-2 or ITR-3
5. Is your income only from salary/pension, ≤ Rs. 50L, no capital gains, resident individual? → ITR-1
6. Is the return for a firm, LLP, or AOP? → ITR-5
7. Is the return for a company? → ITR-6
8. Is the return for a trust, political party, or Section 8 company? → ITR-7

## Key Filing Deadlines

| Category | Due Date |
| --- | --- |
| Individuals not requiring audit | 31 July |
| Individuals requiring audit | 31 October |
| Transfer pricing cases | 30 November |
| Belated return | 31 December |

**Late filing penalty under Section 234F:**
- Rs. 1,000 if total income ≤ Rs. 5 lakh
- Rs. 5,000 if total income > Rs. 5 lakh

## Common Mistakes

1. Using ITR-1 when you have any capital gains — even small LTCG from equity funds.
2. Using ITR-4 when income exceeds Rs. 50 lakh — you must switch to ITR-3.
3. Partners in firms filing ITR-1 instead of ITR-3.
4. NRIs filing ITR-1 (NRIs must use ITR-2 or ITR-3).
5. Not reporting exempt income (like LTCG under Rs. 1 lakh) — it still needs to be disclosed even if not taxable.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. I have only salary income but also sold one mutual fund unit. Do I still need ITR-2?**
Yes. Any capital gains transaction — even for a nominal amount — disqualifies you from ITR-1. You must use ITR-2 if you have no business income.

**Q2. Can I use ITR-4 if I have both business income (44AD) and salary income?**
Yes, provided your total income does not exceed Rs. 50 lakh and you do not have capital gains or foreign income.

**Q3. What happens if I file using the wrong ITR form?**
The return will be treated as defective under Section 139(9). You will receive a notice giving you 15 days to refile with the correct form. If you miss that window, the return may be treated as invalid.

**Q4. I am an NRI. Can I use any ITR form?**
NRIs cannot use ITR-1 regardless of income level. They must use ITR-2 (if no business income) or ITR-3 (if they have business or professional income in India).

**Q5. Is there a fee to file ITR?**
No. Filing directly on the Income Tax portal (incometax.gov.in) is free. Professional fees apply only if you hire a CA or tax advisor.
