When your gross salary sits between roughly Rs. 7 lakh and Rs. 8 lakh, tax planning is dominated by **threshold effects** — especially the Section 87A rebate under the New Tax Regime and how standard deduction interacts with taxable income. Small shifts in taxable income or regime choice can swing your liability from zero to a noticeable outflow.

## Why Rs. 7 Lakh Is the Key Threshold

| Concept | What it means for you |
| --- | --- |
| New regime rebate (FY 2024-25) | Section 87A can reduce tax to nil when **taxable income** is up to Rs. 7 lakh |
| FY 2025-26 onward | Rebate coverage moves to a higher band (up to Rs. 12 lakh taxable under notified rules) — always verify the active year |
| Old regime | Chapter VI-A deductions (80C, 80D, 80CCD(1B), etc.) may pull taxable income down instead |

The rebate applies to **taxable income after standard deduction** (and other adjustments), not to gross CTC.

## New Regime vs Old Regime at Rs. 7–8 Lakh

| Factor | New regime | Old regime |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Standard deduction (salaried) | Rs. 75,000 (from FY 2024-25) | Rs. 50,000 |
| 80C / 80D / HRA | Not available | Available |
| Zero-tax window | Often achievable near Rs. 7L taxable (FY 2024-25 rules) | Depends on deduction stack |

## Four Strategies That Work in This Band

1. **Standard deduction + NPS 80CCD(1B)** — In the **old regime**, Rs. 50,000 NPS (Tier I) sits **outside** the Rs. 1.5 lakh 80C cap and directly cuts taxable income.
2. **Choose regime with a spreadsheet** — Compare **both** regimes using your actual Form 16 components; do not assume one is always better.
3. **Use 80C only where it fills real gaps** — Map EPF, tuition fees, and LIC first so you do not over-lock funds (old regime).
4. **Health insurance under 80D** — Up to Rs. 25,000 for self/family (and more for senior parents) remains one of the cleanest deductions (old regime).

## Worked Example: Rs. 7.5 Lakh Gross Toward Nil Tax

**New tax regime (illustrative FY 2024-25 slab + rebate rules):**

| Line item | Amount (Rs.) |
| --- | --- |
| Gross salary | 7,50,000 |
| Less: Standard deduction | (75,000) |

With taxable income at Rs. 6.75 lakh, **Section 87A can eliminate tax** under the new regime for FY 2024-25 (subject to applicable surcharge/cess rules). Always validate with the official utility for your exact assessment year.

**Old regime contrast:** If Chapter VI-A deductions exceed roughly Rs. 1.25–1.75 lakh for your profile, re-run the comparison — old regime can win when deductions are aggressive.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Is the Rs. 7 lakh limit based on gross salary or taxable income?**
Taxable income **after** standard deduction and other adjustments — not gross pay.

**Q2. Does employee EPF reduce tax in the new regime?**
Employee EPF does **not** get an extra deduction like 80C in the new regime; it still builds retirement savings.

**Q3. Can salaried individuals switch regimes next year if they choose wrong?**
Generally **yes** for salary-only cases year on year; business income has stricter rules — confirm with your CA.
