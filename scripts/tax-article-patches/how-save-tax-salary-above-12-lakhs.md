From **FY 2025-26**, the Union Budget narrative positions the **New Tax Regime** as increasingly attractive for middle incomes because Section **87A** rebate coverage expands — with notified thresholds moving toward **zero tax up to Rs. 12 lakh of taxable income** (verify exact legal text and utility for your assessment year). Rs. 12 lakh gross salary is therefore no longer “automatically high tax” if most income is salary and you use standard deduction.

## The Rs. 12 Lakh Zero-Tax Idea (New Regime)

| Element | Practical takeaway |
| --- | --- |
| Rebate u/s 87A | Can eliminate tax if taxable income stays within notified limits |
| Standard deduction | Rs. 75,000 for salaried taxpayers reduces taxable base first |
| Documentation | Employer deductions ≠ regime choice — you still elect regime in ITR |

## Why Rs. 12.1 Lakh (or Similar) May Still Trigger Tax

Crossing a rebate threshold does **not** always scale taxes gently:

| Scenario | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Taxable income **just above** rebate limit | Marginal relief may cap payable tax so it does not exceed the income excess |
| Larger gap | Normal slab rates apply on income above basic exemption |

**Marginal relief** prevents a tiny breach from causing tax larger than the incremental income — but it is not a substitute for planning.

## New vs Old in the Rs. 12–15 Lakh Corridor

| Factor | New regime angle | Old regime angle |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Slabs | Lower headline rates | Higher slabs |
| Deductions | Mostly unavailable | 80C, 80D, HRA, 24(b), etc. |
| Planning style | Salary structure + regime election | Investment + reimbursement proof |

## Three Strategies Just Above Rs. 12 Lakh Taxable

1. **Recalculate taxable salary** — Allowances incorrectly taxed, perquisites, or arrears timing can shift brackets.
2. **If old regime wins — stack 80CCD(1B) + 80D first** — Efficient rupees outside / beside crowded 80C.
3. **Time discretionary bonuses** — Where compliant, spreading recognition across years may reduce marginal spikes (employer policy permitting).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Does gross Rs. 12 lakh mean zero tax automatically in FY 2025-26?**
Not automatically — **taxable income after standard deduction** must fall within the rebate conditions notified for that year.

**Q2. What is marginal relief in simple terms?**
A mechanism so that **tax on slightly exceeding** a surcharge/rebate threshold does not exceed the **extra income** that caused the crossing — subject to legal limits.

**Q3. Should high renters still consider the old regime at Rs. 12–15L?**
Often **yes** — HRA + home loan interest can still overpower new regime simplicity; compute both.
