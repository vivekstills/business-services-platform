At Rs. 10–12 lakh gross, a meaningful slice of income falls in the **20% and 30% slabs** under the old regime, while the new regime offers lower slabs but almost no investment deductions. This is the income band where **running both regimes side-by-side** stops being optional and becomes mandatory.

## Break-Even Thinking (Rules of Thumb)

| Your situation | Lean toward |
| --- | --- |
| Total deductions **below ~Rs. 3.75 lakh** | New regime often cheaper |
| Home loan + HRA + full 80C/80D/NPS | Old regime often cheaper |
| Rising income toward Rs. 12L+ | Re-check rebate thresholds annually |

## Four Optimization Moves

1. **Fill 80CCD(1B) before random 80C products** — Extra Rs. 50K outside the Rs. 1.5L cap (old regime).
2. **Coordinate EPF + voluntary 80C** — Do not duplicate locked investments once EPF already consumes the limit.
3. **Claim 80D even if premium is small** — Every rupee saves **20–30% + cess** at this band (old regime).
4. **Audit Form 16 vs payslips** — Employer-reported deductions must match proofs before filing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Is the new regime always better at Rs. 10 lakh?**
No. High renters with HRA + loan interest + insurance can still tilt old regime ahead.

**Q2. Should I buy tax-saving FDs at this income?**
Only if they fit liquidity needs — ELSS or NPS may offer better risk/reward; compare lock-ins.

**Q3. How often should I re-run regime comparison?**
At least **once every financial year** and after any loan, job change, or rent move.
