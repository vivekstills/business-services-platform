Tax planning is not one-size-fits-all. A person earning Rs. 10 lakh per year needs a very different strategy from someone earning Rs. 1 crore. The deductions available, the marginal tax rates applicable, the role of surcharge, and the regime choice all shift significantly as income grows. This guide breaks down the optimal approach for each major income bracket.

## Old Regime vs. New Regime — Quick Reference

Before diving into income-level strategies, understand the regime trade-off:

| Feature | Old Tax Regime | New Tax Regime |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tax rates | Higher slab rates | Lower slab rates |
| Deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, etc.) | Available | Not available |
| Standard deduction | Rs. 50,000 | Rs. 75,000 (from FY 2024-25) |
| Best suited for | High deductions | Simple income structures |
| Section 87A rebate threshold | Rs. 5 lakh | Rs. 7 lakh (Rs. 12L from FY 2025-26) |

**General Rule:** If total eligible deductions exceed Rs. 3.75 lakh, the Old Regime usually wins. Below that, the New Regime is typically better.

## Tax Strategy by Income Level

### Rs. 10 Lakh Income

**New Regime:** With Section 87A rebate (up to Rs. 7L) and Rs. 75,000 standard deduction, effective tax can be zero or negligible for many in this bracket from FY 2025-26.

**Old Regime strategy:**
- Standard deduction: Rs. 50,000
- Section 80C (EPF + ELSS): Rs. 1,50,000
- Section 80CCD(1B) – NPS: Rs. 50,000
- Section 80D – health insurance: Rs. 25,000
- Total deductions: Rs. 2,75,000 → Taxable income: Rs. 7,25,000

**Verdict:** Run the numbers. The new regime's zero-tax-up-to-Rs.-7L rebate makes it compelling at this level unless deductions are very high.

### Rs. 20 Lakh Income

At Rs. 20 lakh, you're firmly in the 20–30% bracket depending on regime.

**Old Regime strategy:**
- Maximize 80C (Rs. 1,50,000) + 80CCD(1B) (Rs. 50,000)
- Claim HRA if in rented accommodation
- Section 80D: Rs. 25,000 (self) + Rs. 50,000 (senior citizen parents) = Rs. 75,000
- Home loan interest under Section 24(b): up to Rs. 2,00,000 if applicable
- Total potential deductions: Rs. 4,75,000+
- Taxable income: Rs. 15,25,000 → significant savings vs. flat-rate new regime

**Verdict:** Old regime is likely better at this level if you have a home loan and HRA.

### Rs. 30 Lakh Income

You are in the 30% slab. Every deduction saves you Rs. 31,200 per lakh (including cess).

**Old Regime strategy:**
- Stack all standard deductions (80C, 80CCD(1B), 80D, 24(b))
- If business income, explore Section 44ADA (50% presumptive deduction for professionals)
- Consider National Pension System employer contribution structuring (80CCD(2)) — unlimited within 10% of salary
- Loss harvesting from equity investments to offset STCG

**Verdict:** High deduction discipline pays off maximally at this slab. Rs. 5L in deductions = Rs. 1.56 lakh saved.

### Rs. 50 Lakh Income

Surcharge of 10% kicks in above Rs. 50 lakh. This means effective marginal tax rate becomes **34.32%** (30% + 10% surcharge + 4% cess).

**Old Regime strategy:**
- Everything at Rs. 30L level, plus:
- Employer NPS contribution (80CCD(2)): up to 10% of basic salary — outside the Rs. 1.5L 80C ceiling
- Section 54EC bonds if selling property (Rs. 50L cap)
- Marginal relief: if income is Rs. 50.5L, the tax on the excess Rs. 0.5L above Rs. 50L cannot exceed the actual excess income — check marginal relief before planning

**Verdict:** At this level, employer NPS structuring and careful surcharge-boundary planning can save Rs. 1–2 lakh annually.

### Rs. 1 Crore+ Income

Surcharge jumps to **15%** above Rs. 1 crore → effective marginal rate: **35.88%**.

**Old Regime strategy:**
- All lower-level strategies remain applicable
- Salary restructuring: optimize Leave Travel Allowance, food coupons, company car, phone/internet allowances
- Section 80G donations to qualifying NGOs/institutions
- Family HUF: if family income includes rent, interest, or agricultural income, a HUF can receive it as a separate taxable entity — separate Rs. 1.5L 80C limit applies
- Losses from equity/F&O: set off against eligible income heads

**New Regime consideration:** At Rs. 1Cr+, if your company allows no deductions in salary structure and you have no home loan, the new regime's lower rates may still make sense — always compare both.

## Optimization Tips

1. **Run the comparison every year.** Regime choice is annual. Life changes (home loan taken/paid off, child's birth, parent's health) shift the math.
2. **Maximize employer NPS before individual contributions.** 80CCD(2) (employer) does not touch your 80C ceiling and gives the same tax benefit.
3. **Don't ignore surcharge thresholds.** At Rs. 50L and Rs. 1Cr, crossing the threshold by Rs. 1 lakh can cost Rs. 1.5–2 lakh extra in tax. Marginal relief provisions can offset this but need careful calculation.
4. **Use family deductions.** Health insurance premiums paid for senior citizen parents (Section 80D) gives Rs. 50,000 deduction beyond the personal Rs. 25,000 limit.
5. **Harvest tax losses before March 31.** Book unrealised equity losses to offset capital gains before year-end — repurchase after 30 days to reset cost basis.

## Mistakes to Avoid

1. Choosing a regime without calculating both options for the year.
2. Missing 80CCD(2) employer NPS benefit thinking it counts against 80C.
3. Not claiming marginal relief when income is just above a surcharge threshold.
4. Ignoring HUF as a tax planning vehicle for higher incomes.
5. Treating all deductions as always applicable — some (80EE, 80EEA) have specific year-of-purchase conditions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. At what income level does the New Regime become definitively better?**
There is no fixed answer — it depends on your deduction profile. However, if your total eligible deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + 24(b) + 80CCD(1B)) are below Rs. 3.75 lakh per year, the New Regime typically results in lower tax. Above Rs. 3.75 lakh in deductions, the Old Regime usually wins.

**Q2. Does the Rs. 12 lakh zero-tax benefit apply for FY 2025-26 in the New Regime?**
Yes. From FY 2025-26, under the New Regime, the Section 87A rebate covers income up to Rs. 12 lakh (effectively zero tax). This makes the New Regime extremely attractive for incomes at or below this threshold.

**Q3. Can I switch tax regimes every year?**
Salaried individuals can switch regimes year to year. However, if you have business or professional income, you can switch away from the new regime only once, after which you cannot switch back.

**Q4. Is surcharge applicable in the New Tax Regime?**
Yes. Surcharge rates apply in both regimes. However, the New Regime caps surcharge at 25% even for incomes above Rs. 5 crore (Old Regime surcharge goes up to 37%). This makes the New Regime more attractive for ultra-high incomes above Rs. 5 crore.
