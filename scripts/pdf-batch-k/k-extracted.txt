What  Is  the  Health  Security  to  National  Security  Cess?  
A  cess  in  Indian  taxation  is  a  surcharge  levied  over  and  above  the  standard  tax  rate,  
earmarked
 
for
 
a
 
specific
 
purpose.
 
Unlike
 
general
 
tax

that
 
flows
 
into
 
the
 
Consolidated
 
Fund
 
of
 
India,
 
cess
 
revenue
 
is

to
 
a
 
separate
 
fund
 
and
 
can
 
only
 
be

for
 
the
 
stated

The  HSNSC  Act  2026  creates  a  unified  cess  that  pools  funding  for:  
●  Strengthening  pandemic  preparedness  and  bio-security  infrastructure  ●  Funding  cybersecurity  and  border  security  initiatives  ●  Supporting  health  emergency  response  mechanisms  ●  Upgrading  critical  national  infrastructure  at  the  intersection  of  health  and  defense  
This  dual  mandate  is  what  makes  the  cess  unique  —  it  explicitly  recognizes  that  health  
security
 
and
 
national
 
security
 
are
 
interlinked
,
 
particularly
 
in
 
a
 
post-COVID

where

How  Is  the  Cess  Calculated?

Formula:

Understanding  Handling  Fees  and  Rain  Fees  
Before  diving  into  GST,  let's  establish  what  these  charges  actually  are:  
Handling  Fee:  A  charge  levied  by  warehouse  operators,  port  authorities,  freight  forwarders,  
or
 
customs

agents
 
(CHAs)
 
for
 
physically
 
loading,
 
unloading,

sorting,
 
or

It
 
covers
 
the
 
labor,
 
equipment,
 
and
 
operational

of
 
physically
 
moving
 
goods.
 
Rain  Fee  (also  called  Inclement  Weather  Fee  /  Monsoon  Surcharge):  A  surcharge  levied  
by

logistics

or
 
freight

when

is

—

or

It
 
compensates
 
for

additional

measures
 
(tarpaulins,
 
covered

and

●  Who  is  the  service  provider  (GTA,  registered  logistics  company,  port  trust,  etc.)  ●  Who  is  the  recipient  (B2B  or  B2C)  ●  Whether  the  services  qualify  for  any  exemption  under  Notification  No.  12/2017-CT  
(Rate)
 
or
 
9/2017-IT
 
(Rate)

For  businesses  receiving  handling  and  rain  fee  invoices,  the  Input  Tax  Credit  (ITC)  on  18%

Why  Is  a  Rent  Agreement  Required  for  GST  Registration?  
Under  Rule  8  of  the  CGST  Rules,  2017,  every  application  for  GST  registration  must  include  
proof
 
of

of
 
business
.
 
The

has
 
a

For  owned  premises,  a  property  tax  receipt  or  electricity  bill  suffices.  For  rented  or  leased

●  A  copy  of  the  valid  rent/lease  agreement,  AND  ●  A  supporting  utility  document  (electricity  bill,  water  bill,  or  NOC  from  owner)

What  Must  the  Rent  Agreement  Contain?  
Not  every  rent  agreement  passes  GSTN  scrutiny.  The  document  must  clearly  mention:  
Mandatory  Elements:

get

purposes
 ●  Clear  identification  of  the  portion  being  rented  (if  partial  occupancy)
