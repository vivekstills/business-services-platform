## What Is Section 87A?

Section 87A provides a **tax rebate** that reduces income tax liability to zero for taxpayers whose total income falls below specified thresholds. It is one of the most impactful provisions for middle-income taxpayers.

## Rebate Limits — FY 2025-26

| Regime | Income Threshold | Maximum Rebate |
|---|---|---|
| **New Tax Regime** | ==Rs. 12,00,000== | ==Rs. 60,000== (full tax up to Rs. 12L waived) |
| **Old Tax Regime** | ==Rs. 5,00,000== | ==Rs. 12,500== |

> ⚠️ **Critical:** The rebate applies to total income **after standard deduction and other Chapter VI-A deductions** as per rules for each regime. For salaried individuals, the ==Rs. 75,000== standard deduction under the New Regime means effective zero-tax **gross** salary can be around ==Rs. 12,75,000== when all income is salary.

## How the Rebate Works

The rebate is applied **after computing tax on income** but **before adding cess**.

**Example — New Regime, FY 2025-26:**

| Detail | Amount |
|---|---|
| Gross Salary | ==Rs. 12,75,000== |
| Less: Standard Deduction | (==Rs. 75,000==) |
| Total Income | ==Rs. 12,00,000== |
| Tax on Rs. 12,00,000 (slabs) | ==Rs. 60,000== |
| Less: Section 87A Rebate | (==Rs. 60,000==) |
| Tax After Rebate | **Rs. 0** |
| Health & Education Cess (4%) | Rs. 0 |
| **Final Tax Liability** | **Rs. 0** |

## The Cliff Edge — Rs. 12,00,001

> ⚠️ **Just above the threshold:** If total income exceeds ==Rs. 12,00,000==, rebate may fully disappear — liability jumps materially vs staying **on or below** ==Rs. 12 lakh==.

**Marginal relief** can limit tax so that the incremental tax does not exceed incremental income over ==Rs. 12 lakh== — verify using the official utility for your exact facts.

## Special Capital Gains Rule

> ⚠️ Section 87A rebate is **NOT available** against:

> - Special rate tax on LTCG under **Section 112A** (equity gains above ==Rs. 1.25 lakh== exemption)

> - Special rate tax on STCG under **Section 111A** (listed equity — typically **15%**)

Even if total income is under ==Rs. 12 lakh==, such gains can still attract tax at special rates without rebate offset.

## Compliance Tips

> ✅ Reconcile **Form 16 / AIS / broker statements** before regime selection — special-rate gains decide whether rebate clears full liability.

> ⚠️ Year-end **bonus deferral** or **80CCD(1B)** may keep taxable income on the favourable side of ==Rs. 12 lakh== — model before March.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. I earn Rs. 11.8 lakh salary. Do I pay zero tax under the New Regime?**
Often yes — ==Rs. 75,000== standard deduction reduces taxable income; if all income is slab-rated and within ==Rs. 12 lakh==, **full 87A rebate** can apply. Confirm with the ITR utility.

**Q2. My income is Rs. 12.5 lakh. How much tax do I pay?**
Tax on ==Rs. 12.5 lakh== (new regime slabs) ≈ ==Rs. 67,500== before cess — **rebate = Nil** above ==Rs. 12 lakh==. **Marginal relief** can cap tax so the incremental tax does not exceed incremental income over ==Rs. 12 lakh== (here ≈ ==Rs. 50,000== excess income). Final tax is often near ==Rs. 50,000== + ==4% cess== — always verify on the official ITR utility.

**Q3. Old regime — I earn Rs. 5 lakh. Is there really zero tax?**
Yes — tax on ==Rs. 5 lakh== (old slabs) = ==Rs. 12,500==; Section 87A rebate = ==Rs. 12,500==; net tax = **Rs. 0**.
