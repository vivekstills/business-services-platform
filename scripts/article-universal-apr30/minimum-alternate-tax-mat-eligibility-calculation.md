## What Is MAT?

MAT (Section 115JB) ensures companies pay a minimum tax of **15% on their Book Profit**, even when regular tax liability is reduced to near-zero through deductions, exemptions, or losses.

> 📌 MAT applies **only to companies** — not to individuals, HUFs, firms, LLPs, or sole proprietors.

## When Does MAT Kick In?

| Situation | Tax Payable |
|---|---|
| Regular income tax > MAT | Pay regular tax (MAT not triggered) |
| Regular income tax < MAT | Pay MAT (15% of Book Profit) |

## Book Profit Calculation

Book Profit ≠ taxable income. It starts from the net profit in the P&L account and adjusts as follows:

| Add Back | Deduct |
|---|---|
| Income tax provision | Depreciation per Companies Act |
| Deferred tax provision | Brought-forward losses or depreciation (whichever is lower) |
| Dividends from free reserves | Income exempt under the IT Act (Sec 10 etc.) |
| Depreciation per IT Act | — |

**MAT = 15% × Book Profit** (+ applicable surcharge + ==4% cess==).

## MAT Credit Mechanism

> ✅ When MAT paid > regular tax, the excess becomes **MAT Credit** — carried forward for **15 years** and offset when regular tax exceeds MAT in future years.

**Example:** FY 2025-26 — Regular tax: ==Rs. 10 lakh==. MAT: ==Rs. 25 lakh==. MAT paid: ==Rs. 25 lakh==. MAT Credit earned: ==Rs. 15 lakh==. In a later year when regular tax is ==Rs. 30 lakh== and MAT is ==Rs. 15 lakh==, the company can use ==Rs. 15 lakh== credit — net payable ==Rs. 15 lakh==.

## Companies Exempt from MAT

- Power generation, distribution, transmission companies
- Companies opting for **Section 115BAA** (22% flat rate) or **115BAB** (15% for new manufacturers) — fully outside MAT

## Compliance Tips

> ✅ Prepare **Book Profit** working papers alongside audited financials — MAT is a Board-level cash-flow item.

> ⚠️ Track **MAT credit** ledger year-wise; missed utilization wastes prior-year MAT paid.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. MAT rate currently?**
**15%** of Book Profit (reduced from 18.5% in Budget 2019) + surcharge + ==4% cess==.

**Q2. Can a loss-making company still have MAT liability?**
Yes — if Book Profit is positive despite taxable losses. Book Profit adds back many deductions that reduce taxable income.

**Q3. Do foreign companies pay MAT?**
Only in limited circumstances. Foreign companies subject to lower rates under a DTAA may be exempt. Post-2019, many foreign companies fall outside MAT.
