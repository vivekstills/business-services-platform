## Are Tax Slabs Different for Women?

**No.** India abolished gender-based tax slabs in FY 2012-13. Women and men face identical income tax slabs under both regimes.

## Tax Slabs — FY 2025-26

### New Tax Regime (Default)

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to Rs. 4,00,000 | Nil |
| Rs. 4L – Rs. 8L | 5% |
| Rs. 8L – Rs. 12L | 10% |
| Rs. 12L – Rs. 16L | 15% |
| Rs. 16L – Rs. 20L | 20% |
| Rs. 20L – Rs. 24L | 25% |
| Above Rs. 24L | 30% |

> ✅ **Section 87A rebate:** Zero tax if total income ≤ ==Rs. 12,00,000== (New Regime, FY 2025-26).

### Old Tax Regime

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to Rs. 2,50,000 | Nil |
| Rs. 2.5L – Rs. 5L | 5% |
| Rs. 5L – Rs. 10L | 20% |
| Above Rs. 10L | 30% |

> ✅ Section 87A rebate under Old Regime: Zero tax if income ≤ ==Rs. 5,00,000==.

## Key Deductions for Women Taxpayers

| Section | Benefit | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Section 80C | EPF, PPF, ELSS, LIC, tuition fees | ==Rs. 1,50,000== |
| Section 80D | Health insurance premium | ==Rs. 25,000== (==Rs. 50,000== for senior citizen parents) |
| Section 80E | Education loan interest | Full interest, 8 years |
| Section 80CCD(1B) | NPS voluntary contribution | ==Rs. 50,000== extra |
| Section 10(13A) | HRA exemption | Actual/formula-based |
| Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana | EEE status — girl child only | Within ==Rs. 1.5L== 80C |

## Example Scenario

**Neha** (salaried, Old Regime): Gross ==Rs. 9.6 lakh==, standard deduction ==Rs. 50,000==, **80C** ==Rs. 1.5 lakh==, **80D** ==Rs. 25,000==. Taxable income falls to a lower slab band — she compares Old vs New each June using Form 12BB projections so payroll TDS matches final **ITR**.

## Planning Tips

> ✅ Co-borrowers on a home loan can each claim **Section 24(b)** interest (up to ==Rs. 2 lakh== each for self-occupied) and **80C** principal within individual caps.

> ⚠️ Stamp duty concessions for **women buyers** in some states are **not** income-tax benefits — still valuable for purchase budgeting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Are there any state-level tax benefits for women?**
Not in income tax. Some states offer reduced **stamp duty** on property registration for women buyers — check local rules.

**Q2. My husband and I jointly own a let-out property. Can I claim full deductions?**
No — each co-owner claims deductions **proportionate to ownership share** in their respective ITR.

**Q3. Does maternity pay/leave salary attract tax?**
Yes — maternity pay from a private employer is taxable as **salary**. Some statutory benefits may be exempt depending on the scheme; confirm with your employer/CA.
