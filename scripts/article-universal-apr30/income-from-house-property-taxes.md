## Overview

Income from house property is one of the five heads of income under the IT Act, 1961. It applies to all buildings and land appurtenant to them — whether rented, self-occupied, or left vacant. Even an unrented property may have a taxable "Annual Value."

## Property Types and Annual Value

| Property Type | Annual Value | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Self-Occupied (1st + 2nd property) | **NIL** | No tax, but interest deduction capped at ==Rs. 2 lakh== |
| Let-Out Property | Higher of actual rent or fair rent | Full interest deductible |
| Deemed Let-Out (3rd+ property) | Deemed fair rent | Full interest deductible |

> 📌 From FY 2019-20: Up to **2 properties** can be treated as self-occupied with NIL Annual Value. Any third or subsequent property is deemed let-out even if vacant.

## Step-by-Step Calculation

1. **Gross Annual Value (GAV):** Higher of actual rent received or fair rent (notional rent), after vacancy allowance.
2. **Municipal taxes:** Deduct amount paid during the year → **Net Annual Value (NAV)**.
3. **Standard deduction:** ==30% of NAV== — fixed; no need to prove actual expenses.
4. **Home loan interest:** Deduct under Section 24(b), subject to ==Rs. 2 lakh== cap for self-occupied property.
5. **Result:** Income or loss from house property.

## Example Calculation

Ritu lets out her flat. Annual rent: ==Rs. 3,60,000==. Municipal taxes: ==Rs. 15,000==. Home loan interest: ==Rs. 1,20,000==.

| Item | Amount (Rs.) |
|---|---|
| Gross Annual Value | 3,60,000 |
| Less: Municipal Taxes | (15,000) |
| Net Annual Value | 3,45,000 |
| Less: 30% Standard Deduction | (1,03,500) |
| Less: Home Loan Interest | (1,20,000) |
| **Income from House Property** | **1,21,500** |

## Loss from House Property

If deductions exceed income, the resulting **loss can be set off against other income** (salary, business) up to ==Rs. 2,00,000 per year==. Any remaining loss carries forward for **8 years** and can be set off only against house property income.

## New Tax Regime — Key Difference

Under the New Regime, **no deduction is available** for home loan interest on self-occupied property. Municipal taxes and standard deduction (30%) are still allowed for let-out properties.

## Compliance and Documentation Tips

> ✅ Preserve rent agreements, municipal tax receipts, and lender interest certificates — they are standard scrutiny asks.

> ⚠️ For deemed let-out properties, be prepared to justify fair rent if it differs materially from local benchmarks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. My flat was vacant all year. Do I still pay tax on it?**
If it is your 2nd or additional property, a deemed annual value applies. One relief: if you genuinely couldn't find a tenant (Section 23(1)(c)), you can show NIL GAV — but this is closely scrutinised.

**Q2. Can I claim pre-construction interest?**
Yes — as 5 equal annual instalments starting from the year of completion, subject to the ==Rs. 2 lakh== cap for self-occupied properties.

**Q3. Joint owners — how do we split income?**
Each co-owner reports their proportionate share of rental income and loan interest in their individual ITR based on ownership ratio.
