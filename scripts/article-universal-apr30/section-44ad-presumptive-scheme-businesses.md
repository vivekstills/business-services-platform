## Overview

Section 44AD lets eligible small businesses declare income at a **fixed % of turnover** instead of maintaining full books of accounts. No separate expense deductions are required — the declared % is treated as net profit after all expenses.

## Eligibility

**Who can use 44AD:**

- Resident individuals, resident HUFs, resident partnership firms (not LLPs)
- Gross turnover or receipts ≤ ==Rs. 2 crore== (or ==Rs. 3 crore== if 95%+ receipts are digital)

**Who cannot use 44AD:**

- Companies, LLPs, non-residents
- Professionals (use **44ADA** instead)
- Commission/brokerage agents
- Transport businesses (use **44AE**)
- Those who opted out of 44AD within the last 5 years

## Presumptive Income Rates

| Mode of Business Receipts | Presumptive Income Rate |
|---|---|
| Cash receipts involved | **8% of turnover** |
| 95%+ receipts via banking/digital channels | **6% of turnover** |

## Five-Year Lock-In Rule

> ⚠️ Once you opt into Section 44AD, you must continue for **5 consecutive assessment years**. If you opt out before 5 years, you cannot use 44AD again for the next 5 years — and a **mandatory tax audit** applies during the lock-out period.

## Example

Ramesh runs a textile shop. Annual turnover: ==Rs. 1.8 crore== (fully digital payments).

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Turnover | Rs. 1,80,00,000 |
| Presumptive Income @ 6% | Rs. 10,80,000 |
| Less: 80C + 80CCD(1B) deductions | (Rs. 2,00,000) |
| Taxable income | Rs. 8,80,000 |
| Tax (new regime) | ~Rs. 60,000 |

No books of accounts needed. No CA required for audit. Files **ITR-4**.

## Key Compliance Notes

> ✅ No tax audit required if declared income ≥ the presumptive rate.

> ⚠️ Advance tax: entire amount in **one instalment by 15 March** (not quarterly).

> ⚠️ No separate depreciation, salary, or expense deductions — all are subsumed in the 6%/8% rate. Only personal deductions (80C, 80D, etc.) remain available.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Opting into 44AD one year and opting out the next without understanding **5-year lock-in** consequences.
- Using 44AD for **professional** income (e.g., consultant or doctor) — they must use **Section 44ADA**.
- Declaring **below** the 6%/8% rate without proper books — triggers **mandatory tax audit**.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can I declare income higher than 6%?**
Yes. You can declare any amount ≥ 6%. If actual profits are 10%, declare 10%.

**Q2. What if I declare below 6%?**
A tax audit under Section 44AB becomes mandatory. You must then maintain full books and get them audited.

**Q3. A freelance designer earning Rs. 40 lakh — can they use 44AD?**
No. Design/creative work is a profession — they must use **Section 44ADA** (50% presumptive, receipts up to ==Rs. 75 lakh== for eligible professions).
