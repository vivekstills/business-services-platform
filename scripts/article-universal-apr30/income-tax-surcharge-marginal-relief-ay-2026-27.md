## What Is Surcharge?

Surcharge is an additional levy on **income tax itself** (not on income) when total income crosses specified thresholds. It is computed as a percentage of the income tax before cess.

## Surcharge Rates — AY 2026-27

### New Tax Regime

| Total Income | Surcharge Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to Rs. 50 lakh | Nil |
| Rs. 50L – Rs. 1 crore | 10% |
| Rs. 1Cr – Rs. 2Cr | 15% |
| Rs. 2Cr – Rs. 5Cr | 25% |
| Above Rs. 5 crore | **25%** (capped — not 37%) |

### Old Tax Regime

| Total Income | Surcharge Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to Rs. 50 lakh | Nil |
| Rs. 50L – Rs. 1 crore | 10% |
| Rs. 1Cr – Rs. 2Cr | 15% |
| Rs. 2Cr – Rs. 5Cr | 25% |
| Above Rs. 5 crore | **37%** |

> ✅ The New Regime **caps surcharge at 25%** for all income levels — a critical advantage for ultra-HNIs above ==Rs. 5 crore==.

## Marginal Relief — Explained

When crossing a surcharge threshold by a small margin, extra tax from surcharge can exceed the incremental income. **Marginal relief caps the extra tax so it never exceeds the extra income.**

**Example:** Income = ==Rs. 50,10,000== (==Rs. 10,000== above the ==Rs. 50 lakh== threshold).

Without relief: surcharge of 10% on full tax = large spike. With marginal relief: extra tax ≤ ==Rs. 10,000==. Relief is auto-calculated by the portal.

## Example Scenario

Anika projects **Rs. 50.4 lakh** taxable income before year-end bonuses. A **Rs. 60,000** bonus pushes her into the 10% surcharge band. She compares: (a) pay tax with marginal relief, or (b) defer bonus / increase ==80CCD(2)== NPS via employer to stay below ==Rs. 50 lakh==. Modelling both avoids surprise cash outflows in March.

## Tips for Filings and Planning

> ✅ Always check marginal relief when income sits near ==Rs. 50L==, ==Rs. 1Cr==, or ==Rs. 2Cr== thresholds.

> ⚠️ Employer **NPS (80CCD(2))** and **80CCD(1B)** can pull income below a surcharge band — the tax saved often exceeds the contribution cost near thresholds.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Does surcharge apply on capital gains too?**
Yes, but surcharge on LTCG (Section 112A) and STCG (Section 111A) on equity is **capped at 15%** regardless of total income.

**Q2. Is cess applied before or after surcharge?**
After. Formula: **(Income Tax + Surcharge) × 4% cess.**

**Q3. My income crossed Rs. 50 lakh by Rs. 2 lakh. Will NPS investment help avoid surcharge?**
Yes — NPS under **80CCD(1B)** (==Rs. 50,000==) plus employer NPS under **80CCD(2)** can reduce taxable income below ==Rs. 50 lakh==, eliminating surcharge entirely and saving far more than the contribution amount in many cases.
