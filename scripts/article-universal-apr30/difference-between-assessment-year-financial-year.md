## Overview

Every income tax filing in India revolves around two periods taxpayers frequently confuse: the **Financial Year (FY)** and the **Assessment Year (AY)**. Using the wrong year when paying challan or filing ITR creates serious mismatches in the system.

| Term | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Financial Year (FY) | 12-month period income is earned | FY 2025-26 = 1 Apr 2025 – 31 Mar 2026 |
| Assessment Year (AY) | 12-month period income is assessed and taxed | AY 2026-27 = 1 Apr 2026 – 31 Mar 2027 |
| Previous Year | Same as Financial Year (legal term used in the IT Act) | FY 2025-26 = Previous Year 2025-26 |

> 📌 **Golden rule:** Income earned in FY 2025-26 is filed and taxed in ==AY 2026-27==.

## Why the Distinction Matters

All Income Tax Department communications — notices, refunds, demands, assessments — reference the **Assessment Year**, not the Financial Year. Paying challan under the wrong AY is one of the most common taxpayer errors. It creates a credit mismatch and can result in demand notices even when tax has been fully paid.

## Reference Table — FY to AY

| Financial Year | Assessment Year | Non-Audit ITR Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| FY 2023-24 | AY 2024-25 | 31 July 2024 |
| FY 2024-25 | AY 2025-26 | 31 July 2025 |
| FY 2025-26 | AY 2026-27 | 31 July 2026 |

## Where AY vs FY Appears in Practice

| Action | Use FY or AY? |
|---|---|
| Filing ITR on portal | AY (e.g., ==AY 2026-27==) |
| Paying advance tax / self-assessment tax via challan | AY |
| Calculating deductions (80C, 80D) | FY (year you made the investment) |
| Responding to notices | AY mentioned on the notice |
| TDS certificates (Form 16, 16A) | FY (salary/payment period) |

## Example

Meera earned ==Rs. 9 lakh== in FY 2025-26 and files ITR in July 2026. She selects **AY 2026-27** on the portal. She pays self-assessment tax via challan — again selects **AY 2026-27**. If she mistakenly selects AY 2025-26, the credit lands under the previous year and a demand notice is generated for AY 2026-27.

> ⚠️ **Common mistake:** Selecting FY instead of AY while paying challan. Always select the AY — the year *after* you earned the income.

## Tips for Challans and Filings

> ✅ Cross-check the **AY** on your challan receipt against the year on your filed ITR acknowledgment.

> ✅ Keep **Form 26AS / AIS** handy — they tie credits to the correct **AY**.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. I received a notice referencing AY 2023-24. Which income year does it relate to?**
AY 2023-24 refers to income earned in FY 2022-23 (1 April 2022 to 31 March 2023).

**Q2. Can a notice arrive in 2026 for AY 2021-22?**
Yes. The IT Department can issue reassessment notices up to 3–10 years after the AY depending on the case type. Always preserve records for at least 10 years.

**Q3. Does India's financial year ever start on a date other than 1 April?**
No. India's FY has always run from 1 April to 31 March. There have been proposals to change it, but none have been implemented.
