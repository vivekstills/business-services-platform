## Overview

The Income Tax Act, 1961 classifies all income into **five heads**. Every rupee you earn must be placed under one of these heads before computing tax. The head determines what deductions apply and how income is calculated.

## The Five Heads

| Head | Section | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Salaries** | Sec 15–17 | Wages, pension, perquisites, allowances from employment |
| **2. Income from House Property** | Sec 22–27 | Rent from owned buildings/land; notional rent on vacant/let-out properties |
| **3. Profits and Gains from Business or Profession (PGBP)** | Sec 28–44 | Business profits, professional income, presumptive income |
| **4. Capital Gains** | Sec 45–55A | Gains from sale of capital assets (property, shares, gold, mutual funds) |
| **5. Income from Other Sources** | Sec 56–59 | Residual head — interest, dividends, gifts, lottery winnings, family pension |

## Head 1: Salaries

Includes basic salary, HRA, DA, bonus, commission, perquisites (company car, accommodation), and ESOPs on exercise. Deductions: Standard deduction (==Rs. 75,000== New Regime / ==Rs. 50,000== Old Regime), HRA exemption, LTA, professional tax.

## Head 2: House Property

Rent from buildings. Annual Value (higher of actual rent or fair rent) minus municipal taxes, ==30%== standard deduction, and home loan interest. Self-occupied property (up to 2 properties) can have **NIL** Annual Value.

## Head 3: PGBP

Business/professional income after allowable deductions under Sections 30–37 (rent, repairs, depreciation, salaries paid, etc.). Presumptive schemes (**44AD**, **44ADA**, **44AE**) simplify compliance for small taxpayers.

## Head 4: Capital Gains

Gains from transfer of capital assets. Two broad ideas:

- **STCG** — shorter holding periods (e.g., listed equity typically ≤ 12 months)
- **LTCG** — longer holding — often **special rates** (e.g., Section **112A** for equity above exemption)

Rates depend on asset class and holding period — always verify for the relevant FY.

## Head 5: Income from Other Sources

Catch-all head:

- Savings/FDR interest, dividends
- Gifts received exceeding ==Rs. 50,000== from non-relatives (subject to conditions)
- Family pension (after deduction)
- Lottery/gambling winnings (**30%** flat tax in many cases)

## Set-Off Between Heads

| Can set off against | Allowed? |
|---|---|
| Business loss vs Salary income | ✅ Yes |
| House property loss vs Salary income | ✅ Yes (up to ==Rs. 2 lakh==) |
| Capital loss vs Business income | ❌ No |
| Speculation loss vs Normal business profit | ❌ No |
| Within capital gains | ✅ STCG/LTCG pairing rules apply |

## Tips for ITR Classification

> ✅ Use **ITR-3** when you have business/professional income alongside salary unless **ITR-4** presumptive rules fit cleanly.

> ✅ Reconcile **AIS** with bank/broker interest — misclassified head triggers mismatch notices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. My freelance income — which head?**
**PGBP (Head 3)** — professional income. If receipts ≤ ==Rs. 75 lakh==, you may use **Section 44ADA** (50% presumptive) when eligible.

**Q2. Dividend from mutual funds — which head?**
**Head 5** (Income from Other Sources). Dividends are generally taxable at slab rates since 2020.

**Q3. I sold my car at a loss. Is that a capital loss?**
Personal-use vehicles are typically **not** capital assets for this purpose — generally **no** capital gain/loss on sale of a personal car.
