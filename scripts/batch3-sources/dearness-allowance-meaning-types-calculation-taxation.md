# Dearness Allowance (DA): Meaning, Types, DA Calculation and Tax Treatment

Dearness Allowance (DA) is a cost-of-living adjustment paid by the Central Government, State Governments, and PSUs to their employees and pensioners to offset the impact of inflation. DA is fully taxable as salary income for most employees. This article explains the two types of DA - CDA and IDA - the AICPI-based calculation formula, current DA rates for central government employees as of 2026, tax treatment, DA for pensioners, and how DA interacts with other salary components like HRA.

## What is Dearness Allowance?

Dearness Allowance is a component of the salary structure of government employees and pensioners designed to protect their real purchasing power against inflation. As the price level in the economy rises, the purchasing power of a fixed salary erodes. DA is periodically revised upward to compensate for this erosion, ensuring that the effective salary keeps pace with inflation.

DA was introduced post-World War II when prices rose sharply and fixed-income government employees faced severe hardship. It has since become a permanent and substantial component of government employee compensation. For central government employees under the 7th Pay Commission, DA can form a significant percentage of the basic pay - in many cases, DA itself exceeds the basic pay when accumulated revisions are considered.

## Two Types of DA - CDA vs IDA

| Feature | Central DA (CDA) | Industrial DA (IDA) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Applicable to | Central Govt employees, All India Service officers, central PSU employees on CDA pay scales | Central PSU employees on IDA pay scales (e.g., ONGC, BHEL, NTPC, SAIL, BSNL) |
| Revision frequency | Twice a year - January 1 and July 1 | Quarterly - January, April, July, October |
| Basis of calculation | 12-month average of All India CPI (Industrial Workers) - Base Year 2016=100 | 3-month average of All India CPI (Industrial Workers) - Base Year 2016=100 |
| Expressed as | Percentage of basic pay | Percentage of basic pay (different formula) |
| Announcement body | Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA) | Board of Directors of respective PSU / DPE guidelines |
| Merger with basic | Never merged (unlike earlier pay commissions) | Partially merged at regular pay revision intervals |

## How DA is Calculated - The AICPI Formula (CDA)

For central government employees under CDA, the DA rate is determined using the All India Consumer Price Index for Industrial Workers (AICPI-IW) with base year 2016=100. The formula used by the 7th Central Pay Commission is:

### CDA Calculation Formula

**DA% = [ (Average AICPI-IW for past 12 months - 261.33) / 261.33 ] x 100**

Where: 261.33 is the AICPI-IW index value corresponding to the basic pay of the 7th CPC (January 2016 base).

**Example (Illustrative for January 2026 revision):**

- 12-month average AICPI (Feb 2025 - Jan 2026): ~645
- DA% = [(645 - 261.33) / 261.33] x 100 = [383.67 / 261.33] x 100 = 146.8% (rounded to nearest whole number)

This DA% is applied to the 7th CPC Basic Pay to compute DA amount.

Employee with Basic Pay Rs. 50,000:

- DA @ 147% = Rs. 73,500 per month

## DA and HRA Linkage

House Rent Allowance (HRA) for central government employees is linked to the city classification (X, Y, Z cities) as a percentage of basic pay. However, when DA crosses 25% and 50% thresholds, HRA rates are revised upward. When DA crossed 50% (January 2024), HRA rates for X-cities moved from 27% to 30%, Y-cities from 18% to 20%, and Z-cities from 9% to 10% of basic pay. This DA-HRA linkage means DA revisions indirectly increase HRA as well.

## Tax Treatment of Dearness Allowance

Dearness Allowance is fully taxable as salary income for all employees - both government and private sector. There are no exemptions available for DA under the Income Tax Act for regular employees. DA is included in gross salary and subject to TDS by the employer.

| Category | Tax Treatment of DA | Special Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Central Govt / State Govt employees | Fully taxable - included in salary | No exemption; standard deduction of Rs. 75,000 applies on total salary including DA |
| PSU employees (CDA scale) | Fully taxable | Same as above |
| PSU employees (IDA scale) | Fully taxable | Same as above |
| Private sector employees receiving DA | Fully taxable | Same treatment |
| Pensioners receiving DR (Dearness Relief) | Fully taxable | DR is treated as salary income; family pension DR is partly exempt |

## Dearness Allowance for Pensioners - Dearness Relief (DR)

Retired central government employees receive Dearness Relief (DR) on their basic pension, in lieu of the DA paid to serving employees. DR is revised at the same rate as DA and follows the same AICPI-based calculation. DR is fully taxable as pension income. The employer (paying authority - pension sanctioning authority) deducts TDS on pension plus DR under Section 192.

For family pensioners (spouse/dependents receiving family pension after death of pensioner), the basic family pension and DR thereon are taxable under 'Income from Other Sources'. A standard deduction of 1/3rd of family pension or Rs. 25,000 (whichever is lower) is available under Section 57(iia) - this limit was enhanced to Rs. 25,000 in Budget 2024.

## DA and Its Role in Other Salary Calculations

DA is not merely an inflation compensator - it forms the base for computing several other salary benefits:

- **Provident Fund (EPF/GPF):** Employer and employee PF contributions are computed on Basic Pay + DA in many PSUs. This increases the PF accumulation significantly.
- **Gratuity:** Under the Payment of Gratuity Act, gratuity is computed as (Last Drawn Salary including DA / 26) x 15 x years of service. Higher DA = higher gratuity.
- **Leave Encashment:** Leave encashment at retirement is computed on Basic Pay + DA. The tax exemption ceiling under Section 10(10AA) is Rs. 25 lakh.
- **HRA:** As explained above, DA thresholds trigger HRA revisions for government employees.
- **House Building Advance (HBA):** The loan limit for HBA from government is based on 34 months of Basic Pay + DA.

### Pro Tip

Government employees should ensure their DA arrears (if received for prior months) are correctly reflected in Form 16 for the year of actual receipt. Arrear DA is taxable in the year of receipt. If the arrear relates to prior years and results in a higher tax burden, claim relief under Section 89(1) by filing Form 10E on the IT portal before filing your ITR.

## Key Takeaways

1. DA is a cost-of-living adjustment paid to government and PSU employees - it is fully taxable as salary income with no exemption.
2. CDA (Central DA) is revised twice yearly (January and July) based on 12-month average AICPI-IW; IDA is revised quarterly.
3. For central govt employees under 7th CPC, DA as of July 2025 stands at 58% of basic pay.
4. When DA crosses 25% and 50% thresholds, HRA rates for government employees are revised upward.
5. Gratuity, leave encashment, EPF, and HBA are all calculated on Basic Pay + DA - higher DA directly increases these benefits.
6. Dearness Relief (DR) for pensioners follows the same rates as DA and is fully taxable as pension income.
7. DA arrears received in a lump sum can trigger higher tax - claim Section 89(1) relief by filing Form 10E before filing ITR.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is there any DA exemption for private sector employees?

No. Dearness Allowance paid by private sector employers is fully taxable under the head 'Salaries' without any exemption. Unlike HRA (which has a partial exemption formula), there is no provision in the Income Tax Act that exempts DA for private sector employees. The entire DA amount is added to gross salary and subject to TDS. Only employees of certain state governments who receive DA under notified conditions (prior to service rules for retirement benefit computation) may have limited specific exemptions - but these are rare and state-specific.

### My DA was merged with basic pay during a pay revision. Is the merged amount treated differently for tax purposes?

No. Once DA is merged with basic pay (as happened during earlier Pay Commission revisions), it becomes part of basic pay for all purposes - including tax. The merged amount is fully taxable as salary. However, for computation of perquisites, the 'basic salary' used in formulas (like HRA, PF, gratuity) is the revised basic after merger. The merger does not create any tax benefit or disadvantage - it is purely a structural change in how salary is presented.

### How is DA different from HRA? Can both be exempt from tax?

DA and HRA serve different purposes. DA compensates for inflation and is fully taxable. HRA compensates for accommodation costs and has a partial exemption formula under Section 10(13A) - the exempt portion is the least of: actual HRA received, rent paid minus 10% of basic salary, or 50%/40% of basic salary (metro/non-metro). So HRA has a conditional partial exemption; DA has none. A government employee receives both DA and HRA, but only HRA qualifies for partial exemption.

### Are DA arrears (for previous years) taxed in the year of receipt or accrual?

DA arrears are taxed in the year of actual receipt (cash basis for salary income), not the year they relate to. If DA for April 2024 to September 2024 is received in January 2025, the entire arrear is taxable in FY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26). This bunching of income may push you into a higher slab. To mitigate this, file Form 10E on the IT portal and claim relief under Section 89(1) when filing your ITR, which spreads the tax over the years to which the arrears relate.

### A central government employee retired with DA at 58%. Is their pension calculated on basic pay alone or basic pay plus DA?

Pension for central government employees is calculated on the last drawn basic pay only - NOT on basic pay + DA. The pension formula under the 7th CPC is: Pension = Last Basic Pay / 2. After retirement, Dearness Relief (DR) is added separately on the basic pension, starting at 0% and revised periodically in line with DA revisions for serving employees. So the pensioner eventually receives pension + DR, which replicates the DA benefit on their pension amount.
