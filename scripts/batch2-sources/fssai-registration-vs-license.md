# FSSAI Registration vs FSSAI License: Key Differences Explained

## Introduction

The Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) is the regulatory body responsible for ensuring that food sold across India meets safety and quality standards. Whether you are starting a home bakery, a cloud kitchen, a restaurant chain, or importing food products, you will need FSSAI authorisation before you can legally operate.

The confusion for most food business operators is whether they need a basic registration or a state/central license. These are not interchangeable. Using the wrong category can result in penalties, cancellations, and business disruptions. This guide clarifies the differences definitively.

## The three types of FSSAI authorisation

| Type | Who it is for | Issued by | Validity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Basic registration | Very small food businesses | FSSAI directly | 1 to 5 years |
| State license | Medium-sized businesses | State Food Safety Authority | 1 to 5 years |
| Central license | Large, multi-state, import-export businesses | FSSAI Central Authority | 1 to 5 years |

## FSSAI basic registration

### Who needs basic registration?

Basic Registration (Form A) is for petty food business operators whose annual turnover does not exceed Rs. 12 lakhs.

This includes:

- Hawkers, vendors, and small retailers
- Home-based food businesses (home bakers, tiffin services, pickle makers)
- Petty manufacturers with annual turnover up to Rs. 12 lakhs
- Temporary stall owners at fairs, exhibitions, and festivals
- Small food storage operators

### What is not covered under basic registration?

- Businesses with turnover above Rs. 12 lakhs
- Businesses operating in more than one state
- Manufacturers producing more than specified quantities (for example, dairy, meat)

### Documents required for basic registration

| Document | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Photo of the applicant | Passport-size |
| Identity proof | Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, or driving licence |
| Address proof of business premises | Utility bill, rent agreement |
| Form A (declaration) | Duly filled and signed |

## FSSAI state license

### Who needs a state license?

State License (Form B) is required for medium food businesses operating within one state, with annual turnover between Rs. 12 lakhs and Rs. 20 crores.

This includes:

- Restaurants, hotels, and eateries (turnover above Rs. 12 lakhs)
- Cloud kitchens and food aggregators below central license threshold
- Manufacturers with production capacity between specified limits (for example, dairy below 50,000 litres per day)
- Cold storages, warehouses, and distributors within a single state
- Caterers for events with more than 100 persons

### Turnover and capacity thresholds for state license

| Category | Threshold for state license |
| --- | --- |
| Annual turnover | Rs. 12 lakhs to Rs. 20 crores |
| Dairy units | Up to 50,000 litres per day |
| Slaughterhouses | Up to 50 large animals or 150 small animals per day |
| Vegetable oil processing | Up to 2 MT per day |
| Proprietary food units | Up to 2 MT per day |
| Hotels (star category) | Below 5-star |

## FSSAI central license

### Who needs a central license?

Central License is mandatory for:

- Food businesses with annual turnover above Rs. 20 crores
- Importers and exporters of food products
- Businesses operating in more than one state
- Food manufacturers supplying directly to government agencies or defence establishments
- E-commerce food business operators (Swiggy, Zomato, Amazon Fresh, etc.)
- Five-star hotels and above
- Food businesses at airports, ports, and railways stations

### Special cases requiring central license irrespective of turnover

- 100% Export Oriented Units (EOUs)
- Units operating under a central government ministry

## Side-by-side comparison table

| Parameter | Basic registration | State license | Central license |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Annual turnover | Up to Rs. 12 lakhs | Rs. 12 lakhs to Rs. 20 crores | Above Rs. 20 crores |
| Applicability | Very small or petty operators | Medium businesses (intra-state) | Large, multi-state, importers |
| Application form | Form A | Form B | Form B |
| Issuing authority | Registration officer | Designated state officer | Central Licensing Authority |
| FSSAI number digits | 14-digit | 14-digit | 14-digit (starts with 1) |

| Processing time | 7 days | 30 days | 60 days |

| Penalty for non-compliance | Up to Rs. 25,000 | Up to Rs. 5 lakhs | Up to Rs. 10 lakhs |

## Application process

### Step 1: Determine the correct category

Use the turnover and capacity thresholds to identify whether you need basic registration, a state license, or a central license.

### Step 2: Apply on the FoSCoS portal

Visit `https://foscos.fssai.gov.in` and create an account as a Food Business Operator (FBO). Select the appropriate application type.

### Step 3: Fill in business details

Provide details of:

- Business name, type, and ownership structure
- Address and premises details
- Nature of food business activities
- Installed capacity or turnover estimates

### Step 4: Upload documents

Upload all required documents based on the license type. Central license applications require additional documents such as plant layout, water test reports, and list of food products.

### Step 5: Pay the fee

Fee is based on the type of authorization and the duration (1 to 5 years).

### Step 6: Inspection (if required)

For state and central licenses, a food safety officer may inspect the premises before issuing the license.

### Step 7: Receive the license

Upon approval, the 14-digit FSSAI number is issued. This must be displayed prominently at the business premises and printed on all food labels.

## Penalties for non-compliance

| Violation | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Operating without registration or license | Imprisonment up to 6 months plus fine up to Rs. 5 lakhs |
| Selling unsafe food | Up to Rs. 10 lakhs fine; life imprisonment if death results |
| Misleading advertisement | Up to Rs. 10 lakhs |
| Failure to comply with FSSAI directions | Up to Rs. 2 lakhs |
| Obstructing FSSAI officer | Up to Rs. 10,000 |

## Frequently asked questions

**Q1. If I run a home bakery and sell only to friends and neighbours with turnover under Rs. 12 lakhs, do I still need FSSAI registration?**

Yes. FSSAI registration is mandatory for all food business operators regardless of scale, including home-based businesses. Even if your turnover is Rs. 5,000 per month, selling food commercially without FSSAI registration is a legal violation. Basic registration (Form A) is simple, affordable, and can be obtained online within 7 working days.

**Q2. Does a restaurant on Swiggy or Zomato need a central license or a state license?**

The restaurant (cloud kitchen or physical restaurant) needs a state license based on its own turnover. The food aggregator platform (Swiggy, Zomato) needs a central license since it operates across multiple states and is classified as an e-commerce food business operator. These are two separate obligations: one for the platform and one for each listed restaurant.

**Q3. My turnover crossed Rs. 20 crores mid-year. Do I need to upgrade my license immediately?**

Yes. As soon as your turnover is projected to exceed Rs. 20 crores or your operations expand to multiple states, you must apply for a central license. Operating with a state license beyond the applicable threshold is a compliance violation. You should apply for upgrade before the threshold is crossed, not after.

**Q4. Is the FSSAI license transferable if I sell my food business?**

No. FSSAI licenses and registrations are non-transferable. When a food business changes ownership, the new owner must apply for a fresh license or registration in their own name. The previous license must be surrendered for cancellation.

**Q5. Can I display the FSSAI number on my product packaging even during the registration renewal period when the old license has expired?**

No. Operating or labelling products with an expired FSSAI license is non-compliant. You must apply for renewal at least 30 days before the expiry date. If the renewal is pending, you can continue operations if you have proof of having applied for renewal, but you cannot label products with an expired number. Always apply for renewal well in advance.
