# Complete guide to GST registration in India 2024

The Goods and Services Tax (GST), introduced on July 1, 2017, replaced over a dozen central and state taxes with a unified indirect tax structure. Every business supplying taxable goods or services above the prescribed threshold must obtain GST registration. Operating without GST registration when required is a serious legal offence, attracting penalties equal to 100% of the tax evaded (subject to a minimum of Rs. 10,000).

Beyond compliance, GST registration enables you to collect tax from customers, claim input tax credit on your purchases, and establish credibility with corporate buyers who require your GSTIN for their own ITC claims.

## Who needs to register for GST?

### Mandatory registration (threshold-based)

| Category | Threshold for mandatory registration |
| --- | --- |
| Supplier of goods (normal states) | Rs. 40 lakhs aggregate turnover |
| Supplier of services (normal states) | Rs. 20 lakhs aggregate turnover |
| Supplier of goods (special category states) | Rs. 20 lakhs aggregate turnover |
| Supplier of services (special category states) | Rs. 10 lakhs aggregate turnover |

Special Category States include Manipur, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tripura, Meghalaya, Sikkim, Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir, and Arunachal Pradesh.

### Mandatory registration regardless of turnover

| Situation | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| Inter-state supply of goods | Any cross-state goods sale requires GST registration |
| E-commerce operators | Platforms like Amazon, Flipkart |
| Casual taxable persons | Temporary businesses in a state where they don't have a fixed place of business |
| Non-resident taxable persons | Foreign businesses supplying in India |
| Suppliers under reverse charge mechanism | When reverse charge applies |
| Input Service Distributor (ISD) | Distributing ITC across branches |
| Persons deducting TDS under GST | Government departments deducting GST TDS |
| Suppliers of online information services from outside India to India (OIDAR) | Foreign digital service providers |

## Types of GST registration

| Type | Who it is for |
| --- | --- |
| Regular registration | Standard businesses above threshold |
| Composition scheme | Small businesses below Rs. 1.5 crore (Rs. 75 lakhs for services) |
| Casual taxable person | Businesses operating temporarily in a state |
| Non-resident taxable person | Foreign businesses operating temporarily in India |
| Input Service Distributor | Head offices distributing ITC to branches |
| TDS deductor | Government entities deducting GST TDS |
| TCS collector | E-commerce operators collecting TCS on behalf of sellers |

## Composition scheme: a simpler alternative

Small businesses can opt for the Composition Scheme under Section 10 of the CGST Act.

| Type of business | Threshold | Rate of tax |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Manufacturers (except notified goods) | Rs. 1.5 crore turnover | 1% (0.5% CGST plus 0.5% SGST) |
| Traders (goods) | Rs. 1.5 crore turnover | 1% |
| Restaurants not serving alcohol | Rs. 1.5 crore turnover | 5% |
| Service providers | Rs. 50 lakhs turnover | 6% |

Composition taxpayers cannot collect GST from customers, cannot issue tax invoices, and cannot claim ITC. They are suitable for purely local businesses with B2C customers who are not concerned about ITC.

## Step-by-step GST registration process

### Step 1: Access the GST portal

Visit `https://www.gst.gov.in` and click Services, Registration, New Registration.

### Step 2: Part A of Form REG-01

Enter:

- Type of taxpayer (Regular, Composition, etc.)
- State and district
- Legal name as per PAN
- PAN (auto-validated against Income Tax database)
- Email and mobile number for OTP verification

An Application Reference Number (ARN) is generated after Part A submission.

### Step 3: Part B of Form REG-01

This is the detailed application. Fill in:

- Business details (trade name, constitution of business)
- Principal place of business address
- Additional places of business (branches)
- HSN codes for goods / SAC codes for services supplied
- Bank account details
- Details of promoters, partners, or directors
- Authorized signatory details

Upload all required documents as per the entity type.

### Step 4: Aadhaar authentication

From August 2020, GST registration includes Aadhaar-based authentication. An OTP is sent to the mobile linked with Aadhaar. This step determines whether a physical verification of the business premises is required:

- If Aadhaar authentication is successful: registration approved within 3 working days (no physical verification unless risk-profiled)
- If Aadhaar authentication is skipped: physical verification of premises by GST officer within 30 days

### Step 5: Verification by GST officer

If required, a GST officer may visit the business premises and issue clarifications. The officer may issue Form GST REG-03 asking for additional information. You must respond within 7 working days using Form GST REG-04.

### Step 6: Approval and GSTIN issuance

Upon approval, the GST officer issues Form GST REG-06 containing your:

- GSTIN (15-digit GST Identification Number)
- Registration certificate

The GSTIN format: 2 digits (state code) plus 10 digits (PAN) plus 2 digits (entity number) plus 1 check digit.

## Understanding the GSTIN structure

| Position | Digits | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 to 2 | 2 | State code (for example, 27 for Maharashtra, 29 for Karnataka) |
| 3 to 12 | 10 | PAN of the taxpayer |
| 13 | 1 | Number of registrations in the state (1 = first registration) |
| 14 | 1 | Alphabet Z (default) |
| 15 | 1 | Check digit |

| --- | --- | --- |

## GST rates: a quick reference

| Rate | Examples of goods / services |
| --- | --- |
| 0% (exempt) | Fresh vegetables, milk, eggs, unprocessed food grains, healthcare services, educational services |
| 5% | Packaged food items, economy class air travel, essential medicines |
| 12% | Processed food, business class air travel, non-AC restaurants |
| 18% | Most services (IT, consulting, banking), electronics, construction services |
| 28% | Luxury goods, automobiles, tobacco, aerated drinks, cement |
| 28% plus cess | Pan masala, tobacco, luxury cars (cess is levied over 28%) |

## Penalties for GST non-compliance

| Violation | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Not registering when required | 10% of tax evaded or Rs. 10,000, whichever is higher |
| Tax evasion with fraudulent intent | 100% of tax amount (Rs. 10,000 minimum) |
| Late filing of GST returns | Rs. 50 per day (Rs. 20 per day for nil returns); maximum Rs. 10,000 |
| Late payment of GST | Interest at 18% per annum on unpaid amount |
| Incorrect ITC claim | 10% of ITC claimed, subject to Rs. 10,000 minimum |
| E-way bill violations | Rs. 10,000 or tax evaded, whichever is greater |

## Frequently asked questions

**Q1. If my turnover is below Rs. 20 lakhs but I supply goods to another state, do I still need GST registration?**

Yes. Inter-state supply of goods mandates GST registration regardless of turnover. The threshold limits apply only to businesses that supply goods or services within a single state. The moment you make even one cross-state sale of goods, you are required to register for GST and collect IGST on that supply. Inter-state supply of services, however, is exempt from mandatory registration if turnover is below the threshold.

**Q2. Can I claim input tax credit on purchases made before GST registration?**

Yes, but with restrictions. Under Section 18(1) of the CGST Act, a newly registered taxpayer can claim ITC on inputs held in stock and inputs contained in semi-finished or finished goods on the date immediately preceding the date of registration. This claim must be made within 30 days of becoming liable to register. Capital goods purchased before registration are also eligible under certain conditions.

**Q3. My business has branches in three different states. Do I need separate GST registrations for each state?**

Yes. GST registration is state-specific. Each state where you have a fixed place of business (including branches, offices, or warehouses) requires a separate GSTIN. However, all GSTINs will be linked to your PAN. You can designate one branch as the Head Office (registered as ISD) to distribute ITC across branches for centrally procured services. A single GSTIN covering multiple states is not permitted.

**Q4. I want to cancel my GST registration because I have closed my business. What is the process?**

You can apply for cancellation through Form GST REG-16 on the GST portal. You must provide the reason for cancellation, last return filed details, and details of stock held on the date of cancellation (tax is payable on the closing stock). The GST officer processes the application and issues a cancellation order in Form GST REG-19. Even after filing the cancellation application, you must continue filing returns until the cancellation is officially approved.

**Q5. What is the difference between GST registration suspension and cancellation?**

GST registration can be suspended by the tax officer if they have reason to believe that the registration is liable to be cancelled (for example, non-filing of returns for six or more months, or the business premises is found to be non-existent). During suspension, the taxpayer cannot make taxable supplies, issue tax invoices, or claim ITC. Cancellation is the permanent ending of registration. Suspension is a provisional measure while cancellation proceedings are ongoing; the taxpayer gets an opportunity to respond before final cancellation.
