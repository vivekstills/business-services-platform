## What Is a Nil Return? Who Needs to File It and Why

### When You Owe Zero Tax, You Might Still Need to File

There is a widespread assumption among salaried individuals and small earners that if their tax liability is zero -- either because their income is below the taxable limit or because deductions wipe out the tax -- they have no obligation to file an Income Tax Return. This assumption is only partially correct, and acting on it without verification can create unnecessary complications. Enter the concept of a Nil Return.

## What Is a Nil Return?

A Nil Return is an Income Tax Return (ITR) filed by an individual whose net tax payable for the financial year is zero. The term "nil" refers to the tax liability, not necessarily the income. A person may have earned Rs. 8 lakh in a year, paid no tax because deductions and rebates reduced their liability to zero, and still file a nil return to inform the Income Tax Department of their income and financial activity.

It is important to understand: a nil return is not a special type of ITR form. It is a regular ITR filed using the applicable form (ITR-1, ITR-2, etc.), with the resulting tax payable shown as zero.

## Three Distinct Situations That Lead to a Nil Return

| Situation | Example | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Income below basic exemption limit | Income of Rs. 2.5 lakh under old regime | Tax = nil |
| Income taxable but offset by deductions | Rs. 7 lakh income, full deductions under 80C, 80D, etc. | Tax = nil |
| Tax payable offset by rebate under Section 87A | Rs. 7 lakh income under new regime | Tax = nil after rebate |
| Tax payable but fully covered by TDS already deducted | Rs. 5 lakh income, employer deducted Rs. 15,000 TDS | Net payable = nil |

## Is Filing a Nil Return Mandatory?

This is the key question most people ask. The answer depends on specific conditions:

### When Nil Return Filing Is Mandatory

Under Section 139(1) of the Income Tax Act, ITR filing is compulsory if your gross income (before deductions) exceeds the basic exemption limit, regardless of whether tax is payable:

- Old regime: Gross income above Rs. 2.5 lakh (below 60 years), Rs. 3 lakh (60-80 years), Rs. 5 lakh (above 80 years)
- New regime: Gross income above Rs. 3 lakh (for FY 2024-25 onwards)

Additionally, filing is mandatory regardless of income level if any of these conditions are met:

| Trigger | Threshold |
| --- | --- |
| Deposited in savings account | More than Rs. 50 lakh in a year |
| Paid electricity bill | More than Rs. 1 lakh in a year |
| Spent on foreign travel | More than Rs. 2 lakh in a year |
| TDS/TCS deducted from income | More than Rs. 25,000 (Rs. 50,000 for senior citizens) |
| Owns foreign assets or has foreign income | Any amount |
| Is a company or firm | Always mandatory |

### When Filing Is Optional (But Still Advisable)

If your income is below the basic exemption limit and none of the above triggers apply, filing is technically voluntary. But experts almost universally recommend filing anyway, for reasons covered in the next section.

## Why Should You File a Nil Return Even When Not Mandatory?

### 1. Proof of Income

A filed ITR serves as an official proof of income, particularly useful when applying for:

- Home loans and personal loans
- Credit cards
- Visa applications (some countries ask for 2-3 years of ITRs)
- Tender applications for government contracts

### 2. Carry Forward of Losses

If you incurred a capital loss or business loss during the year, you cannot carry it forward to offset against future gains unless you file the ITR by the due date. A nil return (with losses reported) preserves this benefit.

### 3. Claiming Refund of TDS

If your employer or bank has deducted TDS but your overall tax liability is nil (due to deductions or rebate), you can only claim a refund of that TDS by filing an ITR. The refund is not automatic.

### 4. Establishing Financial Track Record

Lenders, embassies, and government agencies consider filing history when evaluating trustworthiness. Consistent ITR filing builds a verifiable financial identity.

### 5. Avoiding Future Complications

The Income Tax Department increasingly uses data analytics and third-party reporting (from banks, registrars, and market intermediaries) to identify high-value transactions. If you have made significant financial moves but have not filed, you may receive a notice asking you to explain.

## How to File a Nil Return

The process is identical to any regular ITR filing:

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Collect all documents: Form 16, bank interest certificates, investment proofs |
| 2 | Log in to the Income Tax e-Filing Portal (incometax.gov.in) |
| 3 | Select the applicable ITR form (ITR-1 for most salaried individuals) |
| 4 | Select the correct Assessment Year |
| 5 | Fill in income details, deductions, and verify that tax payable = zero |
| 6 | e-Verify using Aadhaar OTP, net banking, or DSC |
| 7 | Keep the ITR-V acknowledgement for your records |

The entire process takes 15-30 minutes for salaried individuals with simple income structures.

## Practical Example

Sanjay, age 32, works as a junior sales executive. His gross salary for FY 2025-26 is Rs. 5.5 lakh. He invests Rs. 1.5 lakh in PPF (80C deduction) and has health insurance premium of Rs. 25,000 (80D deduction). He is under the old tax regime.

- Gross salary: Rs. 5,50,000
- Less standard deduction: Rs. 50,000
- Less 80C: Rs. 1,50,000
- Less 80D: Rs. 25,000
- Net taxable income: Rs. 3,25,000
- Tax on Rs. 3,25,000 at slab rates: Rs. 3,750
- Less Section 87A rebate (applicable since income is under Rs. 5 lakh): Rs. 3,750
- Tax payable: Rs. 0

Sanjay's return is a nil return. But he must still file because his gross income (Rs. 5.5 lakh) exceeds Rs. 2.5 lakh. He also receives a TDS certificate from his employer showing Rs. 0 deducted (since employer also calculated nil tax). Sanjay files ITR-1, confirms nil tax, and e-verifies.

## Important Notes

A nil return must be filed by the same deadline as a regular return: July 31 for individuals (non-audit cases). Filing after July 31 but before December 31 is a belated nil return, which attracts a late fee of Rs. 1,000 (for income up to Rs. 5 lakh) or Rs. 5,000 (for income above Rs. 5 lakh) under Section 234F.

Losses cannot be carried forward if the return is filed late, even if it is a nil return. This is one of the most financially damaging consequences of missing the deadline.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. My income is Rs. 2.2 lakh -- below the exemption limit. My savings bank account interest added another Rs. 12,000. Do I need to file a nil return? Your total income is Rs. 2.32 lakh (salary + interest), which is still below the basic exemption limit of Rs. 2.5 lakh (old regime) or Rs. 3 lakh (new regime). Mandatory filing is not triggered. However, if your savings bank deposits exceeded Rs. 50 lakh in the year (unlikely at this income), filing would be mandatory. You may still choose to file voluntarily for documentation purposes.

Q2. I have a capital loss of Rs. 40,000 from selling mutual funds. My total income is below the taxable limit. Should I file a nil return? Yes, you should file a nil return and declare the capital loss within the return. Only losses declared in a timely ITR can be carried forward to future assessment years. If you skip filing, that Rs. 40,000 loss is forfeited permanently and cannot be used to offset future capital gains.

Q3. I filed a nil return last year. The IT department sent me an intimation under Section 143(1). Should I be worried? Not at all. An intimation under Section 143(1) is a routine communication sent by the Centralised Processing Centre (CPC) after your return is processed. It is essentially a confirmation or a minor adjustment notice. If it says "no demand, no refund," it means the department has accepted your nil return as filed. Review the intimation for any discrepancies in figures and respond within 30 days if you disagree.

Q4. Can I file a nil return even if I have no income at all during the year -- for example, if I was between jobs? Yes. A nil return can be filed even with zero income. In fact, if you were employed earlier, had TDS deducted, and then had no income for part of the year, filing a nil return ensures that any excess TDS is refunded. There is no restriction on filing a nil return with zero income.

Q5. My spouse has no income. Can I file a nil return on their behalf to build a financial record for visa purposes? Your spouse can file a nil return independently in their own name (using their own PAN). They do not need your involvement -- they file as an individual with zero income. Visa applications from embassies such as those of the UK, Schengen countries, and the US often request ITR acknowledgements for all adult applicants. A nil return from your spouse with zero income is valid for submission as a supporting document.
