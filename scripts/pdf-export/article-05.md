## What Is ITR? Everything a Beginner Needs to Know

### Your Annual Report Card to the Government

Every year, the government of India asks its citizens a simple question: here is the tax system, here are the rules -- how much did you earn, how much tax did you pay, and do we need to settle up? Your answer to that question is your Income Tax Return, commonly called ITR.

An ITR is not just a tax payment form. It is a comprehensive financial disclosure -- a structured document where you report your income from all sources, claim deductions and exemptions, state the taxes already paid (through TDS, advance tax, or self-assessment), and compute whether you owe more tax or are owed a refund.

## ITR: The Full Picture

| Aspect | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Full Form | Income Tax Return |
| Filed With | Income Tax Department of India |
| Filing Portal | incometax.gov.in |
| Governing Law | Section 139 of the Income Tax Act, 1961 |
| Who Must File | Persons whose income exceeds the basic exemption limit and others as prescribed |
| Filing Period | For FY 2025-26: April 1, 2026 to July 31, 2026 (without late fee) |
| Assessment Year | AY 2026-27 for FY 2025-26 income |
| Verification Method | e-Verify via Aadhaar OTP, net banking, or physical ITR-V to CPC Bengaluru |

## Why Is Filing ITR Important?

### Legal Compliance

The most obvious reason: if your income exceeds the basic exemption limit, you are legally required to file. Non-filing can invite notices under Section 148 or even prosecution under Section 276CC for willful default.

### Getting Your TDS Refund

Your employer, bank, or mutual fund may have deducted TDS on your income. If the total TDS deducted exceeds your actual tax liability (which is common for people with deductions and rebates), the only way to reclaim that excess is by filing an ITR and requesting a refund.

### Loan and Credit Applications

Banks require ITRs (typically for 2-3 years) when processing home loans, business loans, and high-limit credit cards. No ITR often means no loan.

### Visa Applications

Countries like the USA, UK, Canada, Australia, and Schengen nations often ask for proof of financial stability. ITRs serve as the most credible evidence of your earnings.

### Carrying Forward Losses

Capital losses and business losses can only be carried forward to future years if you file the ITR before the due date. A missed ITR means those losses die.

## Types of ITR Forms

Different taxpayers have different income profiles. The Income Tax Department provides separate forms for each profile:

| Form | Who Should File |
| --- | --- |
| ITR-1 (Sahaj) | Resident individuals with salary, one house property, and other sources income up to Rs. 50 lakh |
| ITR-2 | Individuals/HUFs with capital gains, more than one property, or foreign assets/income (no business income) |
| ITR-3 | Individuals/HUFs with business or professional income (detailed books required) |
| ITR-4 (Sugam) | Individuals/HUFs/firms opting for presumptive taxation under 44AD/44ADA/44AE |
| ITR-5 | Firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs |
| ITR-6 | Companies (other than those claiming exemption under Section 11) |
| ITR-7 | Trusts, political parties, charitable institutions, research associations |

## Choosing the Right ITR Form — Common Scenarios

| Your Situation | Correct Form |
| --- | --- |
| Salaried, single property, FD interest | ITR-1 |
| Salaried with capital gains from stocks | ITR-2 |
| Freelancer, doctor in private practice | ITR-3 or ITR-4 (if opting for presumptive) |
| Small business owner with turnover under Rs. 3 crore | ITR-4 |
| Partnership firm | ITR-5 |

## What Information Goes Into an ITR?

A typical ITR for an individual contains several schedules:

| Schedule | What You Disclose |
| --- | --- |
| Schedule S | Salary income breakup |
| Schedule HP | House property income or loss |
| Schedule PGBP | Business or professional income |
| Schedule CG | Capital gains (short-term and long-term) |
| Schedule OS | Income from other sources |
| Schedule VIA | Deductions under Chapter VI-A (80C, 80D, 80G, etc.) |
| Schedule TI | Total income computation |
| Schedule TTI | Tax on total income computation |
| Schedule TDS | TDS details (from Form 26AS / AIS) |
| Schedule IT | Advance tax and self-assessment tax paid |
| Schedule FA | Foreign assets (if any) |
| Schedule AL | Assets and liabilities (if income above Rs. 50 lakh) |

## Step-by-Step: How to File an ITR

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Collect documents: Form 16, Form 26AS, AIS, bank statements, investment proofs |
| 2 | Visit incometax.gov.in and log in with your PAN |
| 3 | Navigate to "File Income Tax Return" |
| 4 | Select AY and ITR form |
| 5 | Pre-filled data appears from Form 26AS and AIS -- review carefully |
| 6 | Add any income not pre-filled (FD interest, rent, capital gains) |
| 7 | Claim all applicable deductions |
| 8 | Verify tax computation and check for refund or balance payable |
| 9 | Pay any balance tax via Challan 280 (self-assessment tax) |
| 10 | e-Verify the return within 30 days of filing |

## Practical Example: Ramesh's ITR at a Glance

Ramesh, 40, is a salaried engineer in Pune with some investments. Here is a summary of what his ITR would contain:

| Item | Amount (Rs.) |
| --- | --- |
| Gross salary | 14,00,000 |
| Less: Standard deduction | 75,000 |
| Net salary | 13,25,000 |
| Interest on FD | 35,000 |
| Total income before deductions | 13,60,000 |
| Less: 80C (PPF + ELSS + PF) | 1,50,000 |
| Less: 80D (health insurance) | 25,000 |
| Net taxable income | 11,85,000 |
| Tax computed at slab rates | 1,72,500 (approx.) |
| Health and education cess @ 4% | 6,900 |
| Total tax | 1,79,400 |
| TDS deducted by employer | 1,70,000 |
| TDS by bank on FD | 3,500 |
| Self-assessment tax payable | Rs. 5,900 |

Ramesh pays Rs. 5,900 via Challan 280 before filing, then files ITR-1 and e-verifies. His return is filed.

## What Happens After You File?

| Event | Timeline |
| --- | --- |
| Acknowledgement generated (ITR-V) | Immediately after submission |
| e-Verification required | Within 30 days of filing |
| Processing by CPC | Typically 15-45 days after e-verification |
| Intimation under Section 143(1) | Issued after processing |
| Refund credited (if applicable) | Usually within 30-60 days of processing |
| Scrutiny notice under 143(2) | Within 3 months of filing date (rare) |

## Important Notes

An unverified ITR is treated as if it was never filed. e-Verification is not optional -- it is a mandatory step. If you cannot e-verify online, send the signed physical ITR-V acknowledgement by ordinary post (not courier) to CPC, Bengaluru within 30 days.

Pre-filled data on the portal (from Form 26AS, AIS, and TIS) may contain errors. Do not blindly accept it. Cross-check with your actual Form 16, bank statements, and broker reports. Accepting incorrect pre-filled data and then getting a mismatch notice is preventable.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. I filed my ITR but forgot to include my FD interest income. Can I correct this? Yes. You can file a revised return under Section 139(5) to correct any omission or mistake in your original return. The revised return must be filed by December 31 of the Assessment Year. So if you filed your original return for AY 2026-27 in July 2026 and later realised you missed FD interest, you can file a revised return anytime up to December 31, 2026.

Q2. My Form 26AS shows TDS deducted by my bank, but I have not received the money yet (FD is still running). Do I need to show this interest in my ITR this year? Interest accrued on an FD is taxable on an accrual basis each year, even if you do not actually withdraw it. You should include the interest accrued during FY 2025-26 (not just what was paid) in your ITR. Your bank issues a TDS certificate (Form 16A) at the end of the year reflecting both the accrued interest and TDS deducted. Use this to fill your IFOS schedule.

Q3. I am a homemaker with no income. My husband files a joint return in the USA. Does India have joint returns too? India does not have the concept of joint income tax returns. Every individual in India files independently. A homemaker with no income has no mandatory filing obligation (unless specific high-value transaction thresholds are triggered). However, if you have income from investments held in your name, you must file individually.

Q4. I e-filed my return but did not e-verify it. It has been 35 days now. What happens? If 30 days have passed without e-verification, your return is technically treated as invalid (not filed). You should e-verify immediately, even if it is late. The Income Tax Department typically accepts late verification with some processing delays, but your return will be treated as having been filed on the date of verification (not the original filing date). This affects loss carry-forward eligibility. Go to the portal, find your pending verification, and complete it via Aadhaar OTP or net banking.

Q5. I received an intimation under Section 143(1) showing a demand of Rs. 2,500 that I disagree with. What should I do? Do not ignore it. Log in to the e-filing portal, navigate to the "Pending Actions" section, and respond to the intimation within 30 days of receiving it. If you believe the demand is incorrect (for example, due to a TDS credit mismatch that has since been resolved), you can file a rectification request under Section 154. If you agree with the demand, pay it through Challan 280. Ignoring the demand leads to interest under Section 220(2) and possible recovery action.
