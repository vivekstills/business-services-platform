## Section 87A Rebate — How to Pay Zero Tax on Income Up to Rs. 12 Lakh

### The Most Powerful Tax Benefit Most People Take for Granted

If you have heard that individuals earning up to Rs. 12 lakh pay no income tax in India under the new regime, Section 87A of the Income Tax Act is the provision that makes it possible.

Yet a surprising number of salaried individuals do not know how this rebate works, what its exact limits are, whether it applies to all types of income, and when it does not apply despite your income being below the threshold.

Understanding Section 87A completely prevents missed tax savings and avoids the unpleasant surprise of receiving a tax demand you thought you were exempt from.

## What Is Section 87A?

Section 87A provides a rebate (not a deduction) from the income tax payable by a resident individual, provided their total income does not exceed a specified threshold. Unlike a deduction (which reduces taxable income), a rebate directly reduces the tax payable -- making it far more powerful dollar-for-dollar.

| Aspect | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Applicable to | Resident individuals only (not NRIs, HUFs, firms, companies) |
| Income threshold (new regime, FY 2025-26) | Up to Rs. 12,00,000 (total income after deductions) |
| Income threshold (old regime, FY 2025-26) | Up to Rs. 5,00,000 (total income) |
| Rebate amount (new regime) | Equal to income tax payable (i.e., full rebate) |
| Rebate amount (old regime) | Up to Rs. 12,500 (i.e., if tax is less, rebate equals tax) |
| Result | Effective zero tax for qualifying individuals |

## How Section 87A Works — The Mechanism

The rebate is applied after computing the income tax on taxable income but before adding cess. Here is the sequence:

| Step | Computation |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Compute gross total income |
| 2 | Apply deductions (if old regime) or standard deduction |
| 3 | Arrive at net taxable income |
| 4 | Compute income tax at applicable slab rates |
| 5 | Check if income is within 87A threshold |
| 6 | If yes: apply rebate (reduce tax to zero or by Rs. 12,500) |
| 7 | Add cess @ 4% on remaining tax (cess on zero = zero) |
| 8 | Final tax payable = Rs. 0 (if full rebate applies) |

## Section 87A Under the New Regime (FY 2025-26)

Under the new regime, the rebate covers the full tax liability if total income (after standard deduction for salaried individuals) does not exceed Rs. 12 lakh:

| Total Income (After Standard Deduction) | Tax Before Rebate | Rebate | Tax After Rebate |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Rs. 7,00,000 | Rs. 15,000 | Rs. 15,000 | Rs. 0 |
| Rs. 9,00,000 | Rs. 45,000 | Rs. 45,000 | Rs. 0 |
| Rs. 11,00,000 | Rs. 75,000 | Rs. 75,000 | Rs. 0 |
| Rs. 12,00,000 | Rs. 80,000 | Rs. 80,000 | Rs. 0 |
| Rs. 12,10,000 | Rs. 81,500 | Rs. 0 (exceeds threshold) | Rs. 81,500 + cess |

The cliff effect: Moving from Rs. 12 lakh to Rs. 12,10,000 of taxable income suddenly results in a Rs. 81,500 tax bill. This is a well-known concern about how the Section 87A benefit is structured. The government has introduced a marginal relief provision to soften this for incomes just above the threshold.

## Marginal Relief: Preventing a Disproportionate Tax Jump

To address the harsh cliff at Rs. 12 lakh, marginal relief ensures that the tax payable does not exceed the income exceeding Rs. 12 lakh.

### How Marginal Relief Works

If your taxable income is Rs. 12,10,000:

- Normal tax (no rebate, no relief): Rs. 81,500 + cess
- Income exceeding Rs. 12 lakh: Rs. 10,000
- Marginal relief: Tax is capped so that tax payable does not exceed Rs. 10,000

So your effective tax on Rs. 12.1 lakh is Rs. 10,000 + 4% cess = Rs. 10,400. Much better than paying Rs. 84,760.

This relief tapers off as income rises further above Rs. 12 lakh.

## Section 87A Under the Old Regime

The old regime's Section 87A is more modest:

- Maximum rebate: Rs. 12,500
- Applicable if total income is Rs. 5 lakh or below
- Effectively makes tax zero for total income up to Rs. 5 lakh (tax at 5% on Rs. 2.5 lakh = Rs. 12,500, fully covered by rebate)

| Total Income (Old Regime) | Tax Before Rebate | Rebate | Tax Payable |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Rs. 3,00,000 | Rs. 2,500 | Rs. 2,500 | Rs. 0 |
| Rs. 4,00,000 | Rs. 7,500 | Rs. 7,500 | Rs. 0 |
| Rs. 5,00,000 | Rs. 12,500 | Rs. 12,500 | Rs. 0 |
| Rs. 5,50,000 | Rs. 22,500 | Rs. 0 (exceeds threshold) | Rs. 22,500 + cess |

## What Income Is Excluded From the 87A Rebate?

This is the most critical and often misunderstood aspect of Section 87A. The rebate does not apply to all types of income, even if your total income is below the threshold. Specifically, income taxed at special rates is excluded from the rebate:

| Type of Income | Flat Rate | Eligible for 87A Rebate? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Long-term capital gains on equity (Section 112A) | 12.5% | No (post Budget 2024 clarification) |
| Short-term capital gains on equity (Section 111A) | 20% | No |
| Lottery/gambling income (Section 115BB) | 30% | No |
| Online gaming winnings (Section 115BBJ) | 30% | No |
| All other income taxed at slab rates | Slab | Yes |

### Practical Example: Suresh

Suresh has a salary of Rs. 8 lakh (taxable after deductions). He also has LTCG of Rs. 2.5 lakh from selling equity mutual funds. His total income is Rs. 10.5 lakh (below Rs. 12 lakh).

- Tax on salary of Rs. 8 lakh: Rs. 30,000 (before rebate)
- Rebate under 87A on salary tax: Rs. 30,000 (fully covered)
- LTCG tax: 12.5% on Rs. 1.25 lakh (LTCG above Rs. 1.25 lakh exemption) = Rs. 15,625
- No rebate on LTCG tax
- Cess on Rs. 15,625: Rs. 625
- Total tax payable: Rs. 16,250

The salary portion is effectively tax-free, but LTCG tax stands and cannot be rebated even though total income is below Rs. 12 lakh.

### Practical Example: Salaried Employee at Exactly Rs. 12.75 Lakh

Pooja is a salaried employee earning Rs. 12,75,000 gross.

| Computation | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Gross salary | Rs. 12,75,000 |
| Less: Standard deduction (new regime) | Rs. 75,000 |
| Taxable income | Rs. 12,00,000 |
| Tax at new regime slabs on Rs. 12 lakh | Rs. 80,000 |
| Less: 87A rebate | Rs. 80,000 |
| Net tax | Rs. 0 |
| Health and education cess | Rs. 0 |
| Total Tax Payable | Rs. 0 |

Pooja pays zero income tax on a Rs. 12.75 lakh gross salary. This is one of the most significant benefits introduced in Budget 2024 and remains in force for FY 2025-26.

## Who Is NOT Eligible for Section 87A?

| Category | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| NRIs (Non-Resident Indians) | Section 87A is for resident individuals only |
| HUFs (Hindu Undivided Families) | Not an individual |
| Companies and firms | Not an individual |
| Resident individuals above Rs. 12 lakh income (new regime) | Above threshold |
| Resident individuals above Rs. 5 lakh income (old regime) | Above threshold |
| Persons with special rate income (STCG, LTCG) on that specific portion | Specific income type excluded |

## Important Notes

Section 87A is applied automatically by the ITR filing system once you enter your income correctly and select the applicable regime. The portal calculates the rebate without any manual entry. However, the system does not always handle the LTCG and STCG exclusion correctly for all scenarios -- cross-verify your tax computation sheet before submitting.

Also note: the 87A rebate reduces tax to zero -- it does not create a negative tax or refund by itself. If you have had TDS deducted, the refund comes from excess TDS, not from the rebate itself.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. I earned Rs. 11.5 lakh in salary and Rs. 80,000 in FD interest. My total income is Rs. 12.3 lakh. Does the 87A rebate apply? Your total income is Rs. 12.3 lakh (after standard deduction of Rs. 75,000, your net income would be Rs. 11.55 lakh if both are salary -- but FD interest is IFOS, not eligible for standard deduction, so let's compute carefully). Gross salary Rs. 11.5 lakh minus Rs. 75,000 standard deduction = Rs. 10.75 lakh + FD interest Rs. 80,000 = total income Rs. 11.55 lakh. Since Rs. 11.55 lakh is below Rs. 12 lakh, the full 87A rebate applies and your tax is zero.

Q2. If the 87A rebate makes my tax zero, does my employer still need to deduct TDS from my salary? Your employer must deduct TDS based on the projected tax for the year. If the full-year calculation shows zero tax after the rebate, the employer should deduct zero TDS. You can submit a declaration to your employer (or your employer's payroll team may request your investment declarations) to ensure TDS is computed correctly at zero from the start of the year, rather than having tax deducted and then refunded later.

Q3. I am 75 years old and a super senior citizen with income of Rs. 4 lakh from pension. Do I get the 87A rebate? Under the old regime, super senior citizens (80 years and above) have a basic exemption of Rs. 5 lakh, so no tax arises at all without needing the rebate. For citizens between 60-80, the basic exemption is Rs. 3 lakh under the old regime, and the 87A rebate of Rs. 12,500 covers any tax up to Rs. 5 lakh income. Under the new regime, the basic exemption is Rs. 3 lakh for all ages, but the 87A rebate covers full tax up to Rs. 12 lakh income.

Q4. I sold my ancestral residential property this year and made an LTCG of Rs. 8 lakh. My total income is Rs. 9 lakh. Is my LTCG from property eligible for the 87A rebate? LTCG on immovable property (land and buildings) is taxed at 12.5% under Section 112 (without indexation, post July 2024). Unlike equity LTCG (Section 112A), LTCG on property under Section 112 is not specifically listed as excluded from Section 87A in the law. Historically, the CBDT's position and portal behaviour suggest that 87A applies to slab-rate income and not special-rate income. Since LTCG on property is taxed at a special flat rate (12.5%), the rebate may not apply to this portion. This is an area of ongoing interpretation -- consult a CA for your specific computation.

Q5. My income under the new regime is Rs. 12 lakh exactly. The portal is showing a small amount of tax even after the rebate. Is that an error? This typically happens when a small amount of income -- such as interest on income tax refund or a minor special rate income item -- pushes your total income marginally above Rs. 12 lakh, or when the system computes cess before fully applying the rebate. Review the tax computation sheet carefully. If the total income is exactly Rs. 12 lakh or below and all income is at slab rates, the rebate should reduce tax to exactly zero. Raise a feedback/grievance on the portal if the error persists after correctly entering all income details.
