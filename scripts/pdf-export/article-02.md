## Difference Between Assessment Year (AY) and Financial Year (FY)

### A Confusion That Trips Up Almost Every First-Time Filer

Every April, millions of Indians look at their tax filing software and pause at two dropdowns: Financial Year and Assessment Year. Most people take a guess, occasionally pick the wrong one, and either get a mismatch notice or simply lose track of which return they filed for which period. This confusion is preventable. The difference between AY and FY is actually quite simple once you understand the logic behind it.

## What Is a Financial Year (FY)?

A Financial Year (FY) is the 12-month period during which you earn your income. In India, the financial year runs from April 1 to March 31 of the following year. So if you are working from April 2025 to March 2026, that period is called FY 2025-26.

Think of the Financial Year as the year you work and earn. All your salary slips, invoices, rent receipts, and bank interest statements for this period fall under this FY.

## What Is an Assessment Year (AY)?

An Assessment Year is the 12-month period that immediately follows the Financial Year. It is the year in which you evaluate, compute, and pay tax on the income you earned during the previous financial year. The Income Tax Department processes your return and assesses your liability during the Assessment Year.

So the income earned in FY 2025-26 (April 2025 to March 2026) is assessed and taxed in AY 2026-27 (April 2026 to March 2027).

## The Relationship Between FY and AY

| Activity | Financial Year (FY) | Assessment Year (AY) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Period | April 1 to March 31 | April 1 to March 31 (following year) |
| What Happens | You earn income | You file return and pay tax |
| Example Period | April 1, 2025 to March 31, 2026 | April 1, 2026 to March 31, 2027 |
| Common Name | Previous Year (in legal terminology) | Assessment Year |
| ITR Filing Deadline (typical) | No filing in this year | July 31 (for non-audit cases) |
| Budget Announcements | New tax slabs announced and applicable | Slabs from that budget are used to assess |

## Why the Income Tax Act Uses "Previous Year"

The Income Tax Act, 1961 does not use the term "Financial Year" -- it uses the term "Previous Year." Under Section 3, the previous year is defined as the financial year immediately preceding the assessment year. So what we commonly call FY 2025-26 is the "Previous Year" in the law's language, and AY 2026-27 is when it is assessed.

This is purely a legal convention. For practical purposes, FY = Previous Year = the year you earn. AY = the year you file and get assessed.

## Practical Example: How AY and FY Work Together

Kavya joined a software company in May 2025. Over the course of the year (May 2025 to March 2026), she earns Rs. 9 lakh in salary.

- FY 2025-26: This is when Kavya earned the Rs. 9 lakh
- AY 2026-27: This is when she files her ITR (by July 31, 2026) and pays any remaining tax

When Kavya logs into the ITR portal to file, she selects AY 2026-27. The system automatically knows she is reporting income earned in FY 2025-26.

## Where AY vs. FY Appears in Real Life

| Document / Process | Which Year Is Referenced |
| --- | --- |
| Form 16 issued by employer | Shows the FY for which salary is paid |
| ITR filing on e-filing portal | Always select the AY (e.g., AY 2026-27) |
| Income tax notice from department | Refers to the AY under scrutiny |
| Advance tax payment challan | Select the AY in which assessment will happen |
| TDS certificates (Form 16A) | Show FY of deduction |
| Tax audit report (Form 3CD) | References the previous year (FY) |
| Intimation under Section 143(1) | Refers to AY |
| Refund status on portal | Shows AY for which refund is being processed |

## Common Mistakes Caused by AY-FY Confusion

### Mistake 1: Selecting the Wrong AY While Filing

Filing under AY 2025-26 when you should be filing for AY 2026-27 means your return is filed for the wrong period. The department may treat this as a return filed for income that was already assessed (or a revised return for a closed year), leading to mismatches.

### Mistake 2: Paying Advance Tax Under Wrong AY

If you are liable to pay advance tax and you fill the challan with the wrong AY, your payment may not get credited correctly against your outstanding liability.

### Mistake 3: Confusing Budget Year with Tax Filing Year

The Union Budget for FY 2025-26 is presented in February 2025 and announces tax slabs effective from April 1, 2025. These slabs apply to income earned in FY 2025-26 and are used when filing ITR in AY 2026-27. People sometimes confuse which year's budget applies to their filing.

## Important Notes

The last date to file an ITR for a given AY is July 31 (for individuals without audit). If you miss this, you can still file a belated return by December 31 of the same AY, but with a late fee under Section 234F. After December 31, the return for that AY cannot be filed unless special permission is granted.

Tax notices can be issued for up to 3 years from the end of the relevant AY in routine cases, and up to 10 years in cases involving income over Rs. 50 lakh that was escaped from assessment.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. My employer issued Form 16 for FY 2025-26. Which AY should I select when filing my ITR? You should select AY 2026-27. Form 16 always covers the financial year in which salary was paid. The ITR is filed in the immediately following year, which is the Assessment Year. So income shown in Form 16 for FY 2025-26 is always reported in AY 2026-27.

Q2. I received a tax notice mentioning AY 2023-24. Which period's income does it relate to? A notice for AY 2023-24 relates to income you earned during FY 2022-23 (April 1, 2022 to March 31, 2023). The department is assessing your tax liability for that earning period.

Q3. Can two different financial years overlap? For example, if my company follows a different financial year? For income tax purposes in India, the financial year is fixed: April 1 to March 31. This applies to all individual taxpayers, HUFs, firms, and most companies. However, companies incorporated outside India and certain other entities may have different accounting years -- but even they must report income to the Indian tax department based on the April-March financial year.

Q4. The advance tax instalments I need to pay -- which AY do I enter on the challan? When paying advance tax during FY 2025-26 (i.e., in June, September, December 2025 and March 2026), you must enter AY 2026-27 on the challan. The advance tax is being paid against the liability that will be finally assessed in AY 2026-27.

Q5. I want to revise my ITR that I filed for AY 2025-26. Until when can I do this? A revised return for AY 2025-26 can be filed up to December 31, 2025 (three months before the end of AY 2025-26, or before the assessment is completed, whichever is earlier). After that date, no further revision is permitted for that AY.
