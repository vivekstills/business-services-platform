## What is Form 26QB?

Form 26QB is the challan-cum-statement used by buyers of immovable property to deposit TDS deducted under Section 194IA with the government. Unlike regular TDS returns filed quarterly, Form 26QB is transaction-specific and must be filed separately for each property purchase.

It serves a dual purpose: it is both the payment challan (like Challan 281) and the TDS return in one. After successful filing and payment, the buyer can download Form 16B from TRACES as the TDS certificate for the seller.

---

## Key Details of Form 26QB

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Form Name | Form 26QB |
| Purpose | TDS payment and return for property purchase (Section 194IA) |
| Filed By | Buyer of immovable property |
| TDS Rate | 1% of sale consideration (or stamp duty value, whichever is higher) |
| Threshold | Property value above Rs. 50 lakhs |
| Payment Deadline | Within 30 days from end of month of deduction |
| TDS Certificate | Form 16B (generated from TRACES) |
| Portal | https://www.tin-nsdl.com or https://www.incometax.gov.in |

---

## Who Must File Form 26QB?

Any buyer purchasing immovable property (excluding agricultural land) for a consideration exceeding Rs. 50 lakhs from a resident seller must file Form 26QB. This applies to:
- Individual buyers (salaried or self-employed)
- Companies and LLPs
- HUFs
- Every co-buyer in a joint purchase (separate 26QB per buyer)

---

## Threshold and Rate Summary

| Condition | TDS Rate | Form Required |
|---|---|---|
| Property value above Rs. 50 lakhs, seller has PAN | 1% | 26QB |
| Property value above Rs. 50 lakhs, seller has no PAN | 20% | 26QB |
| Property value at or below Rs. 50 lakhs | Nil | No filing required |
| Seller is NRI | As per Section 195 | Different form/process |

## Step-by-Step Filing Process for Form 26QB

### Step 1: Access the Portal
Visit the TIN NSDL website (https://onlineservices.tin.egov-nsdl.com) or the new Income Tax portal. Navigate to TDS on Property (Form 26QB).

### Step 2: Fill in Buyer Details
Enter:
- Buyer's PAN
- Buyer's name, address, email, and phone number
- Category of buyer (individual, company, HUF, etc.)

### Step 3: Fill in Seller Details
Enter:
- Seller's PAN
- Seller's name and address
- If multiple sellers, all must be listed

### Step 4: Property Details
Enter:
- Complete address of the property
- Date of agreement or booking
- Total sale consideration
- Stamp duty value (if higher, this becomes the base for TDS)

### Step 5: TDS Computation
- Enter the amount of TDS deducted
- The portal auto-calculates based on inputs
- Verify the figure before proceeding

### Step 6: Payment
Select the payment mode:
- Net banking (most common)
- Over-the-counter at authorised bank branches

Generate the acknowledgement number once payment is successful.

### Step 7: Download Form 16B
After payment reflects (usually within 5 working days), log into TRACES (www.tdscpc.gov.in), navigate to "Downloads > Form 16B", and download the TDS certificate for the seller.

## Example Calculation

**Scenario:** Priya buys a commercial shop in Chennai for Rs. 62,00,000. The stamp duty value is Rs. 65,00,000. She pays Rs. 20,00,000 as first instalment.

**TDS on first instalment:**
Since the total consideration (Rs. 62 lakhs) or stamp duty value (Rs. 65 lakhs) exceeds Rs. 50 lakhs, TDS applies.

For installment-based payments, TDS is calculated on the amount being paid in each installment:
TDS on Rs. 20,00,000 = 1% = Rs. 20,000

Priya pays the builder: Rs. 20,00,000 - Rs. 20,000 = Rs. 19,80,000
She files Form 26QB and deposits Rs. 20,000 within 30 days from the end of that month.

## Common Errors in Form 26QB and How to Fix Them

| Error Type | How to Correct |
|---|---|
| Wrong PAN of buyer or seller | File a correction request on TRACES |
| Incorrect property address | File a correction on TRACES |
| Wrong amount entered | File a correction on TRACES with supporting documents |
| PAN not validated | Ensure PAN is active and not inoperative; link with Aadhaar |

> Correction requests on TRACES for Form 26QB require consent from both buyer and seller (through OTP verification), so ensure all communication is smooth.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Default | Penalty / Interest |
|---|---|
| Late deduction | Interest at 1% per month (Section 201(1A)) |
| Late deposit of TDS | Interest at 1.5% per month (Section 201(1A)) |
| Late filing of 26QB | Rs. 200 per day under Section 234E |
| Non-filing of 26QB | Penalty up to Rs. 1,00,000 under Section 271H |
| Failure to issue Form 16B | Penalty under Section 272A |

## FAQs

**Q1. Can Form 26QB be filed offline?**
Form 26QB is primarily an online filing. However, buyers without internet banking can visit designated bank branches (authorised by NSDL) with a filled physical form and pay over the counter. The bank generates the challan and forwards details electronically.

**Q2. What is the deadline for filing Form 26QB after each instalment payment?**
The due date is 30 days from the end of the month in which TDS was deducted. For example, if TDS is deducted in July, Form 26QB must be filed and TDS deposited by August 30.

**Q3. Do I need to file a fresh 26QB for each property payment instalment?**
Yes. A separate Form 26QB must be filed for each payment made to the seller. If a property has three instalments, three separate 26QB filings are required.

**Q4. How does the seller verify that the buyer has deposited TDS?**
The seller can log into their account on the Income Tax portal or TRACES and check Form 26AS. Once the buyer files 26QB and makes payment, it reflects in the seller's Form 26AS within a few days.

**Q5. What if the buyer and seller mutually decide not to deduct TDS?**
This does not exempt either party from the legal obligation. TDS under Section 194IA is a statutory requirement. If TDS is not deducted, the buyer becomes a defaulter under Section 201 and is liable to pay the TDS amount plus interest and penalties.
