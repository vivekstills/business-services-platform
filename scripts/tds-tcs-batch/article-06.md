## Overview of Section 194C

Section 194C of the Income Tax Act requires any person (other than an individual or HUF not subject to tax audit) to deduct TDS when making payments to contractors or sub-contractors for carrying out any work, including the supply of labour for carrying out any work.

This is one of the most frequently applied TDS provisions in business, covering everything from printing and advertising contracts to construction, catering, cleaning, and transportation.

---

## Who is a Contractor Under Section 194C?

The term "work" under Section 194C is broadly interpreted and includes:
- Advertising
- Broadcasting and telecasting
- Carriage of goods or passengers by any mode of transport (excluding railways)
- Catering
- Construction, repair, renovation, or demolition of any building or road
- Manufacturing or supplying a product according to customer's specifications (job work)

---

## Rate Table: TDS Under Section 194C

| Payee Type | TDS Rate |
|---|---|
| Individual or HUF contractor | 1% |
| Any other contractor (company, firm, LLP, etc.) | 2% |
| Transporter furnishing PAN and engaged in plying/hiring goods carriages | Nil (Section 194C(6)) |
| Contractor without PAN | 20% |

---

## Threshold Limits

| Condition | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Single payment to a contractor | Rs. 30,000 |
| Aggregate payments in a financial year | Rs. 1,00,000 |
| Either threshold crossed | TDS applicable from first payment |

If any single payment exceeds Rs. 30,000 or aggregate payments in the year cross Rs. 1,00,000, TDS applies on all payments (not just the excess).

---

## Applicability

### Who Must Deduct?
- Central and State Government bodies
- Local authorities
- Statutory corporations
- Companies
- Cooperative societies
- Trusts
- Firms
- Individuals and HUFs liable for tax audit under Section 44AB

### Who Is Exempt from Deducting?
Individuals and HUFs not subject to tax audit are exempt from deducting TDS under Section 194C. However, if they cross the turnover threshold triggering audit, the obligation kicks in from that year.

## Example Calculation

**Scenario:** Mumbai Builders Pvt. Ltd. engages three contractors during the year:

| Contractor | Nature | Amount Paid | Rate | TDS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ramesh (Individual) | Civil work | Rs. 5,00,000 | 1% | Rs. 5,000 |
| XYZ Constructions (Company) | Electrical work | Rs. 8,00,000 | 2% | Rs. 16,000 |
| Fast Freight (Transporter with PAN) | Goods transport | Rs. 2,00,000 | Nil | Nil |
| Shyam (No PAN) | Painting | Rs. 50,000 | 20% | Rs. 10,000 |

Total TDS to be deducted and deposited: Rs. 31,000

## Important Notes on Job Work vs. Contract for Goods

If a contractor manufactures a product as per the buyer's specifications using the buyer's material, it is considered job work and TDS under 194C applies. However, if the contractor supplies a standard product from stock without customisation, it may qualify as a sale of goods (no TDS applies). This distinction is frequently contested and must be evaluated case by case.

---

## Compliance Steps

1. Obtain TAN if not already done
2. Deduct TDS at the time of credit or payment, whichever is earlier
3. Deposit TDS using Challan 281 by the 7th of the following month
4. File quarterly TDS return in Form 26Q
5. Issue Form 16A to each contractor after each quarter (downloadable from TRACES)
6. Maintain records of contracts, invoices, and TDS challans

---

## Penalties

| Default | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Non-deduction | Interest at 1% per month + disallowance of 30% of expense under Section 40(a)(ia) |
| Non-deposit after deduction | Interest at 1.5% per month |
| Late filing of return | Rs. 200 per day under Section 234E |
| Non-filing | Penalty up to Rs. 1,00,000 under Section 271H |

## FAQs

**Q1. Is TDS applicable on reimbursements paid to contractors?**
Generally, reimbursements of actual expenses (supported by original bills) are not subject to TDS under Section 194C. However, if the reimbursement is a composite payment without separate billing for expenses, the entire amount may be subject to TDS. The CBDT has clarified that reimbursements strictly on actual cost basis, without profit element, are not subject to TDS.

**Q2. Does Section 194C apply to software development contracts?**
This is a grey area. If software is developed as per the client's specific requirements (custom development), it may qualify as "work" under Section 194C. If it is off-the-shelf software, it is treated as a sale of goods. Post-Finance Act 2012, payments for use of software may also attract TDS under Section 194J. Seek professional advice for specific situations.

**Q3. What is the nil TDS rate for transporters under Section 194C(6)?**
A transporter who is engaged in the business of plying, hiring, or leasing goods carriages and furnishes their PAN to the deductor is exempt from TDS under Section 194C. The deductor must report such payments in the TDS return with "Y" marked in the transporter column.

**Q4. Can sub-contractors also deduct TDS on payments to their sub-contractors?**
Yes. If a sub-contractor makes further payments for work to another sub-contractor, and the sub-contractor is otherwise liable to deduct TDS (i.e., they are subject to tax audit or are a company), they must also deduct TDS under Section 194C.

**Q5. Is GST included in the amount on which TDS is calculated under Section 194C?**
No. TDS under Section 194C (and most other sections) is calculated on the base amount excluding GST. The CBDT has clarified through circular that if GST is separately billed, it should be excluded from the TDS base. If the invoice does not separately bifurcate GST, TDS must be calculated on the gross amount.
