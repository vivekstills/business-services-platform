## What is Section 194T?

Section 194T is a relatively new provision, introduced by the Finance Act 2024 and effective from April 1, 2025. It requires a partnership firm (including a Limited Liability Partnership or LLP) to deduct TDS on certain payments made to its partners. This marks a significant shift in how partnership income is taxed at source, and firms that have not yet built this into their compliance systems need to act now.

Prior to Section 194T, payments to partners such as salary, remuneration, bonus, commission, and interest were not subject to TDS. Section 194T changes this landscape entirely.

---

## Payments Covered Under Section 194T

The following payments made by a firm or LLP to its partners are covered:
- Salary
- Remuneration
- Commission
- Bonus
- Interest on capital

---

## Rate Table: TDS Under Section 194T

| Nature of Payment | TDS Rate |
|---|---|
| Salary, remuneration, bonus, commission, interest to partner (with PAN) | 10% |
| Payment to partner without PAN | 20% |
| Payments below threshold | Nil |

---

## Threshold Limit

| Parameter | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Annual aggregate payments to a partner | Rs. 20,000 |
| Below this | No TDS required |

If the total of all covered payments to a single partner during the financial year exceeds Rs. 20,000, TDS at 10% is applicable on the entire amount (not just the excess).

---

## Applicability

Section 194T applies to:
- Partnership firms (registered or unregistered)
- Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs)
- Any association of persons treated as a firm for income tax purposes

It applies to payments made to:
- Working partners
- Non-working (sleeping) partners
- Managing partners

All partners, irrespective of their role, are covered if they receive any of the specified payments.

## Example Calculation

**Scenario:** Mehta & Associates LLP has two partners: Rajan and Seema.

Payments made during FY 2025-26:

| Partner | Salary | Interest on Capital | Commission | Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rajan | Rs. 2,40,000 | Rs. 60,000 | Rs. 30,000 | Rs. 3,30,000 |
| Seema | Rs. 1,80,000 | Rs. 40,000 | Rs. 15,000 | Rs. 2,35,000 |

TDS Calculation:
- Rajan: 10% of Rs. 3,30,000 = Rs. 33,000
- Seema: 10% of Rs. 2,35,000 = Rs. 23,500

Total TDS to be deposited by the firm: Rs. 56,500

---

## Why Was Section 194T Introduced?

Previously, partner remuneration and interest payments created a significant TDS leakage. A firm would deduct these as allowable expenses under Section 40(b), reducing its taxable profits, but the partner would declare this income only when filing their ITR, often months later. Section 194T plugs this gap by ensuring tax is collected upfront.

---

## Compliance Steps

1. Identify all payments made or credited to partners that fall under the specified categories
2. Ensure each partner has furnished a valid PAN
3. If aggregate payment to any partner in the year exceeds Rs. 20,000, deduct TDS at 10%
4. Deduct at the time of payment or credit, whichever is earlier
5. Deposit TDS by the 7th of the following month using Challan 281
6. File quarterly TDS return in Form 26Q (same return as other non-salary deductions)
7. Issue Form 16A to each partner quarterly

---

## Penalties

| Default | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Non-deduction | Interest at 1% per month + 30% disallowance under Section 40(a)(ia) |
| Late deposit after deduction | Interest at 1.5% per month |
| Late filing of 26Q | Rs. 200 per day under Section 234E |
| Non-filing | Penalty up to Rs. 1,00,000 under Section 271H |

## FAQs

**Q1. Does Section 194T apply to LLPs as well as traditional partnership firms?**
Yes. Section 194T explicitly covers both traditional partnership firms and LLPs. Any entity that pays salary, remuneration, bonus, commission, or interest on capital to its partners is covered, regardless of whether it is constituted as a traditional firm or an LLP.

**Q2. Is the Rs. 20,000 threshold applied per payment or in aggregate for the year?**
The threshold of Rs. 20,000 is the aggregate of all covered payments made to a single partner during the financial year. Once this aggregate is expected to exceed Rs. 20,000, TDS must be deducted from that point onwards. It is good practice to deduct from the first payment itself if annual payments will clearly exceed the threshold.

**Q3. Interest on capital credited in the books but not actually paid - does TDS apply?**
Yes. TDS under Section 194T applies at the earlier of payment or credit to the partner's account. If interest is credited to the partner's capital account in the firm's books, TDS must be deducted at that time, even if no cash changes hands.

**Q4. What if the firm's profits are insufficient to pay partners and the firm decides not to make any payment during the year?**
If no payment is made and nothing is credited to the partner's account during the year, TDS does not apply. However, once even a book credit is made (such as crediting interest to the partner's capital account), the TDS obligation is triggered.

**Q5. Can partners submit Form 15G to avoid TDS under Section 194T?**
No. Form 15G and Form 15H are applicable only for specific sections listed under Section 197A, which does not currently include Section 194T. As of FY 2025-26, partners cannot avoid TDS under Section 194T by submitting Form 15G or 15H. The only way to reduce TDS is through a certificate under Section 197 from the Assessing Officer.
