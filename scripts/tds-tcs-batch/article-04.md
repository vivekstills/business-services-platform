## What is Form 26Q?

Form 26Q is the quarterly TDS return that deductors must file for all non-salary payments made to resident Indians from which tax has been deducted at source. It covers a wide range of payment types, from professional fees and rent to commission, interest, and contractor payments.

Unlike Form 24Q (which is exclusively for salary TDS), Form 26Q consolidates multiple TDS transactions under various sections in a single quarterly return.

## Due Dates for Filing Form 26Q

| Quarter | Period | Due Date |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | April to June | July 31 |
| Q2 | July to September | October 31 |
| Q3 | October to December | January 31 |
| Q4 | January to March | May 31 |

---

## Applicability

Form 26Q must be filed by:
- Companies and LLPs
- Individuals and HUFs subject to tax audit
- Firms, trusts, and AOPs
- Government deductors (they file separately under Form 26QE or as applicable)

It applies to all deductors who have made non-salary payments to resident payees and deducted TDS under any of the sections listed above.

---

## Structure of Form 26Q

The return is divided into:

**Part I: Deductor Details**
- TAN, PAN, name, address
- Type of deductor (company, individual, government, etc.)

**Part II: Deductee-wise Payment and TDS Details**
For each deductee:
- PAN
- Name
- Nature of payment (section code)
- Amount paid or credited
- TDS deducted
- Surcharge and cess
- Date of payment and TDS deposit
- Challan details (BSR code, date, serial number)

## Example Calculation

**Scenario:** ABC Pvt. Ltd. makes the following payments in Q1 (April to June):

| Payee | Payment Type | Amount (Rs.) | Section | TDS Rate | TDS Amount (Rs.) |

| XYZ Consulting | Professional fee | 5,00,000 | 194J | 10% | 50,000 |
| Shri Ram Properties | Rent | 3,00,000 | 194I | 10% | 30,000 |
| Fast Courier | Transporter (with PAN) | 2,00,000 | 194C | 2% | 4,000 |
| Total | | 10,00,000 | | | 84,000 |

ABC Pvt. Ltd. deposits Rs. 84,000 and then files Form 26Q for Q1 by July 31, covering all three deductees in a single return.

## How to File Form 26Q

### Step 1: Prepare FVU File
Download the Return Preparation Utility (RPU) from the NSDL TIN website. Use it to enter all deductee details, challan details, and payment information. The software validates entries and generates an FVU (File Validation Utility) file.

### Step 2: Validate the File
Run the generated file through the FVU (also available on TIN NSDL) to check for errors. Address any errors flagged.

### Step 3: Submit on TRACES or TIN FC
File the return at a TIN Facilitation Centre (TIN-FC) or directly on the TRACES portal (if registered as a deductor).

### Step 4: Generate Provisional Receipt
After successful filing, you receive a Provisional Receipt Number (PRN), also called the Acknowledgement Number. Retain this for records.

### Step 5: Issue TDS Certificates
After filing, generate Form 16A for each deductee from the TRACES portal. Form 16A must be issued quarterly.

## FAQs

**Q1. Can a deductor file Form 26Q without depositing TDS?**
No. The challan details (date of deposit, BSR code, challan serial number, and amount) are mandatory fields in Form 26Q. Without actual deposit of TDS, the return cannot be successfully filed.

**Q2. What if Form 26Q is filed with incorrect PAN of a deductee?**
If an incorrect PAN is reported, the credit will not appear in the deductee's Form 26AS, causing problems when they file their ITR. A correction return must be filed through TRACES to update the PAN. This also attracts penalty for furnishing incorrect information.

**Q3. Is it mandatory to file Form 26Q even if TDS amount is zero in a quarter?**
If no TDS transactions took place in a quarter, generally a nil return is not mandatory. However, once a deductor has been registered and has filed even one return, it is good practice (and may be required by the system) to file for subsequent quarters to avoid notices.

**Q4. What is the difference between Form 26Q and Form 27Q?**
Form 26Q covers TDS on non-salary payments to resident Indians. Form 27Q covers TDS on payments made to Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and foreign companies. The sections, rates, and compliance requirements differ significantly for NRI payments.

**Q5. Can a correction return be filed for Form 26Q after original submission?**
Yes. Correction returns can be filed through the TRACES portal. There are different types of corrections available: C1 (deductor details), C2 (challan details), C3 (deductee details), C4 (addition of salary detail), C5 (PAN update). The number of corrections allowed may vary based on the type.
