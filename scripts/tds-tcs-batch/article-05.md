## What is Form 24Q?

Form 24Q is the quarterly TDS return filed by employers for tax deducted from employees' salaries. It is the primary compliance document under Section 192 of the Income Tax Act and forms the basis for the salary TDS certificates (Form 16) issued to employees at the end of each financial year.

Every employer, from a small proprietorship to a multinational corporation, is required to file Form 24Q if it has deducted tax from employee salaries during the quarter.

---

## Key Features of Form 24Q

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Applicable Section | Section 192 (TDS on salary) |
| Filed By | Employers / Deductors |
| Frequency | Quarterly |
| Covers | All salaried employees |
| TDS Certificate Generated | Form 16 (Part A from TRACES, Part B by employer) |
| Annexure | Annexure I (Q1 to Q3); Annexure I + II (Q4) |

---

## Structure of Form 24Q

Form 24Q has two components:

**Annexure I:** Employee-wise details for each quarter covering:
- Employee PAN and name
- Salary paid in the quarter
- TDS deducted and deposited
- Challan details

**Annexure II (only in Q4):** Detailed salary breakup including:
- Gross salary
- Allowances (HRA, LTA, etc.)
- Deductions under Chapter VI-A (80C, 80D, etc.)
- Net taxable income
- Tax computed
- TDS deducted
- Relief under Section 89 (if any)

---

## TDS Rate Under Section 192

Unlike other TDS sections, Section 192 does not have a fixed percentage rate. TDS on salary is calculated based on the **applicable income tax slab rates** for the financial year, considering:

| Income Slab (FY 2024-25, Old Regime) | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to Rs. 2,50,000 | Nil |
| Rs. 2,50,001 to Rs. 5,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs. 5,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above Rs. 10,00,000 | 30% |

Surcharge, health and education cess (4%) are added as applicable.

| Income Slab (FY 2024-25, New Regime) | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to Rs. 3,00,000 | Nil |
| Rs. 3,00,001 to Rs. 7,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs. 7,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000 | 10% |
| Rs. 10,00,001 to Rs. 12,00,000 | 15% |
| Rs. 12,00,001 to Rs. 15,00,000 | 20% |
| Above Rs. 15,00,000 | 30% |

## Example Calculation

**Scenario:** An employee, Kavitha, has the following details for FY 2024-25:

| Component | Amount (Rs.) |
|---|---|
| Basic Salary | 8,40,000 |
| HRA Received | 1,80,000 |
| HRA Exempt (as computed) | 90,000 |
| Standard Deduction | 75,000 |
| 80C Investments | 1,50,000 |
| Gross Taxable Salary | 8,40,000 + 1,80,000 - 90,000 - 75,000 = 9,55,000 |
| Less: 80C | 1,50,000 |
| Net Taxable Income | 8,05,000 |

Tax under old regime:
- Up to Rs. 2.5 lakh: Nil
- Rs. 2.5 to Rs. 5 lakh: 5% of Rs. 2.5 lakh = Rs. 12,500
- Rs. 5 lakh to Rs. 8.05 lakh: 20% of Rs. 3.05 lakh = Rs. 61,000
- Total tax = Rs. 73,500
- Health and education cess (4%): Rs. 2,940
- Total tax payable = Rs. 76,440

Monthly TDS = Rs. 76,440 / 12 = Rs. 6,370 per month

This amount is deducted by the employer monthly and reported in Form 24Q.

## Compliance Steps for Employers

**Step 1: Collect Declarations from Employees**
At the start of the year, collect investment declarations (Form 12BB) from all employees to estimate their deductions and compute projected TDS.

**Step 2: Compute Monthly TDS**
Based on projected annual income and declared deductions, divide the annual tax liability by 12 and deduct monthly.

**Step 3: Deposit TDS**
Deposit TDS by the 7th of the following month (for March: by April 30).

**Step 4: Prepare and File Form 24Q**
Use the RPU from NSDL and file Form 24Q quarterly. For Q4, include Annexure II with the final breakup.

**Step 5: Issue Form 16**
After Q4 return is filed and accepted, download Form 16 Part A from TRACES. Prepare Part B (salary breakup) and issue the combined Form 16 to employees by June 15 each year.

## Penalties

| Default | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Late filing of 24Q | Rs. 200 per day under Section 234E |
| Non-filing | Penalty Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 1,00,000 under Section 271H |
| Wrong PAN or PAN not quoted | Penalty under Section 272B |
| Non-issuance of Form 16 | Penalty Rs. 100 per day under Section 272A |
| Short deduction of TDS | Interest at 1% per month on short-deducted amount |

## FAQs

**Q1. Does a small employer with just two employees need to file Form 24Q?**
Yes. There is no minimum headcount exemption. Any employer who deducts TDS from employee salaries must file Form 24Q, regardless of the number of employees. However, if no TDS was deducted because all employees fall below the taxable threshold, filing a nil return is generally not required.

**Q2. What is the difference between Form 16 Part A and Part B?**
Form 16 Part A is the TDS certificate generated from TRACES, reflecting the TDS actually deposited with the government. Part B is prepared by the employer and contains a detailed breakup of salary, exemptions, deductions, and tax computation. Both parts together constitute the complete Form 16.

**Q3. What if an employee switches jobs mid-year? How does the new employer handle TDS?**
The new employer must ask for Form 12B from the incoming employee, which contains details of salary and TDS from the previous employer. The new employer must consider this while computing TDS for the remainder of the year to avoid over- or under-deduction.

**Q4. Can the employer deduct TDS at a lower rate based on an employee's request?**
Yes, but only if the employee furnishes a certificate under Section 197 from the Assessing Officer authorising lower or nil deduction. Alternatively, an employee can declare tax-saving investments under Form 12BB, which reduces the taxable salary and consequently the TDS. However, the employer cannot arbitrarily reduce TDS without adequate basis.

**Q5. What happens if TDS is over-deducted from an employee's salary?**
If TDS is over-deducted, the employee can claim a refund while filing their Income Tax Return. In the interim, the employer can adjust over-deducted TDS in subsequent months within the same financial year to reduce monthly deductions, bringing the total annual deduction in line with actual tax liability.
