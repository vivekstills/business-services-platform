## Introduction to Section 194J

Section 194J is one of the most critical TDS provisions for businesses that engage professionals such as doctors, lawyers, chartered accountants, architects, engineers, and management consultants. It was also extended to cover fees for technical services and royalties.

The section underwent a significant amendment via the Finance Act 2020, splitting the previously uniform 10% rate into two different rates based on the nature of the service.

---

## Who Qualifies as a Professional Under Section 194J?

The term "professional" refers to persons engaged in specific professions as notified:
- Legal practitioners
- Medical practitioners
- Engineers and architects
- Accountants (CA, CMA, CS)
- Advertising professionals
- Interior decorators
- Technical consultants
- Any other profession notified by the Board

---

## Rate Table: TDS Under Section 194J (Post Finance Act 2020)

| Nature of Payment | TDS Rate |
|---|---|
| Fees for technical services (FTS) | 2% |
| Fees for professional services | 10% |
| Royalty for sale / distribution / exhibition of cinematographic films | 2% |
| Royalty (other than above) | 10% |
| Non-compete fees | 10% |
| Payee without PAN | 20% |
| Call centre services | 2% |

---

## Threshold Limits

| Type of Payment | Annual Threshold |
|---|---|
| Professional fees | Rs. 30,000 per financial year |
| Technical services fees | Rs. 30,000 per financial year |
| Royalty | Rs. 30,000 per financial year |
| Non-compete fees | Rs. 30,000 per financial year |
| Director's fees (non-salary) | No threshold; TDS on every payment |

---

## Applicability

### Who Must Deduct TDS Under 194J?
- All persons other than individuals and HUFs not covered by tax audit
- Individuals and HUFs subject to tax audit under Section 44AB

### Exception:
Individuals and HUFs making payments exclusively for personal purposes are not required to deduct TDS. For example, hiring a plumber for home repair at Rs. 50,000 does not attract 194J for a non-audit individual.

---

## Key Distinction: Technical Services vs. Professional Services

| Category | Nature | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Technical Services | Managerial, technical, consultancy services requiring skill and specialisation but not requiring a licence/degree for practice | 2% |
| Professional Services | Services in notified professions (doctor, lawyer, CA, engineer, etc.) | 10% |

**Example:** Hiring a software company to write code is "technical service" (2%), while hiring a CA firm for tax advisory is "professional service" (10%).

## Example Calculation

**Scenario:** Sunshine Ltd. makes the following payments in Q2:

| Payee | Service | Amount (Rs.) | Section | Rate | TDS (Rs.) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Kapoor & Co. (CA firm) | Statutory audit | 2,00,000 | 194J | 10% | 20,000 |
| TechSolve Pvt. Ltd. | IT support services | 5,00,000 | 194J | 2% | 10,000 |
| Dr. Sharma (Company doctor) | Medical services | 80,000 | 194J | 10% | 8,000 |
| BroadcastPro | Film exhibition royalty | 1,20,000 | 194J | 2% | 2,400 |

Total TDS = Rs. 40,400

---

## Compliance Steps

1. Verify whether the payment qualifies as professional or technical service
2. Apply the correct rate (2% or 10%)
3. Deduct TDS at the time of payment or credit, whichever is earlier
4. Deposit TDS by the 7th of the following month using Challan 281
5. File quarterly return in Form 26Q
6. Issue Form 16A to the service provider after each quarter

---

## Penalties

| Default | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Non-deduction | 1% interest per month + 30% expense disallowance |
| Late deposit | 1.5% interest per month |
| Late / non-filing of 26Q | Rs. 200 per day (Section 234E); penalty up to Rs. 1 lakh |
| Wrong rate applied | May be treated as short deduction; interest applies |

## FAQs

**Q1. A company pays Rs. 25,000 to a lawyer for a one-time matter. Is TDS required?**
No. The threshold for professional fees under Section 194J is Rs. 30,000 per year. A one-time payment of Rs. 25,000 falls below this limit. However, if additional payments to the same lawyer during the year take the aggregate above Rs. 30,000, TDS must be deducted from the next payment (and technically also retroactively applied from the first payment, which may require adjustment).

**Q2. How is TDS treated when a professional charges GST separately?**
TDS under Section 194J is calculated on the base service fee, excluding GST. If the invoice clearly separates the professional fee and GST, TDS is applied only on the fee portion. If the invoice does not bifurcate GST, TDS may apply on the full amount.

**Q3. Does 194J apply to payments made to non-resident professionals?**
No. Section 194J specifically applies to payments made to residents. Payments to non-residents for professional services attract TDS under Section 195, often at rates prescribed by the applicable Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement (DTAA).

**Q4. If a company director provides consultancy services (not as a director but as an independent professional), which section applies?**
If the director is being paid for independent professional services outside their role as director, Section 194J applies at 10%. Payments made to directors in their capacity as directors (sitting fees, commission) are covered separately. However, any salary paid to a whole-time director is covered under Section 192.

**Q5. What is the difference between royalty under Section 194J and Section 194EE?**
Royalty under Section 194J (at 10%) covers payments for the right to use patents, copyrights, literary or artistic works, models, designs, formulae, or processes. Section 194EE pertains to withdrawals from National Savings Scheme accounts. The two are unrelated; the term royalty in 194EE is not used in its traditional sense.
