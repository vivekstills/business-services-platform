## What is Section 194A?

Section 194A governs tax deduction at source on interest income, excluding interest on securities (which is separately covered under Section 193). In practice, Section 194A is most relevant for interest paid by banks, financial institutions, co-operative societies, and businesses to individuals on deposits, loans, and advances.

This section is particularly important for banks, NBFCs, and companies that routinely pay interest on fixed deposits, savings accounts, recurring deposits, and inter-company loans.

---

## Rate Table: TDS Under Section 194A

| Payer | Payee | TDS Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Banking company / cooperative bank / post office | Individual (interest on FD, savings, etc.) | 10% |
| Any other payer (company, firm, individual in business) | Resident individual or entity | 10% |
| Any payer | Payee without PAN | 20% |
| Senior citizens (above 75 years, only bank interest) | Exempt from TDS if 194P conditions met | Nil |

---

## Threshold Limits

| Payer Type | Annual Interest Threshold |
|---|---|
| Banking company, co-operative bank, post office | Rs. 40,000 (Rs. 50,000 for senior citizens) |
| Any other payer | Rs. 5,000 |

If interest in the financial year does not exceed the threshold, no TDS is deducted.

> Important: The threshold for banking institutions was revised to Rs. 40,000 (from Rs. 10,000) to reduce TDS burden on small depositors. For senior citizens, the threshold is Rs. 50,000.

---

## Who Must Deduct TDS Under 194A?

- Banks and cooperative banks
- Post offices
- NBFCs (Non-Banking Financial Companies)
- Companies paying interest on inter-corporate deposits
- Firms and LLPs paying interest to partners (under specific conditions)
- Individuals and HUFs paying interest in the course of business (if subject to tax audit)

---

## Exemptions Under Section 194A

No TDS is deducted in the following cases:
- Interest credited to savings account is below the threshold
- Interest paid by a firm to its own partners
- Interest on loans between sister concerns or companies within a group if covered by exemption notification
- Interest paid under Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal (MACT) awards (exempted through court interpretations)
- Interest paid on compensation awarded by MACT (as per CBDT circulars)

## Example Calculation

**Scenario 1: Bank FD**
Geeta, aged 45, holds a fixed deposit of Rs. 5,00,000 in SBI at 7% per annum. Annual interest = Rs. 35,000.
Since Rs. 35,000 is below the Rs. 40,000 threshold for banks, no TDS is deducted.

**Scenario 2: Same FD with higher balance**
Geeta increases her FD to Rs. 7,00,000. Annual interest = Rs. 49,000.
Since Rs. 49,000 exceeds Rs. 40,000, TDS applies on the entire Rs. 49,000:
TDS = 10% of Rs. 49,000 = Rs. 4,900

**Scenario 3: Inter-company loan**
Prestige Pvt. Ltd. lends Rs. 20,00,000 to another company at 12% per annum.
Annual interest = Rs. 2,40,000.
Since Rs. 2,40,000 exceeds Rs. 5,000, TDS applies:
TDS = 10% of Rs. 2,40,000 = Rs. 24,000

## Form 15G and 15H: Avoiding TDS on Interest

If a person's total income is below the taxable threshold, they can submit:
- **Form 15G**: For individuals below 60 years of age
- **Form 15H**: For senior citizens (above 60)

Upon submission, the payer (bank or institution) is not required to deduct TDS. These forms must be submitted at the beginning of each financial year.

---

## Compliance Steps

1. Check if interest payment exceeds the applicable threshold
2. Obtain PAN from the payee; if not, apply 20% TDS
3. Accept Form 15G / 15H if submitted and eligible
4. Deduct TDS at 10% at the time of credit or payment
5. Deposit using Challan 281 by the 7th of the next month
6. File quarterly TDS return in Form 26Q
7. Issue Form 16A to payees quarterly

---

## Penalties

| Default | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Non-deduction of TDS | Interest at 1% per month from date TDS was deductible |
| Late deposit | Interest at 1.5% per month |
| Non-acceptance of 15G/15H and deducting TDS | May create hardship for payee; no direct penalty but leads to ITR refund processing delays |
| Accepting forged 15G/15H | Payer may be held liable if they did not exercise due diligence |

## FAQs

**Q1. If interest is credited quarterly in a bank account, is TDS calculated quarterly or annually?**
TDS is deducted at the time of credit, but banks typically compute the annual interest accrual and apply the threshold on an annual basis. They deduct TDS when crediting interest if the year-to-date total exceeds the threshold. You will generally not see TDS deducted on every quarterly credit until the cumulative threshold is breached.

**Q2. A cooperative housing society charges interest on delayed maintenance payments from members. Does Section 194A apply?**
Section 194A covers cooperative banks and cooperative societies carrying on banking business. A cooperative housing society collecting maintenance-related interest is not typically covered under 194A unless it qualifies as a cooperative bank. The interest paid by a business entity to the society may, however, be a different matter depending on the nature of the transaction.

**Q3. Does Section 194A apply to interest credited to the account of a minor?**
Yes. The TDS provision applies regardless of the age of the recipient. Interest credited to a minor's fixed deposit account is subject to TDS if it exceeds the threshold. However, the minor's income is clubbed with the parent's income for tax purposes, and any TDS is credited to the minor's PAN. The parent can claim the TDS credit in their return by clubbing the income.

**Q4. Is TDS deducted on interest on the employee provident fund?**
No. Interest credited to a recognised Provident Fund account is exempt from TDS under the Income Tax Act, provided the fund is a recognised one and the employee has served for at least 5 years continuously (or has left service due to ill health, employer's business closure, etc.).

**Q5. What is the impact of linking PAN with Aadhaar on TDS under 194A?**
From July 1, 2023, if the PAN of the payee is inoperative (not linked with Aadhaar), TDS applies at the higher rate of 20% instead of 10%. This has incentivised individuals to link PAN and Aadhaar to avoid higher TDS on their interest income.
