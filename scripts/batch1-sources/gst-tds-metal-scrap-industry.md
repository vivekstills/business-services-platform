# TDS Under GST for the Metal Scrap Industry

From October 10, 2024, the government mandated TDS under GST specifically for the metal scrap industry to curb rampant tax evasion. Registered buyers of metal scrap must deduct 2% TDS (1% CGST + 1% SGST or 2% IGST) at the time of payment and file GSTR-7. This article explains the legal basis, who must deduct, registration requirements, GSTR-7 filing, and the full compliance framework with worked examples.

## Background: Why Metal Scrap Was Specifically Targeted

The metal scrap industry in India is one of the most fragmented and unorganised sectors of the economy. The supply chain involves primary scrap generators (factories, dismantlers, construction sites), aggregators, dealers, and recycling manufacturers. This complex, multi-layered chain has historically been fertile ground for tax evasion - through fake invoices, suppression of turnover, circular trading, and misclassification of goods.

Large volumes of cash transactions, the absence of documentation for small lots, and widespread registration gaps allowed significant GST revenue to escape the tax net. The government estimated that annual tax leakage from the metal scrap sector alone ran into thousands of crores. To address this, the GST Council, in its 54th meeting held on September 9, 2024, recommended extending the TDS mechanism under Section 51 of the CGST Act to cover all registered buyers of metal scrap - not just government entities.

Pursuant to this recommendation, the Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs (CBIC) issued Notification No. 25/2024-Central Tax dated October 9, 2024, making TDS under GST applicable with effect from October 10, 2024.

### Key Legal Reference

CBIC Notification No. 25/2024-Central Tax, dated October 9, 2024. Applicable from October 10, 2024. This notification amends the list of persons who are required to deduct TDS under Section 51 of the CGST Act, 2017 to include all registered persons purchasing metal scrap from registered suppliers.

## Who Must Deduct TDS - Scope of Applicability

Prior to this notification, TDS under GST under Section 51 was limited to government departments, local authorities, governmental agencies, and Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs). The amendment expanded this to include ALL registered persons in the context of metal scrap purchases - a significant widening of the compliance obligation.

| Buyer Category | TDS Obligation | Condition | Threshold |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Registered Manufacturers buying scrap | MUST deduct TDS | Supplier also registered | Contract > Rs. 2.5 lakh |
| Registered Traders / Dealers buying scrap | MUST deduct TDS | Supplier also registered | Contract > Rs. 2.5 lakh |
| Government entities buying scrap | MUST deduct TDS | Always applicable | Contract > Rs. 2.5 lakh |
| Unregistered buyers | NOT required | Not covered | N/A |
| Registered buyers from unregistered supplier | NOT applicable | Sec 51 covers registered-to-registered | N/A |

The Rs. 2.5 lakh threshold applies per contract per supplier. It is not a monthly or annual aggregate. Each distinct contract (or purchase order) for metal scrap is assessed independently. If a single transaction exceeds Rs. 2.5 lakh, TDS applies on the entire taxable value of that transaction. If the transaction is Rs. 2 lakh, no TDS applies even if cumulative purchases from the same supplier are higher.

## TDS Rate, HSN Chapters, and Tax Mechanics

The TDS rate under the metal scrap provision is 2% of the taxable value (excluding GST). In the case of intra-state supplies, this is split as 1% CGST and 1% SGST/UTGST. For inter-state supplies, it is 2% IGST. The TDS is not calculated on the gross invoice value (which includes GST) - it is computed only on the base taxable value.

| Transaction Type | TDS Rate | Breakdown | Example (Tax Value Rs. 5L) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Intra-State (within same state) | 2% | 1% CGST + 1% SGST | Rs. 5,000 + Rs. 5,000 = Rs. 10,000 |
| Inter-State (across states) | 2% | 2% IGST | Rs. 10,000 IGST |
| Union Territory supply | 2% | 1% CGST + 1% UTGST | Rs. 5,000 + Rs. 5,000 = Rs. 10,000 |

Metal scrap covered includes goods under HSN Chapter 72 (Iron and Steel scrap), Chapter 74 (Copper scrap), Chapter 75 (Nickel scrap), Chapter 76 (Aluminium scrap), Chapter 79 (Zinc scrap), Chapter 80 (Tin scrap), and other related metallic waste chapters. The key classification test is whether the goods constitute 'waste and residue' from manufacturing or processing of metals - not new/virgin metal.

## TDS Registration - A Separate Compliance Identity

Every registered buyer who falls within the TDS obligation must obtain a separate TDS registration under the GST law, distinct from their regular GSTIN for business. This is done by filing Form GST REG-07 on the GST portal (www.gst.gov.in). The TDS registration GSTIN will have '51' in positions 3-4 of the GSTIN, differentiating it from a regular business GSTIN.

This separate TDS GSTIN is used exclusively for TDS-related activities - filing GSTR-7, depositing TDS amounts, and issuing TDS certificates (GSTR-7A). It cannot be used for claiming input tax credit or for issuing tax invoices for regular business supply.

### Important

Failure to obtain TDS registration while being liable to deduct TDS attracts penalties under Section 122 of the CGST Act, 2017. The penalty can be up to Rs. 10,000 or the amount of tax evaded, whichever is higher. Buyers who commenced scrap purchases after October 10, 2024 must have applied for TDS registration before making the first purchase covered under this provision.

## GSTR-7: Filing Requirements and Due Dates

GSTR-7 is the monthly return to be filed by every person registered under the TDS provisions. It contains details of all TDS deductions made during the month - supplier GSTIN, invoice details, taxable value, and TDS amounts. Filing GSTR-7 is mandatory even for months where no TDS deduction was made, if the registration is active.

| Compliance Task | Form Mode | Due Date | Penalty for Default |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| TDS Deposit to Government | Challan via Electronic Cash Ledger | 7th of following month | Interest @ 18% p.a. + penalty |
| Monthly TDS Return Filing | GSTR-7 on GST portal | 10th of following month | Rs. 100/day (CGST) + Rs. 100/day (SGST), max Rs. 5,000 |
| TDS Certificate to Supplier | GSTR-7A (auto-generated) | 5 days after due date of GSTR-7 | Rs. 100/day per Act, max Rs. 5,000 |
| TDS Registration (one-time) | Form GST REG-07 | Before first covered purchase | Penalty under Sec 122 |

Once GSTR-7 is filed by the deductor (buyer), the TDS amounts appear in the supplier's GSTR-2B as auto-drafted credit. The supplier can see the TDS credit in their Electronic Cash Ledger and use it to pay their GST liability, or claim a refund if there is excess credit under Section 54.

### Pro Tip

If you are a metal scrap buyer with operations in multiple states, you may obtain a single centralised TDS registration at your principal place of business if you are a single legal entity. You do not need state-wise TDS registrations. This simplifies compliance significantly for multi-location manufacturers and traders.

## Worked Numerical Example

### Numerical Example - TDS on Metal Scrap Purchase

**Scenario:** Steel Forge Ltd. (Registered, Maharashtra) purchases iron scrap from Scrap King Enterprises (Registered, Maharashtra) - Intra-State Transaction

| Item | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Taxable Value of Scrap | Rs. 5,00,000 |
| GST @ 18% (CGST 9% + SGST 9%) | Rs. 90,000 |
| Total Invoice Value | Rs. 5,90,000 |

**TDS Calculation (on Taxable Value only):**

- CGST TDS @ 1% of Rs. 5,00,000: Rs. 5,000
- SGST TDS @ 1% of Rs. 5,00,000: Rs. 5,000
- **Total TDS to be Deducted:** Rs. 10,000

**Payment to Scrap King:** Rs. 5,80,000 (Total Invoice Rs. 5,90,000 LESS TDS Rs. 10,000)

Steel Forge deposits Rs. 10,000 to government by 7th of next month. Scrap King sees Rs. 10,000 TDS credit auto-populated in GSTR-2B. Scrap King uses this Rs. 10,000 to offset its GST liability.

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

| Default | Legal Consequence | Section | Quantum |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Failure to deduct TDS | Penalty equal to TDS not deducted | Sec 122(1)(vi) CGST | 100% of TDS amount |
| TDS deducted but not deposited | Interest + Penalty | Sec 50 + Sec 122 | 18% p.a. interest + equal penalty |
| Late filing of GSTR-7 | Late fee per day | Sec 47 | Rs. 100/day CGST + Rs. 100/day SGST |
| Incorrect details in GSTR-7 | Penalty for misstatement | Sec 122(1)(vii) | Upto Rs. 10,000 |
| Failure to issue TDS certificate | Late fee | Sec 122 | Rs. 100/day per Act |

## Key Takeaways

1. TDS under GST for metal scrap applies w.e.f. October 10, 2024 under CBIC Notification No. 25/2024-Central Tax.
2. Rate is 2% - split as 1% CGST + 1% SGST (intra-state) or 2% IGST (inter-state) on taxable value above Rs. 2.5 lakh per contract.
3. All registered buyers (not just government entities) are now liable - a significant expansion from the prior scope of Section 51.
4. Separate TDS registration via Form GST REG-07 is mandatory; regular GSTIN cannot be used for TDS purposes.
5. GSTR-7 must be filed by the 10th of the following month; TDS must be deposited by the 7th.
6. Supplier automatically receives credit in their Electronic Cash Ledger via GSTR-2B once buyer files GSTR-7.
7. Non-deduction or non-deposit attracts penalty equal to TDS amount plus interest at 18% per annum.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is TDS under GST applicable if the metal scrap supplier is unregistered under GST?

No. The TDS mechanism under Section 51 of the CGST Act applies only when both the buyer (deductor) and the supplier (deductee) are registered persons under GST. If the supplier is unregistered, the TDS under GST obligation does not apply to that transaction. However, the buyer may still have a Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM) obligation for purchases from unregistered suppliers under specific GST notifications, if applicable.

### Is TDS deducted on the full invoice amount including GST, or only on the base value?

TDS under GST is deducted only on the taxable value (base price), not on the GST component. If an invoice is for Rs. 5,00,000 taxable value plus Rs. 90,000 GST (total Rs. 5,90,000), TDS is 2% of Rs. 5,00,000 = Rs. 10,000 only. The GST amount is never included in the TDS calculation base. This is expressly provided in the statutory provision and departmental FAQs.

### Can the supplier (scrap seller) claim a refund if TDS credit exceeds their GST liability?

Yes. Under Section 54 of the CGST Act, if the TDS credit accumulated in the Electronic Cash Ledger of the supplier exceeds the GST payable for the month or year, the excess amount is refundable. The supplier must apply for a refund by filing Form RFD-01 on the GST portal. The refund is processed after verification of the GSTR-7 filed by the buyer matching the supplier's claim.

### What if a manufacturer has multiple small purchases from the same scrap dealer, each below Rs. 2.5 lakh?

The Rs. 2.5 lakh threshold applies per contract or per transaction, not on a cumulative basis. If each individual invoice or purchase order from the same dealer is below Rs. 2.5 lakh, TDS is technically not applicable on each such transaction even if annual purchases are much higher. However, if a single purchase order is structured across multiple invoices to avoid the threshold, this may be treated as tax avoidance and attract scrutiny.

### If both buyer and seller are outside India (say, a foreign buyer of Indian scrap), does TDS apply?

TDS under GST under Section 51 applies to supplies made within India (domestic supplies). If a foreign buyer (non-resident) is purchasing and exporting metal scrap from a registered Indian supplier, the transaction is treated as an export (zero-rated supply under GST). TDS under GST would not apply in this scenario as the foreign buyer is not a registered person under Indian GST law. However, normal export documentation and LUT/refund mechanisms apply to the Indian supplier.
