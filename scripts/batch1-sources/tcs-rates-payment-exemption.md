# Tax Collected at Source (TCS): Rates, Payment and Exemption

Tax Collected at Source (TCS) is a mechanism under the Income Tax Act where the seller collects a percentage of the sale consideration as tax from the buyer. Governed by Section 206C, TCS applies to specified goods - from alcoholic liquor and forest produce to motor vehicles and overseas remittances. This article provides a comprehensive analysis of all TCS provisions applicable for FY 2025-26, including the complete rate table, threshold limits, exemptions, Form 27EQ filing obligations, and how buyers claim credit in their ITR.

## What Is TCS? The Core Concept

Tax Collected at Source (TCS) is a tax collection mechanism introduced to widen the tax base and bring high-value, often cash-intensive transactions into the formal economy. Under TCS, the seller of specified goods or services is required to collect a prescribed percentage of the consideration from the buyer at the time of receipt of payment and deposit it with the government. The buyer gets credit for the TCS in their income tax return, making it essentially a pre-payment of tax collected on behalf of the buyer.

TCS was first introduced for goods like liquor, tendu leaves, and timber - sectors prone to unrecorded transactions. Over the years, its scope has expanded dramatically, covering motor vehicles (2016), overseas remittances (2020), tour packages, and sale of goods by large sellers (2020, under Section 206C(1H)).

## TCS vs TDS - Fundamental Differences

| Feature | TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) | TCS (Tax Collected at Source) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mechanism | Tax deducted by payer before making payment | Tax collected by seller on top of consideration |
| Who is liable | Payer (buyer / deductor) | Payee (seller / collector) |
| Trigger point | At time of credit or payment, whichever earlier | At time of receipt of payment from buyer |
| Primary sections | Sections 192 to 196D of Income Tax Act | Section 206C of Income Tax Act |
| Return form | Form 24Q (salary TDS), 26Q, 27Q | Form 27EQ (quarterly) |
| Certificate issued | Form 16 (salary), Form 16A (other TDS) | Form 27D (TCS certificate to buyer) |
| Credit in buyer's hands | Shown in Form 26AS Part A/A1 | Shown in Form 26AS Part B |
| Higher rate if no PAN | 20% or twice the rate, whichever higher | Twice the applicable rate (min 5%) |

### Budget 2025 Update on LRS TCS

The threshold of Rs. 7 lakh per financial year for LRS remittances continues for FY 2025-26. The 20% rate on non-educational, non-medical LRS remittances (applicable since October 1, 2023) remains unchanged. Banks and authorised forex dealers are responsible for collecting TCS at source on all qualifying LRS transactions.

## TCS Exemptions - Who Is Not Required to Pay TCS

Certain categories of buyers are statutorily exempt from TCS, either by nature of the buyer or through a declaration process:

| Exempt Category | Applicable TCS Provision | Basis of Exemption | Documentation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Central Government / State Government | All TCS provisions | Government cannot pay itself | By statutory provision |
| Embassies and Consulates | All TCS provisions | Diplomatic immunity / treaties | By statutory provision |
| Buyers using goods for manufacturing (not for trading) | Section 206C(1H) only | Anti-cascading principle | Form 27C declaration to seller |
| Buyers who have suffered TDS on the same transaction | Section 206C(1H) only | Prevent double taxation | Automatic by Sec 206C(1H) proviso |
| Importers (goods imported into India) | Section 206C(1H) only | Applies only to domestic sales | By nature of transaction |
| Buyers holding lower TCS certificate from AO | All TCS provisions | Section 206C(9) | Certificate under Sec 206C(9) |

### Important

The Form 27C exemption (for buyers using goods for manufacturing) under Section 206C(1H) is NOT available if the buyer is a trading entity that will resell the goods. If a buyer incorrectly claims manufacturing exemption when they are actually a trader, the seller is still liable for the uncollected TCS. Sellers must exercise due diligence before accepting Form 27C declarations.

## Section 206C(1H) - TCS on Sale of Goods (High-Value)

Section 206C(1H), introduced from October 1, 2020, is the most broadly applicable TCS provision for regular businesses. It mandates that a seller whose total sales, gross receipts, or turnover from business exceeds Rs. 10 crore in the immediately preceding financial year must collect TCS @ 0.1% from buyers when the aggregate sale consideration received from that buyer in the current financial year exceeds Rs. 50 lakh.

The TCS is on the amount exceeding Rs. 50 lakh - the first Rs. 50 lakh in a year from any buyer is exempt. The rate doubles to 1% if the buyer does not furnish PAN or Aadhaar. This provision is applicable on a per-seller, per-buyer, per-year basis.

### Example - Section 206C(1H)

**ABC Traders** - Turnover in FY 2024-25: Rs. 15 crore (qualifies as TCS collector in FY 2025-26)

Total sales to XYZ Pvt. Ltd. in FY 2025-26: Rs. 80 lakh

| | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Exempt threshold | Rs. 50,00,000 |
| Amount subject to TCS | Rs. 30,00,000 |
| TCS @ 0.1% on Rs. 30 lakh | Rs. 30,000 |

If XYZ provides its PAN: TCS = Rs. 30,000

If XYZ does NOT provide PAN: TCS = Rs. 60,000 (@ 1%)

ABC Traders deposits Rs. 30,000 by 7th of next month. XYZ Pvt. Ltd. claims Rs. 30,000 credit in its ITR.

## TCS Compliance Calendar - Form 27EQ and Certificates

| Quarter | Period Covered | Form 27EQ Due Date | Certificate (Form 27D) Due Date |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Q1 | April - June | July 15 | Within 15 days of filing (by July 30) |
| Q2 | July - September | October 15 | Within 15 days of filing (by Oct 30) |
| Q3 | October - December | January 15 | Within 15 days of filing (by Jan 30) |
| Q4 | January - March | May 15 | Within 15 days of filing (by May 30) |

TCS must be deposited to the government by the 7th of the month following the month of collection (monthly deposit). Form 27EQ is filed quarterly on the dates above. Form 27D (TCS Certificate) is auto-generated and available to buyers within 15 days of the Form 27EQ due date.

## How Buyers Claim TCS Credit in Their ITR

For individual or business buyers, TCS collected by the seller automatically reflects in their Form 26AS (Part B) and Annual Information Statement (AIS). The IT portal pre-fills this data when the buyer files their ITR. The buyer simply needs to verify the pre-filled TCS amount in the Tax Paid schedule and confirm it before submitting the return.

The TCS credit is set off against the total tax payable for the year. If after adjusting TDS, TCS, and advance tax, there is still an excess credit, the amount is refunded by the Income Tax Department. There is no need to separately apply for TCS credit - the pre-filling mechanism handles this automatically.

### Pro Tip

Salaried employees who remitted money abroad under LRS or bought a car above Rs. 10 lakh during the year must check their AIS and Form 26AS before filing their ITR. TCS amounts are often omitted during manual ITR preparation, causing excess tax payment or missed credit. Always reconcile your Form 26AS before finalising tax computation.

## Key Takeaways

1. TCS is collected by the seller from the buyer - the reverse of TDS which is deducted by the payer.
2. The LRS TCS rate of 20% on non-educational, non-medical remittances above Rs. 7 lakh per year continues in FY 2025-26.
3. Section 206C(1H) mandates TCS @ 0.1% on sale of goods exceeding Rs. 50 lakh per buyer when seller's turnover exceeds Rs. 10 crore.
4. Quarterly Form 27EQ must be filed by 15th of July, October, January, and May - late filing attracts Rs. 200/day penalty.
5. Buyers receive TCS credit in Form 26AS (Part B) and AIS; it is automatically pre-filled in ITR and set off against tax liability.
6. Government entities and manufacturing buyers (with Form 27C) are exempt from TCS under Section 206C(1H).
7. Non-collection of TCS attracts penalty equal to the uncollected amount; non-deposit attracts 18% interest plus penalty.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### If TDS has already been deducted on a transaction, can TCS also be levied on the same transaction?

Under Section 206C(1H) specifically, there is a statutory carve-out: TCS is not applicable if TDS has been deducted on the same transaction under any provision of the Income Tax Act. This prevents double taxation. The seller must verify from the buyer whether TDS has been deducted. If the buyer confirms TDS deduction in writing, the seller is relieved of TCS collection. For other TCS provisions (scrap, minerals, liquor, etc.), TDS does not override the TCS obligation.

### A car dealer sold a vehicle for Rs. 12 lakh. Is TCS applicable, and on what amount?

Yes. TCS under Section 206C(1F) applies when the value of a single motor vehicle exceeds Rs. 10 lakh. The TCS is charged on the entire consideration - Rs. 12 lakh - not just the excess above Rs. 10 lakh. TCS @ 1% on Rs. 12 lakh = Rs. 12,000. This amount is collected by the dealer from the buyer and deposited with the government. The buyer gets credit for Rs. 12,000 in their ITR.

### I remitted Rs. 15 lakh abroad under LRS for my child's education through a personal account (not an education loan). What is the TCS?

For LRS remittances for education that are NOT funded through an education loan from a financial institution, the TCS rate is 5% on the amount exceeding Rs. 7 lakh. First Rs. 7 lakh: exempt. Remaining Rs. 8 lakh at 5% = Rs. 40,000 TCS. If the remittance were funded by an education loan from a bank/NBFC, the rate would be just 0.5% on the same Rs. 8 lakh excess = Rs. 4,000. Always declare the source of funds to your bank to get the correct TCS treatment.

### My company's turnover last year was Rs. 8 crore. Do I need to collect TCS this year under Section 206C(1H)?

No. Section 206C(1H) applies only to sellers whose total sales, gross receipts, or turnover from business in the immediately preceding financial year exceeds Rs. 10 crore. Since your turnover was Rs. 8 crore, you do not qualify as a TCS collector under Section 206C(1H) for the current year, regardless of how much you sell this year. You would need to monitor this and start collecting TCS from the year following any year in which your turnover crosses Rs. 10 crore.

### What happens if a seller fails to collect TCS from the buyer?

If a seller fails to collect TCS, they are still liable to deposit the TCS amount with the government from their own funds. Under Section 206C(7), interest at 1% per month is charged from the date TCS was collectible to the date of actual deposit. Additionally, Section 271CA imposes a penalty equal to the amount of TCS that was not collected. There is also the risk of prosecution under Section 276BB for wilful failure to pay TCS to the government.
