# How to Make Tax Payment on the Income Tax Portal Without Logging In

You do not need to log in to the Income Tax portal to pay your taxes. Using the e-Pay Tax facility, you can make advance tax, self-assessment tax, TDS deposits, and other payments using Challan 280 or 281 directly. This guide provides a complete step-by-step walkthrough of the process, explains all available payment modes, how to correctly fill in assessment details, how to download the acknowledgment, and what to do if something goes wrong.

## Overview: The e-Pay Tax Facility

The Income Tax Department's portal (www.incometax.gov.in) offers a guest payment facility called 'e-Pay Tax' that allows taxpayers to make tax payments without a registered login ID or password. This facility is particularly useful in situations where a taxpayer needs to make an urgent payment but has not completed registration on the portal, wants to pay on behalf of another person, has a login that is locked, or simply wants to skip the login process for routine payments.

The authentication for this guest facility is done through OTP verification on the mobile number registered against the taxpayer's PAN or TAN in the Income Tax records. This ensures that only the actual taxpayer (or someone with access to their registered mobile) can initiate the payment, maintaining a basic security layer without requiring full portal login.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Paying Tax Without Login

### Step 1 - Navigate to e-Pay Tax

Open your web browser and visit www.incometax.gov.in (the official Income Tax of India portal). On the home page, look for the 'Quick Links' section. You will find the option 'e-Pay Tax' listed there. Alternatively, on the home page header, hover over the 'Our Services' tab and select 'e-Pay Tax' from the dropdown. This directly opens the guest payment gateway - no login required.

### Step 2 - Enter PAN and Verify Identity

On the e-Pay Tax page, enter your PAN (Permanent Account Number) in the first field. Re-confirm the PAN in the second field. Enter your mobile number registered with the Income Tax Department for this PAN. Click 'Continue'. The portal sends a 6-digit One-Time Password (OTP) to your registered mobile number. Enter the OTP within the stipulated time (usually 15 minutes) and click 'Continue' to proceed.

### Step 3 - Select Tax Payment Category

After OTP verification, you are taken to a dashboard showing various tax payment categories as tiles or cards. For regular income tax payments (advance tax, self-assessment tax), click on 'Income Tax'. For TDS/TCS deposits, select the appropriate category. Each tile clearly mentions the applicable challan number.

### Step 4 - Fill in Assessment Year and Payment Details

This is the most critical step - incorrect entries here will cause misapplication of your tax payment. Select the correct Assessment Year from the dropdown. For taxes relating to income of FY 2025-26 (April 2025 to March 2026), the Assessment Year is 2026-27. Then select the Type of Payment from the available options (Advance Tax, Self-Assessment Tax, Regular Assessment Tax, etc.).

On the next screen, fill in the individual tax amounts under the respective heads: Basic Tax, Surcharge, Health and Education Cess, Interest, Penalty, and Fees. You can calculate these amounts beforehand using the IT department's tax calculator or consult your CA. Enter the figures carefully and proceed.

### Step 5 - Choose Payment Mode

The portal offers multiple payment modes. Select the one most convenient for you. The available options are described in detail in the Payment Modes section below.

### Step 6 - Review Challan Summary and Confirm

A summary page displays all the details: PAN, Assessment Year, Type of Payment, Tax amounts, and Total amount payable. Carefully review every field before proceeding. Once you click 'Confirm', the challan is generated and you are redirected to your bank's payment gateway or given NEFT/RTGS details.

### Step 7 - Complete Payment and Download Counterfoil

Complete the payment on your bank's gateway. After successful payment, the portal generates a Challan Counterfoil containing the BSR Code (7-digit bank code), Challan Serial Number, Date of Deposit, and Amount Paid. Download and save this counterfoil immediately. This is your primary proof of tax payment and is required when filing your ITR.

### Important

Selecting the wrong Assessment Year is the single most common and costly error in tax payments. For taxes on FY 2025-26 income, the AY is 2026-27 - NOT 2025-26. Paying under the wrong AY causes the payment to be shown as credit in a different year, which may lead to demand notices for the actual year and refund claims for the incorrect year - a process that can take months to resolve.

## Payment Modes - Complete Comparison

| Payment Mode | Processing Time | Per Transaction Limit | Best For | Availability |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Net Banking | Instant | As per bank daily limit | Most taxpayers; fastest | 24x7 |
| Debit Card | Instant | As per daily card limit | Small to medium payments | 24x7 |
| UPI | Instant | Rs. 1 lakh per txn (NPCI cap) | Mobile-first users | 24x7 |
| RTGS (Real-Time Gross Settlement) | Same day within 30 min | Min Rs. 2 lakh, no upper limit | Large payments (> Rs. 2 lakh) | Bank working hours |
| NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer) | Same day (hourly batches) | No upper limit | Large payments when RTGS unavailable | Bank working hours |
| Pay at Bank Counter (Physical) | Same day (before 15:00 hrs) | No upper limit | Taxpayers without internet banking | Bank working hours only |

### Pro Tip

If you are paying a large advance tax installment (above Rs. 2 lakh), use RTGS for same-day guaranteed settlement. For the March 15 advance tax deadline especially, do not rely on NEFT or debit card as system congestion at banks on the last day can lead to delays in credit.

## Understanding the BSR Code and Challan Counterfoil

The BSR Code (Basic Statistical Return Code) is a 7-digit number assigned to each bank branch authorised to collect taxes. Along with the Challan Serial Number (typically 5 digits) and Date of Deposit, the BSR Code uniquely identifies your specific tax payment in the government's records. This combination is what the Income Tax Department uses to match your payment to your ITR.

When filing your ITR online, there is a schedule called 'Tax Paid' where you must enter these three details: BSR Code, Challan Serial Number, and Date of Deposit. The system validates these against the government's database. If any of these details are entered incorrectly in the ITR, the payment may not be matched, leading to outstanding demand notices.

## How to Verify Your Payment Was Credited

After making a tax payment, verify it has been correctly credited to the government's account before filing your ITR. There are two primary verification methods:

**Method 1 - Form 26AS:** Log in to the IT portal (www.incometax.gov.in) and navigate to Services, then View Form 26AS. Scroll to Part C (Taxes Paid / Collected). Your advance tax or self-assessment tax payment should appear here within 3 to 5 banking days of the payment date.

**Method 2 - NSDL Challan Status Enquiry:** Visit the NSDL TIN website (tin.tin.nsdl.com), click on 'Challan Status Enquiry', and enter the BSR Code, Challan Tender Date, Challan Serial Number, and Amount. This directly queries the government database and provides real-time status.

## Correcting Errors in a Filed Challan

If you discover an error in a challan after payment - such as wrong PAN, wrong Assessment Year, wrong head of payment (minor head), or wrong amount - a challan correction can be requested within specific time limits. For online payments, the IT portal allows corrections within 30 days of the payment date. For payments made at bank branches, the correction must be requested at the branch within 7 days.

Minor head correction (e.g., Advance Tax changed to Self-Assessment Tax) and Assessment Year correction are the most common. For PAN correction, you may need to visit the Assessing Officer. The Challan Correction Mechanism is available under the 'Challan Correction' option in the e-Pay Tax section.

## Key Takeaways

1. The e-Pay Tax facility at www.incometax.gov.in allows full tax payment without portal login - authenticated only via OTP on PAN-registered mobile.
2. Available for Advance Tax, Self-Assessment Tax, TDS (Challan 281), TCS, and other categories without requiring a login ID or password.
3. Correct Assessment Year selection is critical - AY 2026-27 applies for all taxes relating to FY 2025-26 income.
4. Save the Challan Counterfoil with BSR Code, Challan Serial Number, and Date of Deposit - these are mandatory fields in your ITR.
5. Payment reflects in Form 26AS within 3-5 banking days; always verify before filing ITR to avoid demand notices.
6. Challan corrections can be done within 30 days for online payments and within 7 days at bank branches for counter payments.
7. For large payments above Rs. 2 lakh, use RTGS for same-day guaranteed settlement, especially close to advance tax deadlines.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### My PAN is not linked to a mobile number. Can I still use e-Pay Tax without login?

The e-Pay Tax facility requires OTP verification on the mobile number registered against your PAN in the IT department's records. If no mobile is registered, you cannot use the guest payment route. You would need to either log in to the portal (if registered) or link your mobile number to your PAN first. Alternatively, you can visit an authorised bank branch with your challan form (physical Form 280) and PAN to pay over the counter without any digital authentication.

### Can I pay advance tax for someone else (e.g., my elderly parent) without their login?

Yes. You can use the e-Pay Tax facility using the other person's PAN and their PAN-registered mobile number for OTP verification. The challan will be generated in their PAN's name. This is completely legal and there is no restriction on who physically initiates the payment - what matters is that the correct PAN, Assessment Year, and tax head are entered. The challan counterfoil should be kept by the person whose PAN was used.

### I paid tax successfully but the portal shows 'Payment Pending'. What should I do?

If your bank has debited the amount but the IT portal shows 'Payment Pending' or 'Failed', first wait 24 hours as processing delays are common. Then check Form 26AS and your bank statement. If the bank account is debited but no counterfoil was generated and the amount does not appear in 26AS within 5 banking days, raise a complaint with your bank citing the transaction reference number. Banks are required to either generate the challan or reverse the debit within a reasonable period.

### Is there a maximum daily limit for tax payments through the e-Pay Tax facility?

The IT portal itself does not impose an upper daily limit on the total amount payable. However, individual payment modes have their own limits - UPI is capped at Rs. 1 lakh per transaction by NPCI, debit cards have bank-set daily limits, and net banking also has bank-imposed limits. For very large payments (crores), RTGS or NEFT are the appropriate modes as they have no upper limits and offer same-day credited settlement.

### If I pay advance tax in installments through multiple challans, do I need a separate counterfoil for each?

Yes. Each tax payment generates a separate challan with its own BSR Code, Challan Serial Number, and Date of Deposit. All these details must be entered in the Tax Paid schedule of your ITR - one row for each challan. There is no aggregation or linking of challans. The IT system validates each challan entry independently against its records, so maintaining all counterfoils is essential for accurate ITR filing.
