## Do Women Get Different Tax Slabs?

**No.** There is no separate income tax slab for women in India under the current Income Tax Act. Women and men are taxed identically under both the Old and New Tax Regimes.

> 📌 India had a separate (lower) basic exemption limit for women until FY 2011-12 (Rs. 1.9 lakh vs Rs. 1.6 lakh for men under the Old Regime). This was abolished in FY 2012-13. Since then, no gender-based distinction exists.

## Applicable Tax Slabs for Women — FY 2025-26

### New Tax Regime (Default)

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
| --- | --- |
| Up to Rs. 4,00,000 | Nil |
| Rs. 4,00,001 to Rs. 8,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs. 8,00,001 to Rs. 12,00,000 | 10% |
| Rs. 12,00,001 to Rs. 16,00,000 | 15% |
| Rs. 16,00,001 to Rs. 20,00,000 | 20% |
| Rs. 20,00,001 to Rs. 24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above Rs. 24,00,000 | 30% |

> ✅ **Section 87A rebate:** Zero tax if total income ≤ Rs. 12,00,000 (New Regime, FY 2025-26).

### Old Tax Regime

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
| --- | --- |
| Up to Rs. 2,50,000 | Nil |
| Rs. 2,50,001 to Rs. 5,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs. 5,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above Rs. 10,00,000 | 30% |

> ✅ Section 87A rebate under Old Regime: Zero tax if total income ≤ Rs. 5,00,000.

## Deductions Especially Relevant for Women

While no gender-specific tax benefit exists, these deductions apply equally to women and are commonly underutilised:

| Section | Benefit | Limit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Section 80C | EPF, PPF, ELSS, LIC, tuition fees | Rs. 1,50,000 |
| Section 80D | Health insurance premium | Rs. 25,000 (Rs. 50,000 for senior citizens) |
| Section 80E | Education loan interest | Full interest for 8 years |
| Section 80CCD(1B) | NPS voluntary contribution | Rs. 50,000 additional |
| Section 10(13A) | HRA exemption if renting | Actual, 40%/50% of salary, or rent minus 10% of salary |
| Section 54/54F | Capital gains exemption on property sale | Reinvestment in new residential property |

## Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) — An Exclusive Benefit

While not a tax "slab" benefit, SSY is a government scheme exclusively for the girl child. Contributions by parents qualify under Section 80C, and the maturity amount is **fully tax-free** (EEE status). This is the only scheme with an exclusive gender link in Indian tax law.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Is the New Tax Regime better for women with no deductions?**
Yes, especially at incomes up to Rs. 12 lakh — the Section 87A rebate makes tax liability zero. For income above Rs. 12 lakh, compare both regimes based on actual deductions.

**Q2. A working woman whose husband also works — can she claim deductions on joint property?**
Yes. Both co-borrowers on a home loan can independently claim Section 24(b) interest (up to Rs. 2 lakh each for self-occupied property) and Section 80C principal deduction (up to Rs. 1.5 lakh each).

**Q3. Is there any state-level tax benefit for women in India?**
State governments may offer property registration fee concessions for women buyers in some states (e.g., lower stamp duty in Delhi, UP). These are not income tax benefits but are real financial advantages worth checking locally.
