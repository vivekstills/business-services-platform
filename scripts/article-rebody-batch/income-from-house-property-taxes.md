## Overview

Income from house property is one of the **five heads of income** under the Income Tax Act, 1961. It covers income earned from any building or land appurtenant to it — whether from rent or notionally (for self-occupied or deemed let-out properties).

> 📌 **Key principle:** Even if a property is not rented, it may still generate taxable "income" — this is called **Annual Value**.

## What Is Annual Value?

The Annual Value (AV) is the basis for computing house property income. It is determined as:

**Annual Value = Higher of (Actual rent received) OR (Fair rent of the property)**

Exception: For a **self-occupied property**, the Annual Value is **NIL** — meaning no income is taxable, but you can still claim interest deductions.

## Types of Properties and Tax Treatment

| Property Type | Annual Value | Deductions Available |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Self-Occupied (only 1 property) | NIL | Interest on home loan up to Rs. 2,00,000 |
| Let-Out Property | Actual rent received (after vacancy deduction) | Standard deduction (30%) + Interest (no cap) |
| Deemed Let-Out (2nd or additional property) | Deemed fair rent (even if not rented) | Standard deduction (30%) + Interest (no cap) |

> ⚠️ From FY 2019-20 onwards, you can claim **2 self-occupied properties** as NIL Annual Value. Any third (or more) property is deemed let-out.

## How to Calculate House Property Income

### Step-by-Step:

1. **Determine Gross Annual Value (GAV):** Higher of actual rent or fair rent, minus vacancy allowance
2. **Deduct Municipal Taxes paid** → = **Net Annual Value (NAV)**
3. **Deduct Standard Deduction (30% of NAV)** — fixed, regardless of actual expenses
4. **Deduct Home Loan Interest** (Section 24(b)):
   - Self-occupied: up to Rs. 2,00,000
   - Let-out: full interest amount (no cap)
5. Result = **Income / Loss from House Property**

## Example Calculation

Priya owns a let-out apartment. Annual rent received: Rs. 3,00,000. Municipal taxes paid: Rs. 12,000. Home loan interest: Rs. 1,80,000.

| Step | Amount (Rs.) |
| --- | --- |
| Gross Annual Value | 3,00,000 |
| Less: Municipal Taxes | (12,000) |
| Net Annual Value | 2,88,000 |
| Less: Standard Deduction (30%) | (86,400) |
| Less: Home Loan Interest | (1,80,000) |
| **Income from House Property** | **21,600** |

## Loss from House Property

If deductions (interest) exceed income, the result is a **loss from house property**:
- Can be set off against other income heads (salary, business) up to **Rs. 2,00,000 per year**
- Remaining unabsorbed loss can be **carried forward for 8 years**

> ⚠️ The Rs. 2,00,000 set-off limit applies to self-occupied property loss. For let-out properties, the full interest is deductible but set-off against other heads is still capped at Rs. 2 lakh.

## New Tax Regime Impact

Under the **New Tax Regime** (Section 115BAC), you **cannot claim** the standard deduction (30%) or home loan interest deduction under Section 24(b) for self-occupied properties. Only the actual rental income is taxable; municipal tax deduction is still permitted.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. My property is vacant the entire year — do I pay tax on deemed rent?**
Yes, if it is your second or additional property, a deemed annual value applies even if the property is vacant. However, if the property is genuinely vacant throughout the year and you tried but failed to rent it, relief under Section 23(1)(c) may apply — consult a CA.

**Q2. Can I claim home loan interest for an under-construction property?**
Yes, but differently: pre-construction interest is allowed as a deduction in 5 equal annual instalments starting from the year of completion, subject to the Rs. 2,00,000 cap for self-occupied properties.

**Q3. My spouse and I jointly own a property. How do we split income/deductions?**
Each co-owner reports their proportionate share of income and deductions (including home loan interest) in their individual ITRs, based on their ownership ratio.
