## Overview

Section 44BBD is a **presumptive taxation provision** for non-resident companies engaged in the business of **civil construction or erection/testing/commissioning** of plant or machinery in connection with a **turnkey power project** in India.

This section was introduced to simplify the tax treatment of large foreign engineering companies undertaking power sector projects in India, removing the need to maintain detailed account books.

## Scope and Applicability

| Criterion | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Who | Non-resident company |
| Activity | Civil construction OR erection/testing/commissioning of plant/machinery |
| Project type | Turnkey power project approved by Central Government |
| Presumptive income rate | **10% of gross receipts** |

> ℹ️ This is distinct from Section 44BB (oil and gas) and Section 44BBB (civil construction for infrastructure other than power). Section 44BBD specifically covers **power projects**.

## How the Tax Is Computed

The income deemed to arise from qualifying activities = **10% of the amount paid or payable (in or from India) to the non-resident**.

This 10% is the net presumptive income — no deductions for expenses, depreciation, or allowances are permitted over and above this figure.

> ✅ This simplification avoids complex attribution of costs and profit allocation exercises for large project companies.

## TDS Implications

The payer (Indian entity) is generally required to deduct tax at source under Section **195** when making payments to the non-resident, unless a lower or nil withholding certificate is obtained. The applicable rate depends on the nature of payment and any beneficial provision under the applicable **Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement (DTAA)**.

Always verify:
- Whether the payment qualifies as fees for technical services or business income under the treaty
- Beneficial DTAA rate vs. domestic withholding rate
- Form 15CA / 15CB compliance for foreign remittances

## Opting Out of Presumptive Income

Where books of account are maintained and the taxpayer prefers to compute income under normal provisions, Section 44BBD may not apply — subject to documentation, transfer-pricing rules, and section-specific conditions. Compare regular computation vs. 10% presumption each year with professional advice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can a non-resident opt out of Section 44BBD and offer income under normal provisions?**
Often yes if books are maintained and regular computation is more beneficial — subject to documentation, Section 44BBD conditions, and anti-abuse rules. Confirm with your CA before filing.

**Q2. Does GST apply on top of income computed under Section 44BBD?**
GST is levied separately based on supply of goods/services and place-of-supply rules. Coordinate GST invoicing and income-tax reporting.

**Q3. Is advance tax payable on presumptive income under 44BBD?**
If estimated tax liability crosses statutory thresholds, advance tax obligations may arise; failure can attract interest under Sections **234B** and **234C**.
