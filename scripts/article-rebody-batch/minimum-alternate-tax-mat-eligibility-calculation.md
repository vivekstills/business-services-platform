## What Is MAT?

Minimum Alternate Tax (MAT) is a provision under **Section 115JB** that ensures companies pay a minimum level of tax, even if their regular income tax liability is zero or very low due to deductions, exemptions, or losses being carried forward.

> 📌 **MAT applies to companies only** — not to individuals, partnerships, LLPs, or sole proprietors.

## Why MAT Was Introduced

Many profitable companies used to report very low or zero taxable income by using:
- Depreciation adjustments
- Accumulated losses
- Exemptions and deductions

MAT ensures these companies still pay at least **15% tax on their Book Profit** (as per company P&L accounts prepared under the Companies Act).

## How MAT Is Calculated

**MAT = 15% × Book Profit** (plus surcharge and cess)

**Book Profit** starts from the Net Profit in the P&L account and then adjustments are made — adding back certain deductions (like depreciation under the Income Tax Act) and removing certain exemptions.

| Step | Description |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Start with Net Profit per financial statements |
| 2 | Add back: Income Tax provision, reserves, deferred tax, dividends, depreciation under Companies Act |
| 3 | Deduct: Depreciation as per Companies Act, brought-forward losses or depreciation (whichever is lower) |
| 4 | Result = Book Profit |
| 5 | MAT = 15% × Book Profit |

## When Does MAT Apply?

| Situation | Tax Payable |
| --- | --- |
| Regular tax > MAT | Pay regular tax (MAT does not apply) |
| Regular tax < MAT | Pay MAT (Section 115JB kicks in) |

## MAT Credit

If a company pays MAT in a year, it earns **MAT Credit** — the excess of MAT over regular tax. This credit can be carried forward for **15 years** and set off against future tax liability when regular tax exceeds MAT.

> ✅ **MAT Credit prevents double taxation** — you effectively get a refund of the MAT premium in future profitable years.

## Companies Exempt from MAT

- Infrastructure companies (power generation, distribution, transmission)
- Companies opting for the concessional tax rate under Section 115BAA (22%) or 115BAB (15% for new manufacturing companies) — they are completely outside the MAT framework

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. What is the MAT rate for FY 2025-26?**
The MAT rate is **15%** of Book Profit (plus applicable surcharge and 4% cess). It was reduced from 18.5% in the Union Budget 2019.

**Q2. Can a loss-making company still be liable to pay MAT?**
Yes, if the company has a positive Book Profit despite a loss in taxable income. Book profit adds back many deductions that reduce taxable income.

**Q3. Do foreign companies pay MAT?**
MAT provisions apply to foreign companies only in certain situations. Foreign companies with a tax rate lower than 15% under a DTAA may be exempt from MAT.
