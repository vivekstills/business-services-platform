## Overview

Section 44AD of the Income Tax Act, 1961 allows **small businesses** to declare income at a fixed percentage of gross turnover, without maintaining detailed books of accounts. This dramatically reduces compliance burden for traders, retailers, and small service providers.

> 📌 The scheme is optional — you choose to opt in. Once you opt in for an AY, you are bound by it for 5 consecutive years. If you opt out within 5 years, you cannot use the scheme again for the next 5 years.

## Who Can Use Section 44AD?

### Eligible:
- Resident individuals
- Resident HUFs
- Resident partnership firms (excluding LLPs)

### Not Eligible:
- Companies, LLPs, non-resident Indians
- Professionals (doctors, lawyers, CAs, architects) — they must use Section 44ADA
- Businesses earning commission or brokerage income
- Businesses engaged in transportation (use Section 44AE)
- Taxpayers carrying forward business losses from prior years

## Turnover Limit

| Condition | Turnover Limit |
| --- | --- |
| Standard | Up to Rs. 2 crore per FY |
| Extended (if 95%+ receipts/payments are digital) | Up to Rs. 3 crore per FY |

## Presumptive Income Rate

| Mode of Receipts | Declared Income |
| --- | --- |
| Cash transactions (any cash receipt/payment) | 8% of turnover |
| Fully digital/account payee (95%+ digital) | 6% of turnover |

You declare the higher of: the presumptive rate OR actual profits. If actual profits are higher, you must report actual.

## Example Calculation

Ramesh runs a hardware shop with Rs. 1.5 crore annual turnover, fully digital payments.

| Item | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Turnover | Rs. 1,50,00,000 |
| Presumptive income @ 6% | Rs. 9,00,000 |
| Tax (30% slab, old regime after deductions) | ~Rs. 1,87,200 |

Ramesh pays no advance tax (small liability), files ITR-4, and does not need a CA for audit.

## Key Compliance Points

> ✅ **No tax audit required** if you use Section 44AD (as long as declared income ≥ presumptive rate).

> ⚠️ **Advance tax:** Taxpayers under 44AD must pay the entire advance tax by **15 March** in a single instalment (not quarterly).

> ⚠️ **No expense deduction:** Under 44AD, no separate deductions for rent, depreciation, or salaries are permitted — the 6%/8% already accounts for all business expenses. Only Sections 80C–80U personal deductions remain available.

## Mistakes to Avoid

1. Opting in for 44AD one year and opting out the next without understanding the 5-year lock-in consequences.
2. Using 44AD for professional income (e.g., a consultant or doctor) — they must use 44ADA.
3. Declaring below the 6%/8% rate without maintaining proper books — this triggers a mandatory tax audit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can I use 44AD if my turnover exceeds Rs. 2 crore but is under Rs. 3 crore?**
Only if 95% or more of your receipts and payments are through banking/digital channels. Otherwise, the limit is Rs. 2 crore.

**Q2. If I declare 6% under 44AD but my actual profit is 4%, am I liable to pay tax on 6%?**
Yes. You must declare at least the presumptive rate. If actual income is lower, you can declare actual income but then a tax audit becomes mandatory.

**Q3. Can a partnership firm use 44AD?**
Yes, resident partnership firms (not LLPs) can use Section 44AD.
