## What Is Surcharge?

Surcharge is an additional tax levied **on top of income tax** when income exceeds specified thresholds. It is not a deduction — it increases your total tax liability.

**Formula:** Surcharge = (Income Tax before surcharge) × Surcharge Rate

## Surcharge Rates for AY 2026-27

### New Tax Regime

| Total Income | Surcharge Rate |
| --- | --- |
| Up to Rs. 50 lakh | Nil |
| Rs. 50 lakh to Rs. 1 crore | 10% |
| Rs. 1 crore to Rs. 2 crore | 15% |
| Rs. 2 crore to Rs. 5 crore | 25% |
| Above Rs. 5 crore | 25% (capped — not 37%) |

> ✅ The New Regime **caps surcharge at 25%** even above Rs. 5 crore — a key advantage over the Old Regime for ultra-high earners.

### Old Tax Regime

| Total Income | Surcharge Rate |
| --- | --- |
| Up to Rs. 50 lakh | Nil |
| Rs. 50 lakh to Rs. 1 crore | 10% |
| Rs. 1 crore to Rs. 2 crore | 15% |
| Rs. 2 crore to Rs. 5 crore | 25% |
| Above Rs. 5 crore | 37% |

## Marginal Relief — What It Is and Why It Matters

When income crosses a surcharge threshold by a small amount, the extra tax from surcharge can **exceed the extra income earned**. Marginal relief prevents this.

**Marginal relief ensures:** The additional tax payable due to crossing a threshold ≤ the additional income that caused the threshold breach.

### Example:

Anika earns Rs. 50,10,000 (just Rs. 10,000 above the Rs. 50 lakh threshold).

| Without marginal relief | With marginal relief |
| --- | --- |
| Surcharge = 10% on entire tax | Surcharge limited so extra tax ≤ Rs. 10,000 (extra income) |
| Creates cliff-edge effect | Smooth transition |

> ✅ Marginal relief is automatically calculated — you don't need to claim it separately. It is applied by the income tax portal when you file.

## Practical Planning Around Surcharge

> ⚠️ If your income is Rs. 50.5L to Rs. 52L, consider whether additional deductions can bring it below Rs. 50L — saving up to Rs. 1.5L in surcharge.

> ✅ For income between Rs. 50L and Rs. 52L, the effective benefit of reducing income below Rs. 50L is disproportionately high. Employer NPS contributions (80CCD(2)) or additional 80C investments may help.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Is surcharge applicable on capital gains tax too?**
Yes. Surcharge applies to the total income including capital gains, subject to special rates. However, surcharge on LTCG (equity) and STCG (equity) under Sections 112A and 111A is capped at 15%.

**Q2. Does health and education cess apply after surcharge?**
Yes. The 4% Health and Education Cess is calculated on (income tax + surcharge).

**Q3. Does surcharge apply in the New Tax Regime too?**
Yes, but the New Regime caps surcharge at 25% even above Rs. 5 crore, compared to 37% in the Old Regime. For incomes above Rs. 2 crore, the New Regime offers lower surcharge.
