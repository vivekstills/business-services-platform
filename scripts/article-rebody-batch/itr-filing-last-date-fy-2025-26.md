## Due Dates for FY 2025-26

| Category of Taxpayer | Due Date |
| --- | --- |
| Individuals, HUFs, firms not requiring audit | **31 July 2026** |
| Taxpayers requiring audit (businesses, professionals above threshold) | **31 October 2026** |
| Transfer pricing cases (international transactions) | **30 November 2026** |
| Revised return (to correct a filed return) | 31 December 2026 |
| Belated return (filing after original deadline) | 31 December 2026 |
| Updated return (ITR-U, to disclose missed income) | 2 years from end of AY |

> **Note:** These dates are as per standard provisions. The CBDT may extend specific deadlines by notification — always check the Income Tax portal for the latest updates.

## What Happens If You Miss the Deadline

> ⚠️ **Late filing fee under Section 234F:**
> - Income ≤ Rs. 5 lakh: Rs. 1,000
> - Income > Rs. 5 lakh: Rs. 5,000

Additional consequences of late filing:
- You **cannot carry forward** most losses (business losses, capital losses) to future years
- Interest under Section 234A applies on any unpaid tax
- Refunds are delayed
- Higher risk of receiving notices

## Who Must File Regardless of Income

Even if your income is below the taxable limit, you must file if:
- You have foreign assets or income
- TDS has been deducted and you want a refund
- You spent more than Rs. 2 lakh on foreign travel
- You paid electricity bills exceeding Rs. 1 lakh in the year
- You deposited more than Rs. 1 crore in bank accounts

## Practical Tips

> ✅ File by 31 July even if you have a refund due — earlier filing means earlier refund.

> ✅ If your books need audit, ensure your auditor completes the audit report before 30 September (Form 3CA/3CB/3CD must be filed before the ITR).

> ✅ Use the updated return (ITR-U) if you missed declaring any income — it can be filed within 2 years of the assessment year end.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can I file a revised return after 31 December 2026?**
No. Revised returns for FY 2025-26 must be filed by 31 December 2026. After that date, only an updated return (ITR-U) under Section 139(8A) is possible, with an additional 25–50% tax on the undisclosed income.

**Q2. Is there any penalty if my tax liability is zero but I file late?**
No monetary penalty under Section 234F if your total income is below the basic exemption limit (Rs. 3 lakh under New Regime for FY 2025-26). However, other consequences (no loss carry forward, no refund claim) still apply.

**Q3. What if I miss both the original and revised return deadlines?**
File an Updated Return (ITR-U) under Section 139(8A) within 2 years from the end of the relevant AY. You will need to pay an additional 25% tax on the updated income (50% if filed after 1 year from AY end).
